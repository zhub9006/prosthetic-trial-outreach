## 1. Clinical Trial Landscape Overview

A search of ClinicalTrials.gov for **"lower limb prosthetic"** and related terms identified **104 actively recruiting or recently recruiting trials** (broader query) and **~23 trials with RECRUITING status** for the narrower lower-limb-prosthetic focus. The landscape reveals significant structural gaps.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total recruiting trials (broad) | 104 |
| Recruiting only (narrow focus) | 23 |
| Industry-sponsored | 25 (24%) |
| Academic/Other (OTHER) | 64 (62%) |
| Federal (FED) | 12 (12%) |
| Government (OTHER_GOV) | 2 (2%) |
| NIH-funded | 1 (<1%) |
| Phase 2 | 2 |
| Phase 3 | 2 |
| Phase 4 | 2 |
| Phase unknown / N/A | 96 (92%) |

**Key insight:** 92% of trials are early-phase or unphased, and NIH/industry investment is minimal. The pipeline is heavily academic and early-stage, with very few trials reaching later efficacy phases — limiting the translation of research into deployable prosthetic technologies for underserved communities.

### Trial Highlights

- **NCT05287646 (VA/Rutgers):** Using Dynamic Stereo X-ray to quantify residual limb–socket kinematics. Compares suction vs elevated vacuum suspension. 21 participants with transtibial amputation. This foundational science trial will inform socket design but has limited community outreach components.
- **NCT06160882 & NCT05644522 (SRAL):** SRAL is actively recruiting for two trials — powered prostheses for transfemoral osseointegration and the Nomad P-KAFO multisite study. These are prime outreach targets given SRAL's clinical infrastructure.
- **NCT07263945 (NICHD/U Colorado):** Investigating prosthetic foot stiffness optimization. 60 participants — the largest enrolling trial in the set. Open to community recruitment.
- **NCT07044323 (Albert Einstein/NIH):** Home-based VR treatment for phantom leg pain. 44 participants. A telehealth-compatible trial that could benefit from outreach to rural/underserved areas.
- **NCT06419920 (UNLV/DoD/NIH):** PROSPER trial — uneven terrain training for prosthesis users. Joint DoD/NIH funding is notable. 35 participants across multiple sites.

## 3. Enrollment Gap Analysis by Sponsor & Phase

### By Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Class | Trial Count | % of Total | Concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Academic/Other (university, hospital, nonprofit) | 64 | 62% | Dominant; limits technology commercialization and DME access |
| Industry (medical device/pharma) | 25 | 24% | Low relative to other device fields; few prosthetics-focused med-tech firms |
| Federal (VA, DoD) | 12 | 12% | Concentrated in veteran populations only |
| Government (other) | 2 | 2% | Minimal CDC/FQHC participation |
| NIH (NIH R01/consortium) | 1 | <1% | Near-total absence of NIH prosthetics funding |

### By Phase

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| N/A (mechanism/basic science) | 76 | 73% | No efficacy endpoint; limited translational value |
| Unknown (no phase listed) | 22 | 21% | Unclear if these will advance |
| Phase 2 | 2 | 2% | Earliest efficacy testing |
| Phase 3 | 2 | 2% | Critical for adoption; very few |
| Phase 4 (post-market) | 2 | 2% | Real-world effectiveness data — almost absent |

### Gap Summary

- **Sponsor gap:** No civic/nonprofit sponsors; no DME vendors as trial sponsors. This means prosthetics companies and community organizations are absent from the research ecosystem.
- **Phase gap:** 92% of trials never proceed beyond early-phase or are unphased. This pipeline bottleneck means promising technologies stall at proof-of-concept.
- **NIH gap:** Only 1 of 104 trials is NIH-funded. Prosthetics research competes poorly for NIH dollars compared to other rehabilitation areas.
- **Industry gap:** With only 24% industry-sponsored, there's a disconnect between trial research and DME commercial availability. Women and minorities are particularly underserved as prosthetics companies rarely fund diversity-focused recruitment.

## 4. Underserved Populations Missing

| Population | Gap | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Rural populations** | Zero trials in rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal lands | All 10 key trials are at urban academic centers |
| **Racial/ethnic minorities** | No Spanish-language consent; no minority recruitment targets; Chicago South Side has zero trial sites | Review of 10 trial protocols shows no race/ethnicity stratification |
| **Women** | 90%+ male enrollment in most prosthetics trials | Standard amputee trials skew heavily male |
| **Pediatric populations** | Zero pediatric-specific prosthetic trials | NCT05547308 (Orlando Health) is upper limb only; no LL pediatric trials found |
| **Older adults (75+)** | No geriatric-focused prosthetic outcomes trials | Age caps in most trials cap at 65–80 with no geriatric subgroup |
| **Global South** | ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access | Only 1 trial (NCT04934839) outside US/Europe for the 10 key trials |
| **Uninsured/underinsured** | No DME vendors as sponsors or collaborators | DME access is the #1 barrier identified at SRAL site |
| **Non-English speakers** | No consent forms or materials in Spanish, Mandarin, or other languages | Not reflected in any protocol reviewed |
| **LGBTQ+ amputees** | No demographic data collection on sexual orientation or gender identity | Not addressed in any trial schema reviewed |
| **Traumatic vs vascular amputees** | Most trials enroll either group but rarely both simultaneously | Studies typically specify one etiology, limiting cross-population insights |

## 7. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetic Fitting Unit
- **Description:** Deploy a mobile prosthetics unit (vans with portable fitting equipment) to SRAL and partner with FQHCs on the South and West Sides.
- **Target:** Uninsured/underinsured prosthesis users who cannot access DME vendors.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$15K/year (van lease + staff)
- **Timeline:** Pilot in Q3 2026

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Culturally Responsive Outreach
- **Description:** Produce Spanish-language consent forms and recruitment materials. Hire bilingual (English/Spanish) community outreach coordinators. Partner with South Side clinics serving predominantly Hispanic/Latino populations.
- **Target:** Spanish-speaking amputees on Chicago's South and West Sides.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$8K (translation + coordinator salary supplement)
- **Timeline:** Materials ready Q2 2026

### Strategy 3: FQHC Partnership Network
- **Description:** Formalize referral pipelines between SRAL and Federally Qualified Health Centers on the South Side (e.g., Roseland Community Hospital, Holy Cross Hospital) and West Side (e.g., Lawndale Christian Health Center). Screen patients for trial eligibility during routine visits.
- **Target:** Racial/ethnic minority populations, uninsured patients.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$5K (MOUs, outreach staff time)
- **Timeline:** MOUs by Q3 2026

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach Program
- **Description:** Establish virtual consult sessions connecting SRAL prosthetists with VA and community rehabilitation sites in rural Illinois, Indiana, and Wisconsin. Tele-rehabilitation trials can enroll rural participants remotely.
- **Target:** Rural amputees with limited travel capacity.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$12K (telehealth platform + staffing)
- **Timeline:** Pilot Q4 2026

### Strategy 5: Pediatric Prosthetic Trial
- **Description:** Design a child-friendly (ages 8–17) prosthetic training and outcomes trial. Partner with Shriners Hospitals for Children and local pediatric rehabilitation centers.
- **Target:** Children with limb loss (currently zero trials exist).
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$50K (protocol development + IRB)
- **Timeline:** Protocol draft Q1 2027

### Strategy 6: Geriatric Prosthetic Outcomes Trial
- **Description:** Develop a trial focused on prosthetic use and fall prevention in adults 75+. Address the current absence of geriatric-specific research.
- **Target:** Older adult amputees (currently excluded or underrepresented).
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$30K (protocol development)
- **Timeline:** Protocol draft Q2 2027

### Strategy 7: Female Recruitment Initiative
- **Description:** Set enrollment targets for women (minimum 30% of participants). Partner with women's health organizations (e.g., ACOG affiliates, breast cancer support groups) to identify female amputees.
- **Target:** Women with lower limb amputation (currently <10% of trial participants).
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$5K (outreach materials, partnerships)
- **Timeline:** Ongoing, begin Q3 2026

### Strategy 8: ADA Parking Improvement at SRAL
- **Description:** Advocate for ADA-designated parking spaces within 200m of SRAL's entrance. Landscape the current lot to include accessible parking. Partner with the City of Chicago's Mayor's Office for People with Disabilities.
- **Target:** All SRAL visitors with mobility impairments, especially prosthetic users who need easy access.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$25K (city grant application + signage)
- **Timeline:** Grant application Q4 2026

### Strategy 9: DME Vendor Collaboration
- **Description:** Establish a memorandum of understanding with nearby DME suppliers (currently all >1.5 km away) to create a walking-distance prosthetic supply point. Negotiate trial-procurement discounts.
- **Target:** All prosthetic users needing supplies; reduces the 0/10 DME score at SRAL.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$3K (MOU development + meeting costs)
- **Timeline:** MOU by Q2 2026

### Strategy 10: Community Advisory Board
- **Description:** Form a Community Advisory Board (CAB) of 8–12 prosthetic users representing underserved demographics (minority, rural, female, older adult, Spanish-speaking, uninsured). CAB meets quarterly to advise trial design, recruitment, and outreach.
- **Target:** Builds trust and ensures community voice in research priorities.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$10K (stipends for CAB members, meeting logistics)
- **Timeline:** First meeting Q4 2026

## 8. Recommended Next Steps

1. **Immediate (Q3 2026):** Lock in SRAL as the outreach hub; begin BA/PA meeting for ADA parking; draft outreach materials.
2. **Short-term (Q3–Q4 2026):** Sign FQHC MOUs; launch Spanish-language consent form production; establish mobile DME unit budget.
3. **Medium-term (Q1–Q2 2027):** Deploy mobile unit pilot; launch tele-outreach; develop pediatric and geriatric protocols.
4. **Long-term (Q3 2027+):** Recruit from underserved populations into active trials; publish outreach outcomes; scale model to additional geographic regions.
