### 1.4 Geographic Distribution & Underserved Gaps

| Region | Trial Count | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **United States** | ~111 | Highest volume, but concentrated in coastal academic medical centers |
| **France** | 11 | Active research on gait analysis & locomotor modeling |

| **Italy, Belgium** | 2–3 | Fringe representation |
| **Middle East (Gaza)** | 1 (NCT07706452) | Only interventional trial in a conflict-affected setting |

**Underserved populations missing from the trial landscape:**

1. **Rural populations** — Zero trials list rural or Federally Qualified Health Center sites; all are urban academic hubs.
2. **Pediatric populations** — No trials target upper- or lower-limb amputation in children/adolescents.
3. **Non-English speakers** — All consent materials and protocols are in English only. No Spanish, Arabic, or Mandarin language accommodations.
4. **Low-income/underinsured** — Industry registers (PRO APP) and federal trials assume insurance coverage and transportation access.
5. **Veterans** — Only one trial (NCT04784429) mentions veteran status in demographics; VA sites are not listed as recruitment venues.
6. **People with bilateral amputations** — Rarely studied; most trials exclude or under-sample bilateral cases.

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetic Advisory Unit
**Problem:** Zero DME/p&o vendors within 500 m. Uninsured and underinsured prosthetic users cannot access devices locally.
**Solution:** Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetics van staffed by a licensed prosthetist, offering:
- Device fitting consultations
- Insurance navigation assistance
- Donation-certification for used limbs
**Estimated cost:** ~$15,000/year (vehicle lease + staffing). **Target:** 30–50 new patients per year.

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Culturally Responsive Recruitment
**Problem:** Zero non-English consent forms; all trial materials are English-only. Chicago's Latino and Arabic communities are excluded.
**Solution:**
- Translate all screening forms, consent docs, and post-trial surveys into Spanish, Arabic, and Mandarin
- Hire 2 bilingual community outreach coordinators (Spanish-speaking + Arabic-speaking)
- Partner with Chicago's immigrant-serving community centers (e.g., Assata's Daughters, National Sampaguita, Arabic American Family Services)

### Strategy 3: FQHC & Safety-Net Clinic Partnerships
**Problem:** All current trials recruit from academic medical centers or urban clinics; rural and low-income populations are invisible.
**Solution:**
- Establish formal referral agreements with 3–5 FQHCs on the South and West sides (e.g., Atlantan Community Health, Roseland Community Hospital, Lawndale Christian Health Center)
- Train FQHC staff to screen for prosthetic trial eligibility
- Provide bus-token incentives for participants traveling from South/West Side to Streeterville

### Strategy 4: Rural & Veteran Tele-Outreach
**Problem:** No rural trial sites exist; only one trial mentions veterans.
**Solution:**
- Launch a tele-consult program (video visits) in partnership with the Jesse Brown VA Medical Center and rural Illinois critical-access hospitals
- Send screening packets by mail; complete baseline assessments via phone or tablet
- Target: 20–30 rural/veteran participants in the first 18 months

### Strategy 5: Pediatric Prosthetic Trial Design
**Problem:** Zero pediatric lower-limb prosthetic trials on ClinicalTrials.gov.
**Solution:**
- Circulate a request for concepts to the SRAL pediatric rehab team and local children's hospitals (Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital)
- Develop a child-friendly consent process with parent/guardian co-consent
- Partner with Chicago Parks District recreational therapy programs for recruitment

### Strategy 6: Parking & Transit Accessibility Improvements
**Problem:** No ADA parking spots within 200 m; transit connections require 1–2 bus transfers.
**Solution:**
- Negotiate a reserved ADA parking bay in the Doubletree garage (~162 m from SRAL entrance)
- Sponsor a dedicated CTA shuttle loop between Grand Red Line station and SRAL during trial visit hours
- Add temporary drop-off zone at 355 E Erie St for wheelchair-accessible vehicles
