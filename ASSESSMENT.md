### Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** Rarely reported in trial demographics. NCT04427007 enrolled 100% White participants. Most trials lack demographic transparency.
2. **Lower-Limb Specificity:** Many trials overlap with upper-limb prosthetics; several focus only on transtibial users, excluding transfemoral amputees (~40% of lower-limb amputees).
3. **Rural Access:** No trials based in rural Midwest or Southern US. Most sites cluster in major metros (Chicago, Boston, UK, France). Rural amputees face 50+ mile travel burdens.
4. **Age Range:** Many trials cap enrollment at 60–65, excluding older adults who represent a large and growing portion of the amputee population.
5. **Female Representation:** Sex-specific enrollment often not reported; several cohorts are male-skewed (e.g., VA-based studies).
6. **Insurance/Financial Barriers:** Industry trials often require specific insurance coverage or self-pay for devices, creating access barriers for low-income populations.
7. **Phase Gap:** 95% in NA/Unknown phases means very few interventions have undergone rigorous later-stage testing before market release — underserved groups are disproportionately affected.

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 1. Community-Based Screening Events
- **Target:** Rural populations, older adults, racial/ethnic minorities, low-income individuals
- **Approach:** Mobile screening stations at community centers, churches, VA facilities
- **AbilityLab as hub:** Leverage world-class reputation and infrastructure for satellite events

### 2. Tele-Trial Participation Program
- Hybrid model: in-person AbilityLab visits + telehealth check-ins
- Travel stipends and lodging partnerships for participants >50 miles from Chicago
- Virtual arm of REINVENT trial (NCT07666204) could serve as model

### 3. Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
- Match prosthetic device companies (Össur, Stryker, Ottobock, Liberating Technologies) with underserved trial sites
- Pro bono device donations from manufacturers for community trials
- Address the 14% industry sponsorship gap by requiring diversity benchmarks

### 4. Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks
- Mandate demographic reporting in all trial protocols (race, ethnicity, sex, age, income)
- Set minimum diversity targets: ≥25% female, ≥30% minority enrollment
- Public dashboard tracking trial demographics across all active lower-limb studies

### 5. Patient Navigation & Transportation Services
- Dedicated patient navigator for trial eligibility, enrollment, and retention
- Partner with Divvy (adapted cycles), CTA (reduced-fare cards), ride-share (Uber Health, Lyft Access)
- Shuttle service from Red Line station to SRAL for participants with mobility limitations

### 6. Older Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming
- Advocate for broader age ranges in trial protocols (currently capped at 60–80 in many studies)
- Caregiver stipends and on-site support
- Senior-friendly scheduling and extended visit windows

### 7. Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers
- Partner with VA hospitals and Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) in rural Midwest and South
- Deploy mobile prosthetics labs to rural locations — there are currently **zero** rural-based trials
- Use the Chicago Lakeside VA Clinic (350m from SRAL) as a satellite recruitment site

### 8. Data Transparency Dashboard
- Open-access dashboard for trial demographics and geographic coverage
- Community advisory board of prosthetic users from underserved communities
- Monthly public reports on enrollment diversity and trial accessibility

### 9. Address the Phase Gap
- Advocate for FDA guidance requiring Phase 3+ testing for prosthetic devices before market release
- Redirect a portion of the 299 "NA" phase trials toward rigorous efficacy studies
- Create a prosthetic-specific post-market surveillance registry
