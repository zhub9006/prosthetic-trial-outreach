**Key Finding:** Industry-sponsored trials represent only ~27% of active recruitment studies. This limits access to cutting-edge prosthetic technologies for underserved populations who may not qualify for or afford academic or government studies. Federally sponsored trials are concentrated at VA centers, which are geographically limited.

**Key Finding:** 98% of active lower-limb prosthetic trials have no formal phase designation (mostly device trials skipping early phases). Only one Phase 3 study exists. This indicates a significant pipeline gap — promising prosthetic technologies rarely undergo rigorous later-stage efficacy testing before market release, and regulatory-grade evidence for safety/effectiveness is thin.

### Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** Demographics rarely reported; many trials do not mandate minority recruitment.
2. **Rural Access:** Nearly all trials are based at major urban academic centers. No dedicated rural Midwest or Southern US sites.
3. **Age Range Restrictions:** Several trials cap enrollment at 60–70, excluding older adults who are a large segment of the amputee population.
4. **Female Representation:** Sex-specific enrollment often not reported; several cohorts are male-skewed.
5. **Lower-Limb Specificity:** Many focus exclusively on transtibial, excluding transfemoral users.
6. **Insurance & Financial Barriers:** Industry trials may require specific insurance or travel, creating access barriers for low-income populations.
7. **Phase Gap:** 98% in NA/Unknown phases means very few interventions have undergone rigorous later-stage testing.
8. **Geographic Concentration:** US dominates (187 studies), but most single-site at major metros; international representation limited.

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 1. Community-Based Screening Events
- **Target:** Rural populations, older adults, racial/ethnic minorities, low-income individuals
- **Approach:** Mobile screening stations at community centers, churches, VA facilities
- **AbilityLab as hub:** Leverage reputation and infrastructure for satellite events

### 2. Tele-Trial Participation Program
- Hybrid model: in-person AbilityLab visits + telehealth check-ins
- Travel stipends and lodging partnerships for participants >50 miles from Chicago

### 3. Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
- Match prosthetic device companies (Össur, Stryker, Ottobock, Hanger) with underserved trial sites
- Pro bono device donations from manufacturers to reduce cost barriers

### 4. Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks
- Mandate demographic reporting in all trial protocols
- Set minimum diversity targets (≥25% female, ≥30% minority enrollment)

### 5. Patient Navigation & Transportation Services
- Dedicated patient navigator for trial eligibility, enrollment, and completion
- Partner with Divvy (adapted cycles), CTA (reduced-fare cards), ride-share services
- Explore wheelchair-accessible van rentals

### 6. Older Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming
- Advocate for broader age ranges in trial protocols (remove 60–70 caps)
- Caregiver stipends and on-site support

### 7. Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers
- Partner with VA hospitals and FQHCs in rural Midwest and South
- Deploy mobile prosthetics labs to rural locations

### 8. Data Transparency Dashboard
- Open-access dashboard showing trial demographics and geographic coverage
- Community advisory board of prosthetic users from underserved communities
- Quarterly public reporting of enrollment diversity metrics
