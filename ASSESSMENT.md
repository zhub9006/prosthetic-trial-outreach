|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (academic/government)** | 313 | 63% | Dominant; often university-led single-site studies |
| **INDUSTRY** | 59 | 19% | Severely underrepresented; major gap for device access |
| **FED (federal)** | 41 | 15% | VA-led; concentrated at VA medical centers |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2% | Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs |
| **NIH** | 2 | 1% | Minimal NIH funding presence |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | 1% | Multi-site network trials |

### Key Finding: Industry Underrepresentation

Only **19%** of trials are industry-sponsored. This is a critical gap because:
- Industry trials often provide **free prosthetic devices** to participants
- Academic/federal trials rarely include device provision beyond standard care
- Underserved populations (low-income, uninsured) miss access to cutting-edge technology

### Key Finding: Phase Pipeline Gap

**96% of trials have no formal phase (NA + Unknown)**. This means:
- Very few prosthetic interventions advance to rigorous later-stage testing
- Promising technologies may reach market without robust Phase 3 evidence
- Undermines evidence-based prescribing for prosthetic users
- Especially limits guidance for underserved populations who rely on clinical evidence for insurance coverage

## 4. Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

### 4.1 Racial/Ethnic Diversity
- Rarely reported in trial demographics
- Example: NCT04427007 enrolled **100% White** participants
- No trials specifically target minority recruitment strategies

### 4.2 Lower-Limb Specificity
- Some trials overlap with upper-limb prosthetics
- Many focus exclusively on **transtibial** users, excluding **transfemoral** amputees
- Osseointegration and powered prosthesis trials are limited and small (NCT06160882: only 6 enrollees)

### 4.3 Rural Access
- **Zero trials** based in rural Midwest or Southern US
- Most sites concentrated in major metros: Chicago, Boston, UK, France
- Rural amputees (disproportionately older, lower-income, near-veteran populations) have no local trial access

### 4.4 Age Range Barriers
- Many trials cap enrollment at **60–65 years**
- Older adults represent a **large portion** of the amputee population
- Excluding older adults limits generalizability and access for those with highest prosthetic need

### 4.5 Female Representation
- Sex-specific enrollment often **not reported**
- Several cohorts are **male-skewed**
- Prosthetic fit and outcome differences by sex are under-studied

### 4.6 Financial/Insurance Barriers
- Industry trials may require **specific insurance** for follow-up care
- Travel costs for multi-site trials disproportionately affect low-income participants
- No trials explicitly mention travel stipends or financial assistance

## 8. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community-Based Screening Events
- **Target:** Rural populations, older adults, racial/ethnic minorities, low-income individuals
- **Approach:** Mobile screening stations at community centers, churches, VA facilities
- **AbilityLab as hub:** Leverage global reputation and infrastructure for satellite events; host monthly "Prosthetic Trial Open Houses"

### Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation Program
- **Hybrid model:** In-person AbilityLab visits (quarterly) + telehealth check-ins (monthly)
- **Travel support:** Stipends and lodging partnerships for participants >50 miles from Chicago
- **Rationale:** Broader geographic reach; includes rural Midwest/South populations

### Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
- **Match companies with underserved sites:** Partner Össur, Stryker, Ottobock with under-resourced trial sites
- **Pro bono device donations:** Ensure trial participants receive devices regardless of insurance status
- **Community benefit:** Low-income prosthetic users gain access to premium devices through trial participation

### Strategy 4: Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks
- **Mandate demographic reporting** in all trial protocols
- **Set minimum diversity targets:** ≥25% female enrollment, ≥30% minority enrollment
- **Require age analysis:** Report outcomes by age group; remove arbitrary upper-age caps

### Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation Services
- **Dedicated navigator:** Guide prosthetic users through trial eligibility, enrollment, and completion
- **Transportation partnerships:** Divvy (adapted cycles), CTA (reduced-fare cards), ride-share vouchers
- **Shelter advocacy:** Petition CTA for shelters and tactile paving at key bus stops

### Strategy 6: Older Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming
- **Expand age ranges:** Advocate for removing 65+ caps in trial protocols
- **Caregiver stipends:** Compensate caregivers for trial participation time
- **On-site support:** Provide caregiver respite areas during AbilityLab visits

### Strategy 7: Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers
- **Partner with VA hospitals** and FQHCs in rural Midwest and Southern US
- **Deploy mobile prosthetics labs** to rural locations for screening and basic assessments
- **Leverage existing VA infrastructure:** Jesse Brown VA, Hines VA, and other federal sites

### Strategy 8: Data Transparency & Community Advisory Board
- **Open-access dashboard:** Track trial demographics, geographic coverage, and diversity metrics
- **Community advisory board:** Prosthetic users from underserved communities advise on trial design
- **Feedback loop:** Community members report trial access barriers → incorporated into study protocols
