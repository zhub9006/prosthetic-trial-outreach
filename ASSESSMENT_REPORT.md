## 3. Identified Gaps & Underserved Populations

### Critical Gaps

1. **Osseointegration trials** \u2014 Very few sites actively recruiting. The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is one of the leading centers for this, but trial pipeline is thin.
2. **Pediatric prosthetics** \u2014 Almost no active recruiting trials specifically for pediatric lower limb amputation.
3. **Targeted muscle reinnervation (TMR)** \u2014 Emerging technique with very limited trial access.
4. **Socket interface research** \u2014 The most common pain/comfort issue is under-researched due to small device companies.
5. **Racial/ethnic diversity** \u2014 Prosthetic trials historically under-enroll Black and Hispanic participants, despite higher amputation rates in these populations.
6. **Low-income/underinsured** \u2014 Trials often require frequent travel to distant sites, creating a geographic barrier.
7. **Women** \u2014 Prosthetic device designs and trial protocols often default to male body metrics.

### Moderate Gaps

8. **Rural access** \u2014 Most trials are clustered in major metro areas.
9. **Older adults (65+)** \u2014 Many trials have upper age limits; this population is growing.
10. **Bilateral amputees** \u2014 Often excluded from unilateral prosthetic trials.

## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: AbilityLab as a Trial Hub

- Leverage Shirley Ryan AbilityLab's existing clinical relationships to identify eligible patients currently in post-amputation rehabilitation.
- On-site trial navigation: Hire/partner with a clinical research coordinator stationed at AbilityLab to screen patients, explain trial options, and facilitate enrollment.
- Bi-weekly trial information sessions: Host informal gatherings in the AbilityLab lobby/common area where trial sponsors present their studies.

### Strategy 2: Community Transit Navigator

- Partner with CTA to ensure trial participants have reliable, ADA-accessible transit to trial sites.
- Create a transit pass subsidy program for patients traveling to distant trial locations.
- Map all trial sites within 15 miles of AbilityLab and provide clear transit directions.

### Strategy 3: Underserved Population Bridge

- Spanish-language outreach: Partner with local Spanish-speaking community organizations to recruit diverse participants.
- Veteran outreach: Coordinate with VA facilities and veteran service organizations; many VA studies for prosthetics are within driving distance.
- Rural tele-screening: Use telehealth for initial consent and screening, reducing travel burden for participants from outside Cook County.
- Community health worker model: Train bilingual community health workers to identify potential participants in South Side Chicago communities with high amputation rates.

### Strategy 4: Pediatric Pipeline

- Partner with Shriners Hospital for Children (also in Chicago) to identify pediatric amputation cases.
- Advocate for pediatric-specific trial protocols that accommodate growth and developmental needs.
- Host pediatric prosthetics showcase events at AbilityLab demonstrating new technologies.

### Strategy 5: Bilateral & Complex Amputation Advocacy

- Create a bilateral amputee peer support group at AbilityLab that also serves as a pipeline for complex trial enrollment.
- Work with device manufacturers to revise exclusion criteria that systematically exclude bilateral amputees.
- Fund a pilot study on bilateral prosthetic adaptation through AbilityLab's research department.

### Strategy 6: Data-Driven Gap Monitoring

- Maintain a live registry of all lower limb prosthetic trials on ClinicalTrials.gov, categorized by sponsor type, phase, and enrollment status.
- Quarterly gap analysis comparing trial geography against Illinois amputation demographics.
- Publish an annual outreach report tracking outreach metrics: diversity of enrolled participants, geographic distribution, access barriers identified.
