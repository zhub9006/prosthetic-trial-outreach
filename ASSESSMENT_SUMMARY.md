| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Key Observations |

| **OTHER (Academic/Nonprofit)** | 54 | 59.3% | Lead sponsor type, but many studies end as ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING |
| **INDUSTRY (Device/Pharma)** | 22 | 24.2% | Focused on commercial device trials (e.g., Otto Bock Taleo Adjust); limited community benefit |
| **FED (VA, NIH, DoD)** | 12 | 13.2% | VA-led trials dominate; veteran-exclusive eligibility excludes broader population |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 2 | 2.2% | Minor presence |
| **NIH (R01/UL1)** | 1 | 1.1 | **Severely underrepresented** — only 1 NIH-funded trial |

**🚨 Gap Identified:** Only **1.1% of lower limb prosthetic trials have NIH funding**. This creates a critical pipeline bottleneck — academic pilot studies lack resources to scale to diverse, underserved populations. Industry sponsors focus on profit-driven device trials, and federal sponsors (VA) limit enrollment to veterans.

### By Phase

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Key Observations |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (Not Applicable)** | 67 | 73.6% | Device studies/feasibility — no formal clinical phase progression |
| **Unknown** | 20 | 22.0% | Status unclear or unposted |
| **Phase 4** | 1 | 1.1% | Post-market surveillance only |
| **Phase 3** | 1 | 1.1% | Single large-scale confirmatory trial |
| **Phase 2** | 2 | 2.2% | Two dose/efficacy studies |

**🚨 Gap Identified:** **Over 95% of trials are in NA/Unknown phase** — the clinical pipeline is flat. Very few trials advance to Phase 2+ testing, meaning most innovation stays at the concept/early feasibility stage. No trials are in Phase 1 (safety/pilot) either, likely because device trials skip traditional phases.

### By Enrollment Status (of the 10 current trials)

**Key pattern:** Many trials start recruiting then transition to ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING, suggesting **enrollment bottleneck** — especially for underrepresented groups.

## Part 5: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: SRAL as a Community Prosthetic Hub
**Goal:** Position Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as the anchor site for prosthetic trial recruitment and community education.
- **Rationale:** 10/10 amenities score, dense healthcare/transit/parking, existing rehab expertise
- **Actions:** Weekly open-hours for prosthetic users; device demo days; multilingual flyers
- **Budget:** ~$5,000 for initial setup (signage, multilingual materials, outreach coordinator stipend)
- **Timeline:** Launch within 90 days

### Strategy 2: Targeted Recruitment for Underserved Groups
**Goal:** Overcome enrollment bottlenecks for bilateral, minority, rural, and female participants.
- **Actions:**
  - Partner with Chicago's South Side rehab centers (zero current trial sites)
  - Offer Spanish-language informed consent and bilingual study navigators
  - Provide transportation stipends (CTA passes, ride-share credits) for rural participants
  - Create a "community ambassador" program recruiting prosthetic users as peer recruiters
- **Budget:** ~$15,000 for transport stipends, translation services, ambassador compensation
- **Timeline:** 6-month pilot

### Strategy 3: Telehealth-Enabled Remote Enrollment
**Goal:** Reduce travel burden for rural and mobility-limited prosthetic users.
- **Rationale:** Trial NCT05884203 (VA) already proved remote limb-scanning works; this model can be extended to eligibility screening, consent, and follow-up visits.
- **Actions:**
  - Develop a 3-visit remote protocol (remote screening → in-person fitting → remote follow-up)
  - Partner with SRAL's biomechanics lab for remote gait assessment via video
  - Provide low-cost 3D scanners to community helpers (caregivers, prosthetists in rural areas)
- **Budget:** ~$25,000 for scanner procurement, video platform, training
- **Timeline:** 6-month pilot, 12-month expansion

### Strategy 4: Industry-Academic Partnerships for Device Access
**Goal:** Bridge the 24.2% industry / 59.3% academic gap by creating device-sharing programs.
- **Actions:**
  - Negotiate device loan programs with industry sponsors (e.g., Otto Bock, Össur, Fillauer)
  - Create a "prosthetic innovation lab" at SRAL where users can trial next-gen devices
  - Match industry sponsors with community-based recruitment sites in underserved areas
- **Budget:** ~$10,000 for lab setup and coordination
- **Timeline:** 12 months for partnership agreements

### Strategy 5: Pediatric & Congenital Prosthetics Pipeline
**Goal:** Address the complete absence of pediatric prosthetic trials.
- **Actions:**
  - Establish a partnership with children's hospitals (Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital, 20 miles away)
  - Develop age-appropriate consent/assent processes
  - Engage schools for children with upper/lower limb differences
- **Budget:** ~$20,000 for pediatric-adapted materials, child life specialist consultation
- **Timeline:** 18 months to first pediatric protocol

### Strategy 6: Multilingual & Culturally Competent Outreach
**Goal:** Eliminate language and cultural barriers to prosthetics research.
- **Actions:**
  - Translate all study materials into Spanish, Chinese, and Polish (top non-English languages in Chicago)
  - Train study staff in cultural competency for diverse amputation populations
  - Partner with community organizations (e.g., Catholic Charities, Alliance for the Great Lakes) for trusted outreach
- **Budget:** ~$8,000 for translation, training, and community partnerships
- **Timeline:** 6 months to launch first multilingual materials

## Summary: Key Assessment Conclusions

1. **Recruitment bottleneck is the primary crisis** — only 4 of 10 current trials are actively recruiting, and most quickly stall to ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING.
2. **Sponsor diversity gap** — NIH funding (1.1%), industry focus (24.2%), and VA exclusivity (13.2%) leave systemic gaps for underserved populations.
3. **Phase stagnation** — 95%+ of trials never advance past NA/Unknown phase; the clinical pipeline lacks rigor.
4. **Multiple underserved populations excluded** — bilateral amputees, minorities, rural users, women, children, people with comorbidities, and Global South populations are systematically absent.
5. **SRAL is an excellent site** — top-tier healthcare density, public transit, parking, and amenities make it ideal as a community outreach hub.
6. **Urgent need for telehealth, device-sharing, multilingual, and pediatric strategies** to close the enrollment and access gaps.
