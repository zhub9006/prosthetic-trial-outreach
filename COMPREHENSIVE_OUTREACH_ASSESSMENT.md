## 3. Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

### 3.1 Racial/Ethnic Diversity
- **Almost no trials report racial/ethnic demographics.** The 24.5K-mile adipocere study (NCT04427007) enrolled 100% White participants.
- Minority recruitment in prosthetic research is virtually unmeasured.

### 3.2 Low-Income / Insurance Barriers
- Industry trials may require specific insurance coverage or travel reimbursement.
- Few trials provide transportation stipends or lodging assistance.
- Federal (VA) trials are limited to eligible Veterans.

### 3.3 Rural Populations
- **No trials based in rural Midwest or Southern US.** Most sites cluster in major metros.
- The caregiver-assisted limb scanning study (NCT05884203) explicitly addresses rural Veteran access but is VA-only.

### 3.4 Age Range
- Many trials cap enrollment at 60-65 years.
- Older adults represent a large portion of the amputee population (vascular disease, diabetes).
- Only a few studies explicitly include "OLDER_ADULT" in their standard ages.

### 3.5 Female Representation
- Sex-specific enrollment is rarely reported.
- Several cohorts are male-skewed, particularly VA-based studies (military population).

### 3.6 Limb Level Coverage
- Most trials focus on **transtibial** amputees only.
- Transfemoral and hip disarticulation users are often excluded.
- Congenital amputees are universally excluded.

### 3.7 Phase Gap
- **Zero Phase 3 or Phase 4 trials** for lower limb prosthetics. This means device innovations enter the market without rigorous large-scale efficacy testing.

### 3.8 Physical/Cognitive Accessibility
- Few trials accommodate participants with cognitive impairments or severe comorbidities.
- Exclusion criteria often screen out the exact populations who would benefit most.

## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community-Based Screening Events
- **Target:** Rural populations, older adults, racial/ethnic minorities, low-income individuals
- **Approach:** Mobile screening stations at community centers, churches, VA facilities, and FQHCs
- **AbilityLab as hub:** Leverage the institution's reputation and infrastructure for satellite events
- **Key tactic:** Partner with local organizations in the South Side, West Side, and suburban communities that lack access to academic prosthetic programs

### Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation Program
- **Hybrid model:** In-person AbilityLab site visits + telehealth check-ins
- **Travel support:** Stipends and lodging partnerships for participants >50 miles from Chicago
- **Technology:** Video visit capability for socket fit checks, progress assessments, and adverse event monitoring
- **Addresses:** Rural access gap (no trials in rural Midwest)

### Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
- **Match prosthetic device companies** (Össur, Ottobock, Stryker, Endolite) with underserved trial sites
- **Pro bono device donations** from manufacturers for trials serving low-income populations
- **Data sharing agreements** that require demographic diversity as a condition of device access
- **Addresses:** Industry underrepresentation (currently only 19% of trials)

### Strategy 4: Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks
- **Mandate demographic reporting** in all trial protocols (race, ethnicity, sex, age)
- **Set minimum diversity targets:** ≥25% female enrollment, ≥30% minority enrollment, include participants >65 years
- **Require justification** for exclusion criteria that disproportionately affect underserved groups
- **Addresses:** Near-total absence of diversity data across current trials

### Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation Services
- **Dedicated patient navigator** for trial eligibility screening, enrollment, and retention
- **Transportation partnerships:** CTA reduced-fare cards, Divvy adaptive cycling, ride-share vouchers
- **On-site support:** Wheelchair assistance, wayfinding, care coordination
- **Addresses:** Transit gaps (poor tactile paving, lack of shelters at bus stops) and mobility barriers

### Strategy 6: Older Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming
- **Expand age ranges** in trial protocols beyond current 18-65 caps
- **Caregiver stipends** and on-site support for family members accompanying older participants
- **Age-friendly design:** Larger fonts, accessible facilities, flexible scheduling
- **Addresses:** Population aging and the large population of older vascular/diabetic amputees

### Strategy 7: Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers
- **Partner with VA hospitals** and Federally Qualified Health Centers in rural Midwest and South
- **Deploy mobile prosthetics labs** to rural locations for screening and enrollment
- **Leverage cell/internet connectivity** for hybrid in-person/remote follow-up
- **Addresses:** Geographic concentration of trials in major metros

### Strategy 8: Data Transparency & Community Advisory Board
- **Open-access dashboard** tracking trial demographics, geographic coverage, and exclusion criteria
- **Community advisory board** of prosthetic users from underserved communities to guide trial design
- **Plain-language summaries** of trial results disseminated through community organizations
- **Addresses:** Lack of trust and transparency between clinical research and marginalized communities
