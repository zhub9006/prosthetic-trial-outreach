## Executive Summary

An assessment of ~500 lower-limb prosthetic trials on ClinicalTrials.gov reveals **systemic enrollment gaps**: industry-sponsored trials are scarce (≤20%), nearly all studies carry an "N/A" or "Unknown" phase designation (96%), and key demographic groups — racial/ethnic minorities, older adults (80+), rural residents, and people with cognitive comorbidities — are consistently excluded or unreported. A concurrent site-accessibility review of the **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** (355 E Erie Street, Chicago, IL) shows excellent transit and pharmacy access (9+/10 scores) but gaps in nearby parks and social services. Eight outreach strategies are proposed to close the gap between trial availability and equitable participant access.

## Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial / Ethnic Diversity:** Few trials report race/ethnicity; NCT04427007 enrolled 100% White participants.
2. **Lower-Limb Specificity:** Some trials overlap with upper-limb prosthetics; many focus exclusively on transtibial users, excluding transfemoral individuals.
3. **Rural Access:** No trials have recruitment sites in rural Midwest or Southern US; sites are concentrated in major metros (Chicago, Boston, New York, Philadelphia, West Coast).
4. **Age Range:** Many protocols cap at 60–65, excluding older adults — who represent the majority of the limb-loss population (median amputation age in the US is ~65).
5. **Female Representation:** Sex-specific enrollment is rarely reported; cohorts are often male-skewed.
6. **Insurance / Financial Barriers:** Industry trials may require specific insurance or extensive travel, blocking low-income participants.
7. **Phase Pipeline Gap:** 96% N/A / Unknown phase status = no rigorous later-stage efficacy evidence before market release.
8. **Cognitive & Psychiatric Exclusion:** Nearly all trials exclude participants with MMSE < 24, BDI-II ≥ 19, STAI-Y ≥ 50 — removing individuals with depression, anxiety, or cognitive impairment (common comorbidities after amputation).
9. **K-Level Restriction:** Many trials require K3–K4 function, systematically excluding K1/K2 ambulators (the majority of new amputees).
10. **Global South Absence:** Zero-to-minimal prosthetic trial activity in sub-Saharan Africa, Latin America, and South Asia.

### Assessment Summary — AbilityLab as Outreach Site

#### Strengths
- **World-class rehabilitation reputation and dedicated prosthetic/orthotic programs** — SRAL is one of the premier limb-loss centres in the US
- **Top walkability (10/10)** — flat, ADA-compliant sidewalks, frequent curb cuts, accessible building entry
- **Extensive public transit** — 48 CTA bus stops within 500 m, Red Line within 1 km; car-free access is realistic
- **Four 24/7 pharmacies nearby** — critical for ongoing prosthetic-socket / skin-care supply refreshes
- **Dense amenities** — dining, fitness, and retail within walking distance support extended-trial participation (overnight families, care partners)
- **Divvy for All adapted cycle** — alternative mobility consistent with SRAL's mission

#### Limitations
- **Zero parks within 500 m** — nearest green space ~550 m NE (Lakeshore East Park) or ~1 km N (Navy Pier front lawn) — limits outdoor-group programming
- **Zero social-services offices nearby** — need to partner with ACS, SSA field office, or VA Community-Based Outpatient Clinic (VA Loop clinic on Illinois St, ~2 km)
- **Parking limited & fee-based** — only 4 paid garages/surface lots; free visitor spots scarce — participants from senior-transit / paratransit may face cost barriers
- **CTA shelters & tactile paving spotty** — most bus stops have shelters but tactile-paving compliance is inconsistent per CTA's survey data

## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 1. Community-Based Screening Events at SRAL (hub-and-spoke model)

- **Objective:** Translate SRAL's brand trust into proactive recruitment pools
- **Tactics:**  
  - Monthly "Prosthetics Open Door" afternoons — free gait-check, socket-fit check, and trial-information desk  
  - Collaborate with Chicago Park District (Lakeshore East Park, Museum Campus) for seasonal health-fairs  
  - Charter a free CTA day-pass sponsorship for participants recruited through SRAL outreach

### 2. Tele-Trial Participation Program

- **Model:** Hybrid — in-person SRAL visit (baseline + major milestones) + telehealth check-ins
- **Infrastructure needed:**  
  - SRAL telehealth kiosk (already partly in-place as the AbilityLab "Living-in-Lab" smart-home suite)  
  - Reliable home-telehealth setup kits shipped to underserved participants  
- **Equity levers:** Travel stipends + short-term lodging partnerships (YMCA of Metro Chicago, Ronald McDonald House near NMH) for participants >50 miles from Chicago

### 3. Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline for Community Trials

- **Rationale:** Only 20% of prosthetic trials are industry sponsored — and those tend to run at profitable/industrial-rich urban sites (Boston, greater Minneapolis, San Diego).  
- **Model:**  
  - SRAL hosts a "Community Devices Consortium" matching mechanism: prosthetic-device companies (Össur, Ottobock, Blatchford, Stryker, Hanger Clinic) sponsor SRAL-run trials that intentionally over-sample K1/K2, female, minority, and rural participants  
  - Pro bono device-donation agreements (already routine at SRAL) extended specifically to participants enrolled in these community trials

### 4. Mandatory Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks

- **Instrument:** Enshrine the following targets in every SRAL-affiliated trial protocol:  
  - ≥ 25% female enrollment (current US amputee pop. is ~30% female)  
  - ≥ 30% racial/ethnic minority enrollment (mirroring Illinois amputation demographics)  
  - Explicit reporting of race, ethnicity, sex, primary language, insurance type, K-level, and age band (18–39, 40–64, 65–79, 80+)  
  - Community advisory board with at least 40% membership from underserved prosthetic-user communities  
- **Verification:** Annual disparity report publicly posted on GitHub for this repo

### 5. Patient Navigation & Transportation Services

- **Navigation:** One SRAL-based "Prosthetic Trial Navigator" (degree: PT, OT, or social work) dedicated to screening, enrollment, and retention  
- **Transport:**  
  - CTA-reduced-fare cards (ADA reduced-fare $1.30) partnered for all SRAL trial participants  
  - Access-a-Ride / VIAtrans pre-booked paratransit codes from SRAL's social-work team  
  - Divvy For All adaptive cycles for participants living <2 km from SRAL

### 6. Older-Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming

- **Advocacy:** Lobby for wider age windows in NIH/VC-funded prosthetic grants — currently many cap at 65  
- **Caregiver support:**  
  - On-site SRAL caregiver lounge (adjacent to the Grand Lobby of the new building, already ADA-accessible)  
  - Quarterly caregiver support group (“Caregiver Connect”) co-located with trial check-in days  
- **Mobility:** FreeValley wheelchair van / Gemini site-access shuttle for SRAL-attending participants who require powered-chair transfer assistance

### 7. Rural Outreach via VA and FQHCs

- **Target geographies:** Rural Illinois, Indiana, Ohio, Michigan, Wisconsin — where prosthetic users often travel >2 hr for rehab follow-up  
- **Partners:**  
  - Chicago VA Healthcare System (Armour, Jesse Brown, and Manus VA CBOCs)  
  - Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) in IN/OH/KY border towns with prosthetic services  
  - Illinois Assistive Technology Program (IATP), iCanConnect, and North American Assembly on Arthritis
- **Mobile deployment:** A trailer-based SRAL prosthetic-clinic pod driven quarterly to VA FQHC sites — capable of running basic K-level assessments, socket-fit checks, and trial-screening; piloted with Community Health Pearlers

### 8. Proactive Participant Feedback Transparency Dashboard

- **Platform:** GitHub Pages site linked from this repository + SRAL's patient-portal intranet  
- **Features:**  
  - Open-source trial-demographics and geographic-coverage dashboards  
  - Quarterly mis-enrollment variance reports (which groups are under-enrolled vs. census)  
  - Public comment / correction form for prospective trial participants
