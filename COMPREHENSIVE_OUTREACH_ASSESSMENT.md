**Key Gap:** Industry-sponsored trials are severely underrepresented (only 19%). Rural populations, low-income users, and uninsured individuals are systematically excluded from industry trials due to travel and insurance requirements.

**Key Gap:** 96% of trials have no formal phase designation. Very few prosthetic interventions advance to later-stage efficacy testing — a pipeline gap that harms underserved populations.

### 1.4 Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** Rarely reported; NCT04427007 enrolled 100% White. No data to ensure equitable access.
2. **Rural Access:** No trials based in rural Midwest or Southern US. Most sites in major metros.
3. **Age Range:** Many trials cap enrollment at 60-65; WT2 excludes older adults who represent 65% of amputees.
4. **Female Representation:** Sex often "ALL" but actual recruitment male-skewed.
5. **Insurance/Financial Barriers:** Industry trials may require specific insurance or travel.
6. **Phase Gap:** 96% in NA/Unknown phases = no rigorous efficacy data.
7. **Transfemoral Exclusion:** Most trials focus on transtibial only, excluding 30-40% of amputees.
8. **International Reach:** Limited presence in Global South (1 trial in Gaza).

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community-Based Screening at AbilityLab
- Quarterly open-house screening days; free prosthesis evaluations regardless of trial eligibility.
- Outreach via CTA reduced-fare partnerships, church bulletins, VA referrals, FQHC flyers.

### Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation (Hybrid Model)
- 2 in-person AbilityLab visits + telehealth check-ins. Travel reimbursement (CTA passes, gas cards) and lodging for participants >50 miles away.
- Caregiver stipends ($50/visit) for elderly participants.

### Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
- Match device companies (Össur, Stryker, Ottobock) with AbilityLab for pro-bono device donations.
- Ensure open-access publication of trial outcomes.

### Strategy 4: Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks
- Minimum 25% female, 30% minority enrollment; age range 18-85; rural/urban tracking.
- Public diversity dashboard; sites below benchmarks flagged.

### Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation
- Dedicated Patient Navigator (1 FTE) at AbilityLab.
- CTA Reduced Fare Cards, ride-share health credits, parking validation at Doubletree garage.

### Strategy 6: Older Adult & Caregiver Programming
- Advocate for broader age ranges in trial protocols.
- Caregiver lounge, respite coordination, home-visit option for homebound seniors.

### Strategy 7: Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers
- Expand VA trial networks (Jesse Brown, Hines, Great Lakes VAs).
- Mobile prosthetics lab visits to 3-5 rural Midwest FQHCs annually.

### Strategy 8: International & Conflict-Zone Outreach
- Replicate Gaza model in other disaster zones.
- Partner with Chicago refugee resettlement agencies (Cann_util, Heartland Alliance).

### Strategy 9: Community Advisory Board & Data Transparency
- 8-10 member CAB of prosthetic users from underserved communities.
- Public dashboard for trial demographics and enrollment patterns.

### Strategy 10: Consolidated Outreach Hub Model
- Use AbilityLab as a one-stop prosthetic wellness hub leveraging 48 CTA stops and 4 pharmacies.
- Co-locate social workers, insurance navigators, and peer counselors.
- Monthly "Prosthetic Check-up Days" — walk-in, no appointment needed.
