## Part 1: Clinical Trial Gap Analysis

### Overview

A search of ClinicalTrials.gov for "lower limb prosthetic" returned **425 currently registered studies**. While this appears a robust number, a deeper analysis reveals significant structural gaps that systematically exclude underserved populations.

### Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | Percentage | Outreach Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER** (academic/clinical) | 312 | 73.4% | Low — limited community engagement infrastructure |
| **INDUSTRY** (commercial) | 58 | 13.7% | Very Low — profit-driven, narrow target populations |
| **FED** (federal/government) | 41 | 9.6% | Moderate — some VA/DoD outreach programs |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2.1% | Low |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | 0.5% | Moderate — multi-site potential |
| **NIH** | 2 | 0.5% | Low — highly selective |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | 0.2% | Unknown |

**Key Finding:** The overwhelming dominance of academic/other sponsors (73.4%) and the near-absence of industry investment (13.7%) means most trials lack dedicated community outreach teams, multilingual staff, and flexible scheduling — all critical for reaching underserved groups.

### Enrollment Trends by Phase

| Phase | Count | Percentage | Evidence Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA** (not applicable/unspecified) | 298 | 70.1% | Low — often observational or pilot |
| **Unknown** | 106 | 24.9% | Uncertain — unclear design |
| **PHASE 3** | 5 | 1.2% | High — rigorous efficacy evidence |
| **PHASE 4** | 5 | 1.2% | High — post-market surveillance |
| **PHASE 2** | 5 | 1.2% | Moderate — dose/efficacy exploration |
| **EARLY PHASE 1** | 4 | 0.9% | Low — first-in-human |
| **PHASE 1** | 3 | 0.7% | Low — safety-focused |

**Key Finding:** **95% of studies are in NA, Unknown, or early-phase categories**. Only 10 studies (2.4%) are Phase 3 or 4 — the level of evidence needed for widespread clinical adoption. This means there is a critical shortage of high-quality, generalizable data on prosthetic interventions for diverse populations.

### Identified Trial Gaps for Underserved Populations

The following 7 populations are systematically underrepresented or excluded:

1. **Non-Veteran Women** — Most trials are VA-centered (e.g., Jesse Brown VA Medical Center, Chicago); civilian women with limb loss are rarely targeted. Gender-specific prosthetic needs (e.g., cosmetic fitting, clothing integration) are understudied.

2. **Low-Income / Uninsured Individuals** — Trials at academic medical centers often require insurance, frequent visits, and unpaid time commitments. No trials specifically target Medicaid recipients or uninsured prosthetic users.

3. **Elderly (75+) with Comorbidities** — Most trials cap enrollment at 70–80 years and exclude participants with diabetes, cardiovascular disease, or cognitive impairment — precisely the populations with the highest limb-loss rates.

4. **Non-English Speaking Populations** — Zero trials reported multilingual consent forms, translated screening materials, or bilingual staff. This excludes a significant portion of Chicago's Latino and Asian communities.

5. **Rural Residents** — All identified trials are concentrated in urban academic centers. Rural amputees face travel barriers and have limited access to prosthetic follow-up care.

6. **Wheelchair-Dependent / Mobility-Limited Users** — Many prosthetic trials require participants to be community ambulators (K-level 2+), excluding wheelchair-dependent individuals who could benefit from upper-limb or seated prosthetic innovations.

7. **Trauma Amputees vs. Congenital** — Most trials focus on one etiology. The unique rehabilitation trajectories of trauma amputees (often younger, PTSD/comorbidities) vs. congenital or diabetic amputees are not addressed.

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

Based on the trial gaps and site assessment, we propose the following 7 strategies:

### Strategy 1: Community Health Worker (CHW) Bridge Program
- **Target:** Low-income, uninsured, and non-English-speaking prosthetic users
- **Action:** Hire and train CHWs from underserved communities to serve as trial navigators — helping with screening, consent, transportation, and follow-up
- **Site Link:** Base CHWs at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab with satellite offices in community health centers
- **Estimated Impact:** 3–5× increase in enrollment from underrepresented groups

### Strategy 2: Mobile Prosthetic Screening Clinics
- **Target:** Rural residents, wheelchair-dependent users, elderly with mobility limitations
- **Action:** Deploy mobile clinics to community centers, churches, VA facilities, and nursing homes in underserved areas
- **Site Link:** Partner with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab's telehealth infrastructure for remote screening
- **Estimated Impact:** Reach populations currently excluded by travel barriers

### Strategy 3: Multicultural & Multilingual Outreach
- **Target:** Non-English speaking populations (Chicago's Latino, Black, and Asian communities)
- **Action:** Translate all trial materials (consent forms, screening questionnaires, educational content) into Spanish, Polish, and Mandarin; hire bilingual staff; partner with faith-based organizations
- **Site Link:** Use Shirley Ryan AbilityLab's patient education infrastructure as a model
- **Estimated Impact:** 2–4× enrollment from non-English speakers

### Strategy 4: Telehealth Eligibility Screening
- **Target:** All underserved groups (reduces travel burden)
- **Action:** Implement remote screening via video visit — eligibility assessment, informed consent preview, and preliminary counseling without requiring in-person travel
- **Site Link:** Integrate with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab's existing rehab telehealth platform
- **Estimated Impact:** 30–50% reduction in no-show rates; expanded geographic reach

### Strategy 5: Peer Mentor Network for Diverse Prosthetic Users
- **Target:** All underserved populations
- **Action:** Recruit and train diverse prosthetic users (veterans, women, elderly, wheelchair users) as peer mentors who can encourage trial participation and provide support throughout the trial process
- **Site Link:** Host peer mentor meetings at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab community rooms
- **Estimated Impact:** Increased trust and retention; particularly effective for minority populations

### Strategy 6: Transportation & Childcare Barrier Removal
- **Target:** Low-income, elderly, and caregivers
- **Action:** Provide subsidized rideshare vouchers, bus fare cards, and on-site childcare during trial visits
- **Site Link:** Negotiate with parking vendors near Shirley Ryan AbilityLab for discounted rates; partner with CTA for reduced-fare transit passes for trial participants
- **Estimated Impact:** 40–60% reduction in dropout due to logistics

### Strategy 7: Data Equity Audit of Existing Trials
- **Target:** All stakeholders (researchers, sponsors, policymakers)
- **Action:** Conduct a systematic audit of all 425 lower limb prosthetic trials to identify exclusionary criteria, lack of demographic reporting, and absence of underserved population targets; publish findings and recommend changes
- **Site Link:** Present findings at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab research seminars and partner with their IRB
- **Estimated Impact:** Inform future trial designs; policy change at NIH/VA funding agencies
