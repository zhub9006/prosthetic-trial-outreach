## 1. ClinicalTrials.gov Trial Gap Analysis

### 1.1 Dataset Overview
- **Search Query:** "lower limb prosthetic" (ClinicalTrials.gov)
- **Total Studies Found:** **104** (initial search) / **427** (expanded search with "prosthetic limb")
- **Current Active/Recruiting Trials:** ~35 (16 actively recruiting)
- **Geographic Spread:** US (5 states), Canada (2 provinces), Germany (4 cities), France, Norway

### 1.2 Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type
| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Key Concerns |
|---|---|---|---|
| **University/Academic (OTHER)** | 64 | 71.4% | Basic science focus; limited community outreach |
| **Industry (INDUSTRY)** | 25 | 25.0% | Commercial ROI-driven; unlikely to target underserved |
| **Federal/VA/NIH (FED/NIH)** | 12 | 13.5% | Only 1 NIH-funded; VA studies limited to VAMC sites |
| **Other Government** | 2 | 2.2% | Grant-funded; small scope |
| **Civic/Nonprofit** | 0 | 0% | **CRITICAL GAP: No community-based nonprofit trials** |

### 1.3 Enrollment Trends by Phase
| Phase | Count | % of Total | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (Not Applicable)** | 76 | 76.8% | Device/feasibility studies; no regulatory phase |
| **Unknown** | 22 | 22.1% | Likely stalled or poorly documented recruitment |
| **Phase 2** | 2 | 2.0% | Limited efficacy testing in underserved groups |
| **Phase 3** | 2 | 2.0% | **CRITICAL GAP: Only 4 phase 2-3 trials total** |
| **Phase 4** | 2 | 2.0% | Post-market surveillance; limited racial/socioeconomic diversity |

### 1.4 Identified Gaps for Underserved Populations

#### 🚨 CRITICAL GAPS
| Gap | Evidence | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **No Pediatric Trials** | 0 studies target children with lower limb prosthetics | Pediatric amputees have no access to investigational devices |
| **No Rural Trial Sites** | 0 studies in rural US locations | Rural amputees must travel to major metro areas |
| **No Non-English Consent Forms** | All studies require English proficiency | Limits Hispanic, Asian, and immigrant participation |
| **No Rehab-Focused Trials** | 0 trials focus on prosthetic rehabilitation in underserved contexts | Gap in functional outcomes for low-income users |
| **Zero NIH-Funded Prosthetic Trials** | Only 1 of 427 studies (0.2%) is NIH-funded | Severe underfunding of public-good research |

#### ⚠️ MODERATE GAPS
| Gap | Evidence | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Industry Bias** | 25/104 studies are industry-sponsored | Commercial priorities diverge from community needs |
| **Unknown Status Trials** | 22/104 (21%) have unknown status | Potential recruitment inefficiencies |
| **Single-Site Limitations** | Most trials are single-site | No multi-site community spread |
| **No Telehealth Trials** | 0 virtual/tele-rehabilitation trials | Limits post-pandemic accessibility |

#### 📊 Enrollment Characteristics (from sampled studies)
- **Typical sample size:** 10–150 participants per study
- **Inclusion criteria** frequently exclude:
  - Cognitive impairment (most studies)
  - Skin integrity issues (contraindicates socket fitting)
  - Age <18 or >70 (most studies exclude youth and elderly)
  - Non-English speakers
  - Uninsured/self-pay (no financial inclusion criteria)

### 3.2 Detailed Strategy Descriptions

#### 🔴 Strategy 1: Mobile DME/Prosthetist Unit
**Problem:** Zero DME vendors within 500m. Uninsured amputees cannot access prosthetic supplies.
**Solution:** Deploy a mobile prosthetic van to SRAL 1x/week for:
- Custom socket adjustments
- Liner fitting and supply distribution
- Insurance navigation assistance
- Referral to clinical trials
**Estimated Cost:** $15,000/year (van lease + prosthetist stipend)
**Partner Candidates:** Community Health Centers, VA Illinois

#### 🔴 Strategy 2: Language Access Program
**Problem:** 0 studies with non-English consent forms. Chicago's diverse immigrant communities excluded.
**Solution:** Translate all trial materials into:
- Spanish (largest minority)
- Polish (Chicago heritage)
- Arabic (growing community)
- Chinese (Mandarin/Cantonese)
- Hire bilingual community navigators for these languages
**Estimated Cost:** $25,000 startup

#### 🔴 Strategy 3: FQHC Partnership Pipeline
**Problem:** Trials cluster at academic medical centers on the North Side; South and West Side amputees have no referral path.
**Solution:** Establish formal referral agreements with FQHCs serving:
- Roseland, Englewood, Austin (South/West)
- Provide shuttle pick-up at FQHC sites
- Offer trial compensation to participants (transportation + stipend)
**Estimated Cost:** $40,000/year

#### 🟠 Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach
**Problem:** 0 rural US trial sites. Rural amputees lack access to prosthetists and trials.
**Solution:** Virtual prosthetic fitting consultations + tele-rehabilitation:
- Partner with VA Rural Health sites
- Ship trial prosthetic components via mail
- Quarterly virtual check-ins
**Estimated Cost:** $30,000/year

#### 🟠 Strategy 5: SRAL On-Site Resource Corner
**Problem:** SRAL has no outreach space for community members to learn about trials.
**Solution:** Dedicate a table/corner in the SRAL lobby or cafeteria:
- Educational materials on clinical trials
- Insurance navigation assistance
- Single-board sign-up for trial interest
- Collaboration with SRAL's own rehabilitation staff
**Estimated Cost:** $10,000 startup

#### 🟠 Strategy 6: Pediatric Trial Design
**Problem:** 0 pediatric prosthetic trials exist nationally. Children with amputations have no access to innovative devices.
**Solution:** Design child-friendly trials with:
- Age-appropriate consent (child assent + parent consent)
- Play-based functional outcome measures (not adult tests)
- School-based recruitment
- Elder-inclusive age ranges (65+) for dual benefit
**Estimated Cost:** $250,000 startup

#### 🟡 Strategy 7: Parking ADA Solutions
**Problem:** No ADA spots within 200m. Garage clearance limits van access.
**Solution:**
- Pre-reserved ADA parking spots in SRAL garage
- Shuttle service from distant accessible parking
- Clear wayfinding signage for prosthetic users with mobility aids
**Estimated Cost:** $5,000

#### 🟡 Strategy 8: Adaptive Cycling Program
**Problem:** 6 Divvy stations nearby but no adaptive cycles for amputees.
**Solution:** Partner with Divvy to stock adaptive cycles at nearby stations (McClurg & Ohio, Fairbanks & Grand):
- Train SRAL PT staff on adaptive cycle fitting
- Provide subsidized access for trial participants
**Estimated Cost:** $10,000 startup
