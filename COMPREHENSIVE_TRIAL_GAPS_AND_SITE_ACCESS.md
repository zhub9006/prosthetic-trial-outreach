## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### P1: Immediate (0-3 months) — Estimated Cost: ~$111K/year

**1. Patient Navigation & Transportation Program**
- Provide CTA reduced-fare cards and Divvy Adaptive annual passes for trial participants
- Offer ride-share vouchers (Uber Health, Lyft Healthcare) for participants with mobility challenges
- Employ a dedicated Patient Navigator ($10K/yr) to help participants from underserved communities navigate trial enrollment, transportation, and appointments
- **Gap Addressed:** Transportation barrier for South Side and Medicaid populations

**2. Data Transparency Dashboard**
- Build open-source dashboard on GitHub showing trial demographics, gaps, and outcomes
- Visualize enrollment by race, gender, age, geography, and phase
- Update quarterly with new ClinicalTrials.gov data
- **Gap Addressed:** Invisible enrollment gaps; community trust deficit

**3. Community Advisory Board (CAB)**
- Recruit 10–15 prosthetic users from underserved populations (South Side, Medicaid, older adults, Spanish-speaking)
- Include Community Health Workers (CHWs) as board members
- Meet monthly at SRAL or community spaces; provide childcare, transportation, and meals
- **Gap Addressed:** No community voice in trial design; cultural/language barriers

**4. Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks**
- Require ≥25% female enrollment in all SRAL-affiliated trials
- Require ≥30% minority enrollment
- Require ≥15% Medicaid/uninsured enrollment
- Require Spanish-language consent forms for all trials
- **Gap Addressed:** Systemic exclusion bias in trial enrollment

### P2: 6-12 months — Estimated Cost: ~$380K/year

**5. Mobile Screening Events**
- Deploy VA mobile medical units and FQHC mobile health vans to Chicago South Side, Roseland, Auburn Gresham, and Englewood (~10 km)
- Partner with Cook County Health for screening events in underserved neighborhoods
- Quarterly events; provide free prosthesis evaluations and trial information
- **Gap Addressed:** South Side zero-trial-site gap; rural/urban disconnect

**6. Industry-Academic Pipeline**
- Create a pro bono device donation pipeline: industry sponsors provide devices for deployment to underserved trial sites
- Develop "Deployment Trials" — trials designed specifically for community settings, not just academic centers
- Create reimbursement mechanisms for community sites to participate
- **Gap Addressed:** 19% industry trials only at wealthy centers; cost barriers

**7. DME Integration at SRAL**
- **CRITICAL PRIORITY:** Establish a Prosthetic Access Station within 500m of SRAL (currently ZERO DME vendors)
- Partner with Ottobock, Össur, or Fillauer for a community DME depot
- Offer device fitting, adjustments, and repair at the SRAL site for trial participants
- **Gap Addressed:** DME 0/10 score; the #1 site-level barrier

### P3: 6-12 months — Estimated Cost: ~$30K/year

**8. Older Adult & Caregiver Programming**
- Provide caregiver stipends ($50/session) for older adult trial participants
- Offer in-home prosthetic device assessments for homebound seniors
- Partner with senior centers on the North Side (Hardy, Allison, Dunning)
- **Gap Addressed:** No pediatric trials; older adult under-representation; caregiver burden

**9. Tele-Trial Hub**
- Launch virtual trial visit capability (video consultations, device check-ins)
- Allow mail-in device maintenance and adjustment verification
- Develop team-based remote trial protocols for rural participants
- **Gap Addressed:** Rural Outreach; mobility-limited elderly; transportation barriers

### P4: 12+ months — Estimated Cost: ~$350K/year

**10. South Side Permanent Office**
- Establish a permanent (~500 sq ft) satellite office on Chicago's South Side (~10 km from SRAL)
- Staff with CHWs, trial navigators, and a part-time prosthetist
- Partner with Roseland Community Hospital, Holy Cross Hospital, or University of Chicago Medicine
- **Gap Addressed:** South Side zero-trial-site; African American/Hispanic communities (~5+ million in Cook County)

**11. Rural Outreach via VA**
- Deploy mobile prosthetics labs quarterly to VA clinics in IL, MO, IN (Decatur, Marion, Marion IL, etc.)
- Partner with VA Greater Los Angeles, VA Boston, and regional VA systems
- Provide trial screening and enrollment at VA prosthetics clinics
- **Gap Addressed:** Rural Midwest gap; rural Southern US gap; tribal land gap; veteran population

**12. Mental Health Integration**
- Train 5 peer support mentors from amputee peer support organizations (Amputee Coalition, Courage Center, Limbs International)
- Establish support groups at Medrina (~605m) or similar nearby facilities
- Integrate mental health screening into all trial enrollment (BDI-II, STAI-Y, PHQ-9 as done in NCT06883942)
- **Gap Addressed:** Mental health gap; peer support gap; community isolation
