**Key Gap:** Industry trials (device innovation, free prosthetics, intensive monitoring) represent only 15% of studies — low-income & uninsured populations lack access to cutting-edge prosthetic technology.

### Trend Analysis by Phase

| Phase       | Count | %     | Interpretation                                       |
|-------------|-------|-------|------------------------------------------------------|
| NA          | 76    | 78%   | Most trials are observational, no FDA pathway          |
| Unknown     | 19    | 20%   | Phase data missing — transparency gap                  |
| **Phase 1** | 1     | 1%    | ⚠️ Only one early-phase trial — pipeline bottleneck   |
| **Phase 4** | 1     | 1%    | ⚠️ Only one post-market study — inadequate surveillance |

**Key Gap:** 98% of prosthetic trials lack formal phase designation. Devices enter the market without rigorous Phase 1-3 testing, disproportionately harming underserved users who receive untested devices through low-cost channels.

### Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** Rarely reported; one trial enrolled 100% White participants
2. **Rural Access:** Zero trials in rural Midwest/Southern US — rural amputees face 200-400 mile travel
3. **Age Range:** Many trials cap age at 55-65; the typical amputee is 65+
4. **Female Representation:** Sex-specific data rarely required; male-skewed military/trauma cohorts
5. **Insurance/Financial Barriers:** Industry trials may require specific insurance or travel costs
6. **Phase Pipeline:** 98% NA/Unknown phase; no structured evidence pathway for new devices
7. **Lower-Limb Specificity:** Some trials aggregate upper+lower limb; transtibial-only excludes transfemoral users
8. **Disability Accessibility:** Trial sites rarely assess prosthetic/wheelchair mobility barriers in recruitment

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community-Based Mobile Screening Events
- **Target:** Rural populations, age 65+, racial/ethnic minorities, low-income uninsured
- **Approach:** Mobile screening at community centers, churches, VA facilities, FQHCs
- **Pilot:** 3 events (Chicago South Side, rural IL, rural IN) in Q1

### Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation Hybrid Model
- **In-Person:** Quarterly visits at Shirley Ryan for device fitting/gait analysis
- **Telehealth:** Monthly virtual check-ins for adherence and prosthetic troubleshooting
- **Financial Support:** Travel stipends ($500/trip), lodging partnerships, mileage reimbursement

### Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
- Negotiate **pro bono device donations** from Össur, Stryker, Ottobock for trial participants
- Create an "Industry Device Bank" — refurbished devices donated to underserved participants post-trial

### Strategy 4: Mandatory Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks
- Protocol requirements: ≥25% female, ≥30% minority, ≥15% age 65+
- Mandatory demographic reporting with public dashboard
- IRB integration as condition of approval

### Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation Services
- Bilingual Patient Navigators for eligibility screening through study completion
- Partner with Divvy (adapted cycles), CTA (reduced-fare cards), ride-share (subsidized first/last mile)
- "Prosthetic Transit Hub" at Shirley Ryan with accessible rideshare pickup

### Strategy 6: Older Adult & Caregiver Programming
- Advocate for raising age caps to 85+ in all trial protocols
- Caregiver stipends ($30/session), on-site rest/support areas with WiFi

### Strategy 7: Rural Outreach via VA & Federal Health Centers
- Partner with VA hospitals (Champaign IL, Marion IL, Central IN)
- Partner with FQHCs in rural Southern IL, Mississippi Delta, Appalachia
- Deploy mobile prosthetics labs quarterly to these locations

### Strategy 8: Data Transparency & Community Accountability
- Open-source dashboard (on this GitHub repo) with demographics for every trial
- Community Advisory Board: 8–12 prosthetic users from underserved communities
- Biannual public report cards with town halls at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
