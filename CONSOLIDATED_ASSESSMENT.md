## Executive Summary

This report consolidates clinical trial gap analysis, site accessibility assessment, and proposed outreach strategies for underserved lower-limb prosthetic users. Three critical gaps and seven strategies emerge.

**Bottom Line:** The prosthetic clinical trial pipeline is overwhelmingly academic, early-phase, and concentrated in affluent urban academic medical centers — systematically excluding 7 underserved populations. Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is a strong clinical anchor for outreach (4/5), but parking costs, neighborhood affluence, and lost mobility create access barriers that must be addressed.

| Phase | Count | % | Gap |
|---|---:|---:|---|

| Phase III (Efficacy / Comparative) | 5 | 1.2% | Larger enrollment but still excludes elderly, cognitive impairment, multiple comorbidities |

**Key insight:** Only **13 structured interventional trials** (Phases 1–4) exist. 95% are observational, registry, or status-unknown — meaning active evidence is extremely thin and comes from homogeneous populations.

### 1.4 Systematic Exclusions Identified Across Trials

The following 7 underserved populations are consistently excluded or under-represented:

| # | Population | Why They're Missing | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Non-veteran women** | VA trials exclude civilians; few trials stratify by sex | Sexual dimorphism in prosthetic fit and rehabilitation outcomes unstudied |
| 2 | **Low-income / Uninsured** | No trials at FQHCs or safety-net hospitals; industry trials rarely provide financial support | Largest amputee demographic in safety-net systems has no trial access |
| 3 | **Elderly (75+) with comorbidities** | Phase III trials cap age at 65–75; exclude multiple comorbidities | Most amputees are elderly; evidence base doesn't cover their needs |
| 4 | **Non-English speakers** | Eligibility caps English/Spanish only; no multilingual consent/material support | Hispanic, Asian, and other language communities excluded |
| 5 | **Rural residents** | All sites are urban academic centers; no rural satellite sites | Rural amputees face geographic barriers to travel for trials |
| 6 | **Wheelchair-dependent / High-need users** | Many trials exclude bilateral, revision, or wheelchair-dependent amputees | Highest-need population has no evidence base |
| 7 | **Trauma / War amputees (non-VA)** | VA trials serve veterans; civilian trauma centers not engaged as trial sites | High-demand population outside VA system lacks access |

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community Health Worker (CHW) Bridge Program
Recruit and train CHWs from underserved communities to serve as prosthetic trial navigators — explaining eligibility, accompanying participants to visits, and addressing mistrust of academic medical centers.

**Target populations:** Low-income, non-English speakers, rural residents
**Estimated cost:** $150K/year (5 CHWs × $30K)
**Timeline:** 6–12 months to hire and train

### Strategy 2: Mobile Prosthetic Screening Clinics
Deploy mobile prosthetic clinics to FQHCs, VA outpatient clinics, and community centers in underserved neighborhoods for on-site trial screening and pre-screening.

**Target populations:** Low-income, rural, wheelchair-dependent
**Estimated cost:** $200K/year (van + staff)
**Timeline:** 12–18 months to launch

### Strategy 3: Multicultural & Faith-Based Outreach
Partner with churches, mosques, temples, and cultural community organizations to host prosthetic health fairs and trial awareness events in multiple languages.

**Target populations:** Non-English speakers, immigrants, faith communities
**Estimated cost:** $50K/year
**Timeline:** 3–6 months

### Strategy 4: Telehealth Pre-Screening
Implement remote eligibility assessments via telehealth to reduce travel burden for rural and mobility-limited prosthetic users.

**Target populations:** Rural, elderly, wheelchair-dependent
**Estimated cost:** $75K (platform + training)
**Timeline:** 6–9 months

### Strategy 5: Peer Mentor Network
Recruit diverse prosthetic users (women, elderly, non-English speakers, trauma amputees) as peer mentors who guide underrepresented participants through trial enrollment.

**Target populations:** All underserved groups
**Estimated cost:** $60K/year (stipends + coordination)
**Timeline:** 6–12 months

### Strategy 6: Transportation & Childcare Support
Provide rideshare vouchers, bus fare assistance, and on-site childcare to remove practical barriers to trial participation.

**Target populations:** Low-income, rural, caregivers
**Estimated cost:** $100K/year
**Timeline:** Immediate — can pilot in 3 months

### Strategy 7: Data Equity Audit
Conduct a systematic audit of all ongoing lower-limb prosthetic trials on ClinicalTrials.gov for exclusionary criteria, demographic under-representation, and lack of underserved-site inclusion.

**Target populations:** Systemic — benefits all future trials
**Estimated cost:** $80K (analyst + consultant)
**Timeline:** 6–12 months
