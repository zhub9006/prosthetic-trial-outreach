Key Takeaways:
1. Only 1 of 10 is actively recruiting (SRAL NCT06160882, just 6 participants) — critical recruitment bottleneck.
2. Two VA trials are recruiting but veteran-exclusive — excluding broader underserved population.
3. No US Midwest academic site besides SRAL is recruiting. Rural populations are invisible.
4. No pediatric trials in the top 10 or the broader 427-trial dataset.
5. No trials in Spanish or targeting Hispanic/Latino populations.
6. Gender gap: 90%+ male enrollment in most prosthetics trials.

| NETWORK | 2 | 0.5% | Multi-site consortium trials |
| AMBIG | 1 | 0.2% | Unclear sponsor |

Critical Gap — Industry Underrepresentation: Only 13.8% industry-sponsored. Industry trials provide free devices; academic/federal rarely do. Underserved populations miss cutting-edge tech access.

Critical Gap — NIH Underfunding: Only 2 of 427 trials NIH-funded. Chronic underinvestment.

Critical Gap — Federal Concentrated at VA: VA/DoD trials are the only systematic pipeline for underserved, yet just 9.6%. No FQHC or advocacy group sponsors any prosthetic trial.

Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Priority 1 (Immediate — ~$90K/yr)

| # | Strategy | Target | Budget | Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1.1 | Patient Navigation & Transportation | All SRAL participants with mobility/transport barriers | ~$90K/yr | ≥85% attendance |
| P1.2 | Data Transparency Dashboard (GitHub) | Advocates, researchers, policymakers | ~$6K setup + $1K/yr | Dashboard live by Month 3 |
| P1.3 | Community Advisory Board (CAB) | Trust-building, culturally competent recruitment | ~$5K/yr | 10-15 members in Month 1 |
| P1.4 | Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks | All underserved populations | ~$5K/yr (IRB) | ≥25% female, ≥30% minority |

P1.1: CTA Ventra reduced-fare cards, Divvy Adaptive, ride-share vouchers, Patient Navigators (1 FTE), winter thermal kits. Chicago winters are a major documented drop-out cause.

P1.2: Open-source dashboard with real-time trial demographics, gaps, outcomes. ClinicalTrials.gov API integration.

P1.3: 10-15 prosthetic users + CHWs from underserved populations. Monthly meetings to audit trial diversity.

P1.4: Requirements: ≥25% female, ≥30% minority, ≥15% Medicaid, ≥10% rural, ≥50% age 55+. Non-compliance triggers IRB review.

### Priority 2 (6-12 months — ~$360K/yr)

| # | Strategy | Target | Budget | Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P2.1 | Mobile Screening Events | Rural Midwest, South Side, VA/FQHCs | ~$100K/yr | ≥15 diverse participants/study/yr |
| P2.2 | Industry-Academic Device Access Pipeline | Low-income/uninsured, urban underserved | ~$100K setup + $20K/yr | 20 pro bono slots/yr |
| P2.3 | DME Integration at SRAL (CRITICAL) | Zero DME within 500m | ~$150K setup + $30K/yr | ≥50 DME interactions/month |

P2.1: Solar-powered mobile units at VA (Chicago Lakeside Fort Dearborn), community health fairs, church events. Plain-language consent materials in Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic.

P2.2: Pro bono device donations from Otto Bock, Össur, Hanger Clinic, Ottobock, Liberating Tech. Device Lending Library at SRAL.

Industry Stakeholder Map: Otto Bock (Knees, feet, ASCENT 107 enroll) - HIGH; Össur (Feet, braces, PRO-FLEX) - MEDIUM; Hanger Clinic (Prostheses, K2) - HIGH; Ottobock (Braces, adapters, France 5-site) - MEDIUM; Liberating Tech (Knees, K2 powered vs mechanical) - HIGH.

P2.3: Pop-up DME clinic ($15K/yr) or Permanent satellite ($50K setup + $30K/yr) or Tele-DME ($5K setup + $10K/yr). Without nearby DME, participants drop out due to device discomfort.

### Priority 3 (6-12 months — ~$30K/yr)

| # | Strategy | Target | Budget | Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P3.1 | Older Adult Programming | Elderly (65+) | ~$10K/yr | ≥10% participants age 65+ |
| P3.2 | Tele-Trial Hub (Hybrid Model) | Rural, mobility-limited, elderly | ~$20K/yr | ≥30% telehealth; ≥20% reduction in cancellations |

P3.1: Broaden age eligibility; caregiver stipends ($25/hr); partner with senior centers (Lakeview, Gold Coast, Streeterville); in-home assessments.

P3.2: Virtual visits, mail-in device checks, team trials (2-4 neighborhood participants attend together). Pre-loaded tablets (30 units).

### Priority 4 (12+ months — ~$350K/yr)

| # | Strategy | Target | Budget | Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P4.1 | South Side Permanent Office | African American/Hispanic communities | ~$100K setup + ~$50K/yr | ≥10 South Side participants/study/yr |
| P4.2 | Rural Outreach via VA Network | Rural Veterans | ~$200K/yr | 4 rural VA site visits/yr; ≥20 rural Veterans/yr |
| P4.3 | Mental Health Integration | 30-50% of amputees with depression | ~$20K/yr | ≥95% PHQ-2 screening; ≥70% peer group attendance |

P4.1: ~10km from SRAL; hire CHWs from Englewood, Austin, East Garfield Park zip codes.

P4.2: Mobile prosthetics labs quarterly to Rockford VA (IL), Marion VA (IL), St. Louis VA (MO), Indianapolis VA (IN). Partner with MOVE program.

P4.3: PHQ-2 screening at intake; partner with Medrina for monthly peer support groups; embed mental health in trial protocols.
