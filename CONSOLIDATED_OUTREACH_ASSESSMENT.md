## Clinical Trial Gap Analysis

### Enrollment Trends by Phase

Analysis of **427 total lower limb prosthetic studies** on ClinicalTrials.gov reveals a dramatic phase gap:

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (Not Applicable)** | 299 | 70% | Most studies are device/feasibility trials without formal phase classification |
| **Unknown** | 107 | 25% | Studies with unreported or unclear phase designation |
| **PHASE3** | 5 | 1% | Only 5 late-stage efficacy trials identified |
| **PHASE4** | 5 | 1% | Very few post-market surveillance studies |
| **PHASE2** | 5 | 1% | Limited mid-stage testing |
| **EARLY_PHASE1** | 4 | 1% | Minimal early-phase safety work |
| **PHASE1** | 3 | 1% | Few first-in-human pharmacology/device studies |

**🚨 Critical Gap:** **96% of lower limb prosthetic trials have no formal phase designation**, and only 10 studies reach Phase 3-4. This means the vast majority of prosthetic technologies reach the market without rigorous late-stage efficacy or safety testing, directly impacting the quality and reliability of devices available to underserved populations.

### Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Key Insight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER** (academic/government/unknown) | 313 | 73% | Dominated by academic medical centers |
| **INDUSTRY** | 59 | 14% | Device manufacturers — severely underrepresented |
| **FED** (federal) | 41 | 10% | Government-funded trials |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2% | Non-NIH government sponsors |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | <1% | Clinical research networks |
| **NIH** | 2 | <1% | Direct NIH funding — critically low |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | <1% | Ambiguous sponsor classification |

**🚨 Critical Gap:** Industry-sponsored trials represent only **14%** of all lower limb prosthetic research. This creates a significant access barrier — industry-funded trials often have more resources for participant compensation, travel support, and device access, but they are the least available. Additionally, direct **NIH funding accounts for only 2 studies**, indicating a severe underinvestment in publicly funded prosthetic research.

**🚨 Geographic Gap:** While the US dominates trial count, most are single-site studies at major urban academic centers (e.g., Boston, Chicago, Cleveland, Pittsburgh). **Rural Midwest, Southern US, and underrepresented urban communities have zero dedicated trial sites.**

### Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** Minority enrollment rarely reported or tracked. Many trials enroll 80-100% White participants. No systematic diversity requirements in trial protocols.

2. **Rural Access:** Zero trials based in rural Midwest or Southern US. All major enrollment sites are in large metropolitan areas with existing academic infrastructure.

3. **Age Exclusion:** Many trials cap enrollment at 60-65 years old, despite older adults representing the majority of lower limb amputee populations (vascular disease, diabetes-related amputations).

4. **Female Representation:** Sex-specific demographics often not reported; several cohorts are male-skewed, particularly in trauma-related amputation studies.

5. **Transfemoral vs. Transtibial Focus:** Many trials focus exclusively on transtibial (below-knee) amputees, excluding the more clinically complex transfemoral (above-knee) population.

6. **Insurance/Financial Barriers:** Industry trials may require specific insurance coverage or create travel burdens that exclude low-income populations.

7. **Phase Pipeline Gap:** With 96% of studies at NA/Unknown phase, promising prosthetic interventions rarely undergo rigorous later-stage testing before market release — directly impacting device quality for underserved users who depend on insurance-covered options.

8. **NIH Underinvestment:** Only 2 NIH-sponsored studies across 427 total trials indicates critical underfunding of public prosthetic research that would benefit uninsured/underinsured populations.

## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community-Based Screening Events
**Target:** Rural populations, older adults (65+), racial/ethnic minorities, low-income individuals  
**Approach:** Deploy mobile screening units to community centers, churches, VA facilities, and FQHCs in underserved areas. Use Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as the referral hub for eligible participants.

### Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation Program
**Model:** Hybrid in-person AbilityLab visits (quarterly) + telehealth check-ins (monthly)  
**Support:** Travel stipends and lodging partnerships for participants >50 miles from Chicago  
**Technology:** Provide tablets and training for telehealth sessions to reduce digital divide

### Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
**Mechanism:** Match prosthetic device companies (Össur, Stryker, Ottobock, Freedom Innovations) with underserved trial sites  
**Actions:** Secure pro bono device donations, establish equipment lending libraries at community sites  
**Goal:** Increase industry-sponsored trial access from 14% to 25% within 3 years

### Strategy 4: Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks
**Requirements:** Mandate demographic reporting in all trial protocols  
**Targets:** ≥25% female enrollment, ≥30% minority enrollment, ≥15% participants aged 65+  
**Enforcement:** Partner with IRBs to require diversity plans for prosthetic device trials

### Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation Services
**Navigation:** Hire dedicated patient navigators for trial eligibility screening, enrollment, and retention  
**Transportation:** Partner with Divvy (adapted cycle program), CTA (reduced-fare medical cards), ride-share companies for subsidized rides  
**Benefits:** Reduce no-show rates and improve completion for underserved participants

### Strategy 6: Older Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming
**Protocol Advocacy:** Work with IRBs and sponsors to broaden age ranges in trial protocols (remove upper age caps where medically appropriate)  
**Support:** Provide caregiver stipends, on-site childcare, and flexible scheduling  
**Outreach:** Partner with senior centers and AARP chapters for recruitment

### Strategy 7: Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers
**Partnerships:** Formal MOUs with VA hospitals and FQHCs in rural Midwest and Southern US  
**Mobile Units:** Deploy mobile prosthetics assessment labs to rural locations 2-4x per year  
**Telehealth:** Establish regular telehealth consultations between AbilityLab clinicians and rural providers

### Strategy 8: Data Transparency & Community Advisory Board
**Dashboard:** Create open-access dashboard showing trial demographics, geographic coverage, and enrollment patterns  
**CAB:** Establish a Community Advisory Board of prosthetic users from underserved communities to guide trial design and recruitment strategies  
**Reporting:** Quarterly public reports on trial diversity metrics and gap trends

### Strategy 9: Pharmacy & Supply Chain Partnership
**Integration:** Partner with the 4 nearby Walgreens locations for prosthetic supply dispensing and medication management  
**Home Delivery:** Establish home delivery programs for prosthetic liners, sockets, and hygiene products for participants with mobility challenges  
**24/7 Access:** Leverage 24/7 pharmacy locations for emergency prosthetic supply needs

### Strategy 10: Transit-Accessible Trial Design
**Site Selection:** Prioritize trial sites with transit scores >8.0 for participants without drivers' licenses  
**Schedule:** Offer early morning and evening appointment slots aligned with CTA schedules  
**Weather:** Provide winter clothing stipends and indoor waiting areas to address Chicago climate barriers
