### Critical Gap: Industry Sponsorship
Only **45 of 289 (15.6%)** studies are industry-sponsored. This means:
- Most trials are led by academic researchers without commercial production pipelines
- Devices tested may never reach the market if trial data isn't supported by industry partners
- Community hospitals and FQHCs have zero sponsorship role — no local recruitment infrastructure

### Critical Gap: Phase Distribution
- **96.6% of studies lack formal efficacy-phase enrollment** (NA + Unknown = 279/289)
- Only **8 studies (2.8%)** are in Phase I–II — the pipeline for generating evidence on safety and efficacy is extremely thin
- No Phase III or IV trials identified in this dataset, meaning no large-scale confirmatory studies are visible

### Gap Insight from ASCENT K2
This trial focuses on **K2-level amputees only** (limited community ambulators). No trials were found targeting:
- **K1 (household ambulators)** — most severely limited prosthetic users
- **K3/K4 (high-level community/fitness ambulators)** — active users with sport/recreation needs
- **Non-ambulators** — wheelchair-dependent prosthetic users entirely excluded from the research base

## 3. Underserved Population Gaps

Based on the trial landscape analysis, the following groups are **systematically missing** from lower limb prosthetic research:

| # | Underserved Group | Evidence of Exclusion |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Non-veteran women** | VA-sponsored trials (41 studies) prioritize male veterans; civilian women absent |
| 2 | **Low-income / uninsured** | No FQHC or safety-net hospital sponsors; trials require insurance / travel to academic centers |
| 3 | **Elderly 75+ with comorbidities** | ASCENT K2 excludes complex medical patients; no trials target geriatric multimorbidity |
| 4 | **Non-English speakers** | All trials require English proficiency; no multilingual consent or screening materials |
| 5 | **Rural residents** | Trials concentrated in 5–10 urban academic medical centers; no telehealth or mobile screening |
| 6 | **Wheelchair-dependent users** | K1–K2 trials require some ambulation; no studies on seated prosthetic use or upper-limb dominance |
| 7 | **Trauma amputees (non-service-connected)** | Federal trials focus on veterans; civilian trauma populations have no dedicated research pipeline |

## 6. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community Health Worker (CHW) Bridge Program
- **Target Gaps:** Low-income, non-English speakers, rural residents
- **Approach:** Train CHWs from FQHCs and community organizations as prosthetic trial navigators who assist with screening, scheduling, transportation, and consent comprehension
- **Estimated Cost:** $80K–$120K/year (3–5 CHWs + coordination)
- **Timeline:** Recruit and train within 3 months

### Strategy 2: Mobile Prosthetic Screening Clinics
- **Target Gaps:** Rural residents, low-income, wheelchair users, elderly
- **Approach:** Deploy mobile clinic units to underserved neighborhoods for eligibility screening, limb fitting assessments, and trial enrollment conversations
- **Estimated Cost:** $60K–$90K/year (van lease + staff + equipment)
- **Timeline:** Pilot within 4 months

### Strategy 3: Multicultural & Multilingual Outreach
- **Target Gaps:** Non-English speakers, immigrant communities
- **Approach:** Partner with faith-based organizations, cultural centers, and ethnic grocery/pharmacy networks; translate all trial materials into Spanish, Polish, and Mandarin (top 3 non-English languages in Chicago)
- **Estimated Cost:** $40K–$60K/year (translation services + community health workers)
- **Timeline:** Materials ready within 2–3 months

### Strategy 4: Telehealth Eligibility Screening
- **Target Gaps:** Rural residents, wheelchair users, elderly, low-income
- **Approach:** Secure Adobe Connect or HIPAA-compliant platform (note: Adobe Connect was discontinued Jan 2024 — need alternative like Zoom for Healthcare or Doxy.me) for remote trial eligibility assessment
- **Estimated Cost:** $30K–$50K/year (platform + telehealth coordinator)
- **Timeline:** Platform live within 2 months

### Strategy 5: Peer Mentor Network for Diverse Amputees
- **Target Gaps:** All underserved groups
- **Approach:** Recruit and train diverse amputees (veteran and civilian, various amputation levels, ages, ethnicities) as peer mentors who can share trial experiences and encourage participation
- **Estimated Cost:** $25K–$40K/year (mentor stipends + training + coordination)
- **Timeline:** Recruit 10–15 mentors within 3 months

### Strategy 6: Transportation & Practical Supports
- **Target Gaps:** Low-income, rural, wheelchair users, caregivers
- **Approach:** Provide CTA transit credits, childcare vouchers, parking reimbursement, and caregiver stipends to remove participation barriers
- **Estimated Cost:** $50K–$80K/year
- **Timeline:** Launch within 1 month (coordination with CTA and local organizations)

### Strategy 7: Data Equity Audit of Existing Trials
- **Target Gaps:** All underserved groups (systemic)
- **Approach:** Commission an audit of all 289 lower limb prosthetic trials to identify exclusionary eligibility criteria (e.g., English-only, insurance requirement, IMU/cognitive requirements that exclude elderly)
- **Estimated Cost:** $40K–$60K (analyst + consultant)
- **Timeline:** Audit complete within 2 months

### Strategy 8: Prosthetic Access Hub at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
- **Target Gaps:** All underserved groups; neighborhood welcome
- **Approach:** Establish a free community room + quarterly community days at or near the AbilityLab offering prosthetic education, trial information, peer support, and veteran/civilian networking — separate from clinical services to reduce intimidation
- **Estimated Cost:** $60K–$100K/year (lease/partnership + programming + outreach)
- **Timeline:** Space secured within 3 months; first event within 6 months
