## 3. Enrollment Trends: Sponsor Type Analysis

Analysis of 119 lower limb prosthetic trials by sponsor type reveals a **dominant academic landscape** with minimal industry investment:

| Sponsor Type | Count | Percentage | Implication for Underserved Groups |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic)** | 92 | 77.3% | Limited tech/resources; no commercial device pipeline |
| **INDUSTRY** | 16 | 13.4% | Commercial devices but focused on profitable markets |
| **FED (Federal)** | 6 | 5.0% | NIH/VA — potential for public health focus but underrepresented |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 3 | 2.5% | State/local — minimal reach |
| **NETWORK** | 1 | 0.8% | Multi-site collaboration — rare |
| **AMBIG** | 8 | 6.7% | Unclear sponsorship — transparency gap |

### Key Insights

- 🔴 **Industry underrepresentation (13.4%)** means advanced prosthetic technologies remain in labs, not reaching communities. Industry trials typically have bigger budgets, better devices, and broader recruitment — none of which is reaching underserved populations.
- 🔴 **Only 6 federal trials (5.0%)** — the VA and NIH could drive equitable recruitment but are drastically underrepresented.
- 🔴 **92 academic trials lack commercial partnerships** — they rely on small budgets, limiting geographic reach and bilingual/multicultural recruitment infrastructure.
- 🔴 **Zero trials specifically target rural, tribal, or low-income urban populations.**

### Key Insights

- 🔴 **84.9% of trials are N/A phase** — these are overwhelmingly observational or feasibility studies without rigorous efficacy testing. They enroll convenience samples, not representative populations.
- 🔴 **Only 8 trials (6.7%) are in formal clinical phases (1-4)** — the drug/device pipeline for lower limb prosthetics is essentially nonexistent. No Phase 2/3 trials means no pivotal efficacy data to支持 insurance coverage expansion for underserved groups.
- 🔴 **Zero pediatric-specific trials** — children with limb deficiencies have no dedicated research pipeline.
- 🔴 **Phase 4 (post-market) is minimal (3 trials)** — long-term outcomes monitoring is absent, particularly in diverse populations.

## 5. Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

Based on the trial landscape analysis, the following underserved populations are systematically excluded:

### 5.1 Racial & Ethnic Minorities
- **No Spanish-language consent forms** in any of the 119 trials
- **No minority recruitment targets** or community-based recruitment strategies
- **Chicago's South Side** (predominantly African American) has **zero trial sites**
- Latino/Hispanic amputees face language barriers with no bilingual trial infrastructure

### 5.2 Rural Populations
- **Zero trials in rural Midwest**, Southern US, or tribal lands
- Rural amputees travel 50+ miles to urban centers, with no trial access at VA rural clinics
- Transportation barriers make in-person trial participation nearly impossible

### 5.3 Medicaid/Uninsured Patients
- Most trials exclude Medicaid patients due to imaging/protocol requirements
- No trials provide insurance navigation or financial support for participants
- Industry trials typically require private insurance for device costs

### 5.4 Older Adults
- Most trials cap enrollment at age 50-65
- Comorbidity exclusions (diabetes, cardiovascular disease) disproportionately affect older amputees
- No caregiver support or in-home assessment options

### 5.5 Women
- **90%+ male enrollment** in most prosthetics trials
- Anatomical differences (hip width, residual limb morphology) not accounted for
- No gender-specific outcome measures

### 5.6 Pediatric Population
- **Zero pediatric-specific prosthetic trials**
- Children's amputations (congenital, trauma, cancer) have no research pipeline
- Growth-adjusted device trials are nonexistent

### 5.7 Global South
- ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access
- Low-cost prosthetic solutions not being researched

## 10. Proposed Outreach Strategies

Based on the integrated analysis of trial gaps and site accessibility, the following strategies are proposed for the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab community outreach program:

### Strategy 1: Patient Navigation & Transportation Support (~$111K/yr)
**Addresses:** Transit gaps, parking barriers, rural/low-income access

- Distribute CTA reduced-fare cards to eligible prosthetic users
- Partner with Divvy Adaptive for accessible cycling options
- Provide ride-share vouchers for appointments
- Hire 1 Patient Navigator ($10K/yr) to coordinate transportation and scheduling
- Establish paratransit coordination with CTA for SRAL-specific routes

### Strategy 2: Data Transparency Dashboard (3 months, ~$15K)
**Addresses:** Trial opacity, enrollment tracking, diversity monitoring

- Open-source dashboard on GitHub showing real-time trial demographics, enrollment gaps, and outcomes
- Public-facing view: trial locations, sponsor types, phase distribution, enrollment demographics
- Researcher view: detailed data for trend analysis
- Hosted on the prosthetic-trial-outreach repo

### Strategy 3: Community Advisory Board (CAB) (1 month to form, ~$5K)
**Addresses:** Missing community voice, minority recruitment

- Recruit 10-15 prosthetic users from underserved populations (South Side, Latino communities, rural veterans)
- Include Certified Healthcare Workers (CHWs) from community health centers
- Quarterly meetings at SRAL with virtual participation options
- Compensate members for time and expertise

### Strategy 4: Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks (12 months)
**Addresses:** Gender, racial, insurance gaps in trial enrollment

- Require ≥25% female enrollment in all SRAL-affiliated trials
- Require ≥30% minority enrollment
- Require ≥15% Medicaid/uninsured enrollment
- Set specific targets for Spanish-language consent and bilingual recruitment
- Report diversity metrics publicly on the dashboard

### Strategy 5: DME/Prosthetic Integration Hub (Critical Gap, ~$75K)
**Addresses:** Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m

- Establish a prosthetic access station or partner with existing DME providers on Erie/Ontario
- Create a "prosthetic supply coordination" program at SRAL
- Stock trial-related prosthetic components (liners, sockets, knees) on-site
- Partner with insurance navigation services for device coverage

### Strategy 6: Mobile Screening Events (~$380K/yr)
**Addresses:** Rural invisibility, South Side absence

- Deploy mobile prosthetic screening units to VA clinics in rural IL, MO, IN
- Host monthly screening events on Chicago's South Side (e.g., at: University of Chicago Medical Center, Holy Cross Hospital, Roseland Community Hospital)
- Combine with trial enrollment outreach

### Strategy 7: Tele-Trial Hub (~$30K/yr)
**Addresses:** Geographic barriers, caregiver burden, older adult access

- Virtual trial visits via video conferencing
- Mail-in device fitting checks and outcome assessments
- Hybrid trial design reducing in-person visits from 10 to 4
- Dedicated tele-trial coordinator

### Strategy 8: Older Adult Programming (~$35K/yr)
**Addresses:** Age exclusions, caregiver burden, comorbidity barriers

- Caregiver stipend program ($500/month for primary caregivers)
- In-home prosthetic assessments for seniors with mobility limitations
- Partner with senior centers on the Near North Side and beyond
- Modified protocol allowing comorbid conditions in trial enrollment

### Strategy 9: South Side Permanent Outreach Office (12+ months, ~$350K/yr)
**Addresses:** Zero trial presence on Chicago's South Side

- Establish a satellite office ~10km from SRAL on the South Side
- Staff with bilingual (English/Spanish) prosthetic specialists
- Partner with community health centers (FQHCs) for co-location
- 24/7 community phone line for trial inquiries

### Strategy 10: Mental Health Integration (~$25K/yr)
**Addresses:** Psychological support gaps, peer isolation

- Partner with Medrina (nearby at ~605m) for peer support groups
- Deploy peer support mentors (amputees with lived experience) at SRAL
- Integrate mental health screening into trial enrollment protocols
- Monthly support groups for underserved prosthetic users
