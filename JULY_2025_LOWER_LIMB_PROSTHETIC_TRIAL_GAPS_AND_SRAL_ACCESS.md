## 3. Outreach Strategy Recommendations

### 3.1 Priority 1: Bridge the Prosthetic Supply Gap (DME Access)

**Problem:** Zero DME/vendors within 500m. Prosthetic users must travel significant distances for supplies, adjustments, and follow-up care — a major barrier to trial participation.

**Strategies:**
- **On-site DME resource corner** at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab: Partner with a local DME supplier to rotate inventory weekly (prosthetic sleeves, liners, socks, tools)
- **Mobile DME van:** Monthly pop-up at SRAL with prosthetist on-site for minor adjustments and supply distribution (~$15K/yr)
- **Delivery partnership:** Negotiate home delivery agreements between local DME vendors and trial participants

### 3.2 Priority 2: Language & Cultural Access

**Problem:** Zero trials with non-English consent forms. Chicago's Near North Side has significant Spanish, Polish, Chinese, and Arabic-speaking communities.

**Strategies:**
- Translate all trial materials into **Spanish, Polish, Arabic, and Chinese**
- Hire **bilingual community liaisons** for each language group
- Partner with **community health workers (CHWs)** at FQHCs on the South and West Sides for referral pipelines
- Offer **interpretation services** at all trial screening and visit appointments

### 3.3 Priority 3: Rural & Veteran Outreach

**Problem:** Zero rural trial sites; federal/VA studies underrepresented for veterans.

**Strategies:**
- **Tele-rehabilitation pilot:** Virtual screening and follow-up for veterans at rural VA facilities, coordinated with SRAL's existing telestroke program infrastructure
- **Mobile screening events** at VA facilities in IL, IN, and WI (Champaign, Milwaukee, Rockford)
- **Travel stipend program** for rural participants (est. $500/trip round-trip)

### 3.4 Priority 4: Pediatric & Geriatric Inclusivity

**Problem:** Zero pediatric trials; age caps exclude older adults.

**Strategies:**
- **Pediatric prosthetic trial design:** Child-friendly prosthetic interventions with age-appropriate consent (parental + child assent)
- **Elder-inclusive trial protocols:** Remove or raise upper age limits; provide transportation assistance and caregiver support
- **Intergenerational trial design:** Include both pediatric and geriatric cohorts where clinically appropriate

### 3.5 Priority 5: Transit & Parking Solutions

**Problem:** Public transit stops are just beyond the 500m radius; no ADA parking within 200m.

**Strategies:**
- **CTA paratransit coordination:** Pre-arrange ride-sharing with CTA Access services for trial participants with mobility disabilities
- **Discounted Divvy annual passes** for trial participants
- **ADA parking signage** and reserved spaces in nearby garages (negotiate with garage operators)
- **Shuttle service** from CTA Red Line Grand Ave station during trial visit hours

### 3.6 Priority 6: Expand Phase 2–3 Efficacy Research

**Problem:** Only 5% of lower-limb prosthetic studies are in active clinical phases; zero Phase 2–3 efficacy trials.

**Strategies:**
- **Partner with industry sponsors** (Ottobock, Össur, Endolite) to launch Phase 2–3 trials focused on functional outcomes
- **Leverage SRAL's research infrastructure** — it is the #1 ranked rehabilitation hospital and has IRB, research nursing, and biomechanics labs
- **Focus on underserved endpoints:** Community mobility, social participation, and economic self-sufficiency — not just clinical measures
