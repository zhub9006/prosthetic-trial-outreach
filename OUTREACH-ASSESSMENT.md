# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Assessment

## Overview
Community outreach project for prosthetic trial recruitment. This report identifies trial gaps, evaluates the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a community outreach site, and proposes outreach strategies for underserved groups.

### Enrollment Trend Analysis — By Sponsor Type

**Key Findings:**
- **Government/Defense Sponsors** (VA, DoD): 2 of 10 trials — **underrepresented**. Veterans with lower-limb amputation face significant recruitment gaps.
- **Academic Centers**: 3 of 10 trials — moderate representation but limited community outreach.
- **Private/Industry Sponsors**: 4 of 10 trials — **heaviest representation**, but exclude uninsured patients.
- **NGO/Global Health**: 1 of 10 trials — **critically underrepresented**.

### Enrollment Trend Analysis — By Phase
- **Phase 1**: 1 trial (10%)
- **Phase 2**: 8 trials (80%)
- **Phase 2/3**: 1 trial (10%)
- **NO Phase 3 trials** — major gap for evidence-based practice at scale
- **NO Phase 4 post-marketing studies** — long-term outcomes data virtually nonexistent

### Underserved Groups Identified

| Group | Gap | Evidence |
|-------|-----|----------|
| **Veterans & Military** | Only 1 VA-sponsored trial; restrictive eligibility | VA enrollment barriers, travel requirements |
| **Pediatric Amputees** | Only 1 pediatric-specific trial | General adult trials exclude children |
| **Bilateral Amputees** | No trials targeting bilateral limb loss | Most trials require at least one intact limb |
| **Low-Income / Uninsured** | Industry trials exclude uninsured patients | Insurance pre-authorization barriers |
| **Geriatric Amputees** | No trials age-stratified for seniors | Age cutoffs at 65-70 in most protocols |
| **Rural/Remote Amputees** | All trials concentrated in urban centers | No rural satellite recruitment sites |
| **Limb-Loss from Diabetes/Vascular** | Vascular amputations underrepresented | Most trials focus on traumatic/military amputations |

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy A: Community Satellite Recruitment Hubs
- Partner with VA Medical Centers and community hospitals to set up satellite screening stations
- Target: Veterans, rural amputees, diabetes-related amputees
- Timeline: Q1 2025

### Strategy B: Transportation & Mobility Support Program
- Offer subsidized ride-share vouchers, CTA monthly passes, wheelchair-accessible transport
- Target: Low-income uninsured patients, elderly amputees, bilateral amputees
- Timeline: Q2 2025

### Strategy C: Pediatric & Bilateral Amputee Outreach
- Create dedicated pediatric/bilateral amputee recruitment pipeline at Shirley Ryan
- Target: Children aged 8-18, bilateral transtibial/knee-disarticulation patients
- Timeline: Q2 2025

### Strategy D: Latino & Non-English Language Outreach
- Translate trial materials to Spanish; hire bilingual coordinators
- Partner with community health centers in Pilsen, Little Village, Humboldt Park
- Target: Spanish-speaking amputees
- Timeline: Q3 2025

### Strategy E: Small Business & Device Company Bridge
- Connect smaller device companies with underserved populations
- Offer reduced-cost free prosthetic trial devices in exchange for diverse enrollment
- Target: Uninsured/underinsured amputees
- Timeline: Q2-Q3 2025

### Strategy F: Virtual Screening & Digital Outreach
- Develop virtual screening platform with telehealth pre-screening
- Use social media targeted at amputation support groups
- Target: Homebound amputees, elderly, caregivers
- Timeline: Q1 2025
