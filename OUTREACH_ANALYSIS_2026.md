This report assesses the current landscape of lower limb prosthetic clinical trials, identifies enrollment gaps affecting underserved populations, and evaluates the **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** (355 E Erie Street, Chicago, IL 60611) as a community outreach site. ### 2.2 Geographic Gaps
- **Zero trials in rural Midwest**, Southern US, or tribal lands
- All trials concentrated in academic medical centers in major cities
- **Travel burden** is the #1 barrier for rural prosthetic users

### 2.3 Gender Gap
- Most prosthetics trials enroll **90%+ male participants**
- Female-specific prosthetic fitting and rehabilitation research is virtually absent

### 2.4 Age Gap
- **No pediatric-specific prosthetic trials** found
- Older adult accommodations (transportation, caregiver support) rarely addressed

### 2.5 Socioeconomic Gaps
- Medicaid coverage gaps for prosthetic devices limit trial eligibility
- No trials specifically addressing low-income access barriers
- Insurance prior-authorization hurdles not studied as trial barriers

### 2.6 Technology Access Gap
- Cutting-edge prosthetics (osseointegration, powered limbs) tested almost exclusively in affluent academic centers
- No deployment trials in community settings or FQHCs
