## Part 1 — Clinical Trial Gap Analysis

### Scope

A search of ClinicalTrials.gov for **lower-limb prosthetic** trials returned **32 actively recruiting/recruiting-but-not-yet-recruiting trials** and **104 total active/historical trials**. Below is a summary of the key trends and gaps.

### Enrollment by Sponsor Type (Recruiting Trials)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Recruiting | Key Characteristic |
|---|---|---|---|
| **INDUSTRY** | 8 | 25% | Device/industry sponsors (e.g., Össur, Ottobock) |
| **OTHER (Academic/Univ.)** | 18 | 56% | Hospital- and university-led observational studies |
| **FEDERAL (FED)** | 5 | 16% | Federal agency-funded; mostly VA, DoD |
| **OTHER GOV** | 1 | 3% | Government hospital systems |

#### Key Gap: Sponsor Imbalance

- **Industry dominates the recruiting pipeline** relative to its share — but the bigger problem is the **near-absence of civic/nonprofit and community-based sponsors**, which means trials designed for underserved populations lack dedicated funding.
- **Federal trials account for only ~16% of recruiting sites** despite being the primary funders for rural and minority health research. The VA and DoD sponsor the most, but their recruitment hubs are concentrated in major metros.
- **Zero civic/nonprofit-funded trials** exist for lower-limb prosthetics — a critical gap for community outreach.

### Enrollment by Phase (All 104 Trials)

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Recruiting? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (Basic / Device Novelty)** | 76 | 73% | Many recruiting |
| **Unknown** | 22 | 21% | Mixed |
| **Phase 2** | 2 | 2% | 0 recruiting |
| **Phase 3** | 2 | 2% | 0 recruiting |
| **Phase 4** | 2 | 2% | 0 recruiting |

#### Key Gap: Phase Pipeline

- **89% of all trials are "NA" or "Unknown"** — the field is stuck at proof-of-concept with virtually no Phase 2/3 pathway to clinical adoption.
- Only **4 trials** exist in Phases 2–4, each with tiny enrollments (2–8 participants each).
- **No dose-ranging, efficacy, or comparative-effectiveness trials** are open for enrollment — underserved populations have no pathway to access advanced prosthetic technologies through trials.

#### Key Gap: Geographic Disparity

- **US dominates the trial landscape but is internally fragmented** — trials are concentrated in major academic hubs (Boston, NY, CA, TX) with **zero sites in rural Midwest, the South, or tribal lands**.
- **Chicago's South and West sides have NO prosthetic trial sites** despite serving large populations of amputees through VA and public hospitals.
- **No trials target non-English-speaking populations** — all consent materials, screening, and follow-up are in English.

## Part 3 — Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile Prosthetic & DME Outreach Van

**Problem identified:** Zero DME vendors within 500 m; no clinic for prosthetic fitting or adjustments. Amputees often cannot travel long distances for routine socket adjustments, liner replacements, or device checks.

**Proposal:** Deploy a **monthly mobile prosthetist van** staffed by a certified prosthetist and orthotist, parked at SRAL or a partner site (e.g., a South Side community center or VA clinic). Services include:
- Socket fitting checks and adjustments
- Liner replacement and skin integrity screening
- Basic device maintenance
- Eligibility screening for upcoming trials

**Estimated cost:** ~$15,000/yr (van lease + staff). **Potential funder:** FED/NIH small grant or industry partner (Össur, Ottobock).

### Strategy 2: Language & Cultural Access Program

**Problem identified:** All current trials have 0 Spanish-language consent forms and 0 minority recruitment targets. Chicago's Hispanic population is ~29% citywide but heavily concentrated in the South and West Sides.

**Proposal:**
- Translate all trial consent forms and screening materials into **Spanish, Polish, and Mandarin** (top non-English languages in Chicago)
- Hire **bilingual community outreach coordinators** for South/West Side canvassing
- Partner with **community health centers** (FQHCs) on the South and West Sides for trial awareness

### Strategy 3: FQHC & Safety-Net Hospital Partnerships

**Problem identified:** Chicago's South and West Sides have zero prosthetic trial sites, despite high populations of amputees treated at John H. Stroger Jr. Hospital, Englewood Hospital, and Provident Hospital.

**Proposal:**
- Establish **referral agreements** with 3–5 FQHCs and safety-net hospitals
- Train community health workers to screen patients for trial eligibility
- Provide transportation vouchers for patients accepted into trials

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach & VA Collaboration

**Problem identified:** Zero rural trial sites; 5 federal trials exist but recruit primarily at VA facilities in major metros (e.g., Jesse Brown VA in Chicago). Rural veterans have limited access.

**Proposal:**
- Launch a **telehealth prosthetic consultation line** for rural Illinois, Indiana, and Wisconsin
- Partner with **Jesse Brown VA** and **Quad Cities VA** for rural screening and referral
- Use mobile van visits at VA community-based outpatient clinics (CBOCs)

### Strategy 5: Pediatric Prosthetic Awareness Campaign

**Problem identified:** 0 recruiting pediatric prosthetic trials in the US. Children with limb loss need specialized prosthetics that grow and adapt, but no trials are open.

**Proposal:**
- Develop a **pediatric-focused outreach curriculum** in partnership with ER at Lurie Children's Hospital and Shriner's Hospital
- Create family-friendly trial education materials (illustrated, plain language)
- Advocate for a **new pediatric prosthetic trial** with public/private funding

### Strategy 6: SRAL Site-Level Improvements

**Problem identified:** SRAL has excellent transit and walkability but critical gaps in DME, parks, social services, ADA parking, and EV charging.

**Proposal:**
- Advocate for **ADA parking spaces** within 200 m on Erie St (contact Chicago DOT)
- Request **temporary EV charging stations** at the SRAL garage for trial participants with electric wheelchairs/vehicles
- Create a **community waiting/lounge area** inside SRAL for trial participants during visits
- Partner with **Walgreens 24/7** (680 N Lake Shore Dr, 180 m) to provide after-hours prosthetic supply pickup
