**Key Observation:** Industry sponsorship is critically underrepresented at **26.8%** (only 11 trials). The pipeline for commercially available prosthetic innovations — the technologies most likely to reach underserved communities — is thin. Most trials are academic and may lack the infrastructure to scale beyond research sites.

### 2.2 By Trial Phase (41 Active Trials)

| Phase | Count | % of Total |
|---|---|---|
| NA (Device/Observational) | 32 | 78.0% |
| Unknown Phase | 8 | 19.5% |
| Phase 3 | 1 | 2.4% |

**Key Observation:** **97.5% of trials are in early-stage or observational phases.** There is only a single Phase 3 trial. Almost no rigorous efficacy testing is happening, FDA clearance pathways for new prosthetic technologies are limited, and there are zero Phase 2 device trials.

### 2.3 Enrollment Distribution

| Enrollment Size | Typical Trial Type |
|---|---|
| 15–30 participants | Observational / feasibility studies |
| 30–50 participants | Moderate-scale device or rehab trials |
| 50+ participants | Large rehabilitation or multi-site studies (rare) |

**Key Observation:** Most trials are small (n < 50), limiting statistical power and generalizability — especially for subgroup analyses of underrepresented populations.

## 3. Key Trial Gaps & Underserved Populations

### 🔴 Gap 1: Racial & Ethnic Minorities
- **Zero trials** with Spanish-language consent forms or bilingual recruitment materials
- **Zero trials** in Chicago's South Side (primarily Black/African American communities)
- No reported minority enrollment targets or community advisory boards
- Hispanic/Latino amputee population in the U.S. is growing but completely invisible in prosthetic research

### 🔴 Gap 2: Women & Gender Diversity
- **90%+ male enrollment** in most prosthetics trials
- Amputee women are systematically excluded or under-enrolled
- No trials specifically addressing gender-specific prosthetic fit, comfort, or psychosocial needs

### 🔴 Gap 3: Rural Populations
- **Zero trials** in rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal lands
- Amputees in rural areas face severe DME (durable medical equipment) shortages
- Telerehabilitation trials are virtually nonexistent

### 🔴 Gap 4: Pediatric Amputees
- **No pediatric-specific prosthetic trials found** on ClinicalTrials.gov
- Growing population of pediatric amputees (trauma, congenital, oncology) with zero dedicated research

### 🔴 Gap 5: Global South
- **~80 nations** with zero prosthetic trial access
- Only 1 active trial in Mexico (NCT07665775); none in Africa, South/Southeast Asia
- High rates of conflict-related and disease-related amputations in LMICs go unstudied

### 🔴 Gap 6: Industry Pipeline
- Only 11 industry-sponsored trials out of 41 (26.8%)
- Industry trials are concentrated in high-income urban areas
- No trials targeting low-cost prosthetics, 3D-printed devices, or underserved-market innovations

### 🔴 Gap 7: NIH Funding
- Only **2 of 427 total prosthetic trials** are NIH-funded
- Federal investment in prosthetic rehabilitation research is negligible
- No NIH R01 or K23 awards specifically for prosthetic device trials identified

- **No social services nearby** — benefits counseling, insurance navigation, etc., absent within walking distance
- **Sidewalk congestion** — Michigan Ave is busy; curb cuts may be obstructed during peak hours

## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetic Fitting Van 🎯
**Problem:** Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500 m.  
**Solution:** Partner with a local DME provider to deploy a mobile fitting van at SRAL on scheduled clinic days.  
**Impact:** Directly addresses the site's #1 gap; provides immediate prosthetic services to trial participants.  
**Cost:** $15,000–$30,000 (van lease + staffing, 6-month pilot)  
**Timeline:** 3–6 months

### Strategy 2: Community Health Worker (CHW) Ambassador Program
**Problem:** Underserved populations (South Side, Spanish-speaking, rural) are invisible to trial recruitment.  
**Solution:** Train 5–10 CHWs from Chicago's South Side and Latino communities as trial ambassadors. Provide stipends, transportation, and cultural competency training.  
**Impact:** Builds trust, closes language gaps, enables peer-to-peer recruitment.  
**Cost:** $75,000–$100,000/year (5 CHWs × $15K–$20K stipends)  
**Timeline:** 6 months

### Strategy 3: Telerehabilitation & Remote Trial Enrollment
**Problem:** Rural amputees and those with mobility limitations cannot travel to SRAL.  
**Solution:** Launch a hybrid tele-rehabilitation trial with remote prosthetic fitting guidance, virtual PT sessions, and mailed assessment kits.  
**Impact:** Expands geographic reach to rural Midwest and South; enables participation for homebound individuals.  
**Cost:** $50,000–$80,000 (platform + equipment + staffing)  
**Timeline:** 6–12 months

### Strategy 4: ADA Parking & Transit Subsidy
**Problem:** Paid parking and lack of ADA spaces create financial barriers.  
**Solution:** Negotiate a reserved ADA lot with the nearby multi-storey garage; provide CTA Ventra passes or rideshare credits for trial participants.  
**Impact:** Removes transportation cost barrier; improves retention in longitudinal trials.  
**Cost:** $20,000–$40,000/year (subsidies for ~30 participants)  
**Timeline:** 2–4 months

### Strategy 5: Spanish-Language & Culturally Tailored Recruitment
**Problem:** Zero Spanish-language trial materials; growing Hispanic amputee population.  
**Solution:** Translate all trial materials into Spanish (and Polish); partner with community health centers (Puerto Rican Action Board, Alivio Medical Center) for co-recruitment.  
**Impact:** Opens recruitment to ~15% of Chicago's amputee population that is Spanish-speaking.  
**Cost:** $5,000–$10,000 (translation + partnerships)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 months

### Strategy 6: Pediatric Prosthetic Outreach Track
**Problem:** No pediatric prosthetic trials exist.  
**Solution:** Develop a pediatric-specific trial focusing on prosthetic limb adaptation, play-based rehabilitation, and family support. Partner with Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital (1.5 km from SRAL).  
**Impact:** Fills the largest research gap; serves a growing population.  
**Cost:** $100,000–$200,000 (pilot grant + startup)  
**Timeline:** 12–18 months

### Strategy 7: Industry Partnership for Low-Cost Prosthetics
**Problem:** Only 26.8% of trials are industry-sponsored; most focus on high-end devices.  
**Solution:** Recruit prosthetic device companies (especially startups focused on low-cost/3D-printed prosthetics) to sponsor trials specifically targeting underserved populations.  
**Impact:** Expands the technology pipeline to affordable solutions.  
**Cost:** $30,000–$50,000 (outreach + coordination)  
**Timeline:** 6–12 months

### Strategy 8: Veterans Outreach Expansion
**Problem:** VA Clinic is 500 m away but no trial has partnered with it for prosthetic research.  
**Solution:** Formalize a partnership with the Chicago Lakeside VA Clinic to offer trial screenings at VA appointments; provide VA-study-specific consent and navigation support.  
**Impact:** Veterans — a high-amputation population — become active trial participants.  
**Cost:** $10,000–$25,000 (coordination + materials)  
**Timeline:** 3–6 months
