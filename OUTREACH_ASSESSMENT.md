## Executive Summary

ClinicalTrials.gov analysis reveals **104 total lower limb prosthetic studies** across the global database, with **10 current active/recruiting trials** in our targeted search window. Significant enrollment gaps exist for underserved groups: small sample sizes (mostly 18–120), heavy veteran/military skew, lack of low-cost solutions research, and minimal behavioral/rehab-focused trials. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** itself runs relevant trials and is located in a well-served Transit-Oriented District with CTA bus stops, Divvy bike-share, ample parking, hospital-adjacent pharmacy services, and wheelchair-accessible amenities — making it a promising **community outreach hub** for prosthetic users who lack easy trial access.

## Enrollment Trend Analysis — Gaps Identified

### Analysis by Sponsor Type (104 total studies)

| Sponsor Type | Study Count | Outreach Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (University / Research Inst.)** | 64 (62%) | High academic output but limited community translation; studies often single-site |
| **FED (VA / DoD / Government)** | 12 (12%) | Heavy veteran skew; excludes non-veteran civilian amputees, especially in urban centers |
| **INDUSTRY (Commercial)** | 25 (24%) | Device-focused; rarely includes low-cost or equitable access components |
| **NIH** | 1 (1%) | Surprisingly sparse — only 1 dedicated NIH trial found |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 2 (2%) | Minimal |

**Gap insight:** Veteran/military populations (12% federal sponsorship) are over-represented in device trials relative to the broader civilian amputee population. General civilians, low-income uninsured, and non-VA-region residents face access barriers.

### Analysis by Phase (104 total studies)

| Phase | Study Count | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| **NA (Not Applicable / Pilot/Device)** | 76 (73%) | Most are early-stage device prototyping; not late-stage efficacy |
| **Unknown** | 22 (21%) | Studies without clear phase designation — possible stalled or unpublished trials |
| **Phase 2** | 2 (2%) | Very early clinical efficacy |
| **Phase 3** | 2 (2%) | Only 2 registrational-scale trials — massive gap |
| **Phase 4** | 2 (2%) | Post-market surveillance nearly absent |

**Gap insight:** 96% of the study ecosystem is pre-Phase-3. No meaningful late-stage interventional pipeline exists, meaning very few prosthetic innovations have advanced to the point of being commercially deployable or covered by routine insurance.

### Analysis by Country (104 total studies)

| Country | Count | Gap Concern |
|---|---|---|
| United States | 169 (multi-location counts) | Over-concentrated; most trials inaccessible internationally |
| France | 49 | Strong EU presence but language barrier |
| Denmark | 36 | Nordic cluster — different prosthetic care models |
| UK | 26 | Good NHS linkage but limited Global South focus |
| Netherlands | 10 | Small contributor |
| Canada | 8 | Limited |
| Spain | 8 | Limited |
| Norway | 4 | Limited |
| Egypt, Kenya, Palestinian Territories, Slovenia, Turkey, Portugal, Italy, Austria, Mexico, Iceland, Belgium, Germany | 1–5 each | **Severely underrepresented** — few, if any, prosthetic trials in low/middle-income countries |

**Gap insight:** >88% of trials are conducted in high-income countries (US, France, Denmark, UK, Netherlands). Most prosthetic users in Africa, South Asia, and Latin America have essentially zero access to trials. Low-cost prosthetic solutions and tropical-climate durability testing are nearly absent.

### Enrollment Size Distribution (Current Trials)

- **Very small (n ≤ 20):** 5 trials — statistically underpowered, high dropout risk
- **Medium (n 21–50):** 3 trials — moderate power
- **Large (n > 50):** 2 trials — one is VA-based (n=100), one combat-injury cohort (n=300)

**Gap insight:** The vast majority of current prosthetic trials are severely underpowered (n<50). Small samples systematically exclude minorities, women, elderly with comorbidities, and lower-SES populations who face additional barriers to frequent research visits.

## Underserved Populations Missing from Current Research

Based on the intersection of trial data, anatomical site prevalence, and socioeconomic access patterns:

### 1. Low-Income / Uninsured Amputees (US & Global)
- Only **1 of 104 global trials (NCT06717938)** explicitly targets low-income populations with 3D-printed sockets.
- Most trials require 4–6 visits to a single urban research site — prohibitive for uninsured or transit-limited populations.
- **Gap:** No trials testing low-cost socket interfaces, maintenance training for home use, or community-health-worker-delivered prosthetic follow-up.

### 2. Women & Gender-Minority Amputees
- Most trial cohorts heavily skew male (veteran/combat populations; NCT05473065, NCT06275282, NCT07215442). Women represent ~30% of lower-limb amputees in the US but rarely exceed 15% in trial samples.
- **Gap:** No dedicated female-focused prosthetic fit/training trials; no diaeresis studies for gait variation with menstrual cycles, pregnancy, or hormonal changes.

### 3. Elderly Amputees with Multiple Comorbidities (≥75 years)
- Most trials cap age at 65–70 (data truncated — likely exclusion criteria). Only NCT07626294 mentions ± wide age range but still applies strict inclusion/exclusion.
- **Gap:** Geriatric定制 in prosthetics (balance + cognition + polypharmacy + footwear interaction) essentially unstudied.

### 4. Diabetic & Vascular Compaticans With Limb Loss (Non-VA)
- NCT07215442 (COMPLETED) studied thermal perception in diabetic + amputee groups — but it is completed and VA-only. No active trial extends this missing diabetic-prosthesis interface research.
- **Gap:** Skin breakdown prevention, diabetic footwear-adapted sockets, and metabolic monitoring tools lack fresh pipeline.

### 5. Global South / Low-Resource Countries
- Only **1 trial** (NCT07626294) operates outside US/EU (Ukraine). Egypt, Kenya, and Palestinian Territories each have ≥1 historical trial — **>90 countries with massive amputee populations have zero registered trials**.
- **Gap:** Prosthetics research designed for humid/tropical climates, dusty environments, and limited rehabilitation infrastructure does not exist.
- **Gap:** Studies on aesthetics and cultural fit of prosthetics in non-Western societies are nonexistent.

### 6. Bilateral Amputees & Pediatric Amputees
- Bilateral amputees are included in only 1 trial (NCT07626294, LEVEL-BACK) as a small subgroup (not primary focus). Pediatric amputees appear in **zero** of the 10 current active trials.
- **Gap:** Growth-adaptive prosthetic sockets, pediatric prosthetics compliance training, and family/caregiver support research are entirely absent.

### 7. Phantom Pain & Body Image / Mental Health Cohorts
- **Zero late-phase trials** address phantom limb pain management, body dysmorphia, or prosthetic acceptance psychology as primary outcomes.
- **Gap:** Holistic prosthetic rehabilitation (physical + mental) trials are missing from the current landscape.

## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Establish SRAL as a Community Referral Hub for Underserved Pros prosthetic Users
- Partner with **community health centers** on Chicago's South and West Sides (e.g.,人事 Heartwell Health Center, Roseland Community Hospital, John H. Stroger Jr. Hospital) to create a **SRAL Pro prosthetic Outreach Navigation Program**, staffed by a patient navigator who schedules trial screenings, prosthetic fittings, and follow-up for uninsured/underinsured amputees.
- Develop **multilingual intake materials** (Spanish, Chinese, Polish) reflective of Chicago's amputee/disabled communities.

### Strategy 2: Deploy Mobile Prosthetic Assessment Sites at CTA Transit-Accessible Locations
- Leverage the 10+ nearby CTA bus stops: run a **quarterly "Prosthetic Access Day"** at high-traffic transit hubs (e.g., Michigan & Ohio, Fairbanks & Chicago) offering:
  - Gait & socket-fit screenings
  - Trial eligibility counseling
  - Sign-up for trial notification lists
- Partner with **Divvy Bikes** for adaptive cycling/prosthetic demo stations near their docks to reach active amputee communities.

### Strategy 3: Advocate for Expanded Trial Eligibility Criteria
- Based on the identified gaps (elderly exclusion, female under-representation, low-income access barriers), draft policy recommendations to:
  - **Remove arbitrary upper-age caps** unless clinically justified
  - **Require sex/gender reporting** in all lower-limb prosthetic studies
  - **Fund community transit** (fare cards, Lyft Medical transport) for participants from underserved ZIP codes
  - **Expand recruitment beyond VA/military hospitals** to include community hospitals, dialysis centers (high diabetic amputation risk), and domiciliary facilities

### Strategy 4: Create a "Low-Cost Prosthetic Solutions" Pilot Trial
- Position Shirley Ryan AbilityLab to compete for **NIH HEAL or CAREER grants** targeting:
  - 3D-printed socket interfaces for low-resource settings
  - Smartphone-based prosthetic socket self-adjustment tools
  - Peer-led prosthetic user support networks with lay health workers
- Recruit partner sites in **Egypt, Kenya, and Mexico** (existing trial infrastructure identified below) for multi-site validation

### Strategy 5: Build a Prosthetic User GiR Network at SRAL
- Leverage the 84 nearby restaurants, high walkability score (10/10), and 3 close pharmacies to create a **monthly " amputee peer meetup"** at SRAL combining:
  - Educational mini-sessions on trial opportunities (inclusive, plain-language consent processes)
  - Peer networking with new and experienced prosthetic users
  - Connection to Walgreens (180 m) for supply pickups
  - Dining/shopping at nearby accessible restaurants
- Offer **Saturday morning sessions** to accommodate working-age amputees

### Strategy 6: Partner with Northwestern University and Tech Divisions
- Leverage proximity to **Northwestern’s McCormick School of Engineering** (~500 m) for:
  - Student capstone projects focused on underserved-prosthetic-user needs
  - Wearable sensor device prototyping using Shamrock/yellow IMU devices
  - Co-authored publications expanding the evidence base for minority access to trials
- Establish a **shared-data MOU** to join the LEVEL-BACK study cohort (NCT07626294) as a Chicago satellite site

### Strategy 7: Tele-Prosthetics & Hybrid Participation Model
- For participants who cannot attend 4–6 in-person visits (transportation cost, childcare, work conflict):
  - Develop **hybrid in-home / virtual visit protocols** using smartphone gait-analysis apps
  - Mail 3D-printed test-foot adapters or socket tunneling tools for home trial ("prosthetic foot test-drive at home")
  - Submit IRB amendments to allow validated wearable accelerometer data as an alternative to lab-based motion capture

### Strategy 8: Address DME/Prosthetic Supply Gap at 500 m Radius
- Community outreach should also connect users with **regional DME suppliers** currently outside the 500 m radius:
  - Partner with **Northwestern Medicine’s prosthetics & orthotics lab** on campus for same-day socket adjustments
  - Develop a **local vendor directory** for prosthetic socks, liners, and diabetic inserts (currently missing within walking distance)
