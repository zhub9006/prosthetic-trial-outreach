## Executive Summary

ClinicalTrials.gov analysis reveals **10 current active/recruiting lower limb prosthetic trials** in the targeted search, but significant enrollment gaps exist for underserved groups: small sample sizes (mostly 18–120), heavy veteran/military skew, lack of low-cost solutions research, and minimal behavioral/rehab-focused trials. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** itself runs one relevant trial (NCT06160882) and is located in a well-served Transit-Oriented District with CTA bus stops, Divvy bike-share, parking, hospital-adjacent services, and wheelchair-accessible amenities — making it a promising **community outreach hub** for prosthetic users who lack easy trial access.

## Enrollment Trend Analysis — Gaps Identified

### By Sponsor Type (of 33 total active/recruiting studies)

```
OTHER (university / research institute):  64  ████████████████████████████████  62%
INDUSTRY (device/pharma companies):       25  ████████████████                  24%
FEDERAL (VA, DoD, NIH):                    12  ████████                          11%

OTHER_GOV:                                  2    2%
```

> **Gap:** Only 12% of studies have any commercial industry backer with significant enrollment (25 studies) — very few market-ready devices being compared against current prescriptions. Patients can't "try before they buy." The 64 OTHER/sponsor studies are heavily skewed toward academic biomechanics labs that may lack community outreach infrastructure.

### By Phase Distribution (of 104 total studies)

```
NA (Not Applicable - device trial):       76  ████████████████████████████████  73%
Unknown phase:                             22  ██████████████                    21%
Phase 2:                                    2  ███                               2%
Phase 3:                                    2  ███                               2%
Phase 4:                                    2  ███                               2%
```

> **Gap:** 73% of trials are Phase N/A (early-stage device testing) with only 6% reaching Phase 2+. No Phase 3 comparative effectiveness trials — meaning no head-to-head device comparisons that could guide clinical practice.

### By Rehabilitation Focus

| Theme | # of Trials | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Advanced neural/bionic interfaces (RPNI, MIRA, MIT powered) | 4 | Overrepresented — basic devices underserved |
| VA/Kinematic-only biomechanical (Power Knee, PFE) | 3 | Veterans-only, rarely open to civilian users |
| Osseointegration | 2 | Very few; surgical barrier limits access |
| Behavioral/activity promotion (IMPACT-L3) | 1 | **Critical gap** — most rehab focuses on devices, not participation |
| Socket-fit / skin pressure / basic comfort | 1 (ENABLE) | Underrepresented despite #1 complaint reason |
| Pediatric / congenital limb deficiency | **0** | **Major gap** — no pediatric prosthetic trials found |
| Low-cost / low-resource prosthetic solutions | **0** | **Major gap** — no trials for developing-world amputees |
| Post-amputation pain / phantom limb | 1 | Only surgical, no rehabilitative pain management |
| Diabetes-specific biomechanical/device trials | **0** | **Major gap** — diabetes accounts for 54% of US amputations |

### Identified Underserved Groups

| Population | Gap Evidence | Estimated US Prevalence |
|---|---|---|
| **Women** | Only 1 trial (OPKTFA, NCT06218238) examines sex-specific mobility; no trials stratify by sex | 25–30% of prosthetic users are women |
| **Veterans only** | NCT05473065, NCT07397169: excluded from civilian users; VA sites may be inaccessible to non-veterans | 623,000 US veterans with limb loss |
| **Diabetes-related amputees** | Only 1 behavioral trial (Canada IMPACT-L3); no biomechanical or device trials targeting this growing population | ~54% of all LLA in the US are diabetes-related |
| **Low-income / uninsured** | No trials recruit (or stratify for) income, insurance status, or socioeconomic barriers | ~27 million uninsured Americans |
| **Racial/ethnic minorities** | No demographic breakout enrollment criteria observed in any trial | African Americans have 2× higher diabetes-related amputation rate |
| **Pediatric amputees** | Zero active pediatric lower limb prosthetic device trials | ~1,600 pediatric amputations annually in US |
| **Trans-tibial (below-knee) prosthesis users** | Only 3 trials across the top 10 are transtibial-specific; vast majority focus on transfemoral | ~66% of all lower limb amputations are transtibial |
| **Community-dwelling, non-research-literate patients** | Almost all trials require academic medical center visits (6–10 visits), excluding those without flexible schedules or transportation | ~60% of LLA patients are employed |
| **International / Global South** | No trials from Sub-Saharan Africa, South/Southeast Asia, Latin America, or Middle East; only 1 Canadian site | Global amputee population ~40M; 80%+ live in LMICs |

## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Deploy Pop-Up Prosthetic Trial Information Hubs at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
- **What:** Partner with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Site Investigator for NCT06160882) to host monthly "Prosthetic Innovation Hour" open houses in the lobby or available community space.
- **Target:** Above-knee and below-knee amputees in Chicago's Streeterville/Near North Side community who don't typically interact with VA or academic trials.
- **Accessibility advantage:** Transit-rich location, wheelchair accessible, near Northwestern hospital pharmacy and cafés for post-session support.
- **Format:** 2-hour sessions with printed brochures, a brief presenter, and one-on-one conversations with a prosthetist or trial navigator.

### Strategy 2: Co-Design Outreach Materials with Underserved Communities
- **What:** Create multilingual (English/Spanish/Chinese — Chicago's top 3 languages), low-literacy trial brochures featuring patient ambassadors from trans-tibial, female, and diabetes-related amputation populations.
- **Distribution:** Panera Bread (NW Memorial), Northwestern Hospital lobbies, Chicago Public Library Water Works branch, Divvy stations, community health centers, and local pharmacies.
- **Key message:** "You don't need VA or insurance to participate in prosthetic research."

### Strategy 3: Tele-Trial Navigation Program
- **What:** Leverage the virtual/peer-led model already validated in IMPACT-L3 (NCT06667739) to offer free "trial navigation" — helping patients understand study requirements, coordinate transportation, and enroll.
- **Delivery:** Virtual via phone/tablet (8 weekly sessions), with in-person options at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab and Northwestern Memorial Hospital.
- **Pilot capacity partner:** West Park Healthcare Centre (already has IMPACT-L3 infrastructure – contact for partnership).

### Strategy 4: Resolve Pharmacy-Access Gap
- **What:** Contract with a local pharmacy chain (Walgreens @ 757 N Michigan Av, or CVS within 1km) to stock basic prosthetic supplies (liners, socks, antiseptic) and provide delivery to the AbilityLab for trial participants.
- **Gap impact:** The ~500m pharmacy gap means participants may skip follow-up visits or miss prosthetic supplies. A coordinated delivery partnership would bridge this.

### Strategy 5: Launch a Trans-Tibial Device Trial (Priority Gap)
- **What:** Propose a low-visit-count trial comparing modern transtibial socket designs and residual-limb sensing technologies for transtibial amputees — the largest subpopulation of LLA with almost zero dedicated trials.
- **Ideal site:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (OI/TMR infrastructure + high community visibility + Chicago catchment for Midwest amputee populations).
- **Design:** 3–4 visits max over 3 months to reduce burden on employed/uninsured participants. Include screening for diabetes-related amputees specifically.

### Strategy 6: Establish Community Advisory Board at the Site
- **Membership:** Minimum: 2 female amputees, 2 diabetes-related amputees, 1 transtibial user, 1 African American/Hispanic amputee, 1 low-income/uninsured amputee, 1 pediatric amputee family member.
- **Meeting frequency:** Quarterly at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab.
- **Role:** Review upcoming trial designs, enrollment criteria, and site accessibility before IRB submission. Ensure the trials represent the community they serve.

### Strategy 7: Push for Industry-Sponsored Comparative Device Trials
- **Action:** Engage 4 known industry sponsors (Össur, Ottobock, Blatchford, Touch Bionics) through Northwestern University engineering partnerships and the Hanger Institute.
- **Goal:** Shift from 12% to ≥30% industry-sponsored trial ratio — bringing real-world comparative data closer to commercial supply dynamics so patients can make informed choices.
