**Key Finding:** There is **zero industry-sponsored** prosthetic trial activity in the current lower-limb-prosthetic pipeline. All trials are academic or government-funded, resulting in limited resources, small sample sizes, and narrow geographic reach. No sponsor is currently running a multi-site or pragmatic effectiveness trial.

### 1.3 Enrollment Trends by Phase

| Phase | # Trials | Typical Enrollment | Notes |
|-------|----------|--------------------|-------|
| Phase I–III | **0** | — | No efficacy or comparative effectiveness trials |
| Phase IV | **0** | — | No post-market surveillance or implementation studies |
| NA (Early/Device Studies) | 5 | 10–128 | Pilot/device feasibility studies only |

**Key Finding:** **No trials have reached Phase III or later.** The entire pipeline consists of early-phase pilot or device-feasibility studies. This means there is a critical gap in: (a) comparative effectiveness research (e.g., different prosthetic components or rehabilitation protocols), (b) large-scale outcome studies, and (c) implementation/pragmatic trials that would inform real-world clinical practice.

### 1.4 Geographic & Population Gaps

| Gap Dimension | Observation | Underserved Group |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Geographic** | No Chicago-area trial is currently recruiting; the Northwestern OPORP trial (most relevant locally) is not yet recruiting (est. start Dec 2026) | Chicago prosthetic users must travel to Boston (MIT) or out-of-state for trial participation |
| **Rural** | No trials target rural or underserved-community populations | Rural amputees face travel, cost, and access barriers |
| **Low-Income / Uninsured** | No trials have eligibility criteria addressing cost barriers or include safety-net hospital sites | Low-income amputees are systematically excluded from trials requiring frequent visits and uncovered devices |
| **Minority / Ethnic** | Only 1 trial (Gaza) explicitly serves a conflict-affected minority; no US trials focus on racial/ethnic minority amputees | Black, Hispanic, Indigenous amputees are underrepresented in prosthetics research |
| **Pediatric** | No trials focus on congenital or pediatric amputation | Children with limb loss have no dedicated prosthetic trial pipeline |
| **Elderly (75+)** | Eligibility upper limits cap at 70–80; trials rarely target the most-need elderly population | Elderly amputees with comorbidities are excluded from most trials |
| **Veterans** | 1 trial exists (Northwestern) but is not yet recruiting | Veteran access is delayed by 6+ months |
| **Bilateral Amputees** | All trials exclude contralateral limb loss | Bilateral amputees — the highest-need group — are systematically excluded |
| **Device Financing** | No trials compare prosthetic provision/funding models | Low-income users who need device financing support are not studied |

### 1.5 Trial Gap Summary

1. **No large (>100 participant) recruiting trials** — most are pilots with <50 participants
2. **No industry sponsor** — limits funding, multi-site reach, and real-world scalability
3. **No Phase III+ effectiveness or implementation trials** — the evidence base for prosthetic rehabilitation remains thin
4. **No Chicago-area recruiting trial** — the closest recruiting site is MIT (Boston), ~850 miles away
5. **No trials for underserved minorities, rural populations, or low-income uninsured users**
6. **No pediatric or bilateral-amputee trials**
7. **No pragmatic or cost-effectiveness trials** examining how prosthetic services should be delivered, not just whether a device works in a lab

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: Prosthetic Trial Navigation Program at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

**Goal:** Bridge the gap between Chicago-area prosthetic users and distant recruiting trials.

- Co-locate a trial-navigator/coordinator at SRAL
- Provide transportation vouchers or telehealth screening for distant trials (e.g., MIT in Boston)
- Target excluded populations: bilateral amputees, elderly 75+, low-income/uninsured, minorities
- **Estimated reach:** 50–100 referrals/year from SRAL's existing prosthetic patient volume

### 3.2 Strategy 2: Advocate for a Multi-Site Pragmatic Trial in Chicago

**Goal:** Address the lack of a recruiting Chicago-area trial and the absence of Phase III+ studies.

- Propose a multi-site trial (Northwestern, SRAL, Jesse Brown VA, UIC) comparing:
  - Standard vs. personalized prosthetic fitting protocols
  - Different rehabilitation intensity models
  - Cost-effectiveness of early vs. delayed prosthesis provision
- Include underserved populations as a specific aim (rural, minority, low-income, bilateral)
- Seek industry co-sponsorship to ensure scale and sustainability
- **Principal investigator network:** Leverage Matthew Major (Northwestern OPORP), existing SRAL research infrastructure

### 3.3 Strategy 3: Community Health Worker (CHW) Pilot for Prosthetic Trial Recruitment

**Goal:** Reach underserved populations not currently engaged in the healthcare system.

- Train CHWs in Chicago's South Side, West Side, and suburban communities with high amputee prevalence (diabetes-related, vascular disease, trauma)
- CHWs conduct outreach at FQHCs, VA enrollment events, amputee support groups, diabetes education programs
- CHWs provide trial education, assist with screening, and connect eligible participants to trial sites
- **Target communities:** South Side (high diabetes/amputation rates), West Side, south suburbs

### 3.4 Strategy 4: Telehealth-First Screening to Reduce Access Barriers

**Goal:** Reduce the travel and cost burden that excludes rural and low-income users from trials.

- Implement virtual screening visits for all prosthetic trial eligibility assessments
- Provide loaner tablets/webcam kits to uninsured participants
- Use the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as the central tele-rehabilitation hub for remote monitoring
- **Advantage:** Aligns with NIH HEAL Initiative and VA telehealth priorities

### 3.5 Strategy 5: Pediatric & Bilateral Amputee Pilot Registry

**Goal:** Fill the most extreme gaps — no trials exist for these populations.

- Create a Chicago-based registry (via SRAL and collaborating hospitals) of:
  - Children with congenital or acquired limb loss
  - Bilateral amputees of all ages
- Use registry to (a) track natural history, (b) enable rapid enrollment in future trials, (c) advocate for dedicated funding
- Partner with Shriners Hospitals for Children (Chicago and Honolulu) for pediatric reach

### 3.6 Strategy 6: Industry Engagement for Co-Sponsored Trials

**Goal:** Address the complete absence of industry-sponsored prosthetic trials.

- Identify prosthetic manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Hanger, Blatchford, Open Bionics) and device pharma partners
- Pitch co-sponsored pragmatic trials with real-world comparators and required inclusion of underserved populations
- Leverage NIH/NCATS APPIT program for device-trial pathways
