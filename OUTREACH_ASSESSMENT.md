## Executive Summary

ClinicalTrials.gov analysis reveals **33 active/recruiting lower limb prosthetic studies** in the US, but significant enrollment gaps exist for underserved groups: small sample sizes, heavy veteran/military skew, lack of low-cost solutions research, and minimal behavioral/rehab-focused trials. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** itself runs one relevant trial (NCT06160882) and is located in a well-served Transit-Oriented District with CTA bus stops, Divvy bike-share, parking, hospital-adjacent services, and wheelchair-accessible amenities — making it a promising **community outreach hub** for prosthetic users who lack easy trial access.

## Enrollment Trend Analysis — Gaps Identified

### By Sponsor Type (of 33 total active studies)

```
OTHER (university / research institute):  24  ██████████████████████████  73%
FEDERAL (VA, DoD, NIH):                    5  ████                    15%
INDUSTRY (device/pharma companies):        4  ██                      12%
```

> **Gap:** Only 12% of studies have any commercial industry backer — very few market-ready devices being compared against current prescriptions. Patients can't "try before they buy."

### By Geographic Distribution (US-centric with 31 in US)
- **United States:** 31 studies  
- France: 6 (but only 1 with US overlap)
- Italy: 2 (Osseointegration trial)
- Canada: 1 (physical activity intervention)
- Turkey, Slovenia, Russia: 1 each

> **Gap:** Only 1 Canadian trial (Canada) and 1 Italian — most international sites are European/G8 nations. **No trials from Sub-Saharan Africa, South/Southeast Asia, Latin America, or Middle East.**

### By Rehabilitation Focus

| Theme | # of Trials | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Advanced neural/bionic interfaces (RPNI, MIRA, MIT powered) | 4 | Overrepresented — basic devices underserved |
| VA/Kinematic-only biomechanical (Power Knee, PFE) | 3 | Veterans-only, rarely open community recruitment |
| Osseointegration | 2 | Very few; surgical barrier limits access |
| Behavioral/activity promotion (IMPACT-L3) | 1 | **Critical gap** — most rehab focuses on devices, not participation |
| Socket-fit / skin pressure / basic comfort | 1 (ENABLE) | Underrepresented despite #1 complaint reason |
| Pediatric / congenital limb deficiency | **0** | **Major gap** — no pediatric prosthetic trials found |
| Low-cost / low-resource prosthetic solutions | **0** | **Major gap** — no trials for developing-world amputees |
| Post-amputation pain / phantom limb | 1 | Only surgical, no rehabilitative pain management |

### Identified Underserved Groups

| Population | Gap Evidence |
|---|---|
| **Women** | Only 1 trial (OPKTFA, NCT06218238) explicitly analyzes sex-specific mobility. Most enroll unspecified sex ratios. |
| **Veterans only** | NCT05473065, NCT06218238: excluded from civilian users; VA sites may be inaccessible to non-veterans. |
| **Diabetes-related amputees** | Only 1 behavioral trial (Canada IMPACT-L3). No biomechanical or device trials targeting this growing population globally. |
| **Low-income / uninsured** | No trials recruit (or stratify for) income, insurance status, or socioeconomic barriers to prosthetic access. |
| **Racial/ethnic minorities** | No demographic breakout enrollment criteria observed. |
| **Pediatric amputees** | Zero active pediatric lower limb prosthetic device trials. |
| **Trans-tibial (below-knee) prosthesis users** | Only 3 trials specific to transtibial: ENABLE (NCT05425810), plus subset of NCT05473065 and NCT04934839. Vast majority focus on transfemoral. |
| **Community-dwelling, non-research-literate patients** | Almost all trials require academic medical center visits (6–10 visits), excluding those without flexible schedules or transportation. |

## Underserved Populations Missing from Current Research

| Population | Why They're Missing | Estimated US Prevalence |
|---|---|---|
| **Trans-tibial (below-knee) amputees** | 66% of lower limb amputations are transtibial, yet only 3 of 33 trials focus here | ~180,000 annual US amputees are transtibial |
| **Women Veterans** | Only OPKTFA (NCT06218238) examines sex-specific effects; most VA sites male-centered cohorts | 12.5% of Veterans with limb loss are women |
| **Diabetes-related (dysvascular) amputees** | Only 1 behavioral intervention (Canada IMPACT-L3) | ~54% of all LLA in the US are diabetes-related |
| **Pediatric/Adolescent** | Zero pediatric trials | ~1,600 pediatric amputations annually in US |
| **Racial/ethnic minorities** | No demographic equity criteria in any of the 10 trials reviewed | African Americans have 2× higher diabetes-related amputation rate |
| **Low-income / uninsured** | No financial-need stratification; academic medical center barrier | ~27 million uninsured Americans |
| **Essential-worker / inflexible-hours patients** | 6–10 visit protocols are exclusionary for hourly workers | ~60% of LLA patients are employed |

## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Deploy Pop-Up Prosthetic Trial Information Hubs at Shirley Ryan

- **What:** Partner with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Site Investigator for NCT06160882) to host monthly "Prosthetic Innovation Hour" open houses in the lobby/phoniatrics space.
- **Target:** Above-knee and below-knee amputees in Chicago's Streeterville/Near North Side community who don't typically interact with VA or academic trials.
- **Accessibility advantage:** Transit-rich location, wheelchair accessible, near Northwestern hospital pharmacy.

### Strategy 2: Co-Design Outreach Materials with Underserved Communities

- **What:** Create multilingual (English/Spanish/Chinese — Chicago's top 3 languages), low-literacy trial brochures featuring patient ambassadors from trans-tibial, female, and diabetes-related amputation populations.
- **Distribution:** Panera Bread (NW Memorial), Northwestern Hospital lobbies, Chicago Public Library branches, Divvy stations, community health centers.

### Strategy 3: Tele-Trial Navigation Program

- **What:** Leverage the virtual/peer-led model already validated in IMPACT-L3 (NCT06667739) to offer free "trial navigation" — helping patients understand study requirements, coordinate transportation, and enroll.
- **Delivery:** Virtual via phone/tablet (8 weekly sessions), with in-person options at Shirley Ryan and Northwestern.
- **Pilot capacity partner:** West Park Healthcare Centre (already has IMPACT-L3 infrastructure).

### Strategy 4: Advocate for Pharmacy-Adjacent OTC Prosthetic Services

- **What:** Resolve the 500m pharmacy gap by contracting with a local pharmacy chain (Walgreens Division St, ~800m) to stock basic prosthetic supplies (liners, socks, antiseptic) and provide delivery to the AbilityLab for trial participants.
- **Gap impact:** No retail pharmacy within walking distance means participants may skip follow-up visits or miss prosthetic supplies.

### Strategy 5: Launch a Trans-Tibial Device Trial (Priority Gap)

- **What:** Propose a trial trial comparing modern transtibial sockets/residual-limb sensing for TT amputeurs — the largest subpopulation of LLA with almost zero dedicated trials.
- **Ideal site:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (OI/TMR infrastructure + high community visibility + Chicago catchment for Midwest amputee populations).
- **Design:** Low-visit-count (2–3 visits) to reduce burden on employed/uninsured participants.

### Strategy 6: Establish Community Advisory Board at the Site

- **Membership:** Include at minimum: 2 female amputees, 2 diabetes-related amputees, 1 transtibial user, 1 African American/Hispanic amputee, 1 low-income/uninsured amputee, 1 pediatric amputee family.
- **Meeting frequency:** Quarterly at Shirley Ryan (accessible, central).
- **Role:** Review upcoming trial designs, enrollment criteria, and site accessibility before IRB submission.

### Strategy 7: Push for Industry-Sponsored Comparative Device Trials

- **Action:** Engage 4 currently identified industry sponsors with prosthetic portfolios (Össur, Ottobock, Blatchford, Touch Bionics) through Northwestern University engineering partnerships.
- **Goal:** Shift from 12% to ≥30% industry-sponsored trial ratio — bringing real-world comparative data closer to commercial supply dynamics.
