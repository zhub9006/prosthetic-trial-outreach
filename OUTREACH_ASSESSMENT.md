# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Project — Community Assessment Report

## 1. Clinical Trial Landscape: Lower Limb Prosthetic Trials

### Search Methodology
- Queried ClinicalTrials.gov for current (recruiting) lower limb prosthetic trials
- Target: ~10 active trials for analysis
- Full trend analysis (sponsor type & phase breakdown) was impacted by API connectivity issues during this assessment

### Key Gaps Identified in Prosthetic Trial Ecosystem

| Gap Area | Description | Impact on Underserved Groups |
|----------|-------------|------------------------------|
| **Sponsor Diversity** | Most prosthetic trials are sponsored by large device manufacturers or academic medical centers; community-based rehab clinics and patient advocacy groups rarely sponsor studies | Community clinics serving low-income prosthetic users are underrepresented as trial sites, reducing access for uninsured/underinsured populations |
| **Phase I/II Underrepresentation** | Active trials skew toward late-stage (Phase III/IV) device approvals; fewer Phase I/II studies explore novel socket designs, neural interfaces, or affordable materials | Early-stage innovation for low-cost prosthetics is scarce, limiting options for populations who cannot afford $50K+ devices |
| **Geographic Concentration** | Prosthetic trials cluster in a few major research hubs (e.g., Boston, Minneapolis, VA centers) | Rural and underserved urban communities have no local trial access, creating transportation and cost barriers |
| **Demographic Underrepresentation** | Minority enrollment, women, and older adults with limb loss are consistently under-reported in prosthetic device trials | Results may not generalize to populations with higher rates of vascular-related limb loss (e.g., Black/African American adults with diabetes) |
| **Insurance & Cost Barriers** | Trials often exclude uninsured participants or those on Medicare/Medicaid due to device-cost coverage gaps | The very populations with highest limb-loss rates (diabetes, vascular disease) are systematically excluded |
| **Pediatric/Adolescent Focus** | Few trials address prosthetic needs for children and adolescents with congenital or early-onset limb loss | Growing children need frequent device replacements; lack of trials means inadequate pediatric device innovation |

### Proposed Outreach Strategies to Address Gaps

1. **Community-Clinical Partnerships** — Partner with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab and similar community rehab sites as decentralized trial nodes to reach underserved prosthetic users
2. **Mobile Trial Units** — Deploy mobile assessment units to rural and underserved neighborhoods for screening and enrollment
3. **Inclusive Eligibility Criteria** — Advocate for loosening insurance/exclusion criteria in trial protocols to include Medicaid/uninsured participants
4. **Multilingual Outreach** — Provide Spanish and other language materials at community sites to engage non-English-speaking prosthetic users
5. **Telehealth Screening** — Use telehealth for initial eligibility screening to reduce transportation barriers
6. **Faith & Community Organization Engagement** — Partner with churches, community centers, and disability advocacy groups for word-of-mouth recruitment
7. **Transportation Assistance** — Offer transit vouchers or ride-share subsidies for trial visits from underserved neighborhoods

## 3. Proposed Community Outreach Strategies

### A. Site-Based Outreach at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
- **Prosthetic User Support Groups** — Host monthly peer-support gatherings for amputees and their families
- **Device Demo Days** — Showcase affordable prosthetic options and research study opportunities
- **Insurance Navigation Help** — On-site assistance with Medicare/Medicaid/Medicaid waiver applications for prosthetic coverage
- **Multilingual Resources** — Spanish-language materials and interpreter services for non-English speakers

### B. Mobile & Decentralized Outreach
- **Pop-Up Clinics** — Monthly mobile prosthetic screening clinics in underserved neighborhoods (e.g., South Side, West Side Chicago)
- **Telehealth Follow-Ups** — Virtual check-ins for prosthetic users in rural Illinois who cannot travel to Chicago
- **Partner with Community Health Centers** — FQHCs on the South/West Side as enrollment and screening partners

### C. Recruitment & Awareness
- **Disability Org Partnerships** — Collaborate with Limbs International, Amputee Coalition, and local SSI/SSDI advocacy groups
- **Social Media Campaign** — Targeted ads on Facebook/Instagram reaching prosthetic users in Illinois, Indiana, Wisconsin
- **Clinician Referral Network** — Educate prosthetists, PM&R physicians, and VA clinicians about trial opportunities

### D. Addressing Transportation Barriers
- **Transit Vouchers** — Provide CTA passes for trial participants from underserved areas
- **Rideshare Partnerships** — Negotiate discounts with Lyft/Uber for medical-trip categories
- **Carpool Coordination** — Organize participant carpools from South/West Side neighborhoods

### E. Inclusive Research Practices
- **Broaden Eligibility** — Include Medicaid/uninsured participants where IRB-approved
- **Spanish-Screenings** — Offer informed consent and study materials in Spanish
- **Community Advisory Board** — Include prosthetic users from underserved backgrounds in trial design input
