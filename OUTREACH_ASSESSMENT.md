### 1.2 Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type (40 Active/Recruiting Trials)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % | Key Observations |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic/Center)** | 23 | 57.5% | Largest group; mostly university-led with small enrollments (3–66) |
| **INDUSTRY** | 11 | 27.5% | Device companies (Otto Bock, Hanger); moderate enrollments |
| **FEDERAL (FED)** | 5 | 12.5% | VA and DoD funded; critical for Veteran populations |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 1 | 2.5% | NHS (UK) |

**Gap Analysis:**
- **Industry only 27.5%** — limits access to cutting-edge devices for underserved populations who can't afford commercial solutions
- **Federal trials (12.5%)** serve Veterans but don't reach non-Veteran underserved groups
- **Academic sponsors (57.5%)** often lack infrastructure for community outreach
- **Zero NIH-funded trials** in this sample — the primary public funder is absent

### 1.3 Enrollment Trends by Phase (40 Trials)

| Phase | Count | % | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unknown** | 8 | 20% | No clear regulatory pathway; may be observational or device-fitting studies |
| **NA** | 31 | 77.5% | Most are device/feasibility studies without regulatory designation |
| **Phase 3** | 1 | 2.5% | ⚠️ **Critically insufficient late-stage efficacy testing** |

**Gap Analysis:**
- 97.5% of trials are in NA or Unknown phase — almost no rigorous later-stage efficacy testing
- No Phase 2 trials found — the pipeline skips directly from feasibility (NA) to the one Phase 3 study
- This pipeline gap means there is insufficient evidence to justify expanding coverage to underserved populations

### 1.4 Underserved Group Gaps

| Gap | Evidence |
|---|---|
| **Rural populations** | 9 of 10 sampled trials in urban/academic hubs. Only NCT05884203 (caregiver-assisted rural limb scanning) targets rural Veterans, based in Seattle. Zero trials in rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal lands. |
| **Racial/ethnic minorities** | No Spanish-language consent documents found. Chicago's South Side has zero prosthetic trial sites. No minority recruitment strategies observed. |
| **Female participants** | Most trials allow "ALL" sexes but enrollment is heavily skewed male. 90%+ male in most prosthetics trials due to combat-related amputations. |
| **Pediatric populations** | Zero pediatric-specific prosthetic trials found across the 427 total on ClinicalTrials.gov. |
| **Low-income/Medicaid** | No trial explicitly targets or reports Medicaid enrollment. Travel/reimbursement policies absent from most protocols. |
| **Global South** | All 10 sampled trials in US, UK, or France. ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access. |
| **Elderly** | Most trials cap at age 70. No trials specifically address age-related comorbidities common in Medicare populations. |
| **DME access at trial sites** | Most trials require participants to already have a prosthesis — no provision for first-time users or those who lost coverage. |
