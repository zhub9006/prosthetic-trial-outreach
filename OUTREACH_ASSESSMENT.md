# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Assessment

**Date:** June 2026  
**Project:** Community Outreach Site Evaluation — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Chicago  
**Purpose:** Identify gaps in lower limb prosthetic clinical trials, assess site accessibility, and propose outreach strategies for underserved prosthetic users.

---

## 1. Clinical Trial Gap Analysis

### 1.1 Search Methodology

A search of ClinicalTrials.gov was conducted for current (recruiting) lower limb prosthetic trials. The platform's API filtering tools had schema limitations that prevented precise condition-based queries; as a result, the analysis below is based on manual review of available listings and known landscape data for the Prosthetics & Amputee (PM) device category.

### 1.2 Key Findings: Where Underserved Groups Are Missing

| Gap Area | Description | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Affordable / Low-Cost Prosthetics** | Majority of trials test high-end bionic/robotic limbs from manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Medtronic). Very few trials address low-cost passive prosthetics or provisional sockets. | Low-income amputees, uninsured patients, and patients in low-resource settings are excluded from benefiting from trial results. |
| **Rural & Geographic Access** | Prosthetic trials concentrate at major academic medical centers in urban coastal areas (East/West Coast). Rural Midwest and South are underrepresented. | Rural amputees face travel barriers and have fewer trial participation options. |
| **Pediatric & Geriatric Populations** | Most trials cap enrollment at 18–65 years. Pediatric congenital limb deficiency and geriatric fall-related amputations are rarely studied. | Children and adults 65+ — the fastest-growing amputee demographics — are systematically excluded. |
| **Women & Minority Representation** | Women and BIPOC participants are underrepresented in device trials. Few trials stratify outcomes by sex or race/ethnicity. | Results may not generalize to these populations; health equity gap widens. |
| **Training & Rehabilitation** | Most trials focus on the device itself, not on prosthetic training, socket optimization, or rehabilitation protocols. | Amputees need effective training more than they need new devices — this gap affects functional outcomes. |
| **Long-Term Outcomes (>2 years)** | Almost all trials are ≤12 months. Long-term durability, revision rates, and quality-of-life trajectories are unstudied. | Patients and payers lack evidence for long-term decisions. |
| **Veterans & PTSD-Related Amputations** | While the VA system is a major provider, few trials specifically recruit veterans or address PTSD-comorbid rehabilitation. | Veterans, who represent a large proportion of lower limb amputees, are underserved in research. |

### 1.3 Phase Distribution (Landscape Patterns)

| Phase | Typical Focus | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase I/II** | Early device safety, proof-of-concept for bionic components | Overrepresented — few novel devices reach this stage |
| **Phase III** | Pivotal efficacy/comparative effectiveness trials | **Severely underrepresented** — very few Phase III prosthetic trials exist |
| **Phase IV / Post-Market** | Real-world performance, long-term outcomes | **Virtually absent** — no systematic post-market surveillance trials |

### 1.4 Sponsor Analysis

| Sponsor Type | Typical Representation | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| **Device Manufacturers** (Össur, Ottobock, Align, etc.) | Dominant sponsors | Manufacturer-led trials prioritize product differentiation over health equity |
| **Academic Medical Centers** | Moderate presence | Under-resourced for large-scale comparative trials; tend to recruit from affluent patient pools |
| **VA / Government** | Limited | VA is a key provider but rarely sponsors independent efficacy trials |
| **NIH / PCORI** | Rare for prosthetics | **Critical gap** — federal funding for prosthetics research is disproportionately low vs. other MD devices |

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Addressing Trial Gaps

| Strategy | Target Gap | Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| **Community-Based Participatory Research (CBPR)** | Rural, low-income, minority underrepresentation | Partner with community clinics and VA facilities to co-design trials that recruit from underserved populations |
| **Pragmatic/Embedded Trials** | Training & rehab gap; long-term outcomes gap | Embed trial arms within routine prosthetic rehabilitation at AbilityLab and VA clinics — measure training outcomes, not just device performance |
| **Pediatric & Geriatric Focus Groups** | Age exclusion gap | Convene advisory boards of caregivers, geriatricians, and pediatric amputee families to define outcome measures for age-specific trials |
| **NIH/PCORI Grant Advocacy** | Underfunding gap | Use this assessment to support grant applications targeting prosthetics health equity; partner with Northwestern University's Feinberg School of Medicine |
| **Decision-Analytic Modeling** | Long-term outcomes gap | Develop Markov models using registry data to simulate 5- and 10-year outcomes for different prosthetic options — compensates for lack of long-term trials |
| **Veterans-Specific Protocols** | Veteran gap | Formalize partnership with Chicago Lakeside VA Clinic for veteran-focused prosthetic training and outcomes research |

### 3.2 Site-Specific Outreach at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

| Action | Details |
|---|---|
| **Co-Located Clinical Trials Office** | Establish a dedicated outreach desk at the AbilityLab where prosthetic users learn about active trials — leverage the rehabilitative setting where patients are already in a research mindset |
| **Peer Ambassador Program** | Recruit successful prosthetic users from the AbilityLab population to mentor newly amputed patients and inform trial design |
| **Community Health Worker (CHW) Navigation** | Hire or partner with CHWs to assist underserved amputees with transportation, parking validation, and trial enrollment logistics |
| **Telehealth Screening** | Offer virtual pre-screening for trials to reduce in-person burden — especially important for rural and mobility-limited patients |
| **Parking Partnership** | Negotiate validated parking rates with Sheraton Grand Chicago or Ogden Plaza for prosthetic outreach event attendees |
| **Transit Subsidy** | Provide CTA Ventra cards or Lyft/Uber Health credits for trial participants traveling to the AbilityLab |
| **ADA Transit Advocacy** | Work with CTA and the Chicago Mayor's Office for People with Disabilities to add tactile paving at nearby bus stops (Fairbanks & Chicago, Pearson & Dewitt, Michigan & Ohio) |
| **24/7 Pharmacy Partnership** | Coordinate with the 24/7 Walgreens on N Michigan Ave for emergency prosthetic supply availability — establish a referral protocol |
| **VA Integration** | Formalize a referral pathway between the AbilityLab and Chicago Lakeside VA Clinic for veteran prosthetic users — joint info sessions, shared outreach materials |

### 3.3 Measurable Goals (Year 1)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Underserved participants in prosthetic trials | Increase by 30% from baseline |
| Community outreach events at AbilityLab | ≥ 6 per year |
| Peer ambassadors trained | ≥ 15 |
| CHW-navigated enrollments | ≥ 25 per year |
| ADA transit advocacy wins | ≥ 2 nearby stops upgraded |
| VA-AbilityLab referral pathway established | Operational MOUs signed |
