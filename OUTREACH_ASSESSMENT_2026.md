### 1.5 Sponsorship Gaps

- **Civic/nonprofit-funded trials: 0.** No community-based organizations are funding prosthetic trials.
- **Industry sponsors** focus on high-end devices (microprocessor knees, sensory feedback) — not affordable, basic solutions.
- **Federal (VA) trials** are the only ones systematically serving underserved veterans, but they are limited to VA facilities.
- **NIH funding** is negligible relative to the burden of need.

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile Prosthetic Outreach Unit 🚐
- **Problem:** No DME vendor, clinic, or pharmacy within 500m
- **Solution:** Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetist van at or adjacent to SRAL for residual limb assessments, socket adjustments, liner replacements, and DME referrals
- **Cost:** ~$15K/year
- **Target:** Uninsured and underserved prosthetic users

### Strategy 2: Language & Health Equity Access 🌍
- **Problem:** 0 trials have non-English consent forms; 0 have health-equity plans
- **Solution:** Translate consent forms into Spanish, Polish, Arabic, Chinese, and Tagalog; hire bilingual patient navigators; partner with Indo-American Center, Centro de Estudios para superar la Violencia, and Asian Human Services
- **Cost:** ~$25K/year
- **Target:** Non-English-speaking and immigrant amputee populations

### Strategy 3: South/West Side Screening Pipeline 🏥
- **Problem:** 0 rural US trial sites; underserved populations not represented
- **Solution:** Partner with FQHCs on the South and West Sides; train community health workers to screen and refer; provide CTA transit vouchers for SRAL visits
- **Cost:** ~$40K/year
- **Target:** Black and Latino amputee populations

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach & Virtual Trials 📡
- **Problem:** 0 rural trial sites; travel barriers for veterans and rural amputees
- **Solution:** Develop virtual screening via telehealth; partner with VA remote sites in central/southern Illinois; mail trial devices with video-guided fitting
- **Cost:** ~$50K/year
- **Target:** Rural and veteran populations

### Strategy 5: Pediatric & Elder-Inclusive Trial Design 👶👴
- **Problem:** 0 pediatric trials; elderly users excluded
- **Solution:** Create child-friendly protocols (play-based assessments, family consent); reduce upper-age caps; partner with Shriners Hospitals and Lurie Children's; develop a "Grand Friends" mentorship program
- **Cost:** ~$20K/year
- **Target:** Pediatric and elderly amputee populations

### Strategy 6: SRAL On-Site Resource Corner 🏠
- **Problem:** No social services, insurance navigation, or peer support nearby
- **Solution:** Create a dedicated space within SRAL for: DME product samples, insurance navigation assistance, walk-in prosthetic adjustments, peer support group meetings, and a "prosthetic resource library"
- **Cost:** ~$10K startup
- **Target:** All prosthetic users in the area
