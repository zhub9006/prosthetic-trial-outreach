## Executive Summary

Our assessment of 427 lower limb prosthetic trials on ClinicalTrials.gov reveals **systematic exclusion** of underserved populations: low-income/uninsured, rural, African American, Hispanic, female, elderly, and Global South communities. The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (SRAL) site is an excellent geographic hub (9.4/10 transit, 8.2/10 healthcare) but has two critical infrastructure gaps: **zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m** and **zero social services offices** within 500m.

## Part 1: Clinical Trial Landscape (427 Trials)

### 1.1 Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic/Non-Profit)** | 313 | 73.3% | Dominant; mostly single-site, limited reach |
| **INDUSTRY (Device Mfrs)** | 59 | 13.8% | ⚠️ Underrepresented — key tech & funding source |
| **FED (VA/DoD)** | 41 | 9.6% | Best positioned for underserved (Veterans) |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2.1% | Small municipal/state-sponsored efforts |
| **NIH** | 2 | 0.5% | ⚠️ Severely underfunded vs. burden of need |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | 0.5% | Multi-site collaboration almost absent |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | 0.2% | Unclear sponsorship |

**Key Insight:** 86% of trials are academic or federally sponsored, not industry. This means:
- Device innovation is slow to reach trials
- Underserved populations (Medicaid, uninsured) are rarely covered for trial device costs
- No large-scale industry commitment to diversity recruitment
- Federal sponsors (VA/DoD) are the only ones systematically serving underserved populations, yet they represent just 9.6% of all studies

### 1.2 Enrollment Trends by Phase

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Not Applicable (NA)** | 299 | 70.0% | Observation/device registries — no efficacy testing |
| **Unknown Phase** | 107 | 25.1% | ⚠️ Nearly a quarter have no phase assigned |
| **Phase 1** | 3 | 0.7% | Safety testing barely underway |
| **Early Phase 1** | 4 | 0.9% | Dose/feasibility studies minimal |
| **Phase 2** | 5 | 1.2% | Earliest efficacy signals |
| **Phase 3** | 5 | 1.2% | ⚠️ Only 5 pivotal trials exist for 427 studies |
| **Phase 4** | 5 | 1.2% | Post-market surveillance almost absent |

**Key Insight:** 96% of trials are in NA or Unknown phase — no rigorous later-stage efficacy testing. This pipeline gap means:
- Device improvements never reach large-scale validation
- Evidence base for prosthetic users is extremely thin
- Insurance coverage decisions lack supportive trial data

### 1.3 Identified Gaps in Underserved Populations

| Gap | Evidence | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Rural Invisibility** | Zero trials in rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal lands | ~30% of amputees in rural settings excluded from research |
| **Racial/Ethnic** | No Spanish-language consent; no minority recruitment benchmarks; Chicago's South Side = 0 trial sites | African American amputation rate is 2–4× higher; trial participants skew white |
| **Gender** | 90%+ male enrollment in most prosthetics trials | Female amputees (~25% of population) systematically excluded |
| **Pediatric** | No pediatric-specific prosthetic trials found | Children with congenital or traumatic limb loss have no trial options |
| **Age (Older Adult)** | Max age typically 80; cognitive exclusion (MMSE<24) | Majority of amputees are >65 with comorbidities; criteria exclude those who need it most |
| **Global South** | ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access | 80% of world's amputee population invisible to research |
| **Medicaid/Uninsured** | No trial coverage for device costs | Prosthetic devices cost $5K–$50K+; uninsured can't participate |
| **Spanish Language** | Zero trials with Spanish consent | ~400K Spanish-speaking amputees in US excluded |

**Pipeline Gap Summary:**

```
│  GIANT RED FLAGS                                                 │

│  • 0 community health center / FQHC sponsors any prosthetic trial│
│  • NIH funding: 2 out of 427 (0.5%)                             │
│  • Phase 3–4: Only 10 trials (2.3%) — no pivotal evidence       │
│  • Rural + Global South: Almost invisible                        │
│  • Racial/ethnic + Gender + Pediatric + Spanish: Zero inclusion  │
│  • Medicaid/uninsured: No cost coverage in trials                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Part 4: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy P1: Community Trial Navigators + Transportation Program ($93K/yr)
**Target:** South/West Side Chicago, seniors, mobility-limited  
**Bucket:** Immediate ($141K Year 1)  
- Hire 2–3 Community Health Workers (CHWs) from Englewood, Austin, East Garfield Park
- Provide CTA Ventra reduced-fare cards ($7.50/day) + Divvy Adaptive access
- Ride-share vouchers ($20K/yr) for supplemental trips
- Warm-weather prosthetic kits (thermal liners, hand warmers) for Lakefront windchill
- Patient navigator (1 FTE) to coordinate transport + appointments

**KPI:** ≥15 new underserved participants/study/yr; ≥85% attendance rate

---

### Strategy P2: On-Site DME / Prosthetic Access Station ($50K setup + $30K/yr)
**Target:** Eliminate the 500m DME gap — **#1 priority**  
**Bucket:** Immediate ($141K Year 1)  
- **Option A (Pop-up, $15K/yr):** Weekly Hanger Clinic or Össur pop-up in SRAL lobby for socket checks, padding adjustments, grip assessments
- **Option B (Permanent, $50K setup + $30K/yr):** Co-locate satellite DME clinic with Northwestern Medicine Galter Pavilion (150m away)
- **Option C (Tele-DME, $5K/yr):** Video consultations + mail-in supplies for simple adjustments

**KPI:** ≥50 DME interactions/month; 10% reduction in dropout due to "prosthesis issues"

---

### Strategy P3: Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks in Trial Protocols ($5K/yr)
**Target:** All underserved populations  
**Bucket:** Immediate ($141K Year 1)  
- Implement minimum demographic reporting in all SRAL-connected trials:
  - ≥25% female enrollment
  - ≥30% racial/ethnic minority (Black, Hispanic, Asian, Indigenous)
  - ≥15% Medicaid/uninsured
  - ≥10% rural
  - ≥50% age 55+
- Non-compliance triggers IRB protocol review — cannot renew
- Establish Community Advisory Board (CAB) of 10–15 prosthetic users to audit reports

**KPI:** 100% of SRAL trials reporting demographics; the 6-participant NCT06160882 must hit ≥25% minority enrollment

---

### Strategy P4: Tele-Trial / Hybrid Participation Model ($20K/yr)
**Target:** Rural prosthetic users, mobility-limited, elderly  
**Bucket:** Immediate ($141K Year 1)  
- Allow virtual visit components (telehealth consent, remote device checks, app-based gait monitoring)
- Combine with travel stipends ($50–100/trip) for in-person visits
- "Team Trials" — 2–4 neighborhood participants attend together for social support + shared travel costs
- Pre-loaded tablets for participants (30 units, $15K one-time)

**KPI:** ≥30% using ≥1 telehealth component; ≥20% reduction in cancellation due to transportation

---

### Strategy P5: Mobile Screening Events via VA/FQHC Network ($200K/yr)
**Target:** Rural Midwest, Chicago South Side, VA centers  
**Bucket:** Mid-term ($521K Year 2)  
- Partner with VA (Chicago Lakeside ~300m) and FQHCs to deploy mobile prosthetic assessment units
- Quarterly visits to VA clinics in Rockford (IL), Marion (IL), St. Louis (MO), Indianapolis (IN)
- Set up at community health fairs, church events, senior centers in South/West Side
- Plain-language consent + Spanish-language materials; travel stipends for participants

**KPI:** ≥20 rural Veterans recruited/yr; ≥15 new diverse participants from underserved zip codes/yr

---

### Strategy P6: Industry-Academic Device Access Pipeline ($100K setup + $20K/yr)
**Target:** Low-income/uninsured, underserved urban  
**Bucket:** Mid-term ($521K Year 2)  
- Negotiate pro bono device donation agreements with Otto Bock, Össur, Hanger Clinic, Ottobock
- Device lending library at SRAL; 20 sponsored slots/year for low-income participants
- Leverage ASCENT K2 (NCT04784429) expansion potential — 60+ US sites, 107 enrolled

**KPI:** 20 pro bono device slots/yr; ≥5 industry partners; zero cost for devices in SRAL trials

---

### Strategy P7: Older Adult Programming & Geriatric Outreach ($10K/yr)
**Target:** Elderly prosthetic users (65+)  
**Bucket:** Immediate ($141K Year 1)  
- Broaden age eligibility (currently many exclude 75+ or >300lbs weight)
- Caregiver stipends ($25/hr) for in-home support during trial visits
- Partner with Lakeview/Gold Coast/Streeterville senior centers for recruitment
- In-home assessment option for elderly unable to travel

**KPI:** ≥10% participants age 65+; ≥5 in-home assessments/yr

---

### Strategy P8: Data Transparency Dashboard (GitHub) ($5K setup + $1K/yr)
**Target:** Community advocates, researchers, policymakers  
**Bucket:** Immediate ($141K Year 1)  
- Open-source dashboard on this repo showing trial demographics, gaps, outcomes
- ClinicalTrials.gov API integration with automated monthly data refresh
- Interactive map of US trial sites; demographic breakdown by sponsor type
- SRAL trial tracker (NCT06160882 enrollment counter)

**KPI:** Dashboard live by Month 3; ≥5 community contributions/issues in first 6 months

---

### Strategy P9: Mental Health & Peer Support Integration ($20K/yr)
**Target:** All prosthetic users; PHQ-2 positive at screening  
**Bucket:** Immediate ($141K Year 1)  
- PHQ-2 screening at trial intake; refer positives to Medrina (~550m)
- Monthly peer support groups at SRAL lobby (leverage hotel adjacent space)
- Embed mental health assessments as secondary outcomes in all SRAL trials
- 90-day post-enrollment check-in with peer mentor

**KPI:** ≥95% PHQ-2 screening; ≥70% peer group attendance

---

### Strategy P10: Global Equity Bridge (MOUs for International Protocols) ($50K start / $50K/yr)
**Target:** 80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access  
**Bucket:** Mid-term ($521K Year 2)  
- MOUs with prosthetics programs in Colombia, India, Pakistan for low-cost standardized protocols
- Remote data sharing; cross-site knowledge transfer
- Tele-trial coordination for international participants

**KPI:** ≥3 international partner sites by Year 2; ≥20 participants from partner sites
