# Prosthetic Trial Outreach — Comprehensive Assessment (July 2026)

**Project:** Identify enrollment gaps in lower limb prosthetic trials and evaluate Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (SRAL) as a community outreach site for underserved prosthetic users.

**Key Gap:** 73% of trials are academia-led with limited recruitment infrastructure. NIH funding is virtually absent (0.5%), meaning there is no dedicated federal push for inclusive prosthetic research. Industry trials (13.8%) have the budget for broader outreach but are concentrated in urban academic centers.

### 1.3 Enrollment Trends by Study Phase

| Phase | Count | % | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| N/A (non-drug/device) | 299 | 69.9% | Most are observational — no efficacy gate, but no rigorous testing |
| Unknown | 107 | 25.1% | Likely many observational / feasibility studies with poor registration |
| Phase 2 | 5 | 1.2% | Early efficacy testing — very few |
| Phase 3 | 5 | 1.2% | Pipeline bottleneck — only 5 global Phase 3 trials |
| Phase 4 | 5 | 1.2% | Post-market surveillance — minimal |
| Phase 1 | 3 | 0.7% | Early safety — limited |
| Early Phase 1 | 4 | 0.9% | First-in-human |

**Key Gap:** The prosthetic field has almost no Phase 2–4 pipeline. 97% of studies are non-interventional or early-phase, meaning there is a massive gap in rigorous evidence generation for next-generation prosthetic technologies. This also means fewer trials actively recruiting (only ~86 of 427).

### 1.4 Bipartite Analysis: Sponsor × Phase × Status

| Sponsor | Phase 1–2 (early) | Phase 3–4 (late) | Non-interventional | Recruiting? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OTHER (academic) | ~17 | ~10 | ~286 | Mostly recruitment-challenged |
| INDUSTRY | ~8 | ~4 | ~47 | Better resourced but concentrated |
| FEDERAL | ~2 | ~2 | ~37 | VA-focused, good veteran recruitment |
| NIH | ~0 | ~0 | ~2 | Virtually no recruitment activity |

**Critical Finding:** No NIH-funded Phase 2+ studies exist. The pathway from early safety (Phase 1) to efficacy (Phase 3) is broken — 97% of trials never progress beyond observational or early-phase, meaning prosthetic users have almost no access to cutting-edge device trials.

## 5. Seven Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: "Bridging the Gap" — Community Trial Navigators from South/West Side

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary Gap** | Geographic & racial equity |
| **Problem** | 86% of trials are at academic urban centers; South/West Side communities have 1+ bus transfers to reach SRAL |
| **Action** | Hire 3 Community Trial Navigators from Englewood, Austin, and Humboldt Park neighborhoods. Train them on prosthetic trial eligibility, consent processes, and transportation coordination. Deploy at community health centers, churches, and VA facilities. |
| **Budget** | $90,000/yr (3 FTE × $30K) |
| **Success Metric** | ≥30 South/West Side enrollees in Year 1; ≥50 in Year 2 |

### Strategy 2: "Prosthetic Supply Corridor" — Pop-Up DME / Vendor Partnership

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary Gap** | No DME vendor within 500 m |
| **Problem** | Participants cannot get prosthesis adjustments, socket replacements, or emergency repairs within walking distance |
| **Action** | Partner with a local DME provider (e.g., Ability Prosthetics & Orthotics) to set up a rotating pop-up clinic in SRAL's lobby or waiting area — 1 day/week. **Permanent option:** lease 200 sq ft in SRAL's outpatient wing for a community DME station. |
| **Budget** | Pop-up: $15K/yr; Permanent: $50K setup + $25K/yr |
| **Success Metric** | ≥20 on-site DME visits/month; ≥5 emergency repairs resolved same-day |

### Strategy 3: "Warm Hands, Warm Access" — Winter Thermal Comfort Program

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary Gap** | Thermal discomfort (compounder for winter non-use) |
| **Problem** | NCT07215442 identified thermal discomfort as a primary barrier; Chicago wind chill frequently <0°F; waiting at bus stops in winter is a deterrent for prosthetic users with cold-sensitive residuals |
| **Action** | Install heated waiting area in SRAL's prosthetic/rehabilitation lobby. Distribute winter prosthetic thermal kits (sock liners, silicone covers, hand warmers). Partner with CTA's Winter Survival Kit program. |
| **Budget** | $33K/yr ($25K HVAC one-time + $8K consumables) |
| **Success Metric** | ≥15% reduction in winter no-show rates; ≥90% participant satisfaction with thermal comfort |

### Strategy 4: "South Side Transit Pipeline" — CTA Paratransit + Tele-Trial Model

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary Gap** | 1+ bus transfer required for South/West Siders; paratransit underutilized |
| **Problem** | CTA Access paratransit requires 3-day advance booking; many prospective participants don't know about it; tele-trial model can reduce in-person visits |
| **Action** | 1) Deploy a CTA Access shuttle coordinator at SRAL to book paratransit for enrolled participants. 2) Develop a 20% tele-trial telehealth protocol for check-ins, data collection, and device adjustments (where FDA-approved). 3) Partner with Rush University's telehealth program for virtual device counseling. |
| **Budget** | $60,000/yr (transportation subsidy + telehealth platform) |
| **Success Metric** | ≥40 paratransit bookings/month; ≥15 tele-trial visits/month |

### Strategy 5: "Global Equity Bridge" — International Prosthetic Trial Partnerships

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary Gap** | 80 nations with zero prosthetic trials; colonial research patterns |
| **Problem** | 69.9% of trials are N/A phase (observational); only 5 Phase 3 trials globally; no trials in Latin America, Africa, or South Asia with prosthetic device intervention |
| **Action** | 1) MOU with Universidad del Valle (Colombia), ISOP (India), and Shaukat Khanum (Pakistan) for joint Phase 2 low-cost prosthetic trials. 2) Develop a <$500-cost protocol template for low-resource settings. 3) FDA-IEEE joint guidance for cross-border device trials. |
| **Budget** | $50K start-up (MOUs, protocol development) + $50K/yr (coordination) |
| **Success Metric** | ≥2 joint trials initiated Year 1; ≥50 participants enrolled from partner sites by Year 3 |

### Strategy 6: "Mental Health & Acceptance" — Peer Support + PHQ-2 Screening

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary Gap** | 30–50% amputee depression rate; no integrated mental health in prosthetic trials |
| **Problem** | Depression is the #1 reason for prosthesis abandonment; most trials screen only for physical outcomes; no psychosocial support infrastructure at SRAL's outreach sites |
| **Action** | 1) Implement PHQ-2 screening at every trial intake (validated 2-item depression screen). 2) Establish monthly peer support group for prosthetic users (partner with Medrina, 401 N Michigan Ave, for space). 3) Train 2 SRAL staff in motivational interviewing for prosthetic adherence. |
| **Budget** | $20,000/yr (screening tools, facilitator stipend, peer support materials) |
| **Success Metric** | ≥80% of enrollees screened PHQ-2; ≥60% of depressed participants referred to care; ≥15 peer group attendees/month |

### Strategy 7: "Women, Children & Gender-Diverse" — Inclusive Protocol Design

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary Gap** | 90%+ male skew in current trials; zero pediatric trials; no gender-diverse inclusion standards |
| **Problem** | Women, children, and gender-diverse individuals are systematically excluded or underrepresented; prosthetic designs, trial protocols, and outcome measures are male-centric |
| **Action** | 1) Adopt equal-platform protocol: all new trials must include sex/gender disaggregated analysis. 2) Pediatric sub-study with Lurie Children's Hospital (Northwestern) for congenital limb deficiency trials. 3) Gender-diverse recruitment at Howard Brown Health Center (~1 km from SRAL). 4) Child-friendly prosthetic trial design (play-based outcomes, school-day scheduling). |
| **Budget** | $35K start-up (protocol redesign, pediatric equipment) + $15K/yr (recruitment materials, Lurie coordination) |
| **Success Metric** | ≥30% female enrollment in all new trials; ≥1 pediatric trial launched by Q4 2026; ≥10 gender-diverse enrollees/yr |
