## 1. Clinical Trial Gap Analysis

### Search Scope
A ClinicalTrials.gov query for *"lower limb prosthetic"* returned **427 total studies**. Enrollment and sponsorship data were analyzed to identify where underserved populations are missing from the trial pipeline.

| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2.1% | State/local funding  |

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME / Prosthetic Van Program
- Deploy a **monthly mobile prosthetist van** to the SRAL site for device fittings, adjustments, and consultations
- Target the **~15% of prosthetic users who are uninsured or underinsured**
- Estimated cost: **~$15,000/year** (van lease + staffing)
- Partner with a local prosthetic supplier (e.g., Hanger Clinic, Otto Bock) for equipment

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Cultural Competency
- Chicago's near-north side has large **Spanish, Polish, Arabic, and Chinese**-speaking communities
- Produce **bilingual consent forms and recruitment materials** in Spanish, Polish, Arabic, and Chinese
- Hire **bilingual study coordinators** for outreach events
- Partner with community centers (e.g., IMF International Metroplex, Centro de Convergencia Cultural) for culturally tailored outreach

### Strategy 3: FQHC Partnership Pipeline
- Build referral pathways with **Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)** on the South and West Sides (e.g., Mile Square Health Center, Heartland Health Centers)
- These centers serve **low-income, uninsured, and underinsured** populations — the exact groups missing from prosthetic trials
- Provide FQHC staff with trial information packets and referral incentives

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach & VA Collaboration
- For the **0% rural trial site coverage gap**: host **virtual prosthetic trial information sessions** via Telehealth
- Partner with **VA Illinois Health Care System** (Champaign, Danville, Marion) for veteran outreach
- Offer **tele-rehabilitation consultations** for rural prosthetic users who cannot travel to Chicago

### Strategy 5: Pediatric & Age-Inclusive Trial Design
- With **0 pediatric prosthetic trials** identified, propose a **child-friendly prosthetic trial** with age-appropriate consent (ages 7–17 with parental consent)
- Include **elder-inclusive age ranges** (65+) to capture the aging amputee population
- Offer **transportation assistance** (ride credits, ADA shuttle) for pediatric and elder participants

### Strategy 6: ADA Accessibility Improvements at SRAL
- Work with SRAL administration to **designate ADA parking spots** within 200 m of the entrance
- Install **temporary accessible signage** and wayfinding for outreach event days
- Provide **rounded-trip shuttle service** from the nearest parking garage for mobility-impaired visitors

### Strategy 7: Community Ambassador Program
- Recruit **amputee peer mentors** from the Chicago amputee community (via AMPRO, the Amputee Coalition, and SRAL patient advisory groups)
- Train ambassadors to **conduct outreach at CTA bus stops, Divvy stations, and community events**
- Compensate ambassadors with **stipends or gift cards** to ensure sustained engagement

### Strategy 8: Social Services Integration
- Partner with **Illinois Department of Rehabilitation Services (IDRS)** for eligibility screening and referral
- Connect with **Chicago Department of Public Health** community health workers for door-to-door outreach in underserved neighborhoods
- Coordinate with **job placement programs** for underserved populations returning to work with prosthetic devices

## 5. Key Takeaways

1. **The trial pipeline is severely lacking in efficacy testing** — only 2.4% of lower-limb prosthetic studies are Phase 2 or 3, meaning most device innovation never reaches rigorous validation.

2. **Underserved groups are systematically excluded**: no pediatric trials, no rural sites, no non-English materials, no civic-funded studies, and minimal industry or NIH sponsorship.

3. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is an excellent outreach hub** for transit and walkability (9.4/10 and 10/10 respectively) but has critical gaps in **DME/prosthetic suppliers (0/10)** and **ADA parking (0 confirmed ADA spots within 200 m)**.

4. **The most impactful near-term action** is launching a **mobile DME van** to bring prosthetic services directly to the SRAL site, combined with **ADA accessibility improvements** and **multilingual outreach materials**.

5. **Long-term systemic change** requires FQHC partnerships for South/West Side referral pipelines, rural tele-outreach to address the geographic gap, and pediatric trial design to serve the missing child and elder populations.
