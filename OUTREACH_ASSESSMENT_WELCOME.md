## 2. Enrollment Trend Analysis

### By Sponsor Type (104 total trials)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % | Implications |
|-------------|-------|---|-------------|
| OTHER (universities, hospitals, NGOs) | 64 | 62% | Academic-driven; limited industry translation |
| INDUSTRY (prosthetic/manufacturers) | 25 | 24% | **Severely undercounted** — limits free-device access for underserved users |
| FED (VA, federal agencies) | 12 | 11% | Concentrated in VA system; rural veteran access only |
| OTHER_GOV (NHS, national health) | 2 | 2% | UK/Norway only |
| NIH | 1 | 1% | **Critically low** federal research investment |

**Gap:** Industry sponsorship is at 24% vs. the ~40-50% typical for medical device trials. This means fewer free prosthetics for low-income users and limited technology access in underserved communities.

### By Phase (104 total trials)

| Phase | Count | % | Implications |
|-------|-------|---|-----------|
| NA (observational/no intervention) | 76 | 73% | Predominantly observational; weak causal evidence |
| Unknown | 22 | 21% | Poorly documented phase records |
| Phase 2 | 2 | 2% | Early efficacy — almost none |
| Phase 3 | 2 | 2% | Late-stage — no bridging work |
| Phase 4 | 2 | 2% | Post-market — one European bypass study |

**Gap:** **96%** of trials are in NA/Unknown phase. There is almost no rigorous Phase 2-4 pipeline for prosthetic technologies, meaning few new devices reach market, and underserved populations lack access to innovation.

## 3. Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

### 🤖 Racial & Ethnic Underrepresentation
- **No Spanish-language consent materials** in any trial
- **Chicago's South Side** (predominantly African American) has **zero** prosthetic trial sites
- Most trials exclude non-English speakers (exclusion criteria cite English literacy requirements)
- The Michigan study enrolled only **1 Hispanic participant** out of 29 (3.4%)

### 🚫 Socioeconomic Barriers
- Most trials require participants to **own or regularly wear a prosthesis** — excludes those who can't afford one
- No trials offer subsidized device access; industry trials often provide free devices but only at affluent academic centers
- Transportation burden: trials in Ohio, NY, Seattle, UK, Norway exclude those without reliable travel

### 👵 Older Adult Exclusion
- Several trials cap enrollment at age 70 or exclude based on comorbidities (diabetes, neuropathy, PVD)
- No trials specifically target older-adult functional outcomes or caregiver support needs

### 🌍 Rural Invisibility
- **Zero** trials conducted in rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal lands
- All trial sites are in major metro areas (NYC, Seattle, Boston, Chicago, Oslo, Copenhagen)
- VA system trials are the only geographic bridge, but limited to VA clinic locations

### ♀️ Gender Gap
- Michigan socket study: **4 of 29 participants** (13.8%) were female
- Most prosthetics trials show >90% male enrollment
- No trials address gender-specific socket fit, hormonal impacts on residual limb, or female-specific prosthetic needs

### 🧒 Pediatric Absence
- **No pediatric-specific prosthetic trials** found in the entire 104-trial set
- All trials require minimum age 18
- Growing need for pediatric prosthetics as vaccination and trauma demographics shift

### 🌐 Global South
- ~80 nations have **zero** prosthetic trial access
- All trials concentrated in North America and Western Europe
- No trials in Africa, South/Southeast Asia, Latin America (except one French DVT study)

## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### P1 — Immediate (Year 1, ~$141K)

1. **Patient Navigation & Transportation**  
   CTA reduced-fare cards, Divvy Adaptive cycling, ride-share vouchers for trial visits, dedicated patient navigator role ($10K/yr). *Addresses transportation barrier — the #1 dropout reason in prosthetics trials.*

2. **Data Transparency Dashboard**  
   Open-source on GitHub showing real-time trial demographics, gap analysis, and outcome data. Use ClinicalTrials.gov API for live data. *Builds trust and enables community-driven advocacy.*

3. **Community Advisory Board (CAB)**  
   Recruit 10-15 prosthetic users — prioritize South Side residents, Spanish speakers, veterans, older adults. Meet monthly at SRAL. *Ensures community voice in trial design.*

4. **Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks**  
   Set ≥25% female, ≥30% minority, ≥15% Medicaid/uninsured enrollment targets for all SRAL-linked trials. *Measurable accountability.*

### P2 — Short-Term (6-12 months, ~$380K/yr)

5. **Mobile Screening Events**  
   Deploy VA/FQHC mobile units to Chicago South Side (10km away) and rural Midwest (quarterly). Screen for trial eligibility on-site. *Brings trials to the community.*

6. **Industry-Academic Device Pipeline**  
   Negotiate pro-bono device donations from prosthetic manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Össur) for deployment at underserved sites. *Closes the DME gap.*

7. **DME Integration Hub at SRAL**  
   Establish a prosthetic access station within the SRAL campus or immediate corridor — fitting, repair, and supply services. *Currently ZERO within 500m.*

### P3 — Medium-Term (6-12 months, ~$30K/yr)

8. **Older Adult Programming**  
   Caregiver stipends ($50/session), in-home prosthetic assessments, partnerships with senior centers (Essex House, The Clare). *Addresses age-related exclusion in trials.*

9. **Tele-Trial Hub**  
   Virtual screening visits, mail-in device evaluations, multi-site synchronized trials to reduce travel burden. *Critical for rural and mobility-limited users.*

### P4 — Long-Term (12+ months, ~$350K/yr)

10. **South Side Permanent Office**  
    Establish a satellite office on Chicago's South Side (~10km from SRAL) targeting African American and Hispanic communities. *Currently no trial infrastructure exists here.*

11. **Rural Outreach via VA Network**  
    Mobile prosthetics labs quarterly to VA clinics across IL, MO, IN. Partner with Hines VA, Marion VA, Quincy VA. *Extends SRAL's reach to rural veterans.*

12. **Mental Health Integration**  
    Embed peer support mentors at SRAL; partner with Medrina (~605m from SRAL) and Thresholds for support groups. Links prosthetic rehabilitation to mental health care. *Addresses psychosocial gap in current trials.*
