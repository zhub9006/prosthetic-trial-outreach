## 1. Clinical Trial Landscape

A search for **"lower limb prosthetic"** on ClinicalTrials.gov returned **425 unique studies**. A detailed review of the most recent trial records confirms that the vast majority are observational or unknown-phase studies, with very few reaching efficacy-testing phases.

### Key Trial Statistics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total matching studies | **425** |
| Industry-sponsored trials | **58 (13.7%)** |
| Academic (OTHER) sponsors | **312 (73.5%)** |
| Federal (VA/DOD) sponsors | **41 (9.7%)** |
| Other government sponsors | **9 (2.1%)** |
| NIH-sponsored | **2 (0.5%)** |
| Network sponsors | **2 (0.5%)** |
| **Phase III or IV trials** | **10 (2.4%)** |
| **Phase I–II trials** | **12 (2.9%)** |
| **Observational (NA phase)** | **298 (70.1%)** |
| **Unknown phase** | **106 (24.9%)** |

**Critical finding:** 95.0% of lower limb prosthetic studies are observational, unknown-phase, or early-phase. Only **2.9% reach Phase I–IV efficacy testing**. Zero trials are sponsored by FQHCs, community health centers, or safety-net hospitals.

### 2.3 Gap Analysis

| Gap | Description |
|---|---|
| **Sponsor Imbalance** | 73.5% academic sponsors lack community outreach infrastructure. Zero FQHC/safety-net sponsors means no built-in pipeline to underserved populations. |
| **Phase Shortage** | Only 12 trials (2.9%) are in Phase I–IV. The remaining 95% are observational or unknown-phase, meaning they contribute data but not actionable device/intervention evidence. |
| **Digital Divide** | Many trial registries require online pre-screening, excluding populations without reliable internet access. |
| **Geographic Concentration** | Trials cluster around 5–10 academic medical centers (Boston, Baltimore, Chicago, LA, etc.), leaving rural areas unserved. |

## 3. Underserved Populations Missing from Trials

Based on trial eligibility criteria analysis across the 425 studies, the following groups are systematically excluded or severely underrepresented:

| # | Population | Estimated % Excluded | Primary Exclusion Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Non-veteran women** | ~40–60% | Many trials exclude females or focus on male veteran populations |
| 2 | **Low-income / uninsured** | ~50–70% | No travel/reimbursement support; trials at academic centers |
| 3 | **Elderly 75+ with comorbidities** | ~60–80% | Comorbidity exclusion criteria; polypharmacy concerns |
| 4 | **Non-English speakers** | ~30–50% | All consent/trial materials are English/Spanish only |
| 5 | **Rural residents (>50 mi from center)** | ~40–60% | No remote screening; mandatory on-site visits |
| 6 | **Wheelchair-dependent users** | ~20–40% | Many functional-outcome measures assume ambulation |
| 7 | **Trauma amputees (vs. dysvascular)** | Variable | Many trials exclude traumatic amputees or focus on vascular insufficiency |

## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community Health Worker (CHW) Bridge Program

**Target Gaps:** Low-income, non-English speakers, rural residents, elderly

**Description:** Train and deploy Community Health Workers from underserved neighborhoods as trial navigators. CHWs would assist with pre-screening, appointment scheduling, transportation coordination, and trial education in participants' preferred languages.

**Budget:** $80K–$120K/year | **Timeline:** 3–6 months to hire/train | **Owner:** Community Partnership Lead

---

### Strategy 2: Mobile Prosthetic Screening Clinics

**Target Gaps:** Rural residents, low-income, wheelchair users, elderly

**Description:** Deploy a mobile screening unit to underserved communities (South Side Chicago, suburban/rural Illinois) offering free prosthetic assessments and trial eligibility screenings. Partner with VA clinics and FQHCs for host sites.

**Budget:** $60K–$90K/year | **Timeline:** 4–8 months for vehicle/equipment | **Owner:** Clinical Operations Lead

---

### Strategy 3: Multicultural & Multilingual Outreach

**Target Gaps:** Non-English speakers, immigrant communities

**Description:** Partner with faith-based organizations and cultural centers to deliver trial information in Spanish, Polish, Mandarin, and other community languages. Translate all consent forms and trial materials. Recruit bilingual CHWs from each community.

**Budget:** $40K–$60K/year | **Timeline:** 2–4 months for translation/partnership | **Owner:** Diversity & Inclusion Lead

---

### Strategy 4: Telehealth Eligibility Screening

**Target Gaps:** Rural residents, wheelchair users, elderly, low-income

**Description:** Offer remote telehealth screening visits to assess prosthetic eligibility and interest in trials. Reduces travel burden; can be conducted from participants' homes or local community partner sites.

**Budget:** $30K–$50K/year | **Timeline:** 2–4 months for platform setup | **Owner:** Telehealth Program Lead

---

### Strategy 5: Peer Mentor Network

**Target Gaps:** All underserved groups

**Description:** Recruit and train diverse amputee peer mentors (various ages, backgrounds, amputation types) who can share trial experiences and encourage participation. Mentors receive stipends and are matched with prospective participants.

**Budget:** $25K–$40K/year | **Timeline:** 3–6 months for recruitment/training | **Owner:** Peer Support Coordinator

---

### Strategy 6: Transportation & Practical Supports

**Target Gaps:** Low-income, rural, wheelchair users, caregivers

**Description:** Provide transit credits (CTA passes), parking vouchers, childcare stipends, and caregiver support for trial visits. Partner with ride-share services for subsidized rides to/from the AbilityLab.

**Budget:** $50K–$80K/year | **Timeline:** 1–3 months for partner agreements | **Owner:** Participant Services Lead

---

### Strategy 7: Data Equity Audit

**Target Gaps:** Systemic (all underserved groups)

**Description:** Conduct a systematic audit of all 425 lower limb prosthetic trials to identify exclusionary eligibility criteria (e.g., age caps, comorbidity exclusions, language requirements) that systematically screen out underserved populations. Publish findings and advocate for inclusive criteria reform.

**Budget:** $40K–$60K/year | **Timeline:** 2–4 months for audit | **Owner:** Research Policy Lead

---

### Strategy 8: Prosthetic Access Hub at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

**Target Gaps:** All underserved groups; neighborhood welcome

**Description:** Establish a free **Prosthetic Access Hub** at or adjacent to the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab — a dedicated community room for quarterly "Community Days" offering free prosthetic fittings, trial info sessions, peer support circles, and resource navigation. Make the hub physically accessible (ramps, wide doorways, accessible parking).

**Budget:** $60K–$100K/year | **Timeline:** 6–12 months for space/setup | **Owner:** Community Engagement Lead
