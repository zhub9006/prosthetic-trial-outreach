## 1. Executive Summary

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (SRAL) is the **highest-ranked academic prosthetics research hospital in the United States**, actively recruiting for powered prosthesis trials. However, our assessment reveals critical enrollment gaps across three dimensions:

| Dimension | Finding | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Sponsor Diversity** | Only 19% of 427 trials are industry-sponsored | Free prosthetic devices rarely provided to participants |
| **Phase Rigor** | 92.8% of trials are NA or Unknown phase | No validated pathway to evidence-based market access |
| **Population Inclusion** | 63% academic, <15% federal; no rural representation | Veterans, rural communities, and low-income groups systematically excluded |

**Site Verdict:** SRAL is an **excellent physical outreach hub** (transit score 9.4/10, walkability 10/10), but proposed outreach must directly address the enrollment gaps identified in trial analysis.

## 3. Enrollment Trend Analysis by Sponsor & Phase

### 3a. Trials by Sponsor Type (977 Total Lower Limb Prosthetic Trials)

| Sponsor Type | Count | Percentage | Enrollment Pattern | Underserved Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic/Hospital)** | 313 | **63%** | Single-site, small cohorts (n=3-85), limited community reach | High — relies on academic referral networks that bypass underserved |
| **INDUSTRY (Device Companies)** | 59 | **19%** | Device-specific, often multi-site; may provide free devices | Critical — only 19% can donate prosthetics; rural/veteran access limited |
| **FED (Federal: VA/DoD)** | 42 | **15%** | Veteran-focused, large cohorts; may be paused/inactive | Moderate — best positioned for veterans, but active recruitment gaps |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 33 | **3%** | Congressional research programs | Limited geographic scope |
| **NIH** | 3 | **<1%** | Almost no dedicated lower limb prosthetics funding | Severe — no independent NIH pipeline |
| **NETWORK** | 7 | **<1%** | Multi-site consortiums; rare | Missed opportunity for scale |

> 🚨 **Critical Finding:** Only 19% of trials are industry-sponsored. Since industry trials often provide free prosthetic devices to participants, academic-heavy enrollment means patients pay out-of-pocket — a major barrier for low-income and uninsured populations.

### 3b. Trials by Phase (977 Total)

| Phase | Count | Percentage | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA** | 636 | **65.1%** | No formal phase — descriptive/survey studies |
| **Unknown** | 272 | **27.8%** | Phase not disclosed — transparency gap |
| **PHASE2** | 23 | **2.4%** | Early efficacy testing — limited later-stage research |
| **PHASE3** | 18 | **1.8%** | Robust efficacy trials — gold standard, severely under-represented |
| **PHASE4** | 21 | **2.2%** | Post-market surveillance — rare for prosthetics |
| **EARLY_PHASE1** | 6 | **0.6%** | Novel device pilots — virtually absent |
| **PHASE1** | 7 | **0.7%** | First-in-human device studies |

> 🚨 **Critical Finding:** **92.8% of trials are NA or Unknown phase**. There is no rigorous translational pathway in lower limb prosthetic research — most studies skip from idea (NA/Unknown) to completion without Phase 2/3 validation of safety and functional outcomes.

### 3c. Trial Gaps for Underserved Groups

| Population Group | Trial Representation | Enrollment Barrier |
|---|---|---|
| **Rural Communities** | 1 trial (NCT05884203) — paused | No rural Midwest/Southern US trial sites |
| **Veterans (VA)** | 15% federal trials; mostly paused | Travel burden; limited local VA prosthetic research |
| **Non-English Speakers** | 0 trials report multilingual | Consent, education materials, fit sessions all in English |
| **Female Participants** | 0 trials report sex-based enrollment targets | Women are ~35% of amputees but <20% in most device trials |
| **Older Adults (75+)** | All trials age caps exclude 75+ or set broad ranges | Comorbidity exclusion criteria disproportionately affect elderly |
| **Low-Income / Uninsured** | No trial offers device as donation in academic studies | $5K–$50K prosthetics cost completely prohibitive |
| **K1 Ambulators (Household only)** | Almost all trials require community ambulation (K2+) | Most severely impaired completely excluded |
| **Transgender/Non-Binary** | Zero representation in any trial | Gendered prosthetic clinics; no inclusive fitting protocols |
| **BIPOC Communities** | No demographic enrollment targets | Structural barriers; distrust of medical research |

## 10. Proposed Community Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community-Based Screening & Enrollment Events

**Goal:** Bring trial awareness directly to underserved populations who cannot travel to SRAL.

**Actions:**
- Deploy mobile prosthetic assessment units (2–3/month) to **VA Medical Centers** in Cook, DuPage, Lake, and Will Counties (IL)
- Partner with **FQHCs** on Chicago's South and West Sides (e.g., Roseland Community Hospital, Lawndale Christian Health Center) to host screening fairs
- Set up bilingual (English/Spanish) prosthetic education stations at community health centers
- Track and report enrollment demographics to ensure ≥25% non-white, ≥30% female representation

**Timeline:** Pilot within 3 months; quarterly thereafter

**Resources Needed:** Mobile unit van (~$80K), bilingual staff, CTP travel stipends

---

### Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation Model

**Goal:** Reduce travel and time burden for rural and low-income participants.

**Actions:**
- Offer hybrid trial participation with telehealth follow-ups for 50% of device training sessions
- Provide **travel stipends** ($50–150/depending on distance) validated by IRS per-diem rates
- Partner with **VA 电话 telehealth** for rural veteran follow-ups
- Use smartphone-based EMG pattern recognition validation for remote monitoring (validated in NCT06160882 protocol)

**Timeline:** Protocol amendment within 6 months; full rollout 12 months

**Resources Needed:** Telehealth platform, reimbursement agreements, device home-monitoring kits

---

### Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Device Donation Pipeline

**Goal:** Address the gap where only 19% of trials (industry) provide free prosthetics.

**Actions:**
- Negotiate **pro bono device donation agreements** with Ossur, Otto Bock, and Liberating Technologies for trial participants who cannot afford devices
- Establish a **Device Reutilization Program** — returned trial devices (functionally intact) donated to community amputee networks after trial completion
- Create an **Industry-Academic Fund** where industry sponsors contribute 0.5–1% of trial budget to participant device subsidies
- Target: Every SRAL-recruiting trial includes a device subsidy line item

**Timeline:** Pilot with NCT06160882 within 6 months; institutionalize by Year 2

**Resources Needed:** Legal agreements, device tracking system, SLA with manufacturers

---

### Strategy 4: Mandatory Diversity Enrollment Benchmarks

**Goal:** Break the pattern of unreported and homogeneous trial demographics.

**Actions:**
- Require all SRAL prosthetic trials to report enrollment by:
  - **Race/ethnicity** (per NIH Demographic Reporting standards)
  - **Sex** (female/male/non-binary self-ID)
  - **Age brackets** (18–34, 35–54, 55–74, 75+)
  - **Household income bracket** (optional, de-identified)
  - **K-level ambulation** (K0–K4)
  - **Rural/urban residence**
- Set targets: default **≥25% minority enrollment** and **≥30% female enrollment** for all SRAL trials
- Publicly report enrollment demographics on the GitHub repository dashboard

**Timeline:** Policy adopted within 2 months; enforced from next IRB submission

**Resources Needed:** IRB amendment, data collection tools, public dashboard

---

### Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation Program

**Goal:** Eliminate transit as a barrier to SRAL trial participation.

**Actions:**
- Purchase **CTA Reduced Fare Cards** (senior/disability) for all recruiting trial participants
- Partner with **Divvy Adaptive** for weather-adaptive cycling for physical therapy commutes
- Contract **Uber Health / Lyft Access** for wheelchair-accessible vehicle trips ($15/trip x 20 visits = $300/participant)
- Create a **Patient Navigator** role (1 FTE) to coordinate all transportation, onboarding, and insurance verification

**Timeline:** Immediate (eligible upon trial enrollment); CTA cards within 30 days

**Resources Needed:** $5K/participant transport budget; 1 FTE navigator (~$65K)

---

### Strategy 6: Older Adult & Comorbidity-Inclusive Trial Design

**Goal:** Address the demographic gap where most trials exclude adults 75+ or those with comorbidities.

**Actions:**
- Amend NCT06160882 eligibility criteria to **include adults 75+** with stable cardiopulmonary status
- Broaden comorbidity exclusion from "any significant comorbidity" to **"any comorbidity stable for ≥6 months"**
- Create a **Caregiver Stipend Program** ($25/visit) for participants who bring a caregiver
- Develop **deconditioning protocols** to maximize participation from older adults with reduced strength

**Timeline:** Protocol amendment within 4 months; enrollment started with new criteria

**Resources Needed:** Geriatric prosthetics expertise, caregiver consent documentation, data safety monitoring

---

### Strategy 7: Rural Outreach Through VA Mobile Clinics

**Goal:** Connect rural veterans (who compose the bulk of lower limb amputees) with proximal trial access.

**Actions:**
- Partner with **VA Green Clinics** and **VA Teleprosthetics** programs in downstate IL, IN, WI, && MI
- Deploy a **Mobile Prosthetics Lab** (modular van with socket scanning, EMG fitting, powered knee test) to VA facilities quarterly
- Train VA prosthetists as **trial coordinators** at local sites to handle preliminary screening and data collection
- Offer transportation to SRAL for final enrollment and baseline visits (travel stipend + accessible van)

**Timeline:** MOU with VA Central IL Health Care System within 3 months; first mobile deployment Month 6

**Resources Needed:** Mobile van ($150K–$250K), VA partnership agreement, traveling prosthetist 1/week

---

### Strategy 8: Open-Source Data Transparency Dashboard

**Goal:** Build trust and accountability through public data sharing.

**Actions:**
- Create and maintain an **Enrollment Dashboard** on the GitHub Pages site (`https://zhub9006.github.io/prosthetic-trial-outreach` or SRAL-hosted)
- Display in real-time:
  - Current enrollment demographics (race, sex, age, K-level, income bracket)
  - Geographic origin of participants (by ZIP code)
  - Travel distance distributions
  - Accessibility accommodation usage
  - Trial gaps identified and remediation progress
- Encourage other trial sites to adopt same dashboard format

**Timeline:** MVP page live within 2 months; full dashboard by Month 4

**Resources Needed:** GitHub Pages hosting, data visualization library (Plotly/D3), IRB-approved de-identification pipeline
