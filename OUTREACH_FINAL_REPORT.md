## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME Unit (Priority: CRITICAL)

**Problem:** Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500 m of SRAL.  
**Solution:** Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetist van at SRAL or a nearby site (e.g., Navy Pier lot) offering: socket adjustments, liner replacements, cosmetic touch-ups, and device inspections.  
**Cost:** ~$15K/yr (van lease + 1 prosthetist/day).  
**Impact:** Removes the single biggest access barrier for prosthetic users in Streeterville/Near North Side.

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Cultural Competency

**Problem:** All trials use English-only consent; no Spanish, Polish, Arabic, or Chinese materials.  
**Solution:**
- Translate all trial materials into the top 4 languages spoken in Chicago's amputee population (Spanish, Polish, Arabic, Chinese)
- Hire bilingual community outreach coordinators
- Partner with local ethnic media (e.g., *Extra News*, *Si Se Puede*, *Arab American News*)
- Provide interpreter services at all SRAL trial events

### Strategy 3: FQHC & Community Health Center Partnerships

**Problem:** Trials recruit from academic medical centers and VA facilities; community health centers serving uninsured/underinsured populations are excluded.  
**Solution:**
- Establish formal referral agreements with FQHCs on Chicago's South and West Sides (e.g., Roseland Community Hospital, Lawndale Christian Health Center, Howard Brown Health)
- Train FQHC staff as trial ambassadors
- Offer telehealth pre-screening to reduce travel burden

### Strategy 4: Rural & VA Tele-Outreach

**Problem:** Rural populations and rural VA sites have no local trial access.  
**Solution:**
- Launch a virtual trial-consent and screening pipeline using the PFE (Prosthetic Foot Emulator) model already tested at VA sites
- Partner with VA tele-rehabilitation programs in rural Illinois, Indiana, Wisconsin
- Offer at-home prosthetic training kits mailed to participants

### Strategy 5: Pediatric & Family-Inclusive Trial Design

**Problem:** No trials include participants under 18; no family-oriented protocols exist.  
**Solution:**
- Design a pediatric-friendly prosthetic trial with age-appropriate consent assent
- Include sibling/family engagement activities during trial visits
- Partner with Shriners Hospitals for Children (Chicago) for recruitment

### Strategy 6: Industry Partnership & Funding Diversification

**Problem:** 0% of current trials are industry-sponsored; all are academic/government.  
**Solution:**
- Pitch SRAL's patient registry and clinic infrastructure to prosthetic manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Fillauer, Blatchford)
- Propose a "prosthetic foot emulator" commercial partnership modeled on the NCT03651830 / NCT05473065 studies
- Pursue NIH R01 / PCORI funding for comparative-effectiveness trials

### Strategy 7: Transportation & Parking Accessibility

**Problem:** No ADA parking within 200 m; low-clearance underground garage; no EV charging.  
**Solution:**
- Reserve 2–3 ADA spots in the Doubletree multi-storey (negotiate COR)
- Coordinate CTA paratransit (Elephant Ride) pickup/drop-off at SRAL entrance
- Install wayfinding signage from nearest bus stops to SRAL lobby
- Map nearby EV charging (5+ stations at Water Tower Place ~600 m) and promote in outreach materials

### Strategy 8: Community Hub Activation

**Problem:** No social services, support groups, or community spaces within 500 m.  
**Solution:**
- Transform SRAL's ground-floor lobby into a monthly "Prosthetic Community Hour" — peer support, device demos, Q&A with prosthetists
- Partner with local disability advocacy orgs (e.g., Cheshire Disability Services, Adaptive Adventures)
- Offer free coffee/kiosk (partner with Yolk or Panera) to drive foot traffic
