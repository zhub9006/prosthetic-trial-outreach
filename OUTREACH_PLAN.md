## 1. Clinical Trial Gaps & Enrollment Trends

### 1.1 Landscape Overview (427 Total Trials)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total lower limb prosthetic trials | **427** |
| Currently recruiting | **~3–5** (<1.2%) |
| Completed | **~180** (~42%) |
| Active, not recruiting | **~40** (~9.4%) |
| Unknown status | **~20** (~4.7%) |

**Over 95% of registered trials are not actively enrolling** — a massive access gap for prosthetic users.

### 1.2 Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | Share | Gap Analysis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic/Nonprofit)** | 70 | 63.1% | Dominant but lacks tech/resources for underserved outreach |
| **INDUSTRY** | 21 | 18.9% | Device-focused; rarely reaches low-income/rural populations |
| **FED (VA/DoD)** | 17 | 15.3% | Veteran-exclusive; excludes civilian underserved groups |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 2 | 1.8% | Minimal non-VA government presence |
| **NIH** | 1 | 0.9% | Critically underfunded at the federal research level |

**Key Finding:** Only **19% of trials are industry-sponsored** — this limits access to cutting-edge prosthetic technologies for underserved populations who cannot afford private devices. **NIH funding is near-zero** (0.9%), meaning the primary federal health research agency is barely investing in this area.

### 1.3 Enrollment Trends by Phase

| Phase | Count | Share | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (Not Applicable)** | 81 | 73.0% | Most trials are device/observational — no phased regulatory pathway |
| **Unknown** | 26 | 23.4% | Lack of phase classification = poor tracking & accountability |
| **Phase 1** | 1 | 0.9% | Virtually no early-stage safety testing in prosthetics |
| **Phase 2** | 3 | 2.7% | Extremely limited efficacy testing pipeline |

**Key Finding:** **96% of trials are in NA or Unknown phase** — there is virtually no rigorous later-stage efficacy testing pipeline. Patients are being enrolled in unvalidated interventions with no phased regulatory oversight.

### 1.4 Identified Underserved Gaps

| Gap | Detail | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 **Rural Invisibility** | Zero trials in rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal lands | Critical |
| 🔴 **Racial/Ethnic** | No Spanish-language consent; no minority recruitment; Chicago's South Side has ZERO trial sites | Critical |
| 🔴 **Gender** | 90%+ male enrollment in most prosthetics trials | High |
| 🔴 **Pediatric** | No pediatric-specific prosthetic trials found | Critical |
| 🟠 **Global South** | ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access | High |
| 🟠 **DME Access** | Zero prosthetic/device vendors within 500m of SRAL | High |
| 🟡 **Mental Health** | No integrated mental health support in trial protocols | Moderate |
| 🟡 **Older Adults** | Limited accommodations for seniors (transport, mobility) | Moderate |

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy P1 — Immediate Impact (0–6 months, ~$111K/yr)

| # | Strategy | Description | Budget | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Patient Navigation & Transportation** | CTA reduced-fare cards, Divvy Adaptive bike-share vouchers, ride-share vouchers (Uber Health), dedicated patient navigator | $10K/yr | Month 1 |
| 2 | **Diversity Benchmarks** | Mandate ≥25% female, ≥30% minority, ≥15% Medicaid in all SRAL-affiliated trials | $0 (policy) | Month 3 |
| 3 | **Community Advisory Board (CAB)** | 10–15 prosthetic users + CHWs from underserved populations (South Side, rural, veteran) to advise on trial design | $15K/yr | Month 1 |
| 4 | **Open Data Dashboard** | Public GitHub dashboard showing trial demographics, enrollment gaps, outcomes — transparency drives accountability | $6K/yr (dev) | Month 3 |
| 5 | **Language Access** | All consent forms and outreach materials in Spanish, Mandarin, and Polish (top 3 non-English languages in Chicago) | $20K/yr | Month 6 |

### Strategy P2 — Mid-Term (6–12 months, ~$380K/yr)

| # | Strategy | Description | Budget | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | **Mobile Screening Events** | VA/FQHC mobile units deploying to rural Midwest towns and Chicago's South Side for prosthetic trial screening | $120K/yr | Month 6 |
| 7 | **Industry-Academic Device Pipeline** | Pro bono device donations from manufacturers + deployment to underserved trial sites; negotiated pricing for Medicaid recipients | $80K/yr | Month 9 |
| 8 | **DME Integration at SRAL** | **CRITICAL GAP:** Establish a prosthetic/device access station within 500m — currently ZERO DME vendors onsite | $100K/yr | Month 9 |
| 9 | **Tele-Trial Hub** | Virtual visits, mail-in device assessments, team-based remote trials to eliminate geographic barriers | $50K/yr | Month 6 |
| 10 | **Veteran Community Partnership** | Dedicated outreach to VA hospitals in IL, IN, MO for veteran prosthetic trial enrollment | $30K/yr | Month 6 |

### Strategy P3 — Sustained (12–24 months, ~$350K/yr)

| # | Strategy | Description | Budget | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| 12 | **Rural Mobile Prosthetics Labs** | Quarterly mobile prosthetics labs to VA clinics in rural IL, MO, IN — brings trials to patients | $80K/yr | Month 12 |
| 13 | **Older Adult Programming** | Caregiver stipends, in-home assessments, senior center partnerships (Via Health, Age Options) | $40K/yr | Month 12 |
| 14 | **Mental Health Integration** | Peer support mentors at Medrina (~605m from SRAL); monthly support groups at community centers | $30K/yr | Month 12 |

### Strategy P4 — Long-Term (24+ months, ~$140K/yr)
