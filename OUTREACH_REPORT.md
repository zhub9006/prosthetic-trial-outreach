### 1.5 Key Gap Analysis — Where Underserved Groups Are Missing

#### 🔴 Critical Gaps

1. **NIH-Funded Research (0.5%)**: Nearly zero NIH-sponsored trials means the **most independent, peer-reviewed funding stream** is absent from lower limb prosthetic research. This limits the evidence base for community-based prosthetic rehabilitation.

2. **Late-Phase Interventional Trials (PHASE 2–4 combined: ~3.2%)**: The overwhelming dominance of NA/Unknown phase studies means there is **very little evidence from randomized controlled trials** on prosthetic interventions for community-dwelling amputees.

3. **"Unknown" Recruitment Status (24.9%)**: Over a quarter of studies have unclear recruitment status, making it **impossible for outreach teams to identify active enrollment opportunities** for patients.

#### 🟡 Moderate Gaps

4. **Community-Based Organizations (1.9%)**: Almost no involvement from community rehab centers, disability advocacy groups, or local health departments as sponsors — meaning **grassroots prosthetic users are disconnected from trial infrastructure**.

5. **Veteran Over-Representation (9.7% FED)**: Federal sponsors (primarily VA) dominate the trial landscape, which means **non-veteran amputees (trauma, congenital, dysvascular community patients) are underrepresented** in clinical research.

6. **Industry Interaction (13.7%)**: While industry-funded trials exist, they focus on device marketing rather than **real-world functional outcomes** for underserved prosthetic users.

#### 🟢 Opportunities

7. **Academic Medical Centers (73.5%)**: The high concentration of academic sponsors suggests these institutions are **prime partners for community outreach** — they need community sites like Shirley Ryan AbilityLab for recruitment.
