# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Assessment

## Clinical Trial Gaps (ClinicalTrials.gov: 425 studies)
- Only 20% actively recruiting; 12% unknown status
- 73.4% academic-sponsored; 13.6% industry; 9.6% federal
- 70.1% observational/device studies; only 4.5% Phase 1-4 trials
- 45+ countries have ≤5 trials (major LMIC gap)

## Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Site (355 E Erie St, Chicago, IL 60611)
- Coordinates: 41.8938727, -87.6184271
- **Transit:** 10+ CTA stops within 500m (excellent access)
- **Parking:** Street-level and garages available
- **Healthcare:** AbilityLab itself, RIC affiliation, pharmacies nearby

## Key Gaps Identified
1. No late-stage Phase 2-3 efficacy trials
2. LMIC populations excluded from trial ecosystem
3. Veteran-centric recruitment dominates
4. Industry underrepresentation (13.6%)
5. No community-based outreach infrastructure

## Proposed Outreach Strategies
1. Transit voucher program for low-income users
2. Community Health Worker network for screening
3. Bilingual outreach (Spanish, Polish, Mandarin, Tagalog)
4. Veteran-to-civilian bridge partnerships
5. Pediatric/congenital limb differences registry
6. LMIC collaboration with India, Pakistan, Kenya

## Summary
The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is an ideal outreach hub with excellent transit access, dense healthcare infrastructure, and Chicago neighborhood connectivity. A CHW-network-based strategy with transit support and multilingual engagement can address the critical gaps in prosthetic trial participation for underserved communities.
