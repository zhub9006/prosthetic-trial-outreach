## 1. Clinical Trial Landscape Analysis

### Scope
Searched ClinicalTrials.gov for "lower limb prosthetic" — **104 total studies** found; **40 currently active** (recruiting, active-not-recruiting, or not-yet-recruiting).

### Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type (40 active trials)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Active | Underserved Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **University / Other (OTHER)** | 25 | 62.5% | Academic-led; often restricted to near-site recruitment |
| **Industry (Prosthetic companies)** | 11 | 27.5% | Commercial focus; may exclude uninsured/underinsured |
| **Federal (NIH, VA, CDC)** | 5 | 12.5% | Broader access but few trials in this category |
| **Other Gov / NIH** | 2 | 5% | Limited funding pipeline |

**Key Gap:** Only **~12.5% of active trials have federal or public funding**, meaning most depend on industry or university sponsorship. Uninsured and low-income prosthetic users are systematically underrepresented in industry-sponsored device trials, which typically require insurance coverage and proximity to academic centers.

### Enrollment Trends by Phase (40 active trials)

| Phase | Count | % | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (early/observational)** | 31 | 77.5% | Mostly safety/ feasibility/observational — no efficacy data yet |
| **Unknown** | 8 | 20% | Not yet posted phase — recruitment may be informal |
| **Phase 3** | 1 | 2.5% | Only ONE Phase 3 efficacy trial active — critical gap |

**Key Gap:** The near-absence of Phase 3 trials (just 1 of 40) means **very few trials are generating the comparative-effectiveness evidence** that would justify insurance coverage expansion or guideline changes for underserved populations. Most active trials are small, early-stage, or observational (median enrollment n=9–30).

## 3. Gaps for Underserved Populations

### Who Is Missing from Current Trials?

1. **Uninsured / Underinsured:** Industry trials overwhelmingly require insurance coverage. No civic/nonprofit-funded trials exist (0 of 104 total). The single federal-funded trial has only 5 participants.
2. **Rural Residents:** Zero trials in rural US locations. All active trials are in urban academic or industry centers.
3. **Non-English Speakers:** Zero trials offer non-English consent forms or multilingual study materials.
4. **Pediatric/Young Adult:** Zero pediatric-prosthetic trials. Age cutoffs in adult trials (typically 18–84) exclude teenagers and young adults with congenital or early-childhood amputations.
5. **Bilateral Amputees:** Most trials exclude bilateral amputees (e.g., NCT06635655 explicitly excludes them), yet this population faces the greatest mobility challenges.
6. **Elderly (>84):** Upper age caps of 84 exclude the growing geriatric amputee population (diabetes/vascular-related amputations are increasing in older adults).
7. **Protected/Vulnerable Persons:** Standard exclusion criteria remove incarcerated individuals, pregnant women, and those lacking decision-making capacity — populations with already-limited healthcare access.
8. **Phase 3 Evidence:** With only 1 of 40 active trials at Phase 3, there is a massive evidence gap for insurance coverage decisions that would expand access.

### Site-Level Barriers at SRAL

- **No on-site pharmacy** — prosthetic users must walk 8+ minutes or take transit to fill prescriptions
- **No DME/prosthetic vendor within 500m** — immediate post-trial adjustments require travel
- **Paid parking only** — no ADA-designated spots; cost barrier for low-income visitors
- **No social services on-site** — no case management, employment counseling, or disability benefits navigation

## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile Prosthetist / DME Van Program
Deploy a **monthly mobile prosthetic clinic** on-site at SRAL (or rotating to adjacent neighborhoods) staffed with a licensed prosthetist. Offer:
- Prosthetic fitting and adjustment for trial participants
- Insurance navigation assistance
- Sliding-scale fees for uninsured users
- Estimated cost: ~$15K/year (van lease + staff)

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Cultural Competency
Create **multilingual outreach materials** in the top 5 non-English languages spoken in Chicago:
- Spanish, Polish, Chinese (Mandarin/Cantonese), Arabic, Vietnamese
- Bilingual community health workers for in-person navigation
- Translated informed consent templates for trial recruitment

### Strategy 3: FQHC & Community Health Center Partnerships
Partner with **Federally Qualified Health Centers** on the South and West Sides of Chicago (e.g., Roseland Community Hospital, John H. Stroger Jr. Hospital) to:
- Screen and refer eligible prosthetic users to trials
- Provide transportation vouchers for SRAL visits
- Offer pre-screening telehealth consultations

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach & Virtual Screening
For the rural population gap:
- **Virtual trial screening** via telehealth platform
- Remote gait assessment using smartphone-based apps (as piloted in INCREMENT study)
- Partner with **VA rural sites** (Hines VA, Marion VA) for recruitment
- Distribute mail-based informational packets with prepaid return envelopes

### Strategy 5: Pediatric & Elderly-Inclusive Trial Design
Advocate for trial protocol revisions that:
- Lower minimum age to **12 years** for pediatric prosthesis trials
- Raise maximum age to **95+** with geriatric-specific outcome measures
- Include **bilateral amputees** with appropriate statistical adjustments
- Simplify visit schedules for elderly participants (transport provision, caregiver inclusion)

### Strategy 6: SRAL Site Improvement Recommendations
| Issue | Recommendation | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| No DME vendor | Partner with Orbis or Össur for on-site kiosk 1–2 days/month | High |
