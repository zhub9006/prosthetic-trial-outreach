### 1.6 Systematic Gaps Identified

| # | Gap Category | Specific Issue | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Sponsor Imbalance** | 73.5% academic vs. 13.7% industry | No community outreach; zero FQHC/safety-net sponsors |
| 2 | **Phase Shortage** | 95% observational/unknown | Only 12 trials (2.9%) in Phase I–IV |
| 3 | **Population Exclusion** | 7 underserved groups systematically missing | Women, low-income, elderly 75+, non-English, rural, wheelchair, trauma amputees |
| 4 | **Geographic Concentration** | Trials in 5–10 academic hubs | Low/Middle-Income countries have <15 studies total |
| 5 | **Language Barrier** | English/Spanish only | No Mandarin, Polish, Arabic, or other translations |
| 6 | **Race/Ethnicity Diversity** | No dedicated minority recruitment | FDA guidance on diversity not implemented in prosthetic trials |
| 7 | **Aging Population Gap** | No trials for elderly with comorbidities | Most trials exclude participants with multiple chronic conditions |
| 8 | **K-Level Exclusion** | Only K2+ ambulators recruited | K0/K1 non-ambulatory amputees completely excluded |
| 9 | **No Digital Divide Consideration** | All trials require in-person visits | Rural and low-tech users cannot participate |
| 10 | **Gender Imbalance** | Women underrepresented in prosthetic trials | Male-dominated amputation population leads to male-skewed samples |

## 3. Outreach Strategies for Underserved Groups

### 3.1 Gap-to-Strategy Mapping

| Underserved Group | Why Missing from Trials | Primary Strategy | Secondary Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Non-veteran amputees** | Trials funded by VA/DOD; veteran-only eligibility | Data Equity Audit | CHW Bridge Program |
| **Low-income / uninsured** | Cost of participation (travel, time off work) | Transportation & Practical Supports | Telehealth eligibility screening |
| **Elderly 75+ with comorbidities** | Age/exclusion criteria in most trials | Peer Mentor Network | Mobile screening clinics |
| **Non-English speakers** | English/Spanish-only consent forms | Multilingual & multicultural outreach | CHW navigators |
| **Rural residents** | Geographic concentration at academic hubs | Telehealth screening | Mobile screening clinics |
| **Wheelchair-dependent users** | Inaccessible trial sites; K-level exclusions | Prosthetic Access Hub | Transportation supports |
| **Trauma amputees** | Focus on congenital/veteran populations | CHW Bridge Program | Data Equity Audit |
| **K0/K1 non-ambulatory** | Trials require K2+ functional level | Data Equity Audit | Expanded inclusion criteria advocacy |
| **Women** | Male-dominated amputation population (~80% male) | Multilingual & multicultural outreach | Gender-specific recruitment materials |
| **People with disabilities beyond limb loss** | Narrow eligibility criteria | Systematic inclusion review | Peer mentor network |

### 3.2 Detailed Outreach Strategies

#### Strategy 1: Community Health Worker (CHW) Bridge Program

**Target Gaps:** Low-income, non-English, rural, trauma amputees

**Description:** Deploy trained CHWs (especially those from amputee communities) as trial navigators who help prosthetic users:
- Understand trial eligibility and requirements  
- Complete enrollment paperwork  
- Attend screening visits  
- Navigate transportation logistics  
- Follow up between visits  

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $80,000–$120,000  
**Key Partners:** FQHCs, VA outpatient clinics, prosthetics providers  
**Timeline:** 3–6 months to hire/train, ongoing  
**Success Metrics:** # of referrals to trials, enrollment completion rates by CHW  

#### Strategy 2: Mobile Prosthetic Screening Clinics

**Target Gaps:** Rural, low-income, wheelchair users, elderly, trauma amputees

**Description:** Bring prosthetic expertise and trial screening directly to underserved communities:
- Partner with VA clinics, FQHCs, and community centers  
- Provide free prosthetic assessment by qualified clinicians  
- Screen for trial eligibility on-site  
- Distribute multilingual informational materials  

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $60,000–$90,000  
**Key Partners:** Mobile health organizations, VA, prosthetics manufacturers  
**Timeline:** 4–8 months to launch pilot  
**Success Metrics:** # of communities served, screening completion rates  

#### Strategy 3: Multicultural & Multilingual Outreach

**Target Gaps:** Non-English speakers, immigrant communities, Hispanic/Latino, Asian-American, Arabic-speaking populations

**Description:** Develop culturally appropriate materials and partnerships:
- Translate consent forms and materials into Mandarin, Polish, Arabic, and other languages  
- Partner with faith-based organizations (churches, mosques, temples)  
- Recruit bilingual community liaisons  
- Host culturally specific prosthetic education events  

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $40,000–$60,000  
**Key Partners:** Religious organizations, cultural centers, immigrant services  
**Timeline:** 2–4 months for translation + pilot events  
**Success Metrics:** # of non-English speakers engaged, language-specific enrollment  

#### Strategy 4: Telehealth Eligibility Screening

**Target Gaps:** Rural, wheelchair users, elderly, low-income, those who can't visit sites

**Description:** Enable remote trial pre-screening:
- Video-based eligibility assessment  
- Digital consent process  
- Remote prosthetic fitting assessment  
- Virtual orientation to trial procedures  

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $30,000–$50,000  
**Key Partners:** Telehealth platforms, prosthetics providers, VA  
**Timeline:** 2–4 months for platform setup  
**Success Metrics:** # of virtual screenings completed, geographic reach  

#### Strategy 5: Peer Mentor Network

**Target Gaps:** All underserved groups

**Description:** Recruit diverse amputees as peer mentors:
- Match new amputees with experienced prosthetic users  
- Provide emotional support and trial information  
- Leverage lived experience to build trust  
- Focus on underrepresented groups (women, young amputees, elderly first-time users)  

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $25,000–$40,000  
**Key Partners:** Amputee coalition organizations, SRAL peer support programs  
**Timeline:** 2–4 months to recruit and train  
**Success Metrics:** # of active mentor-mentee pairs, satisfaction scores  

#### Strategy 6: Transportation & Practical Supports

**Target Gaps:** Low-income, rural, wheelchair users, caregivers

**Description:** Remove logistical barriers to trial participation:
- CTA transit credits for participants  
- Parking vouchers for SRAL visits  
- Accessible transportation coordination (via Divvy adaptive bikes, paratransit)  
- Childcare stipends for trial visits  
- Caregiver support during visits  

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $50,000–$80,000  
**Key Partners:** CTA, accessible transit providers, SRAL  
**Timeline:** 1–3 months to implement  
**Success Metrics:** # of participants using transit support, no-show rate reduction  

#### Strategy 7: Data Equity Audit

**Target Gaps:** All underserved groups (systemic)

**Description:** Systematically audit all 425 lower limb prosthetic trials for exclusionary criteria:
- Review eligibility criteria for unnecessary exclusions  
- Assess demographic data collection practices  
- Identify racial/ethnic/gender/age disparities in enrollment  
- Publish findings and advocate for inclusive criteria changes  
- Create a public dashboard of trial diversity metrics  

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $40,000–$60,000  
**Key Partners:** ProPublica, Roll Call, academic researchers, FDA  
**Timeline:** 1–2 months for audit framework, ongoing  
**Success Metrics:** # of trials with revised criteria, demographic data transparency  

#### Strategy 8: Prosthetic Access Hub at SRAL

**Target Gaps:** All underserved groups; neighborhood welcome

**Description:** Establish a free community space at or near Shirley Ryan AbilityLab:
- Quarterly "Prosthetic Community Days" with free fittings, education, and trial info  
- Ongoing support group meetings for amputees  
- Trial awareness booth with multilingual materials  
- Peer mentoring meetups  
- Resource library (prosthetic supplies, information)  

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $60,000–$100,000  
**Key Partners:** SRAL, community organizations, prosthetics companies  
**Timeline:** 3–6 months to establish space and schedule  
**Success Metrics:** Attendance at community days, # of trial inquiries generated

## 4. Site Suitability Verdict

### overall Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5) — STRONG OUTREACH SITE WITH MODIFICATIONS NEEDED

| Factor | Rating | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Clinical Credibility** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | World-leading rehab hospital; highest referral potential |
| **Transit Access** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 15 CTA bus stops + Divvy; train station 600m away; no tactile paving |
| **Healthcare Proximity** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | On-site hospital + VA clinic + 24/7 pharmacy |
| **Support Amenities** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Extensive food, banking, retail within 500m |
| **Parking** | ⭐⭐ | Only paid options ($15–$30/day); no free/validated parking |
| **Neighborhood Welcomeness** | ⭐⭐⭐ | Affluent Streeterville; may feel economically unwelcoming |
| **Wheelchair Accessibility** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Most amenities accessible; bus stops lack tactile paving |
| **EV/Alternative Transport** | ⭐⭐ | No EV charging; limited adaptive cycling support |

**Key Modifications Recommended:**
1. Partner with SRAL for validated parking or transit subsidies for trial participants
2. Install tactile paving at nearby bus stops (advocacy with CTA)
3. Establish a free transit transfer program with CTA for prosthetic users
4. Create welcoming signage and tactile wayfinding for prosthetic users at SRAL entrance
5. Add wheelchair-accessible bike options at nearest Divvy station
6. Develop a community welcome program to reduce neighborhood intimidation
