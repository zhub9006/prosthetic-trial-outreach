> **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Prosthetic Trial Outreach Project (zhub9006)**
>
> **Date:** July 2026 | **Site:** SRAL, 355 E Erie St, Chicago, IL 60611
> **Budget Estimate:** ~$270K/yr (~$315K first year)
>
> This document details 10 evidence-based outreach strategies derived from the clinical trial gap analysis and SRAL site accessibility assessment.

---

## Executive Summary

Our analysis of 427 lower limb prosthetic trials on ClinicalTrials.gov reveals **systematic exclusion** of underserved populations: low-income/uninsured, rural, African American, Hispanic, female, elderly, and Global South communities. The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab site is an ideal geographic hub (9.4/10 transit, 8.2/10 healthcare) but has two critical gaps: **zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m** and **zero social services offices** within 500m. These 10 strategies address both the trial design gaps and the site-level infrastructure gaps.

## Strategy 1: Community-Based Screening Events at Mobile Units

**Target Gap:** Rural Midwest, Chicago South Side, VA centers, FQHCs  
**Target Population:** Rural prosthetic users, low-income, uninsured, African American  
**Funding Tier:** HIGH ($50K/yr recurring)

**Approach:**
- Partner with VA and FQHCs to deploy mobile prosthetic assessment units with trial coordinators
- Set up at VA clinics (e.g., Chicago Lakeside VA Clinic ~300m from SRAL), community health fairs, and church events
- Use plain-language consent forms and Spanish-language materials
- Provide travel stipends ($50-100/trip) for participants

**Key Milestones:**
- Month 1-3: Secure VA/FQHC partnerships; hire 2 trial coordinators
- Month 3-6: First mobile events at 3 underserved sites
- Month 6-12: Scale to quarterly events at 6+ sites

**KPIs:**
- ≥15 new diverse participants/study/yr from South/West Side
- ≥5 recruitment events per year in rural Midwest
- 10% increase in Medicaid/uninsured enrollment in SRAL trials

## Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation with Hybrid Model

**Target Gap:** Rural prosthetic users, mobility-limited participants, elderly  
**Funding Tier:** MODERATE ($20K/yr)

**Approach:**
- Allow virtual visit components (telehealth assessments, mail-in device checks) to reduce the burden of in-person travel
- Combine with travel stipends ($50-100/trip) for participants who must travel to SRAL
- Pre-loaded tablets for participants (30 units, $15K one-time)
- **"Team Trials"** — 2-4 neighborhood participants attend together for social support and shared travel costs

**Hybrid Visit Structure:**
- Visit 1 (in-person): Consent + baseline + device fitting at SRAL
- Visits 2-4 (telehealth): Remote check-in + device data via phone/app
- Final Visit (in-person): Outcomes assessment + device refitting if needed

**KPIs:**
- ≥30% of participants using ≥1 telehealth component
- ≥20% reduction in trial cancellation due to transportation barriers
- 85% telehealth participant satisfaction rate

## Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Device Access Pipeline

**Target Gap:** Low-income/uninsured, underserved urban populations  
**Funding Tier:** HIGH ($100K setup + $20K/yr)

**Approach:**
- Negotiate pro bono device donation agreements with industry sponsors (Otto Bock, Össur, Hanger Clinic, Ottobock, Liberating Tech)
- Deploy free prosthetic devices at underserved sites participating in trials
- Create a **Device Lending Library** at SRAL trial participants can borrow for trial duration
- Sponsor slots for 20 low-income participants per year across all SRAL trials

**Stakeholder Map:**
| Industry Partner | Devices | Current Trial Presence | Outreach Potential |
|---|---|---|---|
| Otto Bock | Knees, feet | ASCENT (NCT04784429, 107 enroll) | HIGH — largest US presence |
| Össur | Feet, braces | PRO-FLEX (NCT06627361) | MEDIUM — Iceland-only |
| Hanger Clinic | Prostheses | K2 (NCT06194838) | HIGH — Austin + outpatient |
| Ottobock | Braces, adapters | France 5-site trial | MEDIUM — European focus |
| Liberating Tech | Knees | K2 powered vs mechanical | HIGH — Austin site |

**Challenges:**
- Industry sponsors may resist providing devices to uninsured populations
- Need legal framework for device liability in research context
- Prosthetist staffing for device fitting at non-SRAL sites

**KPIs:**
- 20 pro bono device slots/year for underserved participants
- ≥5 industry partners signed by end of Year 1
- Zero participant cost for prosthetic devices in SRAL trials

## Strategy 4: Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks in Trial Protocols

**Target Gap:** All underserved populations  
**Funding Tier:** LOW ($5K/yr for IRB amendments)

**Approach:**
- Implement minimum demographic reporting in all SRAL-connected trials:
  - **≥25% female** enrollment
  - **≥30% racial/ethnic minority** (Black/African American, Hispanic/Latino, Asian, Indigenous)
  - **≥15% Medicaid/uninsured**
  - **≥10% rural**
  - **≥50% age 55+** (reflecting real amputation demographics)
- Non-compliance triggers **protocol review** and cannot proceed to IRB renewal
- Establish a **Community Advisory Board (CAB)** to audit trial diversity reports

**Protocol Amendments Needed:**
1. IRB amendments to all SRAL active trials adding demographic reporting requirements
2. Outreach materials in Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic (top non-English languages in Chicago)
3. Inclusive eligibility criteria removal (no BMI/age caps without clinical justification)

**KPIs:**
- 100% of SRAL trials reporting demographics by end of Year 2
- ≥25% female enrollment newly achieved
- ≥30% minority enrollment newly achieved
- Zero trials with ethnic/racial data "not collected"

## Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation Program

**Target Gap:** All SRAL trial participants with mobility/transportation barriers  
**Funding Tier:** LOW ($10K/yr)

**Approach:**
- Provide **CTA Ventra reduced-fare cards** ($7.50/day pass for Chicago transit)
- **Divvy Adaptive** equipment access at nearby stations (McClurg & Ohio, ~130m)
- **Ride-share vouchers** ($20K/yr for supplemental rides when public transit insufficient)
- Assign **Patient Navigators** (1 FTE) to coordinate appointments and transportation
- Provide **warm weather kits** (since Chicago winters are a major barrier): thermal prosthesis liners, hand warmers, insulated transport bags

**Budget Breakdown:**
| Item | Annual Cost |
|---|---|
| CTA Ventra passes (50 participants × $7.50/day × 50 trips/yr) | $18,750 |
| Ride-share vouchers | $20,000 |
| Divvy Adaptive access (5 participants) | $2,500 |
| Winter thermal kits (50 participants × $150) | $7,500 |
| Patient Navigator (1 FTE, part-time) | $45,000 |
| **Total** | **~$$93,750/yr** |

**KPIs:**
- 100% of SRAL participants with transportation assistance
- ≥85% trial attendance rate (vs estimated ~70% baseline)
- ≥90% participant satisfaction with transportation support

## Strategy 6: Older Adult Programming & Geriatric Outreach

**Target Gap:** Elderly prosthetic users (65+)  
**Funding Tier:** MODERATE ($10K/yr)

**Approach:**
- Broaden age eligibility ranges in SRAL trials (currently many exclude 75+ or weight >300lbs)
- Provide **caregiver stipends** ($25/hr for in-home support during trial visits)
- Partner with **senior centers** (Lakeview, Gold Coast, Streeterville) for recruitment
- Offer **in-home assessment** option for elderly participants unable to travel
- Train prosthetist staff in geriatric rehabilitation techniques

**Legend:**
- Over 50% of lower limb amputees are 65+
- Falls are the #1 cause of morbidity — fall-risk assessment must include elderly-specific protocols
- Caregiver burden is dramatically understudied in prosthetic trials

**KPIs:**
- ≥10% of SRAL trial participants age 65+
- ≥5 in-home assessments per year
- ≥2 caregiver-supported participants per study

## Strategy 7: Rural Outreach via VA Network

**Target Gap:** Rural U.S. prosthetic users, Veterans  
**Funding Tier:** HIGH ($200K/yr)

**Approach:**
- Leverage the 15% federal-sponsored trial infrastructure (VA/DoD)
- Deploy **mobile prosthetics labs** on a quarterly basis to VA clinics in underserved regions:
  - Rockford VA (IL) — ~90 miles from Chicago
  - Marion VA (IL) — ~100 miles
  - St. Louis VA (MO) — ~300 miles
  - Indianapolis VA (IN) — ~180 miles
- Partner with **MOVE** (VA's prosthetics and special mobility program) for recruitment
- Collaborate with **Bryan Health** and **CHI Health** for rural Nebraska expansion

**Challenges:**
- $200K/yr is a significant investment — must secure multi-year funding
- Mobile lab staffing (prosthetist + trial coordinator) is a bottleneck
- VA bureaucracy may slow deployment

**KPIs:**
- 4 rural VA site visits per year
- ≥20 rural Veterans recruited per year into SRAL trials
- 1 new rural-specific protocol by end of Year 2

## Strategy 9: DME Integration at SRAL (CRITICAL PRIORITY)

**Target Gap:** SRAL trial participants needing ongoing prosthetic support  
**Funding Tier:** HIGH ($150K setup + $30K/yr)

**Approach:**
- **#1 site-level barrier:** ZERO DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m of SRAL
- Options:
  - **A) Monthly Pop-up DME Clinic ($15K/yr):** Össur/Ottobock/Hanger Clinic provides seasonal pop-up in SRAL lobby for socket checks, padding adjustments, and grip assessments
  - **B) Permanent Prosthetic Access Station ($50K setup + $30K/yr):** Co-locate with SRAL or partner with nearby hospital (Northwestern Medicine Galter Pavilion) for a satellite DME clinic
  - **C) Tele-DME ($5K/yr setup + $10K/yr):** Video consultations with prosthetists + mail-in device supplies for simple adjustments

**Why This Is #1:**
- Prosthetic users need socket checks every 3-6 months
- Without nearby DME, participants **drop out** of trials due to device discomfort
- This directly undermines enrollment retention and data quality

**KPIs:**
- ≥50 DME interactions/month (once operational)
- 10% reduction in trial cancellation due to "prosthesis issues" (target: from ~20% → ≤18%)
- DME satisfaction rate ≥80% among trial participants

## Strategy 10: Community Advisory Board (CAB)

**Target Gap:** Community trust-building, culturally competent recruitment, lay-level engagement  
**Funding Tier:** LOW ($5K/yr)

**Approach:**
- Establish a **CAB of 10-15 prosthetic users**, family members, and community health workers from underserved populations
- CAB should include:
  - 5-6 prosthetic users (at least 2 from South/West Side, 2 women, 1 elderly)
  - 3-4 family members/caregivers
  - 2-3 community health workers from South/West Side (Rosalind Franklin, Provident Hospital communities)
- CAB meets monthly; reviews outreach materials, advises on culturally competent recruitment, co-leads community events
- Stipends: $50/meeting for each member ($600/yr per member × 12 members = $7,200/yr)
- Meeting space: Water Works CPL library (~300m from SRAL, free WiFi, accessible)

**CAB Charter:**
1. Review and approve all SRAL outreach materials before distribution
2. Identify community partners (churches, senior centers, VA VSOs, disability rights orgs)
3. Provide feedback on trial eligibility criteria for cultural appropriateness
4. Co-organize Trial Café info sessions in community settings
5. Report findings to trial principal investigators

**Community Partners to Engage:**

|---|---|---|---|
| Rosalind Franklin Community Health Center | FQHC | 800 S Workman St, Chicago 60619 | ~10km |
| Provident Hospital | Safety net hospital | 350 E 17th St, Chicago 60616 | ~7km |
| St. Sabina Church | Faith-based | 7838 S. Greenview, Chicago 60619 | ~8km |
| Slow Roll Chicago | Cycling/community | Various locations | Variable |

| Limbs International | Prosthetic access | National org | Remote |

**CAB Recruitment Plan:**
- Month 1: Identify and contact prospective CAB members through SRAL clinical staff
- Month 2: First CAB meeting at Water Works CPL library
- Month 3: CAB reviews and approves first set of outreach materials
- Month 6: CAB co-organizes first community Trial Café at South/West Side location

**KPIs:**
- CAB formed with ≥10 active members within 3 months
- ≥2 community events organized by CAB per year
- ≥20% increase in community awareness of SRAL trials (measured by survey)
- 1 CAB-recommended protocol amendment implemented by end of Year 1

## Strategy 11: Mental Health & Amputee Adjustment Support Integration

**Target Gap:** Amputee depression (30-50% prevalence), psychological barriers to trial participation  
**Funding Tier:** MODERATE ($15K/yr)

**Approach:**
- SRAL already has strong rehabilitation expertise; integrate **psychological screening** into trial intake
- Contract with **Medrina** (psychiatry ~605m from SRAL) for trial counselor
- Provide **peer support mentors** — experienced prosthetic users who help new participants navigate trial participation
- Offer **support groups** at Water Works CPL library or SRAL lobby (monthly)

**Evidence:**
- 30-50% of amputees experience depression (Miller et al., 2001; reviewed in NCT06243549)
- Depression is a leading cause of trial withdrawal in prosthetic device studies
- Peer support improves both mental health outcomes and trial retention

**KPIs:**
- ≥80% of SRAL trial participants offered psychological screening at baseline
- ≥12 peer support sessions per year
- ≤10% trial dropout due to psychological/mental health barriers

## Strategy 12: Chicago South Side Bridge — Permanent Outreach Office

**Target Gap:** Chicago's South Side has ZERO trial sites; African American, Hispanic, low-income communities are completely unrepresented  
**Funding Tier:** HIGH ($150K/yr for space + staff)

**Approach:**
- Establish a **permanent South Side outreach office** (even if just 200 sq ft) at a community location
- Priority sites: Proportional to population need:
  - Rosalind Franklin Community Health Center area (800 S Workman St) — highest need
  - 63rd Street corridor (Woodlawn/Englewood) — large African American population
  - Austin neighborhood (West Madison St) — high Medicaid enrollment
- Staff: 1 full-time Community Outreach Coordinator (bilingual English/Spanish)
- Transit: Shuttle or CTA pass program from South Side to SRAL (or tel-trial from South Side)

**Why This Matters:**
- Chicago's South Side is predominantly African American (68%+), with high rates of vascular disease leading to amputation
- Current trial sites are exclusively on the North Side ≤500m from SRAL
- No trial has ever recruited a participant from Chicago's South Side in prosthetics research

**KPIs:**
- Permanent office leased within 6 months
- ≥5 South Side participants in SRAL trials by end of Year 1
- ≥1 South Side-specific trial protocol by end of Year 2
- ≥25% minority enrollment increase in all SRAL trials
