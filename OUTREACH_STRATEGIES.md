## 2. Trial Gap Analysis — Where Underserved Groups Are Missing

### 2.1 Demographic Gaps

| Group | Population % | Trial Representation | Gap Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Women | ~30% amputees | <10% in most trials | 🔴 Severe |
| African American | ~13% US pop | <5% in trials | 🔴 Severe |
| Hispanic/Latino | ~19% US pop | <5% in trials | 🔴 Severe |
| Adults 75+ | Fastest cohort | Rarely enrolled | 🔴 Severe |
| Children | ~20% limb loss | Virtually absent | 🔴 Critical |
| Low-income/Uninsured | ~25% amputees | <2% in trials (1 trial total) | 🔴 Critical |
| Global South | — | 80 nations = zero access | 🔴 Critical |
| Transgender/Gender-diverse | ~1% est. | Not tracked | 🔴 Critical |

### 2.2 Geographic Gaps

- **US + EU:** >88% of all trials
- **Latin America, Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, Middle East, Oceania:** Near-zero or minimal access

### 2.3 Condition-Specific Gaps

- Vascular/diabetes-related amputations (most common in elderly): Few trials specifically recruit
- Traumatic amputees in young adults: Nearly absent from research
- Pediatric limb loss: Almost zero dedicated trials
- Congenital limb differences: No dedicated trials
- Osseointegration recipients: Emerging (NCT06160882) but limited recruitment

## 4. Seven Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: "Bridging the Gap" — Community Trial Navigators
**Annual Budget:** $90,000  
**Gap:** Geographic & racial access  
**Actions:** Hire 2–3 community health workers from South/West Side; deploy at community centers, churches, VA facilities; provide CTA Ventra transport stipends ($50/participant).

### Strategy 2: "Prosthetic Supply Corridor" — Pop-up DME
**Budget:** $15K (pop-up) to $65K (permanent)  
**Gap:** Zero DME within 500 m  
**Actions:** Monthly pop-up DME station in SRAL lobby; partner with Hanger Clinic, Otto Bock, or College Park; provide insurance navigation support.

### Strategy 3: "Warm Hands, Warm Access" — Thermal Comfort
**Annual Budget:** $33K  
**Gap:** Winter thermal barrier  
**Actions:** Heated waiting area ($25K HVAC one-time); thermal prosthetic liners/socks ($8K/yr); heated CTA paratransit for winter months ($5K/yr).

### Strategy 4: "South Side Transit Pipeline" — CTA Shuttle + Tele-Trial
**Annual Budget:** $60,000  
**Gap:** 1–2 bus transfers for South/West Siders  
**Actions:** CTA accessible shuttle from South Side hubs 2x/week; tele-rehabilitation platform; mobile trial assessment unit for community visits.

### Strategy 5: "Global Equity Bridge" — International Partnerships
**Budget:** $50K start + $50K/yr  
**Gap:** 80 nations with zero trial access  
**Actions:** MOUs with Colombia, India, Pakistan; low-cost scalable protocol ($500/participant); launch 3 international sites within 18 months.

### Strategy 6: "Mental Health & Acceptance" — Peer Support
**Annual Budget:** $20,000  
**Gap:** 30–50% amputee depression  
**Actions:** PHQ-2 screening in all trial intake; monthly peer support group; Medrina partnership; decision aids for enrollment.

### Strategy 7: "Women, Children & Gender-Diverse" — Inclusive Protocols
**Budget:** $35K start + $15K/yr  
**Gap:** 90%+ male skew; pediatric absence  
**Actions:** Equal-platform recruitment (50% gender minimum); pediatric sub-study with Lurie Children's; gender-affirming protocols; family-friendly scheduling; Lakeshore Sport & Fitness partnership.
