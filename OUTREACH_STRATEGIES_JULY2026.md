# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Strategies (July 2026)

> Action-oriented outreach strategies derived from ClinicalTrials.gov gap analysis and Shirley Ryan AbilityLab site assessment.

## Overview

This document outlines concrete outreach strategies for the prosthetic trial outreach project, based on:
1. ClinicalTrials.gov enrollment gaps identified in 427+ lower-limb prosthetic studies
2. Site accessibility assessment of Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (355 E Erie St, Chicago, IL 60611)

---

## Strategy 1: Close the "Unknown" Trial Gap

**Problem identified:** 25% of trials have unknown phase/status; likely stalled recruitment.

**Actions:**
- Deploy clinical trial navigators at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab to screen for unknown-status trials
- Re-engage lapsed studies with updated consent forms and simplified eligibility
- Offer transportation and interpretation services to reduce participation barriers

## Strategy 2: Mobile DME & Prosthetic Supply Unit

**Problem identified:** Zero DME vendors within 500m — #1 site-level barrier.

**Actions:**
- Launch a monthly mobile prosthetist van for uninsured amputees (~$15K/yr)
- Provide free emergency adjustments, socket checks, and fitting assistance
- Stock basic prosthetic supplies (sheaths, liners, socks) from pooled industry donations

## Strategy 3: Rural & Tele-Rehabilitation Outreach

**Problem identified:** 73% academic-sponsored; 0% rural trial sites.

**Actions:**
- Virtual screening visits via tele-rehabilitation platforms
- Monthly in-person telehealth kiosks at rural libraries, VA clinics, and FQHCs
- Ship trial devices to participants with home-based PT coaching

## Strategy 4: Expand Demographic Inclusion

**Problem identified:** No trials recruit by race, ethnicity, age, or language.

**Actions:**
- Language access: Spanish, Polish, Arabic, Chinese consent forms
- Bilingual community health workers as trial navigators
- Evening/weekend clinic hours for hourly workers
- Demographic equity dashboard tracking enrollment vs. Chicago demographics
- Pediatric sub-studies (ages 10–17) and geriatric-inclusive age ranges (18–85)

## Strategy 5: Diverse Community Partnerships

**Actions:**
- Partner with Amputee Coalition of Chicago, Hispanic Council for Reform, Southeast Asian community centers
- FQHC partnerships on South/West Sides for screening and referral pipeline
- Community advisory board with amputeemembers
- Quarterly town halls to present findings and gather feedback

## Strategy 6: Industry Device Access Fund

**Problem identified:** Industry trials (14%) favor insured participants.

**Actions:**
- Negotiate pooled device inventory with Ottobock, Össur, Endolite, Blatchford
- Sliding-scale pricing for uninsured participants
- Cross-reference with foundation grants (Limbs International, Open Socket, Prosthetic Hope International)

## Strategy 7: Optimize SRAL Site Infrastructure

**Problem identified:** No ADA parking within 200m; no DME nearby.

**Actions:**
- Negotiate designated ADA parking at Doubletree or 777 N Michigan garages
- Establish DME drop-off/pick-up service with Magnificent Mile vendor
- Request Divvy adaptive cycling station near main entrance
- Install bilingual wayfinding signage from Michigan & Erie bus stop (~250m)
