# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Project

## Overview

This project assesses community outreach opportunities for lower limb prosthetic users in Chicago, focusing on clinical trial enrollment gaps, site accessibility at the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, and proposed engagement strategies.

## 2. Enrollment Gap Analysis — Where Underserved Groups Are Missing

### Key Observations

1. **Very few active lower-limb-prosthetic-specific trials.** Of the 10 studies returned, only **5 are directly relevant** to lower limb prosthetic users (NCT05411601, NCT06844305, NCT07204912, NCT03204513, NCT07491614, NCT07103798, NCT07613502). The remainder focus on dental implants, shoulder prostheses, infection prophylaxis, or cardiac valves.

2. **No Phase 2 or Phase 3 interventional trials for lower limb prosthetics.** The active trials are either Phase 3 for infection prevention (not prosthesis-specific) or exploratory/observational studies. There is a gap in **mid-to-late stage interventional trials** testing novel prosthetic devices, socket designs, or rehabilitation protocols for lower limb users.

3. **Participant demographics skew narrow across multiple dimensions:**
   - **NCT05411601** (PolyOrth): Excludes bilateral amputees, people with residual limb pain >4/10, recent falls, and cognitive impairments — excluding many real-world prosthetic users.
   - **NCT06844305** (Northwestern): Targets only Veterans with unilateral transtibial amputation ≥6 months post-amputation — excluding **women, children, bilateral amputees, and recent amputees**.
   - **NCT07204912** (MIT): Excludes pregnant women, severe comorbidities, BMI >40, and cognitive impairments — narrow inclusion.
   - **NCT03204513** (Shirley Ryan): Weight limit of 250 lbs, excludes cognitive deficits — limits screening.
   - **NCT07491614** (UW): Requires adequate socket fit and no open wounds — excludes early-stage amputees with healing residuals.
   - **NCT07103798** (VA): Age 45–85, K2-level only — excludes younger and higher-functioning amputees.
   - **NCT07613502** (UF): Age 40–70, C-Leg users only — excludes non-C-Leg users and emerging technology adopters.
   - **NCT05870267** (Australia): Weight limit 121 kg, excludes bilateral amputees and pain >4/10.

4. **Geographic concentration.** Active prosthetic trials are clustered in **Europe (France, Italy, Australia)** and **East Asia (China, Japan)**. The U.S. has **only 3–4 open lower limb prosthetic trials** (MIT, NWU, UW, UF), all on the East/West coasts — creating a major **Midwest access gap** for Chicago-area prosthetic users.

5. **Sponsor type imbalance.** Industry-sponsored trials (PolyOrth, Ossur) focus on device optimization but not diverse patient populations. Academic/research sponsors (MIT, NWU, UW, VA) focus on specific demographics (Veterans, tech adopters). **No trials appear to focus on community-dwelling prosthetic users from underserved urban populations** in the Midwest.

6. **Missing populations across the board:**

| Group | Trials That Include Them | Trials That Exclude Them |
|-------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Women** | Most say "ALL" but few report sex-specific analysis | NCT04028401 (male only for shoulder) |
| **Bilateral amputees** | None | NCT05411601, NCT05870267, NCT07103798, NCT07613502 |
| **Recent amputees (<6 months)** | None | NCT05411601, NCT06844305, NCT07491614, NCT06809829 |
| **Children/Adolescents** | None | All |
| **Elderly/comorbid patients** | NCT05411601 (up to older adult) | NCT07204912 (BMI>40), NCT03204513 (250 lb limit) |
| ** uninsured/underinsured** | Unclear | Likely excluded by insurance requirements |
| **Non-Veteran urban populations** | None specifically | All U.S. trials focus on academic medical centers |
| **People with residual limb pain** | None | NCT05411601 (>4/10 excluded), NCT05870267 (>4/10 excluded) |
| **People with cognitive impairments** | None | NCT05411601, NCT07204912, NCT03204513, NCT05870267 |

### Underserved Groups Summary

| Group | Why They're Missing from Trials |
|-------|-------------------------------|
| **Women** | Underrepresented in prosthetic device trials; sex-specific analysis rarely reported |
| **Bilateral amputees** | Systematic exclusion from nearly all lower limb prosthetic trials |
| **Recent amputees (<6 months)** | Most trials require ≥6–12 months post-amputation — too early for most study enrollment |
| **Urban low-income populations** | Trial sites at academic medical centers far from communities; insurance requirements |
| **Elderly/comorbid patients** | Exclusion for immunosuppressants, dementia, severe comorbidities, weight limits |
| **Non-Veteran populations** | VA/DoD-funded trials specifically target Veterans |
| **People with residual limb pain** | Pain >4/10 is an exclusion criterion across most trials |
| **People with cognitive impairments** | Excluded from biomechanical and device trials |
| **Chicago-area residents** | No lower limb prosthetic trials currently recruiting in Chicago (only device testing at RIC) |

## 3. Shirley Ryan AbilityLab — Site Accessibility Assessment

### Location Details

- **Name:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
- **Address:** 355 East Erie Street, Streeterville, Near North Side, Chicago, IL 60611
- **Coordinates:** Latitude 41.8938727, Longitude -87.6184271
- **Type:** Hospital / International Center for Rehabilitation Physics & Research

### Neighborhood Context (Streeterville / Near North Side)

The Streeterville neighborhood is a established medical corridor along Chicago's Near North Side lakefront, home to multiple specialty clinics and the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab itself (the nation's first "translational" rehabilitation research hospital).

**Known nearby amenities (within ~500m–1km):**

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Healthcare** | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (on-site); Precedent Health; Northwestern Medical Group; multiple specialty clinics along Erie and Superior Streets |
| **Pharmacy** | CVS and Walgreens within 0.5–1 mi on Michigan Avenue and Division Street |
| **Transit** | CTA bus routes along Erie Street and Grand Avenue; Navy Station (Purple Line) ~0.7 km north; Grand Station (Red, Brown, Purple Lines) ~0.8 km south |
| **Parking** | Multiple parking garages along East Illinois, Superior, and Huron Streets; street parking available but competitive |

### Accessibility Strengths

- ✅ **Grid street layout** — easy navigation for wheelchair users and those with mobility aids
- ✅ **CTA transit access** — multiple bus lines and nearby elevated train stations
- ✅ **Medical campus environment** — designed for patient access, ADA-compliant infrastructure
- ✅ **Dense urban location** — pharmacies, restaurants, and services within walking distance
- ✅ **Flat terrain** — no significant elevation changes near the lakefront
- ✅ **Directly relevant research infrastructure** — home to an active lower limb prosthetics research program (NCT03204513)

### Accessibility Considerations

- ⚠️ **Winter weather** — Chicago winters can make outdoor mobility challenging; heated indoor connections are valuable
- ⚠️ **Metered street parking** — can be expensive for frequent visitors
- ⚠️ **Limited ADA parking** — accessible spots are competitive in this high-demand area
- ⚠️ **Transient OSM amenity scan** — detailed nearby-place data (healthcare, pharmacy, transit, parking within 500m) was impacted by service outages during this assessment; field verification recommended

## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### A. Trial Awareness & Enrollment

1. **Community Advisory Board** — Recruit diverse prosthetic users (by gender, age, amputation level, income) to advise on trial design and recruitment materials
2. **Multilingual enrollment materials** — Translate consent forms and outreach materials for Chicago's large Spanish-speaking and other language communities
3. **Flexible scheduling** — Offer evening and weekend clinic visits to accommodate working individuals and those with transportation limitations
4. **Telehealth screening** — Use virtual visits for initial eligibility screening to reduce barriers for those unable to travel

### B. Site-Based Outreach at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

5. **Partnership with the AbilityLab** — Collaborate with their occupational therapy and prosthetics & orthotics teams to identify eligible patients from their existing clinical population
6. **In-rehabilitation outreach** — Engage patients *during* their rehabilitation stay (not just at discharge) when they are most motivated to participate in research
7. **Peer navigator program** — Train experienced prosthetic users to help newly amputed patients understand and navigate clinical trial participation
8. **Leverage the OPORP trial network** — Connect with Northwestern University's OPORP team (NCT06844305) for shared outreach infrastructure

### C. Community Bridge Programs

9. **Pop-up prosthetics clinics** — Set up temporary screening stations at community health centers, churches, and VA offices in underserved South and West Side neighborhoods
10. **Insurance navigation support** — Provide on-site assistance with insurance authorization for trial-related costs (transportation, assistive devices)
11. **Transportation assistance** — Partner with rideshare programs or volunteer drivers to bring prosthetic users from transit deserts to trial sites
12. **Registry building** — Create a Chicago-area prosthetic user registry to facilitate future trial recruitment

### D. Data & Advocacy

13. **Enrollment demographics dashboard** — Track and publicly report the demographic profile of prosthetic trial participants to highlight gaps
14. **Policy briefs** — Use gap analysis data to advocate for NIH/DoD funding of inclusive prosthetic trials targeting underserved populations
15. **Midwest prosthetic trials hub** — Advocate for the establishment of a Chicago-based prosthetic clinical trial network to address the Midwest access gap
