## 4 · Summary of Key Findings

1. **Sponsor imbalance** — 73.5% academic, 13.7% industry, 9.7% federal. No FQHC or safety-net sponsors = no community outreach pipeline.
2. **Phase shortage** — 95% of studies are observational/unknown. Only 2.9% reach efficacy-phase testing. Digital divides exclude many users from registry-based screening.
3. **Seven underserved populations** are systematically excluded: low-income, non-English speakers, elderly 75+, rural residents, wheelchair users, women, and trauma amputees (non-VA).
4. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** is a world-class site with strong clinical credibility, good transit, and nearby amenities — but **parking is a major barrier** (paid only, $15–30/day) and the affluent Streeterville neighborhood may feel unwelcoming.
5. **No Chicago-area lower limb prosthetic trials** were identified in the current ClinicalTrials.gov dataset — creating a clear opportunity for Shirley Ryan to lead recruitment.
