## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: "Bridging the Gap" — Community Trial Navigators ($90K/yr)
| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | South/West Side residents face 1+ bus transfers; no culturally concordant recruitment |
| **Action** | Hire 4 bilingual (English/Spanish) community health workers; embed in South Side clinics |
| **Success Metric** | 30% increase in Hispanic/African American enrollment within 12 months |

### Strategy 2: "Prosthetic Supply Corridor" — Pop-Up DME in SRAL Lobby ($15–50K)
| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | No DME/prosthetic vendor within 500 m; users cannot drop in |
| **Action** | Partner with Hanger Clinic/Össur for weekly pop-up; permanent station if validated |
| **Success Metric** | 100+ walk-in contacts/month; 20% conversion to trial screening |

### Strategy 3: "Warm Hands, Warm Access" — Heated Waiting Area + Thermal Kits ($33K/yr)
| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Winter thermal discomfort (NCT07215442 evidenced this); wind chill <0°F near lake |
| **Action** | $25K one-time HVAC for heated lobby area; $8K/yr for prosthetic thermal kits (neoprene covers, hand warmers) |
| **Success Metric** | Winter dropout rate <10% (from historical ~35%) |

### Strategy 4: "South Side Transit Pipeline" — CTA Paratransit + Tele-Trial Model ($60K/yr)
| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 1+ bus transfer barrier for South/West Siders; tele-trial dropout ~25% |
| **Action** | CTA Access补贴 (paratransit); tele-trial with $50 hardware stipend (tablet + tablet mount) |
| **Success Metric** | 40% of enrollees from South/West Side zip codes |

### Strategy 5: "Global Equity Bridge" — MOUs with Colombian, Indian, Pakistani Sites ($50K start / $50K/yr)
| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 80 nations with zero trial access; Global South entirely absent |
| **Action** | Standardize <$500-cost protocol (smartphone + wearable); MOUs with 3 partner sites |
| **Success Metric** | 100+ Global South participants enrolled in 24 months |

### Strategy 6: "Mental Health & Acceptance" — PHQ-2 Screening + Monthly Peer Group ($20K/yr)
| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 30–50% of amputees experience depression; screening + acceptance protocols absent |
| **Action** | PHQ-2 at intake; monthly peer group (with Medrina psychiatry partner); motivational interviewing |
| **Success Metric** | PHQ-2 positive rate documented; 50% of participants engaged in peer group monthly |

### Strategy 7: "Women, Children & Gender-Diverse" — Equal-Platform Protocol ($35K start / $15K/yr)
| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 90%+ male skew; zero pediatric protocols; no gender-diverse criteria |
| **Action** | Equal-platform recruitment protocol; pediatric sub-study with Lurie Children's Hospital; gender-inclusive consent forms |
| **Success Metric** | 30% female enrollment; first pediatric prosthetic trial within 18 months |

**💰 Total Annual Operational Budget:** **~$315,000/yr** ($300K first year including one-time costs)

## 5. Summary & Recommended Next Steps

1. **Immediate (Month 1–3):** Launch Strategy 2 (pop-up DME corridor) + Strategy 3 (heated area) — lowest cost, highest local impact. Full budget: ~$48K first year.
2. **Short-term (Month 3–6):** Activate Strategy 1 (4 Community Navigators) + Strategy 6 (PHQ-2 screening). Budget: ~$110K.
3. **Medium-term (Month 6–12):** Roll out Strategy 4 (transit pipeline) + Strategy 7 (diversity protocol). Budget: ~$95K.
4. **Long-term (Month 12–24):** Establish Strategy 5 (Global Equity Bridge). Budget: ~$100K in year 2.
