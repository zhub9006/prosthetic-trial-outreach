## Executive Summary

A comprehensive assessment of lower limb prosthetic clinical trials on ClinicalTrials.gov reveals **systemic gaps in sponsor diversity, trial phases, geographic reach, and population eligibility**. Only **13.6% of trials are industry-sponsored** and **95% are observational, unknown-phase, or early-phase** — meaning almost no efficacy-testing infrastructure exists for prosthetic users. Seven underserved populations are systematically excluded across these trials.

The **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** (355 E Erie St, Chicago, IL 60611) emerges as a strong clinical anchor site (⭐⭐⭐⭐, 4/5), with excellent transit access (15+ CTA bus stops, 5 Divvy bike stations within 500m) and on-site healthcare, but paid parking and an affluent neighborhood context create access barriers. A total of **17 parking facilities**, **4 pharmacies**, **1 VA clinic**, **12+ transit platforms**, and **6 Divvy stations** are within the 500m radius.

The report proposes **8 outreach strategies** targeting the most critical gaps, with estimated annual costs ranging from $25K to $120K.

## Trial Enrollment Gaps by Sponsor & Phase

### Key Findings

1. **Sponsor Imbalance:** 73.5% of trials are academic/observational. Only 13.7% are industry-sponsored, meaning there is no commercial viability-driven pipeline for prosthetic devices in clinical trials. **Zero trials are sponsored by FQHCs, community health centers, or safety-net hospitals.**

2. **Phase Shortage:** Only 2.9% of all lower-limb prosthetic studies reach Phase I–IV efficacy testing. The overwhelming majority (95%) are observational, unknown-phase, or early-phase studies that don't test real-world prosthetic interventions.

3. **No Digital-Division Bridge:** Trial registries and consent forms universally require English proficiency and digital literacy, systematically excluding elderly, non-English-speaking, and low-literacy populations.

4. **Veteran Surplus vs. Civilian Gap:** Federal-sponsored trials (9.7%) are entirely VA/DOD-focused, meaning non-veteran prosthetic users have almost no federally sponsored trial options.

5. **Geographic Concentration:** Trials are concentrated in 5–10 academic medical centers (primarily in academic states). Rural and suburban populations are systematically excluded.

## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community Health Worker (CHW) Bridge Program

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Target Gap** | Low-income, non-English speakers, rural communities |
| **Description** | Train CHWs at FQHCs and community clinics to serve as trial navigators for prosthetic users. CHWs assist with eligibility screening, trial enrollment, transportation coordination, and follow-up. |
| **Partnership Targets** | 3–5 FQHCs in Chicago (e.g., Erie Family Health, Heartland Alliance, Access Community Health Network) |
| **Estimated Cost** | $80K–$120K/year (2–3 CHWs + training + materials) |
| **Timeline** | Months 1–6 for pilot |

### Strategy 2: Mobile Prosthetic Screening Clinics

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Target Gap** | Rural residents, wheelchair users, elderly, low-income |
| **Description** | Deploy mobile screening units to underserved communities for on-site prosthetic assessments and trial eligibility screening. Units equipped with basic prosthetic fitting tools, questionnaires, and telehealth links to trial sites. |
| **Estimated Cost** | $60K–$90K/year (vehicle lease, staffing, equipment) |
| **Timeline** | Months 3–9 for deployment |

### Strategy 3: Multicultural & Multilingual Outreach

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Target Gap** | Non-English speakers, immigrant communities |
| **Description** | Partner with faith-based organizations, cultural centers, and community media to disseminate trial information in Spanish, Polish, Mandarin, and other prevalent languages. Train bilingual outreach coordinators. |
| **Materials** | Translated consent forms, trial descriptions, and educational videos |
| **Estimated Cost** | $40K–$60K/year (translation, coordination, media buy) |
| **Timeline** | Months 2–5 |

### Strategy 4: Telehealth Eligibility Screening

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Target Gap** | Rural, wheelchair users, elderly, low-income, non-English speakers |
| **Description** | Offer remote eligibility screening via telehealth platforms. Patients complete initial assessments from home using phone/video visits, reducing travel burden. Integration with EHR for seamless referral. |
| **Platform** | Partner with existing telehealth infrastructure at AbilityLab |
| **Estimated Cost** | $30K–$50K/year (platform costs, staffing) |
| **Timeline** | Months 2–4 |

### Strategy 5: Peer Mentor Network

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|

| **Description** | Recruit and train diverse amputee mentors (varying ages, amputation types, K-levels, backgrounds) to serve as peer guides for prosthetic users navigating trial participation. Mentors provide emotional support, practical advice, and motivation. |
| **Estimated Cost** | $25K–$40K/year (mentor stipends, training, coordination) |
| **Timeline** | Months 2–6 |

| Component | Detail |
