## 2. Enrollment Trend Analysis

### Key Findings

1. **Heavily front-loaded with early-phase/feasibility studies.** Nearly 4 out of 5 studies (77.5%) are classified as "NA" phase or equivalent, meaning they are device-development or proof-of-concept trials rather than pivotal efficacy studies. Only 1 Phase 3 trial exists (DAICY — hip cement, not directly a prosthetic limb device). This suggests a pipeline gap.

2. **Small sample sizes dominate.** The largest prosthetic-specific trial enrolls 100 participants. Most trials enroll 12–25 participants, limiting generalizability.

3. **Geographic concentration.** VA facilities (Seattle, Minneapolis, New York) account for 4 trials; Northern Europe (Norway, Sweden, UK, France) for 4 trials; and Upper Midwest/Northeast US for 2 trials. The South, Southwest, and rural US are heavily underrepresented.

4. **Sponsor diversity imbalance.** Academic institutions dominate (57.5%), with only 12.5% federal/VA-sponsored trials despite Veterans being a major prosthetic user population.

## 3. Identified Gaps & Underserved Populations

### Population Gaps

| Underserved Group | Evidence of Gap | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Rural amputees** | Only 1 trial (NCT05884203) addresses rural access | Travel burden, limited trial participation |
| **Elderly (>65)** | Most trials cap enrollment at 65–70 | High amputation rates from vascular disease unstudied |
| **Bilateral amputees** | Exclusion criteria in nearly every trial | ~15–20% of amputees have no dedicated trials |
| **Non-veteran populations** | VA trials restrict to Veterans | Civilian amputees lack research pipeline |
| **Low-income / uninsured** | No financial inclusion strategies | Cost barriers to participation |
| **People with comorbidities** | Diabetes, PVD, obesity, neuropathy commonly excluded | Most vulnerable systematically excluded |
| **Pediatric / young adult** | Minimum age typically 18 | Young amputee population unstudied |

### Geographic Gaps

- **South & Southwest US** — zero trials
- **Rural Midwest & Mountain West** — 1 trial only
- **Global South** — no trials in Africa, South/Southeast Asia, or Latin America

### Trial Design Gaps

- **No Phase 3 pivotal trials** for most lower limb prosthetic devices
- **Few comparative effectiveness studies** beyond lab-based simulations
- **Limited community-based outcome measures**
- **Minimal health equity data collection**
- **Short follow-up periods**

## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Address Trial Enrollment Gaps

- **Co-recruit from AbilityLab patients** — streamline referral pathway from clinical encounters to trial screening
- **Develop a mobile outreach unit** for rural/underserved areas (leveraging NCT05884203 caregiver-assisted limb scanning model)
- **Create a Chicago metro registry** of eligible prosthetic users for efficient enrollment
- **Expand eligibility criteria** to include elderly (>65), bilateral amputees, and people with comorbidities

### Strategy 2: Leverage the AbilityLab as a Hub

**Site Advantages:**
- World-class rehab facility with existing prosthetic user population
- 12+ CTA bus stops within 500m
- Nearby hospital (Prentice/Northwestern)
- 24/7 pharmacy within walking distance
- Accessible parking

**Recommended Hub Activities:**
- Monthly **"Prosthetic Device Demo Days"** with industry partners
- **Community peer support groups** for amputees
- **Prosthetic fit clinics** using socket-fit testing technology
- Continuing education for prosthetists and PTs

### Strategy 3: Fill Geographic & Demographic Gaps

- **Partner with VA facilities** for Veteran co-enrollment
- **Establish satellite screening sites** in South/Southwest US (Atlanta, Houston, Phoenix)
- **Spanish-language outreach materials** for Chicago's Hispanic amputee community
- **Economic inclusion measures:** transportation stipends, parking reimbursement, flexible visit schedules

### Strategy 4: Advance the Trial Pipeline

- **Advocate for Phase 3 pivotal trials** — 77.5% of current studies are early-phase
- **Push for comparative effectiveness designs** in real-world community settings
- **Mandate health equity data collection** (race, ethnicity, income, insurance, geography)
- **Build a multi-site consortium** to achieve adequate sample sizes across diverse populations

### Strategy 5: Community Engagement Calendar (First 6 Months)

## Summary

The lower limb prosthetic trial landscape is **innovative but narrow**: dominated by small, early-phase, academic studies clustered in a few geographic regions, with systemic exclusion of rural, elderly, bilateral, and comorbid populations. The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is an **excellent candidate community outreach site** — world-class rehabilitation infrastructure, strong transit access, nearby healthcare and pharmacy services, and accessible parking. The proposed strategies aim to close enrollment gaps, broaden demographic reach, and establish the AbilityLab as a recognized center for prosthetic community engagement and innovation.
