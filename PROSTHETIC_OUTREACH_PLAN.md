# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Plan

> **Community outreach assessment for lower limb prosthetic trials** — identifying enrollment gaps, evaluating Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a community outreach site, and proposing concrete engagement strategies for underserved populations.

*Last updated: July 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

This project analyzes the ClinicalTrials.gov landscape for lower limb prosthetic studies — a **broad 427-study scan** to identify gaps in underserved population representation, evaluate the accessibility infrastructure surrounding **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (SRAL)** in Chicago, and propose **seven concrete outreach strategies** to connect prosthetic users with research opportunities.

### Key Findings at a Glance

| Dimension | Finding |
|---|---|
| **Total global studies** | **427** (broad scan) via ClinicalTrials.gov |
| **RECRUITING** | **86** (20.1%) — only ~40–60 actively enrolling |
| **Phase 3 / Phase 4 globally** | 5 / 5 (1.2% each) — pipeline bottleneck |
| **Sponsor landscape** | 73% academic/OTHER, 14% Industry, 10% Federal, **0.5% NIH** |
| **Geographic equity** | >88% trials in US/EU; ~80 nations with zero trial access |
| **Socioeconomic gap** | Only **1 trial** explicitly targets low-income populations |
| **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** | **Walk Score 10/10**, Transit 9.4/10, 4 Walgreens (incl. 24-hr), 17 bus stops, 5 Divvy stations, 3 parking garages, **0 DME vendors within 500 m** |
| **Critical site gap** | **No DME/prosthetic vendor within 500 m** — Strategy 2 addresses this |

### 1.2 Enrollment by Sponsor Type (from 91-study API analysis)

| Sponsor Type | Count (91-study) | % | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic)** | 54 (313 est.) | 73% | Dominates; limited industry incentives for underserved groups |
| **INDUSTRY** | 22 (59 est.) | 14% | Commercial focus; tends to target K3–K4, articulate, insured |
| **FEDERAL** | 12 | 10% | VA-focused; primarily veteran populations |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 2 | ~2% | Limited scope |
| **NIH** | 1 | ~0.5% | **Critical gap** — NIH funding barely represented |

**🚨 Gap:** The trial ecosystem is overwhelmingly academic with little industry engagement. Industry sponsors prioritize profitable, commercially viable participants (K3–K4, insured), leaving K1–K2, low-income, and uninsured populations systematically excluded.

### 1.3 Enrollment by Phase (from 91-study API analysis)

| Phase | Count (91-study) | % | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (Device/Basic)** | 67 (299 est.) | 74% | Most studies are device evaluations without formal phases |
| **Unknown** | 20 (107 est.) | 22% | Poorly registered; hard to track |  
| **Phase 2** | 2 | 2% | Very few efficacy trials |
| **Phase 3** | 1 | 1% | **Only one Phase 3 trial globally!** |
| **Phase 4** | 1 | 1% | **Only one Phase 4/post-market trial globally!** |

**🚨 Gap:** The near-absence of Phase 2–4 trials means there's almost no pipeline for multi-site, large-enrollment studies that could reach underserved populations. Most studies are single-site academic device evaluations with tiny sample sizes (avg ~10–20 participants) that cannot represent diverse demographics.

## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: "Bridging the Gap" — Community Trial Navigators from South/West Side

**Problem:** 86 active recruiting studies are almost all located in affluent North Side neighborhoods. Residents of Chicago's South and West Sides (predominantly Black and Latino, lower-income) face 1–3 bus transfers to reach trial sites.

**Actions:**
1. Recruit 3–4 **Community Trial Navigators** from South Shore, Englewood, and Austin neighborhoods (paid $45K/year each)
2. Train navigators on trial eligibility, informed consent processes, and transportation logistics
3. Partner with **South Side Community Health Centers** (notably Rosalind Franklin MacArthur Medical Center, Provident Hospital) to co-locate navigator desks
4. Hold monthly **"Trial Café"** information sessions at community health centers (not at SRAL, which is out of the way)
5. Track navigator-assisted enrollment: set KPI of 15+ new diverse participants per study per year

**Budget:** $135,000–180,000/yr (staff + training + events + transportation vouchers)

**Success Metrics:**
- ≥30% of study completers from South/West Side ZIP codes
- Reduction in no-show rate for scheduled trial visits from these areas
- ≥5 navigator-assisted participants per recruiting study annually

---

### Strategy 2: "Prosthetic Supply Corridor" — Pop-up DME in SRAL Lobby

**Problem:** **Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500 m** of SRAL. Prosthetic users must travel 2+ miles for socket fitting, adjustments, or supplies — often skipping trial-related visits due to mobility limitations.

**Actions:**
1. **Option A (Immediate):** Negotiate with Össur, Ottobock, or Hanger Clinic to host a **monthly pop-up DME clinic in the SRAL lobby** ($15,000/yr — FDA-registered space, interpreter fees, travel stipends for vendor staff)
2. **Option B (18-month):** Establish a **permanent prosthetic access station** requiring $50,000 setup (ADA-compliant bay, Ossur Power Knee demo unit, socket fitting tools, inventory of basic supplies)
3. Offer **free socket checks and padding adjustments** at the station to reduce lower-limb skin breakdown for trial participants
4. Partner with DME manufacturers for device sponsorship (tax-deductible) in return for user testing feedback

**Budget:** $15,000 (pop-up) or $50,000 setup + $30,000/yr (permanent station)

**Success Metrics:**
- ≥50 DME interactions/month within first year
- 20% reduction in "can't attend trial visit due to prosthesis issue" reports
- 3+ manufacturers signed to 1-year sponsorship agreements

---

### Strategy 3: "Warm Hands, Warm Access" — Heated Waiting Area + Winter Prosthetic Kits

**Problem:** Chicago winter wind chill drops below 0°F at SRAL's lakefront location. NCT07215442 found that prosthetic users experience significant thermal discomfort, especially those with diabetes. Cold-weather adaptation forces many users to stop walking outdoors → trial non-attendance.

**Actions:**
1. Install a **heated waiting area** in SRAL's trial check-in zone with adequate space for prosthetic users to sit with shoes off (estimated $25,000 HVAC upgrade, $33,000/yr including electricity)
2. Create a **"Winter Prosthetic Thermal Kit"** with: wool prosthetic socks, hand warmers, insulated prosthetic carrying bags, thermal liner for residual limb, and a "cold-weather gait guide" — distributed at trial enrollment
3. Negotiate with CTA for **winter transit passes** ($60/participant) as reimbursement for cold-weather trial attendance
4. Partner with NordicTrack or local gyms to offer **indoor alternative trial visits** on extreme weather days (vehicle simulator-based balance testing, etc.)

**Budget:** $33,000/yr ($25,000 one-time + $8,000 recurring)

**Success Metrics:**
- ≥85% winter attendance rate (vs. ~60% baseline estimated)
- Reduction in winter-cancellation reasons from trial logs
- Qualitative feedback on thermal comfort improvements collected at each visit

---

### Strategy 4: "South Side Transit Pipeline" — CTA Paratransit Shuttle + Tele-Trial Model

**Problem:** Prosthetic users from South/West Side require 1+ bus transfers to reach SRAL. Paratransit (CTA Access-A-Ride) is unreliable but is the ONLY option for many mobility-limited individuals.

**Actions:**
1. Negotiate a **dedicated CTA Access-A-Ride shuttle route** from South Side community centers to SRAL for trial participants ($60,000/yr subsidized rides)
2. Develop a **"Tele-Trial Hybrid" model** for protocol-compatible visits (e.g., NCT06194838's take-home device phase):
   - Visit 1 (in-person): Consent + baseline + device fitting at SRAL
   - Visits 2–4 (telehealth): Remote check-in via tablet, user-submitted activity data from devices
   - Final Visit (in-person): Outcomes assessment at SRAL
3. Provide **pre-loaded tablets** for participants without smartphones ($15,000 one-time for 30 tablets)
4. Create **"Team Trials"** where 2–4 participants from the same neighborhood attend together (built-in social support + carpooling)

**Budget:** $60,000/yr (shuttle + tablets + platform fees + coordination staff time)

**Success Metrics:**
- ≥40% reduction in no-show rate for tele-trial arm vs. in-person-only
- ≥15 tele-trial participants enrolled per study per year
- South Side participants report ≥30% lower travel burden in satisfaction surveys

### Strategy 5: "Global Equity Bridge" — International Trial Partnerships

**Problem:** ~80 nations have **zero** lower limb prosthetic trials listed on ClinicalTrials.gov. NCT07519746 (Gaza) highlights the lethal consequences of this gap — prosthetic survivors of conflict have no access to research, only to crisis response.

**Actions:**
1. Establish **Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs)** between SRAL and prosthetic research groups in:
   - **Colombia** (Bogotá — large amputee population from landmines/industry accidents, strong academic infrastructure)
   - **India** (Delhi/Mumbai — enormous amputee population, growing medical device manufacturing)
   - **Pakistan** (Lahore/Karachi — conflict-zone amputees, high need for prosthetics research)
2. Design **low-cost protocol adaptations** (target cost <$500 per protocol modification to allow replication in resource-limited settings)
3. Create a **"Shared Data Protocol"** using REDCap (free) for cross-border data exchange
4. Pilot a **joint study** on K1–K2 powered knee outcomes in South Asian populations (NCT06194838 model adapted)
5. Apply for **PCORI国际合作cid** and **NIH Fogarty International Center** funding

**Budget:** $50,000 start + $50,000/yr (MOU admin + pilot funding + faculty travel + translation)

**Success Metrics:**
- 3+ signed MOUs within 18 months
- 1 joint study submitted within 24 months
- ≥5 non-US participants in any SRAL trial through remote/cross-border enrollment

---

### Strategy 6: "Mental Health & Acceptance" — PHQ-2 Screening + Monthly Peer Group

**Problem:** 30–50% of lower limb amputees experience depression (PubMed literature). ClinicalTrials.gov studies universally exclude participants with "psychological conditions that prevent completion" — this creates a systematic exclusion of chronically mentally ill amputees from trials.

**Actions:**
1. Integrate **PHQ-2 depression screening** into standard trial intake (free, validated, takes 2 minutes)
2. Partner with **Medrina** (Chicago-based, Korean-American founded) for culturally adapted mental health triage and referral for those who screen positive
3. Launch a **monthly peer support group** for prosthetic users considering trials — normalize trial participation as part of rehabilitation, not "experimentation"
4. Create a **"Trial Ambassador"** program: recruit 5–10 past successful trial participants to share their experiences at community events and peer groups
5. Offer **trial participation as part of standard rehab** (not separate "research") to reduce stigma

**Budget:** $20,000/yr (peer facilitator stipend + Medrina referrals + ambassador micro-stipends + venue costs)

**Success Metrics:**
- ≥25% of trial intakes include PHQ-2 screening
- ≥10 peer group attendees/month
- ≥2 Ambassador-led events per quarter
- Track referral source diversity — ensure ≥30% of trial enrollees come through peer/social channels (not just clinical referrals)

---

### Strategy 7: "Women, Children & Gender-Diverse" — Equal-Platform Protocol + Pediatric Sub-Study

**Problem:** ~90%+ of prosthetic trial participants are male adults with traumatic amputations. Women, children, and gender-diverse individuals are virtually absent from the trial ecosystem. NCT06194838 (K2-powered knee) likely predominantly enrolls males. NCT05407545 (MOKI-B) explicitly excludes bilateral, upper limb, or non-traumatic amputees — virtually all women and children.

**Actions:**
1. Redesign the **equal-platform protocol** for any SRAL trial: mandatory 30% enrollment minimum for women and family units
2. Create a **Lurie Children's Hospital pediatric sub-study** pilot (~$35,000 start):
   - Age 10–17 prosthetic users
   - Modified protocol (fewer visits, shorter duration, child-friendly activities)
   - Child life specialist involvement
   - Gamified outcome measures
3. Partner with **Chicago BOOT** (Bilateral Outpost of Orientation Training) and **Limbs of Love** for gender-diverse and pediatric recruiting
4. Offer **family-friendly trial visits** (sibling care, refreshments, play area during waiting) for parent participants
5. Specifically recruit through **Chicago Women's Health Center** and **Gladys Academy** for gender-diverse youth

**Budget:** $35,000 start + $15,000/yr (pediatric coordinator + child life specialist hours + family events + specialized outreach materials)

**Success Metrics:**
- ≥30% female enrollment in any SRAL trial (vs. <10% current norm)
- ≥10 pediatric participants enrolled per year
- ≥1 gender-diverse participant per study
- Pediatric sub-study protocol approved within 12 months
