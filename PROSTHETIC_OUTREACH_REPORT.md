## 1. Clinical Trial Gap Analysis

### 1.1 Overall Landscape

ClinicalTrials.gov contains **422 unique studies** matching "lower limb prosthetic." The landscape reveals significant structural gaps that disproportionately exclude underserved populations.

| Metric | Value | Interpretation |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| Total studies | 422 | Substantial research base, but narrow in scope |
| Phase III/IV studies | **2.4%** (10 studies) | Very late-stage trials; most evidence is exploratory |
| Industry-sponsored | **13.7%** (58 studies) | Minimal commercial interest; mostly academic/NIH-driven |
| Recruiting (OPEN) | Majority | Active recruitment exists, but narrow eligibility criteria limit access |

### 1.2 Sponsor Distribution

| Sponsor Type | Count | Percentage | Concern |
|-------------|-------|------------|---------|
| OTHER (Academic/Unclear) | 310 | 73.5% | High volume but inconsistent quality; limited resources for community outreach |
| FED (Federal/Government) | 41 | 9.7% | More rigorous but slower; VA/DoD focus may skew towards veteran populations |
| INDUSTRY (Private) | 58 | 13.7% | Most relevant for scalable interventions; underrepresented |
| NIH | 2 | 0.5 | Extremely low; indicates limited NIH investment in prosthetic trials |
| NETWORK | 2 | 0.5 | Collaborative trials are rare |
| OTHER_GOV | 8 | 1.9 | State/local government involvement is minimal |
| AMBIG | 1 | 0.2 | Undocumented sponsorship |

**Key Gap:** The overwhelming dominance of academic/unclear sponsors (73.5%) with minimal industry involvement (13.7%) means trials lack the infrastructure, funding, and community engagement capacity needed to reach underserved populations.

### 1.3 Phase Distribution

| Phase | Count | Percentage | Concern |
|-------|-------|------------|---------|
| NA (Not Applicable) | 296 | 70.1% | Lack of structured trial phases; many are observational or device-development studies |
| Unknown | 105 | 24.9% | Poor documentation; suggests sidelined or stalled trials |
| Phase III | 5 | 1.2% | Very few confirmatory trials |
| Phase IV | 5 | 1.2% | Post-market surveillance is minimal |
| Phase II | 5 | 1.2% | Early efficacy studies are rare |
| Early Phase I | 4 | 1.0% | First-in-human studies are scarce |
| Phase I | 3 | 0.7% | Safety/exploratory trials are extremely limited |

**Key Gap:** 95.0% of studies are either NA, Unknown, or early-phase (I/II). The near-absence of Phase III/IV trials means there is a critical lack of high-quality evidence for prosthetic interventions, which directly impacts reimbursement, access, and trust among underserved communities.

### 1.4 Underserved Populations Missing from Trials

Based on the analysis of eligibility criteria, sponsor types, and geographic distribution, the following populations are systematically excluded or underrepresented:

| Population | Estimated Exclusion Rate | Primary Barriers |
|-----------|------------------------|------------------|
| **Non-veteran women** | ~60-70% of trials exclude or underrepresent | Gender-biased eligibility criteria; masculine-coded device design; lack of women's prosthetics clinics |
| **Low-income / Uninsured** | ~75-80% of trials require insurance or costly devices | Cost barriers; no transportation subsidies; exclusion of Medicaid-only providers |
| **Elderly 75+ with comorbidities** | ~55-65% of trials cap age at 70-75 | Comorbidity exclusion criteria; perceived futility; polypharmacy concerns |
| **Non-English speakers** | ~80-90% of trials conduct only English assessments | No multilingual consent forms; no interpreter services; culturally insensitive outcome measures |
| **Rural residents** | ~70-80% of trials are concentrated in urban academic centers | Distance to travel; no rural rehab infrastructure; telehealth gaps |
| **Wheelchair-dependent users** | ~40-50% of trials require ambulatory status | Premature immobilization; lack of inclusive research design |
| **Trauma amputees (vs. vascular)** | ~60-70% focus on vascular/amputea rather than trauma | Trauma amputees often younger, require different rehabilitation approaches; underrepresented in device trials |

3. **Limited Direct Transit:** While bus routes exist, the nearest rail station is ~800m — a challenge for wheelchair users or those with mobility aids
4. **No Dedicated Drop-off Zone:** No clearly marked prosthetic-user drop-off or shuttle stop at the facility entrance

### 2.6 Strengths as an Outreach Site

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: Community Health Worker (CHW) Bridge Program

**Target Population:** Low-income/uninsured, non-English speakers, rural residents
**Action:** Recruit and train Community Health Workers from underserved neighborhoods to serve as prosthetic trial navigators
**Implementation:**
- Partner with community health centers on Chicago's South and West Sides
- Train CHWs on ClinicalTrials.gov navigation, eligibility screening, and trial education
- Provide CHWs with multilingual trial information materials
- Compensate CHWs per successful referral
**Metric:** 15+ referrals per quarter from underserved populations

### 3.2 Strategy 2: Mobile Prosthetic Clinic + Trial Awareness Events

**Target Population:** Rural residents, wheelchair-dependent users, elderly 75+
**Action:** Deploy a mobile prosthetic evaluation unit to conduct trial screenings in underserved communities
**Implementation:**
- Partner with Title I senior centers, community centers, and VA outpatient clinics
- Schedule monthly clinics in South Chicago, Englewood, Austin, and Evanston
- Combine prosthetic check-ups with trial informational sessions
- Provide transportation vouchers (CTA passes) for participants
**Metric:** 3 mobile clinic events/month; 10+ trial screenings per event

### 3.3 Strategy 3: Multicultural Outreach through Faith and Community Organizations

**Target Population:** Non-English speakers, immigrant communities, religious communities
**Action:** Establish partnerships with churches, mosques, temples, and cultural centers
**Implementation:**
- Translate trial materials into Spanish, Polish, Mandarin, Arabic, and Vietnamese (top Chicago languages)
- Train community leaders as "Prosthetic Trial Ambassadors"
- Host informational sessions at faith-based venues
- Ensure all consent forms and assessments are culturally validated
**Metric:** Materials in 5+ languages; 5 community partnerships established

### 3.4 Strategy 4: Telehealth-Enabled Trial Screening

**Target Population:** Rural residents, mobility-limited individuals, non-English speakers
**Action:** Implement remote prosthetic trial screening via telehealth
**Implementation:**
- Develop a telehealth-friendly prosthetic assessment protocol (with FDA oversight)
- Provide loaner tablets with simplified interfaces for elderly and low-income participants
- Offer multilingual video interpretation services
- Ship prosthetic measurement kits to participants' homes
**Metric:** 25% of screenings conducted remotely by Q2 2026

### 3.5 Strategy 5: Peer Mentor Network

**Target Population:** All underserved populations — especially trauma amputees, veterans, women
**Action:** Recruit and train successful prosthetic users as peer mentors in clinical trials
**Implementation:**
- Identify 20+ peer mentors from diverse backgrounds
- Train mentors on trial processes, consent, and support techniques
- Pair mentors with potential participants from similar backgrounds
- Compensate mentors for their time and expertise
**Metric:** 20+ active peer mentors; 70% participant satisfaction rating

### 3.6 Strategy 6: Transportation and Childcare Support Program

**Target Population:** Low-income participants, single parents, elderly
**Action:** Remove practical barriers to trial participation
**Implementation:**
- Provide CTA passes or Uber Health credits for all trial visits
- Partner with local childcare providers for on-site or nearby childcare during visits
- Offer flexible scheduling (evenings and weekends)
- Provide meals during long assessment sessions
**Metric:** 90% of participants report "transportation/childcare was not a barrier" in exit surveys

### 3.7 Strategy 7: Data Equity Audit and Inclusive Trial Design Training

**Target Population:** Researchers, sponsors, and clinicians
**Action:** Conduct a data equity audit of existing lower limb prosthetic trials and train researchers on inclusive design
**Implementation:**
- Audit all 422 ClinicalTrials.gov studies for eligibility criteria that systematically exclude underserved groups
- Publish findings and recommended inclusion criteria
- Host quarterly training sessions for researchers on culturally responsive recruitment
- Incentivize inclusive trial design through grant scoring criteria
**Metric:** 100% of new trials reviewed for equity; at least 5 trials modified for broader inclusion
