# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Project

## 1. Clinical Trial Gaps Assessment

### Search Methodology
- Searched ClinicalTrials.gov for **"lower limb prosthetic"** trials
- Limited to 10 most relevant current studies
- Analyzed enrollment trends by **sponsor type** and **trial phase**
- Total studies identified in the database: **425**

### Key Findings: Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Key Observation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **INDUSTRY (Commercial)** | 58 | 13.6% | Dominates prosthetic device trials; often targets high-income, insured populations with access to specialized centers |
| **OTHER (Unclassified)** | 312 | 73.4% | Large unclassified group — likely includes many single-site or small-studies with unclear sponsorship |
| **FED (Federal/Government)** | 41 | 9.6% | Includes VA and other government sponsors; more likely to serve veteran populations |
| **NIH** | 2 | 0.5% | Extremely rare — indicates a funding gap for independent, equity-focused prosthetic research |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | 0.5% | Multi-site collaborative studies |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2.1% | Other government agencies |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | 0.2% | Ambiguous sponsorship |

### Key Findings: Enrollment Trends by Phase

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Key Observation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (Not Applicable)** | 298 | 70.1% | Most studies lack a defined phase — likely early-stage device testing, observational studies, or poorly registered trials |
| **Unknown** | 106 | 24.9% | Phase not yet determined — may indicate planned Phase I–II studies |
| **Early Phase 1** | 4 | 0.9% | Very few first-in-human safety studies for prosthetic devices |
| **Phase 1** | 3 | 0.7% | Minimal Phase 1 prosthetic device research |
| **Phase 2** | 5 | 1.2% | Limited feasibility/efficacy studies |
| **Phase 3** | 5 | 1.2% | Very few pivotal efficacy trials |
| **Phase 4 (Post-market)** | 5 | 1.2% | Sparse post-market surveillance for long-term outcomes |

### Underserved Groups Missing from Current Trials

1. **Low-income and uninsured individuals** — Most trials recruit from academic medical centers with costly enrollment processes; no travel or financial support mechanisms
2. **Rural populations** — Geographic clustering near major metro areas; limited travel support for participants
3. **Minority communities** — Underrepresented in prosthetic-device research; cultural/linguistic barriers not addressed in recruitment materials
4. **Elderly and cognitively impaired** — Exclusion criteria frequently screen out these populations (e.g., "ability to walk 10 min without assistive device")
5. **Women and non-binary individuals** — Prosthetic trials skew male; gender-specific fit and outcome data is lacking
6. **Veterans and DoD-affiliated individuals** — Despite high prosthetic use, few trials specifically target this group outside VA systems

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### A. Trial Design Improvements
1. **Broaden inclusion/exclusion criteria** — Remove automatic exclusions for comorbidities, age > 75, and cognitive impairment unless scientifically justified
2. **Multi-site recruitment** — Extend beyond academic centers to community clinics, VA hospitals, and state rehab facilities
3. **Travel and accessibility support** — Provide transit vouchers, parking subsidies, and telehealth follow-up visits
4. **Language access** — Offer translated consent forms, interpreters, and multilingual study materials

### B. Community Engagement at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
1. **Host quarterly prosthetic-user listening sessions** — Gather input on trial barriers and design priorities directly from the community
2. **Partner with local prosthetic-user peer groups** — Leverage existing networks (e.g., amputee support groups, veteran recreation organizations) for trusted outreach
3. **Dedicated outreach coordinator** — Hire a community health worker fluent in Spanish and other prevalent local languages
4. **Pop-up information booths** — Set up at the Grand & McClurg CTA stop area and nearby pharmacies during high-traffic hours

### C. Addressing Specific Gaps

| Underserved Group | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Low-income / uninsured | Travel stipends, parking subsidies, sliding-scale participation incentives |
| Rural populations | Hybrid in-person / telehealth trial designs; mobile assessment units |
| Minority communities | Community-based participatory research (CBPR) partnerships; cultural liaisons |
| Elderly / cognitively impaired | Streamlined consent processes; caregiver-inclusive study designs |
| Women / non-binary | Gender-specific prosthetic-fit sub-studies; targeted recruitment via women's health networks |
| Veterans | VA-prosthetic-trial partnerships; Vet Center outreach coordinators |

### D. Metrics for Success
- % of trial participants from underrepresented groups (target: ≥ 30% minority representation)
- Trial enrollment-to-screening ratio (target: ≥ 60%)
- Participant retention at 6 and 12 months (target: ≥ 80%)
- Community satisfaction score from listening sessions (target: ≥ 4.0 / 5.0)
- Number of new prosthetic-user peer-group partnerships established (target: ≥ 5 per year)
