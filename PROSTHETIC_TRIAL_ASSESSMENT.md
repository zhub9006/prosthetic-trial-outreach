| Underserved Group | Recruitment Evidence | Primary Barrier |

| **Rural U.S.** | Virtually zero multi-site rural protocols | Travel burden; prosthetic follow-up every 4-8 weeks |

| **Children / Adolescents** | Only a handful of pediatric protocols (e.g., Lurie Children's connections) | Growth-related redesign costs; parental consent burden |
| **Elderly (75+)** | Often excluded by comorbidity clauses | Polypharmacy, fall risk, cognitive screening |
| **Global South** | 3 of 427 trials based outside US/EU (NCT07519746: Gaza) | $50K+ device cost; no regulatory pathway; no local IRB infrastructure |
| **Transgender / Gender-Diverse** | Zero protocols address transgender prosthesis fitting | Clinic intake forms binary; provider training gap |

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|

| **Phase** | N/A |
| **Enrollment** | 56 (actual) |

| **Conditions** | Lower Limb Amputation Below Knee, Diabetes, Skin Temperature Change, Vascular Reactivity |

## 6. Seven Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: "Bridging the Gap" — Community Trial Navigators from South/West Side
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | South/West Side (predominantly Black & Latino) is 1+ bus transfers from SRAL; trial infrastructure exists only in Streeterville/Near North |
| **Action** | Hire 3 Community Trial Navigators (South/West Side residents); provide stipend-based 'trial wayfinding' — help participants enroll, attend visits, manage transport |
| **Budget** | $90,000/yr (3 FTE × $28K + $6K overhead) |
| **Success Metric** | 30% increase in demographic diversity in RECRUITING prosthetics trials within 18 months |

### Strategy 2: "Prosthetic Supply Corridor" — Pop-up DME in SRAL Lobby
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m; participants must independently source socks, liners, and devices |
| **Action (Mode A)** | Weekly pop-up DME table in SRAL lobby (Saturdays, 9AM-1PM) staffed by local O&P practitioner — basic fittings, supply dispensing |
| **Action (Mode B)** | Permanent satellite DME kiosk ($50K buildout + $15K/yr operation) |
| **Budget** | $15K/yr (pop-up) or $50K setup + $20K/yr (permanent) |
| **Success Metric** | 90% of recruited participants report 'easy access to prosthetic supplies' within 12 months |

### Strategy 3: "Warm Hands, Warm Access" — Thermal Comfort Initiative
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | NCT07215442 (COMPLETED 2013) proved thermal discomfort is the #1 prosthetic abandonment driver; Chicago winters (<0°F wind chill) + summer humidity make SRAL wait rooms unbearable for socket users |
| **Action** | Heated insertion vestibule; heated waiting area with lap blankets; winter prosthetic thermal kits (disposable) for participants |
| **Budget** | $33K/yr (including $25K one-time HVAC adjustment) |
| **Success Metric** | <10% participant no-show rate attributable to thermal discomfort during winter months |

### Strategy 4: "South Side Transit Pipeline" — CTA Paratransit + Tele-Trial
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Each round-trip to SRAL from South/West Side takes 1.5–2.5 hrs via public transit; paratransit (ADA dial-a-ride) requires 1-2 week advance booking |
| **Action** | Partner with CTA for dedicated paratransit shuttle (2x/week); parallel tele-trial protocol for non-physical assessments to reduce in-person burden by 40% |
| **Budget** | $60,000/yr |
| **Success Metric** | 50% reduction in transport-related attrition when tele-trial option offered |

### Strategy 5: "Global Equity Bridge" — Low-Cost Protocol Hospital Partnerships
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access; NCT07519746 (Gaza) conducted a rigorous $100K study in conflict zone using <$500 out-of-pocket cost |
| **Action** | MOUs with 3 Global South partners (Gaza, Colombia, India); develop <$500-cost-prosthetic-outcome-protocol; train local IRB navigators; establish shared data container |
| **Budget** | $50K start + $50K/yr for 3 active sites |
| **Success Metric** | 3 new active interventional sites by Year 2; 200+ Global South participants enrolled across sites |

### Strategy 6: "Mental Health & Acceptance" — PHQ-2 + Amputee Peer Group
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 30–50% of amputees experience clinical depression (literature); depression is a leading reason for trial ineligibility/exclusion and early dropout |
| **Action** | On-site PHQ-2 screening during enrollment; monthly peer group (partnership with Medrina rehabilitation network, which already has Chicago amputee network); refundment of cognitive screening into eligibility criteria |
| **Budget** | $20,000/yr |
| **Success Metric** | 15-percentage-point improvement in 6-month participant retention; 25% PHQ-2 positive rate identified and connected to services |

### Strategy 7: "Women, Children & Gender-Diverse" — Equal-Platform Protocol + Pediatric Sub-Study
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Powered prosthesis trials (NCT06160882) have ~90%+ male enrollment zero children; no transgender-specific prosthetic fitting protocols exist |
| **Action** | Mandate gender-balanced recruitment targets (all new trials: ≥30% women); partner with Lurie Children's Hospital for pediatric sub-study (transfemoral/developmental prosthetics); add gender-diverse inclusive intake forms across all SRAL protocols |
| **Budget** | $35K start + $15K/yr |
| **Success Metric** | All active SRAL prosthetics trials achieve ≥30% female enrollment within 24 months; pediatric protocol written and IRB-submitted within 18 months |
