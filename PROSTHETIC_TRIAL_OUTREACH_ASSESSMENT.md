### 1.7 Identified Trial Gaps for Underserved Populations

1. **Phase 3 Evidence Gap:** Only 5 Phase 3 trials exist across all lower limb prosthetic research — insufficient for establishing standard-of-care evidence for underserved communities.

2. **Community-Based Recruitment Gap:** Most trials are hospital-based at major academic centers, with no community-based recruitment strategies for prosthetic users in underserved areas.

3. **Diversity & Representation Gap:** Few studies report demographic data on race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, or rural/urban residence — making it impossible to assess whether trials reach underserved populations.

4. **Bilateral Amputation Gap:** Very few trials focus on bilateral amputees, who face unique mobility and rehabilitation challenges.

5. **Aging & Comorbidity Gap:** Limited trials address prosthetic rehabilitation for older adults with multiple comorbidities — a population often excluded from trials.

6. **Rural Access Gap:** Geographic concentration in major metro areas means rural prosthetic users have limited access to trial participation.

7. **Rehabilitation-Only Gap:** Many trials focus on surgical/device outcomes but lack rehabilitation-focused community integration strategies.

8. **Post-Market Surveillance Gap:** Only 5 Phase 4 trials and 106 studies with "Unknown" status suggest poor long-term follow-up of prosthetic users in real-world settings.

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: Community-Integrated Trial Recruitment Hubs

**Problem:** Most prosthetic trials (73.4% academic-sponsored) are centralized at major research hospitals, creating access barriers for underserved populations.

**Proposal:** Establish **satellite outreach hubs** at:
- **Community health centers** in underserved Chicago neighborhoods (South Side, West Side)
- **Veterans' centers** (leverage the TOSS/Walter Reed connection)
- **Rural rehabilitation centers** across Illinois and the Midwest
- **Faith-based organizations** with large disability congregations

**Action Items:**
- Partner with Chicago Department of Public Health to identify high-need neighborhoods
- Develop multilingual recruitment materials for non-English-speaking communities
- Train community health workers as "prosthetic trial navigators"

---

### 3.2 Strategy 2: Phase 3 Evidence Expansion Initiative

**Problem:** Only 5 Phase 3 trials exist across all lower limb prosthetic research (1.1% of total studies).

**Proposal:** Advocate for and co-design **Phase 3 multi-site trials** that:
- Include diverse enrollment targets (age 18–80+, rural and urban)
- Require demographic reporting (race, ethnicity, SES, disability type)
- Include community-based rehabilitation endpoints
- Partner with VA, NIH, and nonprofit prosthetic organizations

**Action Items:**
- Draft a white paper on the Phase 3 evidence gap
- Present at RESNA (Rehabilitation Engineering and Assistive Technology Society of North America)
- Seek NIH R01 or PCORI funding for community-based Phase 3 trial design

---

### 3.3 Strategy 3: Pharmacy & Medical Supply Access Partnerships

**Problem:** No pharmacies or medical supply stores within 500m of Shirley Ryan AbilityLab.

**Proposal:** Partner with:
- **CVS, Walgreens, or hospital-based pharmacies** within 0.5–1 mile to establish prosthetic-user prescription delivery programs
- **ProsMed, Otto Bock, or Hanger Clinic** to establish referral pathways from the clinical site
- **Mail-order pharmacy programs** for prosthetic users with mobility limitations

**Action Items:**
- Negotiate delivery agreements for post-surgical wound care kits
- Create a "prosthetic supply list" with recommended vendors for different amputation levels
- Establish a prosthetist-on-call referral system

---

### 3.4 Strategy 4: Rural & Underserved Outreach Program

**Problem:** Geographic distribution data shows minimal trial representation in rural areas, Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and Latin America.

**Proposal:** Launch a **mobile prosthetic outreach unit** that:
- Travels to rural Illinois communities monthly
- Provides prosthetic trial information and screening
- Offers virtual telehealth consultations with trial investigators
- Partners with VA telerehabilitation programs

**Action Items:**
- Identify 5–10 rural Illinois communities with high amputation incidence
- Partner with OSF HealthCare, Presence Health, or similar rural systems
- Develop a telehealth platform with adaptive interfaces for prosthetic users

---

### 3.5 Strategy 5: Cultural & Linguistic Competency Initiative

**Problem:** Trial recruitment materials are typically English-only and culturally homogeneous.

**Proposal:** Create **multilingual, culturally tailored outreach materials** in:
- **Spanish** (significant Chicago Hispanic population)
- **Polish** (large Polish-American community in Chicago)
- **Mandarin/Chinese** (growing Chinese community)
- **Arabic/Haitian Creole** (growing Middle Eastern and Caribbean populations)
- **Easy-read/Brain Injury-friendly formats** for cognitive accessibility

**Action Items:**
- Partner with the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab patient advocacy office
- Recruit community translators from local universities
- Test materials with focus groups from underserved prosthetic communities

---

### 3.6 Strategy 6: Affordable Transportation & Parking Solutions

**Problem:** All parking near Shirley Ryan AbilityLab requires fees; no public transit rail station is immediately adjacent; no confirmed ADA parking.

**Proposal:**
- Negotiate **discounted parking rates** with the Doubletree hotel multi-storey lot
- Coordinate **CTA paratransit (Dial-a-Ride)** for prosthetic users with mobility challenges
- Establish a **community shuttle van** for monthly trial screening days
- Advocate for **ADA-accessible parking spaces** with the Chicago Department of Transportation

**Action Items:**
- Contact Chicago DOT about designated accessible parking on Erie Street
- Set up a ride-share voucher program for trial participants
- Identify 2–3 accessible parking options within 0.5 miles

---

### 3.7 Strategy 7: Post-Trial Follow-Up & Community Re-Engagement

**Problem:** 218 studies (51.3%) are marked COMPLETED with no clear pathway for participants to receive trial results or ongoing care.

**Proposal:** Build a **prosthetic user registry** that:
- Tracks trial participants post-study
- Connects them with prosthetic services and support groups
- Provides updates on trial results and new study opportunities
- Serves as a living database for future trial recruitment

**Action Items:**
- Partner with the Amputee Coalition of America and Limbs International
- Develop a HIPAA-compliant participant tracking system
- Create annual "prosthetic community check-in" events at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

---

### 3.8 Strategy 8: Industry-Academic Partnership Catalyst

**Problem:** Industry sponsors represent only 13.6% of lower limb prosthetic trials, limiting community-embedded research.

**Proposal:** Establish a **Prosthetics Community Advisory Board** that:
- Includes prosthetic users, families, and caregivers
- Meets quarterly with academic and industry researchers
- Reviews trial designs for community feasibility
- Advises on recruitment strategies for underserved populations

**Action Items:**
- Recruit 10–15 prosthetic users from the Chicago area
- Partner with Hanger Clinic and Otto Bock to co-sponsor quarterly meetings
- Create a formal advisory board charter with clear roles and responsibilities
