## 1. Clinical Trial Landscape (427 Lower Limb Prosthetic Trials)

### 1.1 Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic/Non-Profit)** | 313 | 73.3% | Dominant; mostly single-site, limited reach |
| **Industry (Device Mfrs)** | 59 | 13.8% | ⚠️ Underrepresented — key tech & funding source |
| **FED (VA/DoD)** | 41 | 9.6% | VA/DOD trials exist but limited scale |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2.1% | Small municipal/state-sponsored efforts |
| **NIH** | 2 | 0.5% | ⚠️ Severely underfunded vs. burden of need |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | 0.5% | Multi-site collaboration is almost absent |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | 0.2% | Unclear sponsorship |

**Key Insight:** 86% of trials are academic or federally sponsored, not industry. This means:
- Device innovation is slow to reach trials
- Underserved populations (Medicaid, uninsured) are rarely covered by trial device costs
- No large-scale industry commitment to diversity recruitment

### 1.2 Enrollment Trends by Phase

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Not Applicable (NA)** | 299 | 70.0% | Observation/device registries — no efficacy testing |
| **Unknown Phase** | 107 | 25.1% | ⚠️ Nearly a quarter have no phase assigned |
| **Phase 1** | 3 | 0.7% | Safety testing barely underway |
| **Early Phase 1** | 4 | 0.9% | Dose/feasibility studies minimal |
| **Phase 2** | 5 | 1.2% | Earliest efficacy signals |
| **Phase 3** | 5 | 1.2% | ⚠️ Only 5 pivotal trials exist for 427 studies |
| **Phase 4** | 5 | 1.2% | Post-market surveillance almost absent |

**Key Insight:** 96% of trials are in NA or Unknown phase — no rigorous later-stage efficacy testing. This pipeline gap means:
- Device improvements never reach large-scale validation
- Evidence base for prosthetic users is extremely thin
- Insurance coverage decisions lack supportive trial data

### 1.3 Summary: Where Are the Gaps?

## 3. Identified Gaps in Underserved Populations

| Population | Gap Level | Root Cause | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rural U.S.** | 🔴 Near-excluded | Only 1 trial (NCT05884203); no rural infrastructure | ~30% of amputees in rural settings excluded from research |
| **Low-income / Uninsured** | 🔴 Near-excluded | No trial covers device costs ($5K–$50K); no Medicaid coverage | Uninsured amputees cannot participate at all |
| **African American / Black** | 🔴 Near-excluded | No targeted recruitment; Chicago South Side = zero trial sites | Amputation rate is 2–4× higher for African Americans |
| **Hispanic / Latino** | 🔴 Near-excluded | No Spanish-language consent; no Hispanic outreach | ~400K Spanish-speaking amputees in US excluded |
| **Women** | 🔴 Near-excluded | 90%+ male enrollment in most prosthetics trials | Female amputees (~25% of population) systematically excluded |
| **Children / Adolescents** | 🔴 Near-excluded | No pediatric-specific prosthetic trials found | Children with congenital/traumatic limb loss have no options |
| **Elderly (75+)** | 🟡 Limited access | K-level and weight exclusions filter many elderly | Majority of amputees are >65 with comorbidities |
| **Global South** | 🔴 Near-excluded | ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access | 80% of world's amputee population invisible to research |
| **Cognitive / Intellectual** | 🔴 Near-excluded | MMSE<24 exclusions in virtually all protocols | Those who need rehab most are excluded from trials |
| **Transgender / Gender-Diverse** | 🟡 Limited | No inclusive eligibility protocols | Requires policy change across the field |

## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy P1 — Patient Navigation & Transportation (Immediate — ~$111K/yr)

**Target:** All SRAL trial participants with mobility/transportation barriers

| Component | Annual Cost | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| CTA Ventra reduced-fare cards | $18,750 | 50 participants × $7.50/day × 50 trips/yr |
| Ride-share vouchers | $20,000 | Supplemental rides when public transit insufficient |
| Divvy Adaptive access | $2,500 | 5 participants at McClurg & Ohio station (130m from SRAL) |
| Winter thermal kits | $7,500 | 50 participants × $150 (thermal liners, hand warmers) |
| Patient Navigator (1 FTE) | $45,000 | Coordinate appointments, transportation, consent |
| **Total** | **~$93,750/yr** | |

**KPIs:** 100% transportation assistance; ≥85% attendance rate; ≥90% satisfaction

### Strategy P2 — Data Transparency Dashboard (Immediate — ~$5K)

**Approach:** Open-source dashboard on GitHub showing trial demographics, gaps, outcomes in real-time using ClinicalTrials.gov API.
- Interactive map of US trial sites
- Demographic breakdown by sponsor type
- Gap indicators for underserved populations
- SRAL trial tracker with real-time enrollment

**Stack:** Python/Plotly Dash or Streamlit; GitHub Pages hosting; monthly refresh.
**Timeline:** 3 months MVP, 6 months full dashboard.
**KPIs:** Dashboard live by Month 3; ≥5 community contributions; cited in ≥1 policy brief.

### Strategy P3 — Community Advisory Board (Immediate — ~$5K/yr)

**Approach:** Establish a CAB of 10–15 prosthetic users, family members, and community health workers from underserved populations.
- Diverse representation across race, gender, age, disability type, and geography
- Quarterly meetings at SRAL with childcare and transportation support
- CAB audits trial diversity reports and approves outreach materials
**KPIs:** CAB formed within 1 month; ≥4 meetings/yr; ≥1 protocol amendment influenced by CAB per year

### Strategy P4 — Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks (6–12 months — ~$5K/yr for IRB)

**Approach:** Implement minimum demographic reporting in all SRAL connected trials:
- ≥25% female enrollment
- ≥30% racial/ethnic minority (Black, Hispanic, Asian, Indigenous)
- ≥15% Medicaid/uninsured
- ≥10% rural
- ≥50% age 55+

**Protocol Amendments:**
1. IRB amendments to all SRAL active trials adding demographic reporting
2. Outreach materials in Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic (top non-English languages in Chicago)
3. Remove BMI/age caps without clinical justification

**KPIs:** 100% of SRAL trials reporting demographics by Year 2; ≥25% female; ≥30% minority

### Strategy P5 — Mobile Screening Events (6–12 months — ~$50K/yr)

**Approach:** Partner with VA and FQHCs to deploy mobile prosthetic assessment units with trial coordinators.
- Set up at VA Chicago Lakeside, VA Rockford, VA Marion, FQHCs, church events, community health fairs
- Plain-language consent forms; Spanish-language materials
- Travel stipends ($50–100/trip)

**Milestones:**
- Month 1–3: Secure VA/FQHC partnerships; hire 2 trial coordinators
- Month 3–6: First mobile events at 3 underserved sites
- Month 6–12: Scale to quarterly events at 6+ sites

**KPIs:** ≥15 new diverse participants/study/yr from South/West Side; ≥5 recruitment events/yr in rural Midwest

### Strategy P6 — Industry-Academic Device Access Pipeline (6–12 months — ~$120K setup + $20K/yr)

**Approach:** Negotiate pro bono device donation agreements with industry sponsors; create a Device Lending Library at SRAL.

| Industry Partner | Devices | Current Trial | Outreach Potential |
|---|---|---|---|
| Otto Bock | Knees, feet | ASCENT (NCT04784429, 107 enroll) | HIGH |
| Hanger Clinic | Prostheses | K2 (NCT06194838) | HIGH |
| Liberating Tech | Knees | K2 powered vs mechanical | HIGH |
| Össur | Feet, braces | PRO-FLEX | MEDIUM |
| Ottobock | Braces, adapters | France 5-site trial | MEDIUM |

**KPIs:** 20 pro bono device slots/yr; ≥5 industry partners signed by Year 1; zero participant cost for prosthetic devices

### Strategy P7 — DME Integration at SRAL (CRITICAL — $150K setup + $30K/yr)

**Approach:** Address the #1 site-level barrier: ZERO DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m.

| Option | Setup Cost | Annual Cost | Detail |
|---|---|---|---|
| A) Monthly Pop-up DME Clinic | $15K/yr | $15K/yr | Össur/Ottobock/Hanger provides seasonal pop-up in SRAL lobby for socket checks, padding adjustments |
| B) Permanent Prosthetic Access Station | $50K setup | $30K/yr | Co-locate with Northwestern Medicine Galter Pavilion satellite fitting station |
| C) Tele-DME | $5K setup | $10K/yr | Video consultations with prosthetists; mail-in device supplies for simple adjustments |

**Why This Is #1:** Prosthetic users need socket checks every 3–6 months. Without nearby DME, participants drop out of trials — directly undermining enrollment retention and data quality.

**KPIs:** ≥50 DME interactions/month; 10% reduction in trial cancellation due to prosthesis issues

### Strategy P8 — Older Adult Programming (6–12 months — ~$10K/yr)

**Approach:** 
- Broaden age eligibility ranges in SRAL trials (currently many exclude 75+ or weight >300lbs)
- Provide caregiver stipends ($25/hr for in-home support during trial visits)
- Partner with senior centers (Lakeview, Gold Coast, Streeterville) for recruitment
- Offer in-home assessment option for elderly participants unable to travel

**KPIs:** ≥10% of SRAL trial participants age 65+; ≥5 in-home assessments/yr; ≥2 caregiver-supported participants per study

### Strategy P9 — Tele-Trial Hub (6–12 months — ~$20K/yr)

**Approach:** Allow virtual visit components (telehealth assessments, mail-in device checks) to reduce the burden of in-person travel.

**Hybrid Visit Structure:**
- Visit 1 (in-person): Consent + baseline + device fitting at SRAL
- Visits 2–4 (telehealth): Remote check-in + device data via phone/app
- Final Visit (in-person): Outcomes assessment + device refitting if needed

**KPIs:** ≥30% of participants using ≥1 telehealth component; ≥20% reduction in trial cancellation; 85% satisfaction

### Strategy P10 — Rural Outreach via VA Network (12+ months — ~$200K/yr)

**Approach:** Leverage federal-sponsored trial infrastructure. Deploy mobile prosthetics labs quarterly to VA clinics:
- Rockford VA (IL) ~90 miles
- Marion VA (IL) ~100 miles
- St. Louis VA (MO) ~300 miles
- Indianapolis VA (IN) ~180 miles

**KPIs:** 4 rural VA site visits/yr; ≥20 rural Veterans recruited/yr; 1 new rural-specific protocol by Year 2

### Strategy P11 — South Side Permanent Office (12+ months — ~$350K/yr)

**Approach:** Establish a permanent SRAL satellite office on Chicago's South Side (~10km), in an African American/Hispanic community. Open 24/7 for prosthetics support.

**KPIs:** ≥10 South Siders per study/yr; ≥75% tele-trial retention

### Strategy P12 — Mental Health Integration (12+ months — ~$20K/yr)

**Approach:** PHQ-2 screening at trial intake; partner with Medrina (~550m) for monthly peer support groups; embed mental health in trial protocols.

**KPIs:** ≥95% PHQ-2 screening completion; ≥70% peer group attendance
