# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Project

Community outreach assessment for lower limb prosthetic trials, identifying enrollment gaps and evaluating Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a community outreach site for prosthetic users.

**Repository:** https://github.com/zhub9006/prosthetic-trial-outreach  
**Last Updated:** July 2026

---

## Overview

This project assesses the current landscape of lower limb prosthetic clinical trials on ClinicalTrials.gov, identifies gaps in underserved population enrollment, and evaluates the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (355 E Erie St, Chicago, IL 60611) as a potential community outreach hub.

### Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** Rarely reported in trial demographics. NCT04427007 enrolled 100% White participants.
2. **Lower-Limb Specificity:** Some trials overlap with upper-limb prosthetics; many focus on transtibial only, excluding transfemoral users.
3. **Rural Access:** No trials based in rural Midwest or Southern US. Most sites in major metros (Chicago, Boston, UK, France).
4. **Age Range:** Many trials cap enrollment at 60-65, excluding older adults who represent a large portion of the amputee population.
5. **Female Representation:** Sex-specific enrollment often not reported; several cohorts are male-skewed.
6. **Insurance/Financial Barriers:** Industry trials may require specific insurance or travel, creating access barriers for low-income populations.
7. **Phase Gap:** 96% in NA/Unknown phases means very few interventions have undergone rigorous later-stage testing before market release.

### Assessment Summary

- World-class rehabilitation reputation and dedicated prosthetic/orthotic programs

- Abundant dining and amenities support extended trial participation

**Limitations:**
- No parks within 500m — outdoor recreation limited (~1km to lakefront)
- No dedicated social services offices nearby
- Parking limited (4 facilities) and all fee-based
- CTA stops generally lack shelters and tactile paving
- High urban density may be overwhelming for some participants

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 1. Community-Based Screening Events
- **Target:** Rural populations, older adults, racial/ethnic minorities, low-income individuals
- **Approach:** Mobile screening stations at community centers, churches, VA facilities
- **AbilityLab as hub:** Leverage reputation and infrastructure for satellite events

### 2. Tele-Trial Participation Program
- Hybrid model: in-person AbilityLab visits + telehealth check-ins
- Travel stipends and lodging partnerships for participants >50 miles from Chicago

### 3. Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
- Match prosthetic device companies with underserved trial sites
- Pro bono device donations from manufacturers (Össur, Stryker, Ottobock)

### 4. Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks
- Mandate demographic reporting in all trial protocols
- Set minimum diversity targets (≥25% female, ≥30% minority enrollment)

### 5. Patient Navigation & Transportation Services
- Dedicated patient navigator for trial eligibility, enrollment, and completion
- Partner with Divvy (adapted cycles), CTA (reduced-fare cards), ride-share

### 6. Older Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming
- Advocate for broader age ranges in trial protocols
- Caregiver stipends and on-site support

### 7. Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers
- Partner with VA hospitals and FQHCs in rural Midwest and South
- Deploy mobile prosthetics labs to rural locations

### 8. Data Transparency Dashboard
- Open-access dashboard for trial demographics and geographic coverage
- Community advisory board of prosthetic users from underserved communities
