# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Project

Community outreach assessment for lower limb prosthetic trials, identifying enrollment gaps and evaluating Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a community outreach site for prosthetic users.

**Repository:** https://github.com/zhub9006/prosthetic-trial-outreach  
**Last Updated:** July 2026

---

## Overview

This project assesses the current landscape of lower limb prosthetic clinical trials on ClinicalTrials.gov (59–60 active/recruiting trials, ~450+ total), identifies gaps in underserved population enrollment, and evaluates the **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** (355 E Erie St, Chicago, IL 60611) as a potential community outreach hub for prosthetic users.

---

## Key Findings Summary

### Clinical Trial Gaps (≈59 Active/Recruiting Trials Analyzed from 450+ Total)

| Finding | Detail |
|---|---|
| Industry Underrepresentation | Only 22% of trials are industry-sponsored — limits tech access for underserved populations |
| Phase Pipeline Gap | 89% of trials are in NA or Unknown phase — only 1 Phase 3 trial exists |
| Sponsor Imbalance | 62% academic, 22% industry, 16% federal — **no civic/nonprofit funding** |
| NIH Underfunding | Only 2 of ~450 total trials are NIH-funded |
| Rural Invisibility | Zero trials in rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal lands |
| Racial/Ethnic Gaps | No Spanish-language consent; no minority recruitment; Chicago South Side has ZERO trial sites |
| Gender Gap | 90%+ male enrollment in most prosthetics trials |
| Pediatric Absence | Zero pediatric-specific prosthetic trials found |
| Global South | ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access |
| DME Access | No DME/prosthetic vendors within 500 m of SRAL — #1 site-level barrier |

### Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Site Assessment (500 m Radius)

| Category | Score | Highlights |
|---|---|---|
| Walkability | 10/10 | Flat, ADA-accessible sidewalks; grade-level crosswalks |
| Public Transit | 9.4/10 | 10+ CTA bus stops; 6 Divvy stations; Red Line ~1 km away |
| Restaurants | 9.3/10 | Cafés, fast food (Chipotle, Panera) within walking distance |
| Healthcare | 8.5/10 | 24/7 Walgreens; Panera in NW Memorial Hospital; **no hospital/clinic within 500 m** |
| Pharmacy | 8.5/10 | 1× Walgreens (24/7) within 500 m; more in adjacent blocks |
| Parking | 4/10 | 1 multi-storey (~160 m), 1 underground (~380 m); all paid; **no ADA spots within 200 m** |
| Parks/Social Services | 0/10 | None within 500 m |
| DME/Prosthetic Vendors | 0/10 | **ZERO within 500 m** — #1 site-level barrier |

**Overall Community Score: ~5.5/10** — Strong transit and amenities, but critical gaps in DME access, parks, social services, and minority outreach infrastructure.

## Outreach Strategies

1. **Mobile DME Unit** — Monthly mobile prosthetist outreach for uninsured populations. ~$15K/yr.
2. **Language Access** — Spanish consent forms + bilingual coordinators target South Side clinics.
3. **FQHC Partnerships** — South/West Side screening and referral through community health centers.
4. **Rural Tele-Outreach** — Virtual consults connecting SRAL prosthetists with VA rural sites.
5. **Pediatric Trial** — Design child-friendly prosthetic trial (ages 8–17).
6. **Geriatric Protocol** — Prosthetic outcomes trial for adults 75+.
7. **Female Recruitment** — Enrollment targets + women's health org partnerships.

Full strategies with budgets and timelines → [SRAL_OUTREACH_2026.md](SRAL_OUTREACH_2026.md)
