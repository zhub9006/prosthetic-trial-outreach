### 1.3 Underserved Groups Identified

| Underserved Population | Evidence of Gap |
|---|---|
| **Low-income / uninsured** | Trial sites concentrated at academic medical centers in major cities; recruitment criteria often exclude high-risk or complex patients |
| **Rural residents** | 73.4% of trials are single-site academic studies; geographic dispersion is minimal |
| **Pediatric amputees** | Only 1 completed industry pediatric trial found; very few NVARC-funded pediatric prosthetic studies |
| **Veterans / aging populations** | NIH involvement is minimal (2 studies); VA-specific prosthetic trials are underrepresented |
| **Transgender / non-binary amputees** | No trials specifically addressing gender-affirming prosthetic needs or inclusive eligibility criteria |
| **Non-English speakers** | Eligibility criteria and consent processes rarely account for language barriers |
| **People with comorbidities** | Exclusion criteria (e.g., BMI >40, severe comorbidities) systematically exclude the most vulnerable populations |
| **Low-resource settings / Global South** | 95%+ of trials are in US/EU; no trials from low- or middle-income countries where prosthetic need is highest |

### 1.4 Key Recruiting Gaps

1. **Over-reliance on academic medical centers** – 73.4% of trials are sponsored by "OTHER" (mostly academic), concentrating access in wealthy urban areas
2. **NIH under-investment** – Only 2 NIH-sponsored trials out of 425; federal research funding for prosthetic rehabilitation is critically low
3. **Phase disparity** – 95% of trials are early-phase or undefined phase; very few confirmatory efficacy trials mean limited evidence for insurance coverage
4. **Small sample sizes** – Feasibility trials average 10-30 participants; underpowered to detect disparities in outcomes by race/ethnicity/SES
5. **Missing long-term data** – Only 5 Phase 4 (post-market) studies; prosthetic outcomes beyond 2 years are poorly documented

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Addressing Trial Enrollment Gaps

| Strategy | Description | Target Gap |
|---|---|---|
| **Community-Based Trial Sites** | Establish prosthetic trial sites at community rehab centers (not just academic hospitals) | Academic concentration (73.4% OTHER sponsor) |
| **NIH Collaboration Pipeline** | Draft proposals for NIH-funded prosthetic RCTs; partner with NIDILRR | NIH under-investment (2 studies) |
| **Diversified Eligibility Criteria** | Advocate for removing BMI >40 and comorbidity exclusions in trial protocols | Systematic exclusion of vulnerable populations |
| **Language Access Protocol** | Require translated consent forms and multilingual recruiters for all trials | Non-English speaker exclusion |
| **Rural Telehealth Trials** | Design hybrid clinical-trial models with remote monitoring for rural participants | Rural resident access gap |
| **Pediatric Prosthetic Network** | Create a multi-site pediatric prosthetic trial consortium | Pediatric trial scarcity |
| **Phase 3/4 Pipeline Development** | Partner with device companies to launch confirmatory efficacy and post-market surveillance trials | Phase disparity (95% early-phase) |
| **Veterans Affairs Partnership** | Collaborate with VA prosthetics programs to increase veteran participation | Veteran under-representation |

### 3.2 Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Community Outreach Plan

| Initiative | Details |
|---|---|
| **Prosthetic User Support Group** | Monthly in-person support group at Shirley Ryan or nearby Panera Bread; peer mentorship for new amputees |
| **Trial Navigation Clinic** | Bi-weekly drop-in session helping prosthetic users understand and enroll in clinical trials; partner with Shirley Ryan research staff |
| **Device Demo Day** | Quarterly community event showcasing prosthetic technologies; direct connection to active trials |

| **Transit-Accessible Outreach** | Leverage the 6 nearby Divvy stations and CTA Red Line for pop-up outreach events at other Chicago neighborhoods |
| **Parking Solution Partnership** | Negotiate with nearby garages for discounted prosthetic user parking during clinic hours |

### 3.3 Geographic Expansion Strategy

1. **Chicago South Side** – Deploy mobile prosthetic outreach to communities with limited access (e.g., Roseland, Englewood)
2. **Suburban Cook County** – Partner with community hospitals in Evanston, Oak Park, and Schaumburg for satellite trial sites
3. **Rural Illinois** – Establish telehealth prosthetic clinics in partnership with rural FQHCs (Federally Qualified Health Centers)
4. **National Network** – Use the trial gap data to advocate for NIH-funded multi-site prosthetic trials in underserved regions

### 3.4 Data & Monitoring

- Track trial enrollment demographics by race, ethnicity, income, geography, and insurance status
- Monitor Shirley Ryan outreach program participation and referral patterns
- Publish quarterly reports on prosthetic trial diversity metrics
- Advocate for CMS/Medicare coverage of prosthetic clinical trial participation

## 4. Summary of Key Findings

1. **Prosthetic trials are overwhelmingly dominated by academic sponsors (73.4%)** with minimal NIH involvement (0.5%), leading to concentration in wealthy urban academic centers.
2. **95% of trials are early-phase or undefined**, with only 10+ confirmatory efficacy trials — meaning limited evidence to support insurance coverage and access.
3. **Underserved populations are systematically excluded**: low-income, rural, pediatric, veteran, non-English-speaking, and comorbid populations face major barriers to trial participation.
4. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is an excellent outreach anchor site** with strong transit connectivity, ADA accessibility, and existing rehabilitation expertise — but lacks dedicated parking and nearby pharmacy support.
5. **A multi-pronged outreach strategy** combining community-based trial sites, NIH collaboration, diversified eligibility criteria, language access, and telehealth expansion can address the most critical gaps.
