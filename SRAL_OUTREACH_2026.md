**Key Insight:** The overwhelming majority of trials (62%) are academic/government-sponsored, with only 24% industry-funded. Critically, **zero trials are funded by civic organizations, nonprofits, or community health programs** — meaning no trials specifically target access barriers for low-income or underserved prosthetic users.

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Typical Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unknown** | 22 | 21% | Likely early-phase or not yet classified |

| **Phase 3** | 2 | 2% | Multi-site efficacy |

**Key Insight:** 73% of trials are classified as "NA" — mostly registries and observational studies. Only **4 trials (4%)** are in Phase 2–3, and they are tiny (2–8 participants each). This means the clinical evidence pipeline for **new** prosthetic technologies is extremely thin.

## 3. Identified Gaps for Underserved Populations

### 3.1 Critical Gaps

| Gap | Evidence | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **No civic/nonprofit-funded trials** | 0 out of 104 trials | Uninsured/underinsured prosthetic users completely excluded from research |
| **No pediatric trials** | Zero studies targeting <18 years | Children with limb loss have no age-specific research |
| **No rural trial sites** | All US sites in major metro areas (Chicago, NYC, Phoenix, etc.) | Rural amputees cannot access trials |
| **No non-English consent forms** | All trials require English/Spanish proficiency | Non-English speakers excluded |
| **K2-only focus** | ASCENT K2, Liberating Technologies trials target K2 ambulators only | K1 (household) and K3+ (active) users underserved |
| **No affordability/access trials** | Zero trials on cost, insurance, or DME access | Core barrier to prosthetic use unstudied |
| **Tiny Phase 2–3 pipeline** | Only 4% of trials; smallest enrollment 2 | Novel prosthetic technologies stall in evidence gap |
| **No diversity reporting** | No trials report race/ethnicity disaggregation | Unable to assess equitable enrollment |

### 3.2 Underserved Groups Missing from Trials

- **Low-income/uninsured amputees** — no financial access pathway to trial devices
- **Rural residents** — all US sites in urban corridors
- **Non-English speakers** — no multilingual consent or support
- **Pediatric amputees** — no age-appropriate studies
- **K1 ambulators (household-only)** — trials focus on K2+ (community ambulators)
- **Minority populations** — no trials in predominantly Black/Hispanic neighborhoods
- **Women** — few trials stratify by sex; amputation rates rising in women with diabetes
- **Aging amputees** — ASCENT K2 requires age ≥65; no trials for older adults with comorbidities

## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetic Fitting Unit

**Problem:** Zero DME/prosthetic suppliers within 500m — the #1 site-level barrier for prosthetic users.

**Solution:** Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetics van staffing a certified orthotist/prosthetist for:
- Socket adjustments and fitting consultations
- Device maintenance and troubleshooting
- Insurance navigation assistance
- Trial screening and enrollment

**Estimated Cost:** $15,000/year (van lease + staff time)  
**Measurable Outcome:** Number of unserved patients screened; enrollment in trials

---

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Bilingual Outreach

**Problem:** No trials offer non-English consent forms; limited Spanish support at SRAL.

**Solution:**
- Create Spanish-language outreach materials and consent forms
- Hire bilingual (English/Spanish) community health workers
- Partner with community organizations serving Hispanic/Latino amputee populations
- Offer interpretation services at all outreach events

**Estimated Cost:** $8,000/year (materials + staffing)  
**Measurable Outcome:** # of non-English-speaking patients engaged; # enrolled in trials

---

### Strategy 3: FQHC & Community Health Center Partnerships

**Problem:** No trials on Chicago's South or West Side — predominantly Black/Latino neighborhoods with highest amputation rates.

**Solution:**
- Partner with Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) on the South and West Sides
- Establish referral pipeline from community health centers to SRAL trials
- Train CHWs at FQHCs to identify eligible patients and assist with enrollment
- Host quarterly "Prosthetic Wellness Days" at partner FQHCs

**Estimated Cost:** $25,000/year (staff time, transportation, materials)  
**Measurable Outcome:** # of South/West Side patients screened; enrollment diversity metrics

---

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach Program

**Problem:** Zero rural trial sites; rural amputees face travel and cost barriers.

**Solution:**
- Establish telehealth consultation slots for rural VA and community health patients
- Partner with VA rural sites for virtual pre-screening
- Use mobile van for periodic in-person follow-ups in rural Illinois
- Develop remote outcome measurement protocols (app-based)

**Estimated Cost:** $12,000/year (telehealth platform + travel)  
**Measurable Outcome:** # of rural patients screened; tele-consult completion rate

---

### Strategy 5: Pediatric Prosthetic Trial Design

**Problem:** Zero pediatric-specific trials; children's prosthetic needs are unaddressed.

**Solution:**
- Design a Phase 2 pediatric prosthetic trial (ages 6–17) with child-friendly protocols
- Partner with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab's pediatric rehabilitation program
- Include play-based outcome measures and family support components
- Seek nonprofit/philanthropic funding (NIH R01 unlikely for device trials)

**Estimated Cost:** $150,000 (pilot grant)  
**Measurable Outcome:** Pediatric trial funded; first enrollment within 18 months

---

### Strategy 6: Affordability & Access Research Trial

**Problem:** No trials study insurance barriers, cost, or DME access — the core problem for underserved users.

**Solution:**
- Design an implementation science trial studying interventions to reduce prosthetic access barriers
- Test: (a) insurance navigation counseling, (b) financial assistance programs, (c) mobile DME units
- Partner with Medicaid managed care organizations for data access
- Embed at SRAL as a community-based participatory research project

**Estimated Cost:** $300,000 (2-year pilot)  
**Measurable Outcome:** Policy recommendations; reduced time-to-prosthetic-fitting

## Summary

The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab site has **excellent transit and walkability** but **critical gaps in DME access, parking, and nearby healthcare**. The clinical trial landscape is dominated by registries and industry-sponsored studies that **do not reach underserved populations**. Our outreach project addresses these gaps through mobile DME, language access, FQHC partnerships, rural tele-outreach, pediatric trials, and access research — all anchored at SRAL.
