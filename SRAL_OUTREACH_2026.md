## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Priority Interventions

| Strategy | Target | Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Mobile DME Unit** | Uninsured; South Side | ~$15K/yr | 6 months |
| **2. Language Access** | Spanish-speaking amputees | ~$5K/yr | 3 months |
| **3. FQHC Partnerships** | South/West Side screening | ~$10K/yr | 6 months |
| **4. Rural Tele-Outreach** | VA rural sites | ~$8K/yr | 4 months |
| **5. Pediatric Trial** | Ages 8–17 | ~$50K/grant | 12 months |
| **6. Geriatric Protocol** | Adults 75+ | ~$30K/grant | 9 months |
| **7. Female Recruitment** | Women with amputation | ~$5K/yr | 6 months |

### Site-Specific Recommendations

1. **DME Vendor Partnership** — On-site DME fitting room; the #1 barrier to trial participation.
2. **ADA Parking** — Designate 2–3 ADA spots within 200m of SRAL entrance.
3. **Transit Ambassador** — 48 CTA stops within 500m; create assistance role for prosthetic users.
4. **Community Health Fairs** — Quarterly events at SRAL targeting South/West Side and Latino communities.
5. **Telehealth Kiosk** — For rural follow-up visits.
6. **Diverse Recruitment Board** — Amputee representatives from underserved groups guiding trial design.

### Strategic Priorities

| Priority | Action | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 Immediate | DME access + ADA parking | Removes #1 and #2 site barriers |
| 🔴 Immediate | Spanish consent + bilingual coordinators | Opens to ~50% of Chicago amputee population excluded |
| 🟡 3–6 months | FQHC partnerships + mobile DME | Extends reach to underserved areas |
| 🟡 6–12 months | Pediatric + geriatric protocols | Fills largest demographic gaps |
| 🟢 12+ months | Telehealth + advisory board | Sustainable community-driven infrastructure |
