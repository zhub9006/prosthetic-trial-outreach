## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 5.1 Immediate (0–3 Months)

1. **DME Coordination Pilot** — Establish a quarterly mobile prosthetics fitting day at SRAL. Partner with a DME vendor to bring inventory on-site. Target: 20 uninsured/underinsured patients per quarter.

2. **Language Access Protocol** — Translate all trial consent forms into Spanish; hire 1 bilingual study coordinator. Target: 15% Hispanic enrollment increase.

3. **VA Referral Pipeline** — Formalize referral agreement with Chicago Lakeside VA Clinic (300m away). Logistics screening done at VA; trial participation at SRAL.

4. **FQHC Screening Network** — Partner with 3–5 community health centers on South/West Side for pre-screening and transportation coordination.

### 5.2 Medium-Term (3–12 Months)

5. **Mobile Outreach Van** — Lease a wheelchair-accessible van for weekly routes to underserved areas. Routes: South Side, West Side, suburban VA sites.

6. **Pediatric + Geriatric Trial Launch** — Design and register two new trials targeting children (8–17) and older adults (75+). Leverage SRAL's existing infrastructure.

7. **Community Advisory Board** — Recruit 10–12 prosthetic users from underserved communities to advise on trial design, recruitment materials, and site accessibility.

8. **Industry Sponsor Engagement** — Present SRAL's outreach model to device manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Parker Hannifin) for device sponsorship and co-branded trials.

### 5.3 Long-Term (12–24 Months)

9. **Rural Tele-Outreach Program** — Virtual consults connecting SRAL prosthetists with VA and FQHC sites in rural Illinois, Indiana, and Wisconsin.

10. **Data-Sharing Consortium** — Partner with other academic centers to share de-identified prosthetic outcomes data, addressing the "Unknown phase" problem in 24% of trials.

11. **Policy Advocacy** — Use SRAL outreach data to advocate for NIH/PCORI funding for underserved prosthetics trials.

## 6. Site Feasibility Summary

| Dimension | Rating | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Transit access** | ★★★★★ | Excellent CTA bus access, Divvy bike-share, Red Line nearby |
| **ADA accessibility** | ★★★★☆ | Building-level accessible; parking ADA gap |
| **DME access** | ★☆☆☆☆ | Zero vendors within 500m — critical gap |
| **Community density** | ★★☆☆☆ | Streeterville is commercial/office-dominant; low residential density of target population |
| **Underserved proximity** | ★★☆☆☆ | South/West Side communities are 5–15 miles away; requires outreach infrastructure |
| **Clinical adjacency** | ★★★★☆ | VA Clinic, Northwestern Medicine campus, and pharmacy all within walking distance |
| **Parking** | ★★☆☆☆ | Paid, no ADA spots close; problematic for users with mobility impairments |

**Overall Site Readiness: 3.5/5** — SRAL has strong clinical infrastructure and transit access, but lacks DME proximity and is not situated within the underserved communities it aims to reach. Outreach infrastructure (mobile van, FQHC partnerships, remote screening) is essential to bridge this gap.
