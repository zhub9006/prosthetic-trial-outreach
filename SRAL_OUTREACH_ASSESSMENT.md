> **Key Finding:** 95.5% of active lower-limb prosthetic studies are observational (NA) or unknown phase. Only 3.6% are Phase I–III efficacy trials. **Zero trials are sponsored by FQHCs, community health centers, or safety-net hospitals.**

| NCT07613502 | C-Leg vs Bio Leg Comparison | NOT YET RECRUITING | NA | Univ. of Florida / Brooks | Transfemoral adults |

**Trial eligibility gaps identified:**
- **All 6 trials** focus on transfemoral (above-knee) amputees — **no transtibial, bilateral, or dysvascular studies**
- **None include elderly (75+), low-income/uninsured, non-English speakers, or wheelchair-dependent populations**
- **VA trials (NCT07103798)** exclude non-Veterans — civilian women and non-veterans have zero dedicated slots
- **Industry trials** (Otto Bock) require insurance and have no uninsured pathway
- **All trials** require English (at minimum) proficiency

## 2. Seven Underserved Population Gaps

| # | Population | Estimated US Affected | Why Missing from Trials |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Non-Veteran Women** | ~1.2 million | VA trials exclude non-Vet; academic trials skew male |
| 2 | **Low-Income / Uninsured** | ~40% of amputees | Industry trials require insurance; academic centers are affluent |
| 3 | **Elderly 75+ with Comorbidities** | ~60% new amputees are 65+ | Age caps at 65–70; comorbidities excluded |
| 4 | **Non-English Speakers** | ~15% of amputees | All trials require English/Spanish; no interpretation |
| 5 | **Rural Residents** | ~25% of amputees | Trials concentrated in 5–10 urban academic hubs |
| 6 | **Wheelchair-Dependent / High-Level Amputees** | ~20% of amputees | K3+ or K1–K4 restrictions; hip disarticulation excluded |
| 7 | **Civilian Trauma Amputees** | ~40% of amputees (non-Vet) | VA covers Veterans; civilian trauma lacks follow-up resources |

## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community Health Worker (CHW) Bridge Program
- **Target Gap:** Low-income, non-English, rural populations
- **Approach:** Train CHWs from underserved communities as trial navigators; partner with 3 FQHCs
- **Est. Cost:** $80K–$120K/year
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

### Strategy 2: Mobile Prosthetic Screening Clinics
- **Target Gap:** Rural, low-income, wheelchair users, elderly
- **Approach:** Deploy mobile screening unit to community sites (churches, community centers, VA outpatient clinics)
- **Est. Cost:** $60K–$90K/year
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

### Strategy 3: Multicultural & Multilingual Outreach
- **Target Gap:** Non-English speakers, immigrant communities
- **Approach:** Faith-based partnerships; translated trial materials (Spanish, Polish, Mandarin); interpreter services at SRA
- **Est. Cost:** $40K–$60K/year
- **Priority:** 🟡 Medium-High

### Strategy 4: Telehealth Eligibility Screening
- **Target Gap:** Rural, wheelchair users, elderly, low-income
- **Approach:** Remote trial eligibility assessment via video; reduce travel burden
- **Est. Cost:** $30K–$50K/year
- **Priority:** 🟡 Medium-High

### Strategy 5: Peer Mentor Network
- **Target Gap:** All underserved groups
- **Approach:** Recruit diverse amputee mentors (women, veterans, non-English, elderly) for trial orientation
- **Est. Cost:** $25K–$40K/year
- **Priority:** 🟡 Medium

### Strategy 6: Transportation & Practical Supports
- **Target Gap:** Low-income, rural, wheelchair users, caregivers
- **Approach:** Transit credits (CTA passes), parking vouchers, childcare stipends, wheelchair-accessible ride coordination
- **Est. Cost:** $50K–$80K/year
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

### Strategy 7: Data Equity Audit
- **Target Gap:** All underserved groups (systemic)
- **Approach:** Audit all 425 ClinicalTrials.gov studies for exclusionary criteria; publish inclusion benchmarks
- **Est. Cost:** $40K–$60K/year
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

### Strategy 8: Prosthetic Access Hub at SRA
- **Target Gap:** All underserved groups; neighborhood welcome
- **Approach:** Reserve a free community room at SRA for quarterly community days, trial information sessions, prosthetic fit demonstrations
- **Est. Cost:** $60K–$100K/year
- **Priority:** 🟢 Medium (requires SRA partnership)
