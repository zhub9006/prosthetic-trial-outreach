## 1. Clinical Trial Gap Analysis

A comprehensive search of ClinicalTrials.gov identified **~32 active/recruiting/not-yet-recruiting** lower-limb prosthetic trials and **~450+ total** trials historically. Analysis of the active set reveals significant enrollment gaps:

| Gap Area | Finding | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| **Industry Sponsorship** | Only 22–25% of trials are industry-sponsored — limits access to cutting-edge devices for uninsured/underinsured populations | 🔴 High |
| **Phase 3 Pipeline** | 89% of active trials are in NA or Unknown phase; only 1 Phase 3 trial identified across all data | 🔴 High |
| **NIH Funding** | Only ~2 of ~450 total trials are NIH-funded — critical research underserved by federal investment | 🔴 High |
| **Rural Access** | Zero trials in rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal lands — 1/3 of Veterans live far from VA centers | 🔴 High |
| **Racial/Ethnic** | No Spanish-language consent forms; no minority recruitment targets; Chicago South Side has zero trial sites | 🔴 High |
| **Gender** | 90%+ male enrollment in most prosthetics trials — women effectively excluded | 🔴 High |
| **Pediatric** | Zero pediatric-specific prosthetic trials found | 🔴 High |
| **Global South** | ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access; only US, France, Spain, UK represented | 🔴 High |
| **DME Access** | No DME/prosthetic vendors within 500 m of SRAL — primary site-level barrier for participants | 🔴 High |

### Broader Demographics of Trial Distribution

| Country | Active Trials |
|---|---|
| United States | 110 |
| France | 12 |
| Spain | 7 |
| United Kingdom | 3 |
| Iceland | 2 |
| Norway | 1 |
| Mexico | 1 |
| Italy | 1 |
| Belgium | 3 |
| **All other nations** | **0** |

> **Key takeaway:** Prosthetic trial access is heavily concentrated in the US and Western Europe. The Global South and large portions of the Americas, Africa, and Asia have virtually no trial infrastructure.

## 4. Underserved Groups Missing from Trials

### Groups with Documented Underrepresentation

| Group | Evidence | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Women** | <10% enrollment in most prosthetics trials; no female-specific outcome measures | Sex-specific fit/function data missing |

| **Black/African American** | Zero Chicago South Side trial sites; no targeted recruitment | Missing from urban trial infrastructure |
| **Rural populations** | 0 trials in rural Midwest/South; only rural-serving VA study found (NCT05884203, completed) | 33% of Veterans underserved |
| **Pediatric (ages 0–17)** | Zero pediatric-specific trials; no child-friendly protocols | Growing amputee pediatric population has no options |

## 7. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME Unit
- **Description:** Deploy a mobile prosthetics van to SRAL on a monthly basis, stocked with residual-limb scanners, fittings, and commonly needed components.
- **Target:** Uninsured/underinsured individuals in Streeterville, Gold Coast, and South Side communities.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$15K/year (vehicle lease + staffing + supplies)
- **Timeline:** Q3 2026 pilot

### Strategy 2: Language Access Program
- **Description:** Produce Spanish-language consent forms, recruit bilingual care coordinators, and partner with South Side community health centers serving Hispanic/Latino populations.
- **Target:** Hispanic/Latino amputee communities on Chicago's South and West Sides.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$5K (translation + coordinator stipend)
- **Timeline:** Immediate — forms can be produced within 30 days

### Strategy 3: FQHC Partnership Network
- **Description:** Establish referral pipelines with Federally Qualified Health Centers on the South and West Sides (e.g., LBolt Health Center, Armenian Health Services) for prosthetic trial screening.
- **Target:** Post-institutionalization amputees rejected from traditional prosthetics due to complexity or cost.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$10K (MOUs, outreach materials, coordinator time)
- **Timeline:** Q3–Q4 2026

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach
- **Description:** Virtual consult sessions connecting SRAL prosthetists with VA rural sites (e.g., Tomah, Marion, Coon Rapids) to reduce in-person visit burden for rural Veterans.
- **Target:** Rural Veterans who travel 2+ hours for prosthetic care.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$8K (telehealth platform, pilot coordinator)
- **Timeline:** Q4 2026

### Strategy 5: Pediatric Prosthetic Trial
- **Description:** Design a child-friendly prosthetic trial (ages 8–17) with developmentally appropriate outcome measures, family support, and school-based recruitment.
- **Target:** Pediatric amputees (congenital and acquired) — currently zero trials exist.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$50K (IRB amendment, child-friendly device kits, recruitment)
- **Timeline:** Q1 2027 (requires IRB approval)

### Strategy 6: Geriatric Prosthetic Protocol
- **Description:** Create an outcomes trial specifically for adults 75+, addressing fall risk, comfort, and ease of donning/doffing — variables often excluded from standard trials.
- **Target:** Older adult amputees, many with comorbidities (diabetes, vascular disease, arthritis).
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$30K (specialized equipment, geriatric-coordinator)
- **Timeline:** Q1 2027

### Strategy 7: Female Recruitment Initiative
- **Description:** Set enrollment targets (minimum 30% female), partner with women's health organizations, and incorporate sex-specific outcome measures.
- **Target:** Women with lower-limb loss — currently <10% of trial participants.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$5K (partnerships, recruitment materials)
- **Timeline:** Immediate
