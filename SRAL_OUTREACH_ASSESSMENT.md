## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: "Bridging the Gap" — Community Trial Navigators
- **Target:** South/West Side Chicago (Englewood, Austin, East Garfield Park)
- **Action:** Hire 2–3 CHWs from target neighborhoods to assist prosthetic users with trial screening, transportation, and consent
- **Budget:** ~$90,000/yr
- **Metric:** ≥15 new participants from underserved zip codes/year

### Strategy 2: "Prosthetic Supply Corridor" — On-Site DME Kiosk
- **Target:** Eliminate the 500 m DME gap
- **Action:** Partner with Hanger Clinic or Ottobock for weekly pop-up clinic in SRAL lobby; or permanent satellite fitting station
- **Budget:** $15K/yr (pop-up) or $50K setup + $20K/yr (permanent)
- **Metric:** ≥50 fittings/adjustments per quarter

### Strategy 3: "Warm Hands, Warm Access" — Thermal Comfort Program
- **Action:** Heated waiting area with thermal prosthetic liners; winter prosthetic kits for participants
- **Budget:** ~$33,000/yr ($25K HVAC one-time + annual supplies)
- **Metric:** ≥90% participant comfort rating in winter

### Strategy 4: "South Side Transit Pipeline"
- **Action:** CTA paratransit shuttle for clinic days; tele-trial model (virtual consent, remote training, local provider check-ins)
- **Budget:** ~$60,000/yr
- **Metric:** ≥10 South Side participants per study/year; ≥75% tele-trial retention

### Strategy 5: "Global Equity Bridge"
- **Target:** 80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access
- **Action:** MOUs with prosthetics programs in Colombia, India, Pakistan for low-cost standardized protocols
- **Budget:** $50K start / $50K/yr operational
- **Metric:** ≥3 international partner sites; ≥20 participants from partner sites by Year 2

### Strategy 6: "Mental Health & Acceptance"
- **Action:** PHQ-2 screening at trial intake; partner with Medrina for monthly peer support groups; embed mental health in trial protocols
- **Budget:** ~$20,000/yr
- **Metric:** ≥95% PHQ-2 screening completion; ≥70% peer group attendance

### Strategy 7: "Women, Children & Gender-Diverse"
- **Action:** Equal-platform eligibility criteria; partner with Lurie Children's for pediatric sub-study; gender-inclusive language
- **Budget:** $35K start / $15K/yr
- **Metric:** ≥25% female enrollment; ≥1 pediatric participant per trial

## 6. Summary & Recommendations

1. **The prosthetic trial ecosystem is heavily skewed toward academic/industry sponsors, affluent urban populations, and early-stage device studies.** Only 9.6% of trials are federally sponsored (best positioned for underserved access), and 70% are Phase NA with no formal efficacy stage.

2. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is an excellent candidate site** — 10/10 walkability, 9.4/10 transit, multiple pharmacies, and an active prosthetics trial already running there.

3. **The most critical site gap is the absence of any DME/prosthetic vendor within 500 m**, which directly undermines trial retention for prosthetic users.

4. **Winter thermal discomfort** is a documented but unaddressed barrier for this population during Lake Michigan winters.

5. **Seven outreach strategies totaling ~$315K/yr** address geographic, socioeconomic, cultural, and infrastructure gaps — with the strongest ROI on Strategies 2 (DME corridor) and 4 (transit pipeline).
