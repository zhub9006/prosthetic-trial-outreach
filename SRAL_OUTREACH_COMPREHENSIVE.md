> **Date:** July 2026

## Executive Summary

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (SRAL) is the world's leading research hospital for physical medicine and rehabilitation — and it is actively recruiting for at least one lower-limb prosthetic trial. Located in the Streeterville / Near North Side neighborhood, the site has **strong transit access**, **24/7 pharmacies**, and **walkable urban amenities**, but faces critical gaps in:

- **No DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m** — the number-one site-level barrier for a prosthetic-user outreach hub
- **No parks or social services within 500m**
- **Zero parking options designed for ADA-accessible vehicles within 200m**
- **Enrollment gap for minority, rural, female, older-adult, and pediatric populations** in the broader trial landscape

This document synthesizes trial-gap analysis, site accessibility data, and proposed outreach strategies.

## Part 4: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Prosthetic Access Station at SRAL (Immediate — Low Cost)

**The problem:** Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m of the outreach site.

**Solution:** Partner with a major O&P provider (e.g., Hanger Clinic, Ohio Orthotics & Prosthetics) to establish a **weekly walk-in access station** at SRAL (or a partner pharmacy suite on the same block).

- Drop-in hours 2× / week (e.g., Tuesday AM, Thursday PM)
- Prosthetic inventory display, socket fitting demonstration, residual-limb scanning
- Staffed by a certified O&P prosthetist + community peer mentor

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Partners | 1–2 O&P providers |
| Hours/week | 4–6 |
| Staff | 1 prosthetist (part-time) + 1 peer mentor |
| Cost | ~$25K/yr (stipends + materials) |
| Impact | PRIMARY barrier removal; 50–100 visits/mo |

### Strategy 2: CTA Reduced-Fare & Divvy Adaptive Campaign (Immediate — Low Cost)

- Set up an information booth at SRAL's main entrance
- Dist CTA reduced-fare application forms + Divvy Adaptive bike-share info (5 stations <500m)
- Train clinical staff to guide patients through the application process

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Applications facilitated | 30/month by month 6 |
| Cost | ~$5K/yr (printing + staff time) |

### Strategy 3: Peer Mentor Program (Immediate — Low Cost)

- 10 experienced prosthetic users paired with newly amputated patients
- Meet-ups at SRAL cafeteria or nearby Panera (already within walking distance)
- Monthly "trial and tech" showcases featuring NCT05644522 device demonstrations

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Active mentors | 10 |
| New patient matches | 5–10/month |
| Cost | ~$10K/yr (stipends, coffee, space) |

### Strategy 4: Data Transparency Dashboard (Immediate — $20K setup)

Build an open-source dashboard on GitHub showing:
- Trial demographics & gaps (the analysis in this doc)
- SRAL site accessibility map
- Outreach metrics (enrollment, diversity, attendance)

### Strategy 5: Mobile Screening Events — Midwest & South Side (6–12 months — $250K/yr)

- Deploy VA/FQHC mobile units to free clinic stops in Chicago South Side (~10 km) and rural Midwest VA clinics
- Screen for prosthetic trials; provide device demos; connect people to NCT05644522 and NCT03409133
- Target: 200 evaluations / yr in South Side clinics; 100 / yr in rural VA sites

### Strategy 6: Spanish-Language Outreach (6–12 months — $40K/yr)

- Hire a **Promotora** (community health worker) fluent in Spanish and English
- Translate NCT05644522 and NCT03409133 consent forms into Spanish
- Partner with community health centers on Chicago's South and West sides
- Target: 50 Spanish-speaking evaluations / yr

### Strategy 7: Industry-Academic Pipeline (6–12 months — $100K/yr)

- Negotiate pro bono device donations from prosthetics manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Parker Hannifin)
- Deploy devices to underserved site(s) along with recruitment infrastructure
- Create a "device-in-the-community" bridge between SRAL trials and underserved users

### Strategy 8: Older Adult Programming (6–18 months — $30K/yr)

- Partner with senior centers on the Near North Side
- Provide caregiver stipends, in-home assessments, and accessible transport
- Geriatric-friendly trial screens (relaxed exclusion criteria, poor-health-friendly)
- Target: 30 older-adult evaluations / yr

### Strategy 9: Tele-Trial Hub (6–12 months — $35K/yr)

- Virtual trial visits (telehealth-enabled eligibility screening)
- Mail-in device checks and physical therapy sessions
- Expand trial reach to rural Illinois, Indiana, Missouri where SRAL is the only prosthetics research hub

### Strategy 10: South Side Permanent Office (12+ months — $350K/yr)

- Establish a satellite community engagement office on the South Side (e.g., Roseland or Auburn Gresham)
- 24/7 presence during business hours; dedicated staff for minority recruitment
- Serve as a recruitment hub connecting ~150K residents to SRAL trials

### Strategy 11: Rural Outreach via VA Network (12+ months)

- Partner with VA clinics in IL, MO, IN for quarterly mobile prosthetics labs
- Bring device demos and trial screening to rural veteran populations

### Strategy 12: Mental Health Integration (12+ months — $20K/yr)

- Partner with existing clinical sites (Medrina psychiatrist, VA clinic) for embedded peer support & support groups
- Address psychosocial barriers to trial participation (fear, stigma, depression)
