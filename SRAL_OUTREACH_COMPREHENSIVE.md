## Executive Summary

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (SRAL) is the world's leading research hospital for physical medicine and rehabilitation — and it is actively recruiting for at least one lower-limb prosthetic trial. Located in the Streeterville / Near North Side neighborhood, the site has **strong transit access**, **24/7 pharmacies**, and **walkable urban amenities**, but faces critical gaps in:

- **No DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m** — the number-one site-level barrier for a prosthetic-user outreach hub
- **No parks or social services within 500m**
- **Zero parking options designed for ADA-accessible vehicles within 200m**
- **Enrollment gap for minority, rural, female, older-adult, and pediatric populations** in the broader trial landscape

This document synthesizes trial-gap analysis, site accessibility data, and proposed outreach strategies.

## Part 4: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Prosthetic Access Station at SRAL (Immediate — Low Cost)

**The problem:** Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m of the outreach site.

**Solution:** Partner with a major O&P provider (e.g., Hanger Clinic, Ohio Orthotics & Prosthetics) to establish a **weekly walk-in access station** at SRAL (or a partner pharmacy suite on the same block).

- Drop-in hours 2× / week (e.g., Tuesday AM, Thursday PM)
- Prosthetic inventory display, socket fitting demonstration, residual-limb scanning
- Staffed by a certified O&P prosthetist + community peer mentor

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Partners | 1–2 O&P providers |
| Hours/week | 4–6 |
| Staff | 1 prosthetist (part-time) + 1 peer mentor |
| Cost | ~$25K/yr (stipends + materials) |
| Impact | PRIMARY barrier removal; 50–100 visits/mo |

### Strategy 2: CTA Reduced-Fare & Accessibility Campaign

**The opportunity:** CTA provides reduced-fare transit cards for people with disabilities. Many prosthetic users are unaware.

- Set up an information booth at SRAL's main entrance
- Dist CTA application forms + Divvy Adaptive bike-share info (5 stations <500m)
- Train clinical staff to guide patients through the application process
- Partner with CTA for "Rehab Day" rides where patients can test accessible buses

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Applications facilitated | 30/month by month 6 |
| Cost | ~$5K/yr (printing + staff time) |

### Strategy 3: Peer Mentor Program

- Recruit 5–10 experienced prosthetic users (broad demographics) from the SRAL catchment area
- Pair with newly amputated patients during their hospital stay
- Monthly peer support group at SRAL
- Compensation: $25/session + transportation reimbursement

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Active mentors | 10 |
| New matches | 6–8/month |
| Cost | ~$15K/yr |

### Strategy 4: Community Outreach Events at Neighborhood Anchors

Transportation to SRAL is strong, but some populations (elderly, cognitively impaired, low-income) may not know about the facility. Take the message out:

- **Monthly**: Set up a booth at Encanto Park, Divvy Station activation events, VA Clinic lobby
- **Quarterly**: Open house at SRAL with prosthetic demos, patient stories, trial enrollment info

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Events/month | 1 |
| Reach/event | 30–50 |
| Cost | ~$5K/yr |

### Strategy 5: Recruitment Pipeline for Underserved Groups

Address the specific enrollment gaps identified in the trial landscape:

| Gap | Concrete Action |
|---|---|
| **African American** | Partner with Provident Hospital / South Side ENT groups; 24/7 PR coverage for South Side |
| **Hispanic** | Spanish-language materials (now 0 translations); partner with community health workers (promotoras) on the West Side |
| **Rural** | Place recruitment ads at COA senior centers; partner with VA Mobile Outpatient Clinics (IL, IN, MO) |
| **Female** | Explicit recruitment targets in trial protocols; women's peer mentors |
| **Older Adult** | Caregiver transport subsidies; home-based assessment visits |
| **Medicaid / Low Income** | Slot reservation for Medicaid patients; co-pay reimbursement for trial visits |
| **Pediatric** | Partner with Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital for adolescent amputee outreach |

### Strategy 6: Data Transparency Dashboard

- Build an open-source dashboard on this GitHub repo
- Show: current trial enrollment, demographics (race/gender/age/insurance), geographic reach, wait times
- Updates monthly from ClinicalTrials.gov API

## Part 6: Summary Assessment

### Strengths of SRAL as an Outreach Hub

| Strength | Rating |
|---|---|
| Proximity to trauma/amputation centers | ✅ 5/5 — VA Clinic, Northwestern, Northwestern Medicine ICD |
| Public transit (buses, Divvy) | ✅ 4/5 — 10+ bus stops, 6 Divvy stations, Red Line ~1km |

### Weaknesses Requiring Immediate Action

| Gap | Severity |
|---|---|
| No DME / O&P vendors within 500m | 🔴 **CRITICAL** |
| No parks or outdoor social space | 🟠 Important |
| No EV charging (growing relevance for powered devices) | 🟡 Growing |
| No free ADA parking within 200m | 🟡 Moderate |
| Zero Spanish-language materials (default assumption) | 🔴 **CRITICAL** |
| No minority recruitment pipeline built into SRAL trials | 🔴 **CRITICAL** |

### Overall Assessment

**Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is a strong candidate for a prosthetic trial outreach hub**, with one key actionable change needed: **bringing a prosthetic vendor on-site or onto the block.** All other deficits (transit, pharmacy, weather protection for waiting, peer-support space) are addressable with modest investment.
