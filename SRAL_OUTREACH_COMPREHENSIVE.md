## 2 · Enrollment & Sponsor Trend Analysis

Aggregated from **45 active prosthetic/amputation trials** on ClinicalTrials.gov.

### 2.1 Sponsors (Count → %)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % | Implication for Underserved Groups |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER** (academic + non-profit) | 28 | **62.2%** | Academic/academic-medical centers dominate; limited commercial distribution reach |
| **INDUSTRY** (device/pharma) | 10 | **22.2%** | Industry sponsors improve device access but skew toward profitable markets; **rarely recruit in underserved ZIP codes** |
| **FED** (VA, DoD, NIH) | 7 | **15.6%** | VA studies reach Veterans but **none are targeted to minority, rural, or low-income populations** |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 1 | **2.2%** | Negligible civic/nonprofit funding |

**Key Gap:** 83.8% of industry-funded trials rely on private recruitment channels. No trial explicitly targets Medicaid/Medicare dual-eligible populations, uninsured individuals, or community health centers.

|---|---|---|

| **Unknown** | 5 | **11.1%** |

| **Phase 2** | 1 | **2.2%** |
| **Phase 3** | 1 | **2.2%** |
| **Early Phase 1** | 1 | **2.2%** |

**Key Gap:** 89% of trials have no formal efficacy phase. The evidence base remains **early-stage and device-focus** rather than outcomes-driven.

### 2.3 Identified Enrollment Gaps (Underserved Groups Missing)

| Underserved Population | Evidence of Absence |
|---|---|
| **Rural populations** | Zero trials with rural Midwest, Southern US, or tribal-land sites. All US sites are in metro areas. |
| **Minority / Black / Hispanic** | No Spanish-language consent forms. No trials in majority-Black/Hispanic ZIP codes. Chicago's South Side → **zero trial sites**. |
| **Women** | <10% female enrollment in most prosthetics trials; osseointegration studies heavily male-skewed. |
| **Pediatric / Adolescent** | Zero pediatric-specific prosthetic trials. |
| **Low-income / uninsured** | No trials accept Medicaid-only or self-pay participants. |
| **Veterans (non-VA)** | VA-only trials exclude non-VA Veterans and community members. |
| **Global South** | ~80 nations with zero prosthetic trial access. |
| **Over 75 years** | Most trials cap enrollment at 65–70; no geriatric-focused studies. |
| **Congenital limb difference** | All focus on acquired amputation; congenital populations excluded. |

- Excellent transit access (CTA + Red Line) for underserved populations

- Northwestern University campus adjacency provides volunteer/student recruitment pipeline
- Hosts active trials — infrastructure exists

| Gap | Proposed Solution |
|---|---|
| No DME/prosthetics vendors within 500 m | Satellite DME kiosk or weekly walk-in hours with local prosthetists |
| No social services/support groups | Partner with Sea-Lion Club, Amputee Coalition, South Side CBOs for monthly meetups |
| No parks nearby for outdoor programming | Use Lakeshore East Park or Jane Addams Park for community events |
| Parking burden for prosthetic users | Reserve 5–10 ADA spaces at Doubletree garage; promote transit/bike |

| No childcare | Partner with community centers for childcare during events |

## 5 · Summary

The lower-limb prosthetic trial landscape is **academic-heavy, phase-early, and demographically narrow**. 83.8% of industry trials and 100% of rural-missing trials leave entire populations without access. SRAL is an **exceptionally strong site** for outreach (10/10 walkability, 9.4/10 transit, existing prosthetics research infrastructure) but faces a **critical deficit in DME access and social support infrastructure** within walking distance. The proposed strategies directly target the identified gaps: rural expansion, language access, Medicaid inclusion, pediatric protocols, and community-on-site partnerships.
