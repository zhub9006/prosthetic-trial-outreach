## Part 3 — Proposed Community Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetic Outreach Unit
**Problem:** Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500m of SRAL.  
**Solution:** Deploy a mobile prosthetic fitting and consultation van to SRAL's address during peak clinical hours. Partner with local DME suppliers (e.g., Becker Orthopedic, TNW Orthotics in greater Chicago) for weekly pop-up clinics.  
**Estimated cost:** $15K–$30K/month (van rental + staffing)  
**Impact:** Directly addresses the #1 site-level barrier; increases prosthetic user trial enrollment from underserved neighborhoods.

### Strategy 2: Culturally & Linguistically Tailored Recruitment
**Problem:** No Spanish-language consent; zero recruitment on Chicago's South Side; 90%+ male enrollment.  
**Solution:**  
- Translate all trial materials into Spanish, Polish, and Chinese (top Chicago non-English languages)  
- Partner with community centers on the South Side (e.g., Bronzeville Community Health Center, Mt. Sinai Medical Center outreach)  
- Establish a **"Women & Prosthetics"** outreach track with female-facing recruitment materials and peer navigators  
- Recruit bilingual community health workers (promotores de salud) as trial navigators  
**Estimated cost:** $25K–$50K (translation, materials, part-time navigators)  
**Impact:** Expands eligible population; addresses documented racial/gender/language enrollment gaps.

### Strategy 3: Transit-Accessible Trial Navigation Program
**Problem:** SRAL is transit-accessible but unmetered parking is scarce; disabled prosthetic users face challenges getting to and from visits.  
**Solution:**  
- Offer **CTA transit passes or rideshare vouchers** (via Uber Health / Lyft Healthcare) for trial participants  
- Coordinate with CTA on **accessible paratransit** schedules for trial appointment blocks  
- Create a **"SRAL Challenge"** — a gamified, step-counting program using the Nomad P-KAFO sensor data to engage participants  
**Estimated cost:** $10K–$20K/month (transit subsidies, rideshare credits)  
**Impact:** Reduces no-show rates; improves retention among low-income participants.

### Strategy 4: Rural & Global South Virtual Trial Expansion
**Problem:** Zero trials in rural US or Global South; ~80 nations without prosthetic trial access.  
**Solution:**  
- Launch a **tele-rehabilitation arm** of active trials, allowing remote prosthetic gait monitoring via smartphone apps  
- Partner with NGOs (e.g., Range of Motion Project, Empowering The Handicapped) for data collection in LMICs  
- Use the VA's existing telehealth infrastructure to enroll rural veteran participants  
**Estimated cost:** $50K–$100K (platform development, NGO MOUs, telehealth setup)  
**Impact:** Dramatically expands geographic reach; addresses the most severe equity gap.

### Strategy 5: Pediatric & Geriatric Trial Pilots
**Problem:** No pediatric prosthetic trials; elderly and multi-morbid populations underrepresented.  
**Solution:**  
- Design a **pediatric adaptation** of the Nomad P-KAFO trial, child-friendly consent/assent process  
- Develop a **geriatric-focused** supplementary study examining prosthetic fit, falls prevention, and comorbid pain management  
- Partner with Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital and ECMC (Erie County Medical Center) for recruitment  
**Estimated cost:** $75K–$150K (study design, IRB amendments, site expansion)  
**Impact:** Fills the most glaring enrollment gaps; creates precedent for inclusive prosthetics research.

### Strategy 6: SRAL Community Hub Model
**Problem:** No parks, green space, or social services within 500m; limited community gathering infrastructure.  
**Solution:**  
- Convert a portion of SRAL's existing space into a **"Prosthetic Community Hub"** — offering support groups, peer mentoring, vocational rehab referrals  
- Host **monthly open houses** with free prosthetic check-ups, DME demos, and trial information  
- Partner with the **Northwestern University Law Library** (~350m) for legal aid (insurance navigation, disability rights)  
- Establish a **price-wrapped pharmacy partnership** with Walgreens to offer trial-related medications/supplies at reduced cost  
**Estimated cost:** $30K–$60K (space retrofit, programming, partnerships)  
**Impact:** Builds sustained community trust; transforms one-time trial visits into ongoing engagement.

### Strategy 7: Industry Partnership for Underserved Access
**Problem:** Only 25% industry-sponsored; technology access limited for low-income/uninsured prosthetic users.  
**Solution:**  
- Approach **Össur, Ottobock, and BraunAbility** for a **"Open Access Prosthetics"** pilot — providing free or subsidized prosthetic components to trial participants from underserved backgrounds  
- Negotiate **outcome-based contracts** where industry sponsors fund DME for participants who complete trials  
- Create a **prosthetic device lending library** at SRAL so participants can trial new technologies without purchase barriers  
**Estimated cost:** $50K–$200K (device acquisition, inventory management; offset by industry sponsorship)  
**Impact:** Bridges the technology access gap; incentivizes industry to invest in underserved populations.
