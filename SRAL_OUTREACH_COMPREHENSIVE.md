## 4. Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME Prosthetic Unit
- **Rationale:** Zero DME vendors within 500 m is the #1 site-level barrier.
- **Implementation:** Monthly 1-day pop-up clinic with a licensed prosthetist offering socket adjustments, liner replacements, and basic assessments.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$15,000/yr (vehicle lease, prosthetist stipend, supplies).
- **Target:** Uninsured, Medicaid, and South Side referral populations.

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Minority Recruitment
- **Rationale:** Zero Spanish-language trials; zero minority enrollment.
- **Implementation:** Produce Spanish consent forms; hire bilingual outreach coordinator; partner with community health centers on Chicago's South and West Sides.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$8,000/yr (translator services, coordinator salary supplement).

### Strategy 3: FQHC & Community Health Center Partnerships
- **Rationale:** South/West Side residents have 20%+ amputation rates (diabetes-driven) but no trial access.
- **Implementation:** Formal referral MOUs with 5 South/West Side FQHCs; embedded trial navigator.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$25,000/yr (navigator, transportation vouchers, partnership coordination).

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach
- **Rationale:** Rural veterans and Midwestern amputees face 60+ mile travel to VA prosthetic sites.
- **Implementation:** Virtual consults connecting SRAL prosthetists with VA rural sites; GPS-validated home exercise programs delivered via tablet.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$12,000/yr (tablet devices, telehealth platform license).

### Strategy 5: Pediatric Prosthetic Trial Design
- **Rationale:** ZERO pediatric-specific prosthetic trials in the current landscape.
- **Implementation:** Design a child-friendly trial (ages 8–17) with gamified outcome measures, school-based enrollment, and family travel subsidies.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$50,000 seed funding (grant application + initial cohort).

### Strategy 6: Geriatric Prosthetic Outcomes Protocol
- **Rationale:** Age-related Fit issues (residual limb volume loss, skin fragility) are unaddressed.
- **Implementation:** Prospective observational study of socket fit and skin outcomes in adults 75+; partner with SRAL's geriatric rehab program.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$30,000 (IRB, device rental, data collection).

### Strategy 7: Female-Specific Recruitment & Fit Protocol
- **Rationale:** 90%+ male enrollment in existing trials; female amputees report different fit and satisfaction outcomes.
- **Implementation:** Actively recruit women through women's health organizations; develop female-specific socket assessment criteria.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$10,000 (marketing, partnership fees).

## 7. Conclusion

The prosthetic clinical trial landscape in the United States is dominated by **academic (62%) and federal (16%) sponsors** with virtually **no industry presence in underserved populations** and **zero civic/nonprofit funding**. The evidence base is extremely thin (95% of trials in NA/Unknown phase, only 1 Phase 3 trial). Critical populations — rural residents, people of color, women, children, and the uninsured — are entirely excluded.

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is an excellent **physical site** (10/10 walkability, 9.4/10 transit, 24/7 pharmacy, wheelchair-accessible) for outreach, but it has a **critical DME access gap** (zero vendors within 500 m) and **zero parks/social services** in the immediate radius. The proposed strategies — especially the Mobile DME Unit and FQHC partnerships — directly address these gaps with realistic budgets.
