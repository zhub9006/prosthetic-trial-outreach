### 2.1 Trial Gap Summary

- **Zero trials based in Chicago** — the 6th-largest US metro with ~100K amputees
- **Zero trials in Illinois beyond VA Chicago** (a federal site with limited community visibility)
- **All US trials clustered on coasts** (NY, WA, NC, TX, NV) — Midwest entirely unrepresented
- **Only 1 trial targets fall-related outcomes** (NCT06937242) — a critical public health issue
- **No trials address(socket comfort/alignment monitoring in community settings** — all are lab-based
- **Smallest enrollment: 3** (ENABLE) — underpowered for generalizable findings
- **Largest US trial: 100** (MPK/Kenevo) — still modest for a Phase-equivalent study

## 4 · Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Priority 1 — Immediate (Budget: ~$111K/year)

#### P1.1 Prosthetic Access Station at SRAL 🔴
- **Problem:** Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500 m
- **Solution:** Partner with a local O&P provider (e.g., Hanger, Otto Bock,翻新) to station a prosthetic technician at SRAL 1–2 days/week for walk-in socket adjustments, component checks, and wear-time consultations
- **Cost:** ~$30K/yr (vendor subsidy + space in SRAL lobby)
- **Timeline:** 3–6 months
- **Impact:** Directly eliminates the #1 site barrier; converts SRAL from research-only to service-access hub

#### P1.2 CTA Reduced-Fare & Divvy Adaptive Campaign
- **Problem:** Prosthetic users face transit cost and physical barriers
- **Solution:** 
  - Apply for CTA Reduced Fare permits for trial participants (existing program, apply at CTA website)
  - Partner with Divvy to promote adaptive biking stations; provide Divvy annual passes for low-income users
  - Create transit guide pamphlet: "Getting to SRAL by Bus" with step-by-step accessibility notes
- **Cost:** ~$20K/yr (pass subsidies + printing)
- **Timeline:** 1–3 months

#### P1.3 Peer Mentor Program
- **Problem:** Newly amputated patients lack social support for trial participation
- **Solution:** Recruit 10 experienced prosthetic users (K3–K4) as paid peer mentors; match with newly amputated patients for trial orientation and companionship
- **Cost:** ~$40K/yr (stipends + training)
- **Timeline:** 3–6 months

#### P1.4 Data Transparency Dashboard
- **Problem:** Trial demographics are opaque; underserved groups don't know they're eligible
- **Solution:** Build an open-source dashboard on GitHub (this repo!) showing:
  - Active trials filtered by location, sponsor, eligibility
  - Demographic breakdown of enrollment where available
  - Plain-language trial summaries
  - "Find trials near me" tool
- **Cost:** ~$21K/yr (developer time + hosting)
- **Timeline:** 3–6 months

### Priority 2 — 6–12 Months (Budget: ~$250K/year)

#### P2.1 Mobile Screening Events
- **Problem:** South Side of Chicago (~10 km) and rural Midwest have zero trial access
- **Solution:** Deploy mobile screening unit (van with tablet-based consent, basic eligibility checks) to:
  - Chicago South Side community health centers (e.g., Community Health, Roseland)
  - VA Community-Based Outpatient Clinics (Cook County, Humboldt Park, etc.)
  - Midwest rural VA clinics (Decatur, IL; Quincy, IL; Marion, MO)
- **Cost:** ~$150K/yr (van lease + staff + mileage)
- **Timeline:** 6–12 months

#### P2.2 Spanish-Language Outreach
- **Problem:** Zero Spanish-language consent forms or outreach; Chicago's Hispanic communities have ~30% limited English proficiency
- **Solution:** 
  - Hire bilingual Community Health Worker (Promotora) model
  - Translate all SRAL materials into Spanish
  - Partner with national organizations like Amputee Coalition's Latino outreach
  - Host Spanish-language prosthetic education sessions at SRAL
- **Cost:** ~$50K/yr (staff + translation + events)
- **Timeline:** 6–12 months

#### P2.3 Industry-Academic Device Pipeline
- **Problem:** Underserved users can't access cutting-edge prosthetic technology during trials
- **Solution:** Negotiate pro bono device donations from industry sponsors (Otto Bock, Hanger, Össur) for deployment at SRAL and mobile sites
- **Cost:** ~$50K/yr (coordination + fitting)
- **Timeline:** 6–12 months

### Priority 3 — 6–12 Months (Budget: ~$30K/year)

#### P3.1 Older Adult Programming
- **Problem:** Most trials exclude seniors; age-related mobility and technology barriers
- **Solution:** 
  - Offer caregiver stipends ($50/session) for older participants
  - Conduct in-home pre-screening visits to assess eligibility
  - Partner with senior centers (e.g., Northrup Grumman Senior Center, 1724 W Montrose)
- **Cost:** ~$30K/yr (stipends + staff)
- **Timeline:** 6–12 months

#### P3.2 Tele-Trial Hub
- **Problem:** Geographic and mobility barriers prevent in-person screening
- **Solution:** 
  - Virtual consent and screening visits via HIPAA-compliant platform
  - Mail-in check-in kits for device usage logging
  - Team-based trials allowing remote participation in data collection
- **Cost:** ~$30K/yr (platform + coordinator)
- **Timeline:** 6–12 months

### Priority 4 — 12+ Months (Budget: ~$350K/year)

#### P4.1 South Side Permanent Office
- **Problem:** Chicago's South Side (predominantly Black/African American) has zero trial infrastructure
- **Solution:** Establish a satellite office at a South Side community health center (e.g., Community Health, Englewood) with:
  - Dedicated prosthetic/orthotic fitting space
  - Trial screening and consent
  - Peer mentor office hours
- **Cost:** ~$250K/yr (lease + build-out + staff + device inventory)
- **Timeline:** 12+ months

#### P4.2 Rural Outreach via VA Network
- **Problem:** Rural Midwest amputees face enormous travel barriers to trial sites
- **Solution:** Quarterly mobile prosthetics labs visiting VA outpatient clinics in IL, MO, IN:
  - Decatur IL, Quincy IL, Marion MO, Vincennes IN
  - On-site screening, device fitting, and basic rehabilitation assessments
- **Cost:** ~$100K/yr (van, staff, device consumables)
- **Timeline:** 12+ months

#### P4.3 Mental Health Integration
- **Problem:** Amputees experience elevated rates of depression (30–50%) and social isolation
- **Solution:** Integrate mental health support into SRAL outreach:
  - Peer support mentors with lived experience
  - Monthly support group at SRAL (partner with NAMI or local mental health provider)
  - On-site counselor at mobile events
- **Cost:** ~$200K/yr ( counselor + programming)
- **Timeline:** 12+ months
