### 2.4 Key Strengths for Outreach

- **Transit-first location**: Multiple CTA bus routes, Divvy bike stations, and proximity to the Chicago L (Red Line at Chicago/Grand, ~1 km)
- **Institutional anchor**: SRAL is a globally recognized rehabilitation research center — credibility and patient trust
- **Walkable streetscape**: ADA-compliant, flat, well-lit

- **Parking is paid and limited**: Financial burden for participants from underserved areas
- **No insurance navigation services on-site**: Critical gap — prosthetic coverage is complex and varies widely
- **Language barriers**: No multilingual staff or translated materials currently posted

## Section 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME Prosthetic Unit
- **Goal:** Bring a mobile prosthetist van to SRAL on a recurring schedule
- **Target:** Uninsured and underinsured prosthetic users (~$15K/yr savings)
- **Cost:** ~$15,000/yr (van lease + prosthetist salary stipend)
- **Timeline:** Q3 2026

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Cultural Competency
- **Goal:** Provide Spanish, Polish, Arabic, and Chinese consent forms + bilingual study coordinators
- **Target:** Chicago's 1st Ward Latino and Polish communities; refugee populations
- **Cost:** ~$25,000/yr (translation services + part-time bilingual staff)
- **Timeline:** Q4 2026

### Strategy 3: FQHC / Community Health Center Partnerships
- **Goal:** Establish referral pipeline from South and West Side FQHCs (e.g., Roseland Community Hospital, Sinai Urban Health Institute)
- **Target:** Uninsured, low-income, and Medicaid beneficiaries
- **Cost:** ~$40,000/yr (outreach coordinator + transportation vouchers)
- **Timeline:** Q3–Q4 2026

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach Program
- **Goal:** Virtual consult + tele-rehabilitation for rural VA and critical access hospital sites
- **Target:** Rural amputees with no local trial access
- **Cost:** ~$30,000/yr (telehealth platform + coordinator)
- **Timeline:** Q1 2027

### Strategy 5: Pediatric & Elder-Inclusive Trial Redesign
- **Goal:** Design trials with child-friendly protocols and expanded age ranges (12–85)
- **Target:** Pediatric amputees and geriatric populations currently excluded
- **Cost:** ~$250,000 startup (protocol redesign + IRB + pediatric recruitment)
- **Timeline:** Q2–Q4 2027

### Strategy 6: SRAL On-Site Resource Corner
- **Goal:** Install a permanent info desk with DME samples, insurance navigation, walk-in adjustments
- **Target:** All SRAL visitors — especially underserved prosthetic users
- **Cost:** ~$10,000 startup (furniture, signage, initial supplies)
- **Timeline:** Q4 2026

### Strategy 7: Parking & Transportation Assistance
- **Goal:** Negotiate discounted parking with Doubletree garage; provide CTA Ventra subsidies for trial participants
- **Target:** Participants from low-income and car-free households
- **Cost:** ~$8,000/yr (10 monthly parking passes + 50 Ventra cards)
- **Timeline:** Q3 2026
