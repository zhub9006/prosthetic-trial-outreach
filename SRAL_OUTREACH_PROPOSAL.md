## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetic Supply Van

**Problem:** No specialty pharmacy or DME vendor within 500 m. Users must travel 1+ miles for liners, socks, and skin care — a daily barrier.  
**Solution:** Deploy a refrigerated mobile van with prosthetic supplies (silicone liners, shrinker socks, blister pads, cleansers) staffed by a certified orthotist/prosthetist.  
**Target population:** Uninsured/underinsured prosthetic users, Medicaid recipients, low-income seniors.  
**Estimated cost:** ~$15,000/year (van lease + supplies + staff).  
**Location:** Park at SRAL's Erie St entrance or nearby Divvy station lots.

### 3.2 Strategy 2: Language Access & Cultural Competency

**Problem:** Zero non-English consent forms across all 104 trials. Chicago's Near North Side has large Polish, Chinese, and Spanish-speaking populations.  
**Solution:** Translate all outreach materials and consent forms into Spanish, Polish, Mandarin Chinese, and Arabic. Hire bilingual amputee community navigators.  
**Target population:** LEP communities, immigrant amputees, refugee populations.  
**Estimated cost:** ~$25,000 (translation + navigator salaries for 1 year).  
**Location:** SRAL + community centers in Chinatown, Pilsen, and Albany Park.

### 3.3 Strategy 3: FQHC & Community Health Center Partnerships

**Problem:** Trials recruit through academic medical centers, missing the safety-net population.  
**Solution:** Formal partnership with FQHCs on the South and West Sides (e.g.,ida North, Rosalind Franklin,Heartland Health Center). Train community health workers to screen and refer eligible amputees to trials.  
**Target population:** Medicaid recipients, uninsured, South/West Side residents.  
**Estimated cost:** ~$40,000/year (coordinator + travel + partner stipends).  
**Location:** South Side (Hyde Park, Woodlawn, Englewood) and West Side (Austin, North Lawndale).

### 3.4 Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach & Virtual Trial Screening

**Problem:** Zero rural trial sites in the state. Rural amputees have no access.  
**Solution:** Use telehealth platforms for initial screening, consent, and virtual check-ins. Partner with VA rural sites (e.g., Quincy, Marion, Decatur). Provide wearable sensors for at-home gait monitoring.  
**Target population:** Rural Illinois amputees, Illinois veterans in rural counties.  
**Estimated cost:** ~$30,000/year (telehealth platform + wearable devices + coordinator).  
**Location:** VA telehealth hubs, rural health clinics.

### 3.5 Strategy 5: Pediatric & Elder-Inclusive Trial Design

**Problem:** No pediatric prosthetic trials exist. Most trials use age 18+ eligibility, excluding adolescents and excluding cognitive/intervention studies for older adults.  
**Solution:** Design a pediatric-capable prosthetic trial with age 12+ eligibility. Include adaptive socket designs for growing limbs. Separate protocols for elderly (65+) with frailty considerations.  
**Target population:** Children/adolescents with limb loss, elderly amputees (>65).  
**Estimated cost:** ~$250,000 startup (IRB amendments, pediatric-specific equipment, smaller sockets, child life specialists).  
**Location:** SRAL pediatric rehab + Shriner's Hospital for Children — Chicago.

### 3.6 Strategy 6: SRAL On-Site Community Resource Corner

**Problem:** SRAL is a world-class rehab center but lacks a visible community-facing resource space for prosthetic users.  
**Solution:** Dedicate a small room or alcove at SRAL as a "Prosthetic Resource Corner" with:  
- Sample prosthetic feet/sockets for touch/feel evaluation  
- Insurance navigation guides (Medicare, Medicaid, private)  
- Walk-in adjustment hours with volunteer prosthetists  
- Peer support group meeting space (weekly)  
- Digital kiosk with trial matching tool  
**Target population:** All SRAL patients + walk-in community amputees.  
**Estimated cost:** ~$10,000 startup (furniture, kiosk, supplies, volunteer coordination).  
**Location:** SRAL main lobby or outpatient wing.

### 3.7 Strategy 7: Parking & Drop-Off Solutions

**Problem:** No ADA spots within 200 m; expensive paid garages; no drop-off zone.  
**Solution:**  
- Reserve 2–3 ADA spots immediately outside SRAL's Erie St entrance (~$2,000/year)  
- Coordinate with Doubletree garage for a dedicated残疾人 parking level and reduced rate (~$5,000/year)  
- Establish a clearly marked drop-off zone with an awning for weather protection  
- Create a "Prosthetic Transport Voucher" program with nearby accessible rideshare  
**Target population:** Prosthetic users with mobility limitations, low-income users.  
**Estimated cost:** ~$10,000/year.  
**Location:** SRAL Erie St entrance and Doubletree garage.

### 3.8 Strategy 8: Adaptive Cycling & Active Mobility Program

**Problem:** Divvy bike stations are nearby (6 within 500 m), but standard bikes aren't usable for prosthetic users.  
**Solution:** Partner with Divvy to install 2–3 adaptive tricycle/hand-cycle docks near SRAL. Offer free adaptive cycling sessions for amputees as a rehabilitation and community-building activity.  
**Target population:** Active amputees, rehabilitation graduates, youth.  
**Estimated cost:** ~$10,000 startup (adaptive bike purchases + dock installation).  
**Location:** Divvy stations along McClurg Ct, Grand Ave, and Michigan Ave near SRAL.

## 5. Summary

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is an **excellent candidate** for a prosthetic trial outreach hub due to:
- ✅ World-class reputation and existing infrastructure
- ✅ Outstanding transit access (10+ bus stops, 6 Divvy stations)
- ✅ Flat, walkable, ADA-compliant neighborhood
- ✅ 24/7 pharmacy (Walgreens) and basic amenities nearby

However, **critical gaps must be addressed** before it can serve underserved populations:
- ❌ No DME or specialty prosthetic supply vendor nearby
- ❌ No ADA parking or drop-off zone
- ❌ Zero non-English materials in current trials
- ❌ No pediatric trials, no rural trial sites, no LEP outreach

The proposed strategies — mobile DME van, language access programs, FQHC partnerships, rural tele-outreach, and on-site resource corner — directly target these gaps and would position SRAL as a **national model** for inclusive prosthetic trial outreach.
