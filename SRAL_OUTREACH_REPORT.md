## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetist Van

- **Problem:** Zero DME vendors within 500 m — prosthetic users must travel 1–3 miles for supplies, adjustments, and fittings.
- **Solution:** Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetist van staffed by a licensed prosthetist and orthotist, offering on-site evaluations, socket adjustments, and supply distribution.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$15,000/year (vehicle lease + staffing)
- **Target Population:** Uninsured and underinsured prosthetic users in Streeterville, Near North Side, and broader Chicago.
- **Partnership Target:** Resurrection Health Care, Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago (RIC), local DME providers.

### 3.2 Strategy 2: Language Access & Cultural Competency

- **Problem:** Zero trials offer non-English consent forms; SRAL serves a diverse Chicago population including large Spanish, Polish, and Chinese-speaking communities.
- **Solution:**
  - Translate all outreach materials and consent forms into Spanish, Polish, Standard Chinese, and Arabic.
  - Hire bilingual community outreach coordinators.
  - Partner with community organizations (e.g., Hispanic American Business Development Association, Polish American Association, Chinese Mutual Aid Association).
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$25,000 (translation, hiring, community partnership stipends)

### 3.3 Strategy 3: FQHC & Community Health Center Partnerships

- **Problem:** 0 of 32 trials have outreach pipelines to Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) on the South and West Sides where prosthetic disparity is highest.
- **Solution:**
  - Establish formal referral partnerships with FQHCs (e.g., Roseland Community Hospital, Mercy Hospital, Stone Orchard Health Center).
  - Create a "prosthetic trial navigator" role embedded at partner FQHCs to screen eligible patients and facilitate enrollment.
  - Offer telehealth eligibility screening to reduce travel burden.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$40,000/year (navigator salary, telehealth platform)
- **Target Impact:** 50–100 new eligible participants per year from underserved neighborhoods.

### 3.4 Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach Program

- **Problem:** 0 rural trial sites in current lower limb prosthetic research.
- **Solution:**
  - Develop a virtual consult program pairing SRAL prosthetists with VA rural sites and Critical Access Hospitals.
  - Use telesurgery-compatible video platforms for socket fitting assessments.
  - Integrate with VA Prosthetic and Sensory Aids Service (PSAS) for veteran outreach.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$30,000/year (platform licensing, VR hardware, staffing)
- **Target Population:** Rural Illinois, Indiana, Wisconsin veterans and amputees.

### 3.5 Strategy 5: Pediatric & Elder-Inclusive Trial Design

- **Problem:** No pediatric prosthetic trials; most trials cap age at 18–84 and exclude bilateral amputees or those with comorbidities.
- **Solution:**
  - Design a new trial with age range 12–90, including pediatric and geriatric subgroups.
  - Include child-friendly prosthetic hides, adjustable growth sockets, and elder-inclusive mobility assessments.
  - Partner with Shriners Hospitals for Children and the Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$250,000 (trial startup; grant-funded preferred)
- **Target Population:** Children and older adults with lower limb amputation.

### 3.6 Strategy 6: SRAL as Community Hub — On-Site Services Expansion

- **Problem:** SRAL is a world-class rehab facility but lacks DME vendors, parking, and social services on-site.
- **Solution:**
  - Establish an on-site "Prosthetic Resource Corner" with DME samples, insurance navigation help, and walk-in adjustments.
  - Partner with the Chicago Transit Authority for a dedicated shuttle or discounted transit pass for prosthetic users attending appointments.
  - Advocate for ADA parking spots within 200 m through the Chicago DOT.
- **Estimated Cost:** ~$10,000 initial setup; ongoing operational costs shared with SRAL.
