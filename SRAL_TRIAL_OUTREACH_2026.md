## 1. ClinicalTrials.gov Trial Gap Analysis

### 1.1 Database Scope

Search terms and result counts:

| Search Term | Recruiting Studies | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `lower limb prosthetic` | 85 | Broad; includes hip/knee replacements, dental, hearing |
| `prosthetic rehabilitation` | 75 | Many false positives (dental, hearing, vascular) |

**After manual deduplication and filtering, ~32 actively recruiting studies are genuinely relevant to lower-limb prosthetics.** University of Pittsburgh IRB-approved surgeries are disproportionately referenced by commercial patent filings rather than in enrolled trials. No true Phase 1/2 device trials exist in the US for transfemoral or transtibial prosthetic components post-osseointegration — only a handful of foreign observational registries.

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME (Durable Medical Equipment) Prosthetic Van

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 0 DME vendors within 500m; amputees travel 2km+ for socket/pylon adjustments |
| **Solution** | Monthly mobile prosthetist van parked at SRAL lot; button-up/socket-fit checks |
| **Target Groups** | Uninsured (est. $15K/prosthetic cost), post-amputation patients from South/West Side |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$15K/year (van lease + prosthetist stipend + supplies) |
| **Timeline** | 3 months to secure vehicle; 6 months to hire licensed prosthetist |
| **Enrollment Link** | Directly feeds trans-tibial & transfemoral prosthetic trials (NCT06830876, NCT06134167) |

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Culturally Adapted Consent

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 0 multilingual consent forms; 0 non-English trials; Chicago's 28% foreign-born population (Polish, Spanish, Chinese, Arabic) excluded |
| **Solution** | Translate consent + trial materials into Spanish, Polish, Arabic, Mandarin; hire bilingual outreach coordinators at SRAL |
| **Target Groups** | Hispanic, Polish-American, Chinese-American, Middle Eastern amputee communities — highly underserved |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$25K (translation matrix + coordinator stipend + cultural competency training) |
| **Timeline** | 6 months for translation matrix; 9 months for bilingual coordinator hiring |
| **Enrollment Link** | Enables recruitment into international protocols (NCT06471855, NCT04934839) with language-supported extensions |

### Strategy 3: Federally Qualified Health Center (FQHC) Partnership Pipeline

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | South/West Side amputees have no trial awareness; FQHCs have geographic/poverty barriers to SRAL |
| **Solution** | Partner with Roseland, Provident, and John H. Stroger FQHCs for prosthetic screening; van-based referral from FQHC to SRAL |
| **Target Groups** | Medicaid-dependent limb Loss patients from Englewood, Austin, Brighton Park |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$40K/year (stipend for 2 FQHC community health workers; van fuel/stops) |
| **Timeline** | 6 months to establish MOU and CHW integration |
| **Enrollment Link** | Fills rural/underserved gap; creates demographic data for Dexa matching in auxological studies |

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach for VA & Older Amputees

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 0 rural US trial sites; VA has no prosthetic-specific RCTs; tele-rehabilitation is undefined in current trials |
| **Solution** | Partner with VA Illiana (Hines) & Marion for virtual prosthetic consults; telerehab arm in existing trial protocols |
| **Target Groups** | Rural veterans, homebound elderly amputees (missing from all trial eligibility) |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$30K/year (telehealth platform licensing; VA facilitator stipend; connectivity equipment) |
| **Timeline** | 4 months to activate VA connections; 8 months for tele-rehab protocol integration |
| **Enrollment Link** | Expands NCT04934839 (osseointegration) and NCT06830876 (NMES) to rural/geriatric populations |

### Strategy 5: Pediatric Prosthetic Trial Design (Child-Friendly Protocols)

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | 0 pediatric prosthetic trials; 100% age 18+ exclusion; children's prosthetics require different trial metrics |
| **Solution** | Design child-friendly prosthetic trial at SRAL; include elder-inclusive age ranges (12–17 with parental consent); use VR gaming for rehabilitation engagement |

## 4. Summary & Recommendations

### Top 3 Site Strengths for Outreach
1. **Exceptional transit density** — 48+ CTA bus stops, 6 Divvy stations, 24/7 access, flat terrain, ADA sidewalks
2. **World-class rehab hospital** — SRAL attracts 60K+ patients/year; built-in pre-screened amputee pool
3. **Downtown urban placement** — 10/10 walkability; grocery, pharmacy, bank, restaurant within 500m for families

### Top 3 Site Barriers Requiring Mitigation
1. **Zero DME vendors within 500m** — #1 barrier in site selection; requires mobile prosthetist van (Strategy 1)
2. **No ADA-designated parking spots within 200m** — low-clearance Doubletree garage is only paid option; requires accessible parking advocacy + van
3. **Zero parks/social services within 500m** — no outdoor prosthetic practice or peer support spaces; suggests satellite satellite park partnership with lakefront

### Overall Site Score: 5.4 / 10
- Transit: 10/10
- Walkability: 10/10
- Pharmacy: 4/10
- Parking: 4/10
- DME Vendors: 0/10
- Parks/Social Services: 0/10

### Clinical Trial Landscape Summary
- **32 genuine lower-limb prosthetic recruiting trials** (of 85 total search hits)
- **80% are observational/behavioral (Phase NA)** — no Phase 2–3 efficacy trials
- **73% academic-only** — industry hosts only 15% (13/85), most are foreign (Italy, Brazil)
- **0 pediatric trials, 0 rural trials, 0 multilingual trials, 0 nonprofit/civic-funded trials**
- **Key US trials**: NCT06830876 (transtibial NMES), NCT06134167 (transfemoral compression), NCT04934839 (osseointegration gait)
- **Key foreign trials**: NCT06471855 (MI: psychological + VR rehab), NCT04934839 (Italy: osseointegration vs socket)

### Recommended Path Forward
1. **Immediate (Weeks 1–4):** Establish SRAL Resource Corner (Strategy 6) → $10K startup
2. **Short-term (1–3 months):** Mobile DME Van lease (Strategy 1) + FQHC MOUs (Strategy 3) + Industry Fellow pitch (Strategy 7)
3. **Mid-term (3–9 months):** Language matrix (Strategy 2) + Rural tele-outreach (Strategy 4)
4. **Long-term (12–18 months):** Pediatric trial design (Strategy 5)
