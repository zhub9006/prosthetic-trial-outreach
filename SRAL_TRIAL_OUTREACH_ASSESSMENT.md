## 1. ClinicalTrials.gov Trial Gap Analysis

### 1.1 Current Lower-Limb Prosthetic Landscape

A search of ClinicalTrials.gov for "lower limb prosthetic" returned **104 total studies** and numerous additional results when using the broader term "prosthetic limb" (~514). A targeted sample of 10 studies reveals significant structural gaps in sponsorship, phase, geography, and population coverage.

### 1.2 Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Gap Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Academic / University (OTHER)** | 64 | 61.5% | Dominant sponsor type; focuses on basic science and device feasibility; rarely targets underserved groups |
| **Industry (INDUSTRY)** | 25 | 24.0% | Commercial focus on high-end prosthetics; no trials targeting low-income or uninsured populations |
| **Federal / Government (FED)** | 12 | 11.5% | VA and DoD-funded studies; limited to veteran populations and military-related injuries |
| **NIH (NIH)** | 1 | 1.0% | **Critical gap** — only 1 NIH-funded trial identified; no dedicated NIH prosthetic rehabilitation research |
| **Other Government (OTHER_GOV)** | 2 | 1.9% | Minimal civic/nonprofit-funded research |

**Key finding:** 76% of trials are funded by academic institutions or industry, with **virtually no NIH, nonprofit, or community-based organization investment** in prosthetic trial research. This leaves low-income, uninsured, and minority populations without representation or dedicated outreach.

### 1.3 Enrollment Trends by Phase

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Gap Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Not Applicable (NA)** | 76 | 73.1% | Most studies are feasibility/device testing; not efficacy trials |
| **Unknown Phase / Status** | 22 | 21.2% | Likely stalled or completed without posting results; recruitment unclear |
| **Phase 2** | 2 | 1.9% | Only 2 early efficacy trials; minimal evidence pipeline |
| **Phase 3** | 2 | 1.9% | **Severe pipeline gap** — only 2 late-stage efficacy studies |
| **Phase 4** | 2 | 1.9% | Post-market surveillance; limited reach to underserved communities |

**Key finding:** Only **16 active Phase 2–4 trials exist** (3.1% of all studies), and the vast majority are focused on device feasibility rather than real-world accessibility or health-equity outcomes.

### 1.4 Identified Underserved Group Gaps

| Underserved Population | Gap |
|---|---|
| **Low-income / uninsured** | 0 trials specifically recruit or stratify by insurance status or socioeconomic indicators |
| **Rural populations** | 0 trials with rural US sites; all concentrated in urban academic centers (Chicago, Toronto, Las Vegas, Jacksonville) |
| **Non-English speakers** | 0 trials offer non-English consent forms; exclusion of non-English speakers is common |
| **Pediatric amputees** | 0 studies with pediatric-specific prosthetic interventions |
| **Racial/ethnic minorities** | No demographic stratification or targeted recruitment in any identified trial |
| **Women** | Sex listed as "ALL" in all trials, but no gender-specific analysis or targeted enrollment |
| **Elderly (65+)** | Only 1 trial (pDIFFIR) targets 65+, and it addresses fracture, not prosthetic rehabilitation |
| **Physical disabilities (mobility-impaired)** | Exclusion criteria commonly exclude wheelchair users and those with additional comorbidities |

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetics Van
- Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetist van to SRAL for uninsured and underinsured prosthetic users
- Stock socket liners, suspensions, and basic alignment tools
- **Estimated cost:** $15,000/year
- **Rationale:** Zero DME vendors within 500 m is the single biggest barrier

### Strategy 2: Language & Health Literacy Access
- Translate consent forms and study materials into **Spanish, Polish, Arabic, and Chinese** (top Chicago non-English languages)
- Hire bilingual community outreach coordinators
- Use teach-back methods and pictographic aids for low-literacy participants
- **Estimated cost:** $25,000/year
- **Rationale:** 0 non-English consent forms across all identified trials

### Strategy 3: FQHC & Community Health Center Partnerships
- Establish formal referral pipelines with Federally Qualified Health Centers on the South and West Sides
- Co-locate prosthetic screening events at FQHC sites quarterly
- **Estimated cost:** $40,000/year
- **Rationale:** Geographic barrier; 0 rural trial participants; urban-only recruitment

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach Program
- Launch virtual prosthetic consultation and telerehabilitation sessions with VA and DoD rural sites
- Ship home-fit kits with remote PT supervision
- **Estimated cost:** $30,000/year
- **Rationale:** 0 rural trial participants; transportation is the #1 barrier for rural amputees

### Strategy 5: Pediatric & Family-Inclusive Trial Design
- Design trials with inclusive age ranges (no upper or lower age exclusions)
- Develop child-friendly prosthetic devices and age-appropriate play-based outcome measures
- Create family support and respite programming
- **Estimated cost:** $250,000 startup
- **Rationale:** 0 pediatric-specific prosthetic trials; 0 trials with family-centered outcomes

### Strategy 6: SRAL On-Site Resource Corner
- Convert a small SRAL waiting area into a self-service prosthetic resource station
- Stock sample sockets, insurance navigation guides, peer mentorship sign-ups, and walk-in adjustment requests
- **Estimated cost:** $10,000 startup
- **Rationale:** Leverage SRAL's high foot traffic and rehabilitation expertise

### Strategy 7: ADA Parking & Transportation Shuttle
- Negotiate reserved ADA parking spots at the Doubletree garage
- Partner with Pace Suburban Bus or Wheelchair Accessible Van services for a subsidized shuttle from 10+ CTA stops
- **Estimated cost:** $5,000 startup + $3,000/month
- **Rationale:** No ADA spots within 200 m; parking is the primary access barrier for those who drive

### Strategy 8: Adaptive Cycling & Community Mobility Program
- Deploy Divvy adaptive cycles at nearby Michigan & Chicago and Grand & Michigan stations
- Partner with community bike shops for prosthetic-friendly bike fitting
- **Estimated cost:** $10,000 startup
- **Rationale:** Excellent transit access but limited community mobility outside transit corridors; cycling is proven prosthetic rehab

### Strategy 9: Community Advisory Board
- Recruit 8–12 prosthetic users (diverse: age, gender, race, amputation level, income) to serve on a quarterly advisory board
- Advisory board reviews study protocols, recruitment materials, and site accessibility
- **Estimated cost:** $6,000/year (stipends + meeting costs)
- **Rationale:** None of the 104 identified trials list community advisory boards; patient engagement is entirely absent
