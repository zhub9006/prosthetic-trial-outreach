## Executive Summary

This report evaluates ClinicalTrials.gov enrollment data for lower limb prosthetic trials and assesses the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (355 E Erie St, Chicago, IL 60611) as a community outreach hub for prosthetic users.

**Key Takeaway:** The lower limb prosthetic trial ecosystem is dominated by industry-funded observational registries (70% of all studies). There are severe shortages in Phase 2–3 efficacy trials, civic/nonprofit-funded studies, pediatric trials, and Midwest—or any rural US—research sites. Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is an excellent physical site (walk score 10/10, 14+ CTA bus stops, 6 Divvybike stations within 500 m) but lacks nearby DME vendors and a dedicated prosthetic supply point within 500 m—making it a strong outreach programming hub but not a self-contained service site.

#### ⚠️ Gap #1 — Civic / Non-Profit / Community-Funded Trials

There are **zero** trials sponsored by civic organizations, community health networks, or patient advocacy groups for lower limb prosthetics. The FED category (~10%) is the only channel that systematically reaches uninsured/underinsured populations but its footprint is small.

#### ⚠️ Gap #2 — Industry Trial Design Barriers

Industry trials (Otto Bock Össur, Liberating Technologies) restrict enrollment to K3/K4 (community ambulators) and exclude K0/K1 (household/limited ambulators)—precisely the populations most likely to be low-income, elderly, or rural.

- **NCT04784429** (ASCENT K2): Otto Bock, 107 participants—K-level filtering excludes most underserved.
- **NCT07075198** (Caesar Foot Take-Home): Liberating Technologies, RECRUITING, 18 participants—K3/K4 only, tiny sample, no rural sites.

### 3.2 Outreach Strategies

#### A. Mobile DME & Prosthetic Outreach Unit (~$15K/yr)
Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetist van to underserved South and West Side neighborhoods (Englewood, Austin, Humboldt Park). Services:
- Socket checks and adjustments
- Liner/sock fittings
- Trial screening referrals to SRAL
- Spanish-speaking prosthetic specialist on rotation

**Partners:** Heartland Alliance, Roseland Community Hospital (FQHCs)

#### B. Language Access Program
- Translate all consent forms and participant materials into **Spanish** (and Mandarin, Polish)
- Hire bilingual prosthetic coordinators
- Train staff on cultural competency for LEP populations

#### C. FQHC Screening & Referral Network
- Formal referral agreements with South/West Side FQHCs
- One-page "Is your patient a candidate for a prosthetic trial?" toolkit
- Virtual intake for South/West Side patients (CTA bus routes serve Erie/McClurg corridor from South/West Side)

#### D. Rural Tele-Outreach
- Partner with VA rural sites in IL, IN, WI, IA
- Virtual prosthetic clinic visits via telehealth
- Ship trial devices with prepaid return shipping
- Monthly in-person clinic days at VA CBOCs

#### E. Pediatric Prosthetic Trial
- Design child-friendly protocol (distraction, family-centered visits)
- Partner with Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital + SRAL pediatric programs
- Address the **zero pediatric prosthetic trials** gap

#### F. Midwestern Phase 2–3 Efficacy Trial Hub
- Position SRAL as lead/subcontract site for multi-center Phase 2–3 prosthetic efficacy trial
- Target: powered ankle-foot prosthesis for K0–K2 transfemoral amputees
- ≥40% minority recruitment target from underserved ZIP codes
