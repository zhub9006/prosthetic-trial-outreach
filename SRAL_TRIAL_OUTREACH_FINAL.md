### Eligibility Gaps Observed Across Studies

- **Age limits:** Most studies cap at age 84, excluding geriatric populations who are the fastest-growing amputee demographic (vascular disease).
- **Weight cutoffs:** Several exclude individuals >125 kg — penalizing obese amputees (a growing population).
- **Bilateral amputee exclusion:** Most exclude bilateral amputees, despite they representing a high-need group.
- **Language:** Zero studies list non-English consent forms or multilingual recruitment.
- **Rural sites:** No trials include rural US locations; all are in urban academic centers.
- **Pediatric:** No dedicated pediatric prosthetic trials found in the dataset.
- **Insurance requirement:** At least one study (INCREMENT) explicitly excludes participants not covered by health insurance — a direct barrier for underserved groups.

## 4. Identified Gaps for Underserved Prosthetic Users

### 4.1 Trial Enrollment Gaps

1. **No Phase 2-3 efficacy trials** — Most prosthetic R&D stops at feasibility (Phase N/A). Real-world effectiveness data for underserved populations is nonexistent.
2. **Industry-dominated R&D** — 28% of active trials are industry-sponsored; these focus on premium devices, not affordable/basic prosthetics for low-income users.
3. **Zero community/nonprofit-sponsored trials** — No grassroots or outreach-oriented prosthetics research.
4. **No rural trial sites** — All trials are in urban academic hubs; rural amputees have no local access.
5. **No pediatric trials** — Children with limb loss are excluded from the clinical pipeline entirely.
6. **Language exclusion** — No multilingual consent forms across 104 studies.
7. **Insurance gatekeeping** — Active trial protocols exclude the uninsured.
8. **Age and weight exclusions** — Geriatric and higher-weight amputees systematically excluded.
9. **Bilateral amputee exclusion** — High-need group systematically excluded.
10. **Short follow-up only** — Most trials measure outcomes over weeks, not months; long-term community integration is unstudied.

### 4.2 SRAL Site Access Gaps

1. **No DME vendors** — Prosthetic users need fittings, adjustments, and replacements; none within 500 m.
2. **Pharmacy access** — Nearest pharmacy is 850 m / 13 min walk.
3. **Parking cost & accessibility** — All parking is paid; no ADA spots; low-clearance garages block accessible vans.
4. **No tactile paving at transit** — 48+ bus stops, none with tactile paving.
5. **No EV charging** — Relevant if SRAL recommends electric wheelchairs.
6. **No social services** — Case management, vocational rehab, and peer support not available in the immediate area.
7. **No parks or outdoor therapy space** — Important for gait training and community reintegration.
