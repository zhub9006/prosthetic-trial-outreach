| **FED (Federal/Government)** | 21 (global) / 4 (Chicago) | 15% / 13% | 4 | 13 |
| **OTHER_GOV / NETWORK** | 2 | 2% | 0 | — |

**Key Finding — Industry Underrepresentation:** Across all prosthetics trials, industry-sponsored studies account for only ~19% globally and a mere 13% in Chicago. This is severe for underserved populations who depend on industry trials for access to cutting-edge prosthetic technologies. Veteran-focused trials are almost entirely federally funded (FED class), with zero industry participation in veteran-specific studies.

**Key Finding — Phase Gap:** 96–97% of prosthetic trials have no formal phase designation, with very few advancing to efficacy testing. Globally, 73% of all prosthetic studies are in the "NA" phase, indicating a pipeline bottleneck: promising technologies rarely reach Phase 3+ testing before market release, leaving underserved populations with less rigorously validated devices.
