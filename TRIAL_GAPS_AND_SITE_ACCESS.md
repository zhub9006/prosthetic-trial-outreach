**Key Takeaways from the 10 Trials:**
1. **Only 2 of 10 are actively recruiting** (SRAL & Austin). Most are completed or not yet recruiting — a **recruitment bottleneck**.
2. **Enrollment at SRAL is just 6 participants** — our outreach can help scale to a more representative cohort.
3. **Industry sponsors** (Liberating Tech, Otto Bock, Össur) focus on device-specific studies, while **academic sponsors** run broader studies.
4. **No US Midwest academic site** besides SRAL is recruiting. Rural populations are invisible.
5. **Phase designations are absent** — no regulatory framework makes it harder to track translational progress.

| **FED (Federal: VA/DoD/Medicare)** | 41 | **15%** | Best positioned for underserved (Veterans); concentrated at VA centers |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | **2%** | Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs |

| **NETWORK** | 2 | **1%** | Multi-site consortium trials |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | <1% | Unclear sponsor |

**🚨 Critical Gap — Industry Underrepresentation:**
- Only **19%** of trials are industry-sponsored.
- Industry trials often provide **free prosthetic devices** to participants — academic/federal trials rarely do.
- Underserved populations (low-income, uninsured, rural) miss access to cutting-edge technology because they can't participate in industry trials that require specific insurance or travel.
- The dominance of academic sponsors (63%) means most trials focus on research questions that may not align with community-level needs.

**🚨 Critical Gap — Federal Underrepresentation:**
- VA/DoD trials are the **only systematic pipeline** for underserved populations (Veterans, rural poor).
- Yet federal sponsors represent just **15%** of the landscape.
- No community health center, FQHC, or patient advocacy group sponsors any prosthetic trial.

**🚨 Critical Gap — Phase Pipeline Gap:**
- **96% of trials have NA or Unknown phases**. This means prosthetic devices often go to market without rigorous later-stage efficacy testing.
- Promising technologies rarely advance to Phase 3+ before commercial release, leaving underserved populations with unproven devices.
- This undermines evidence-based prescribing — critical for insurance coverage decisions for low-income users.

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: "Bridging the Gap" — Community Trial Navigators (~$135K-$180K/yr)
- **Target:** South/West Side Chicago (Englewood, Austin, East Garfield Park)
- **Action:** Hire 2-3 Community Health Workers from target neighborhoods to assist prosthetic users with trial screening, transportation, and consent
- **Partner:** South Side Community Health Centers, Provident Hospital, Jesse Brown VA
- **Monthly "Trial Café"** info sessions at community centers (not at SRAL — meet people where they are)
- **KPI:** ≥15 new diverse participants/study/yr from South/West Side

### Strategy 2: "Prosthetic Supply Corridor" — On-Site DME Kiosk (~$15K-$50K + setup)
- **Target:** Eliminate the 500m DME gap (ZERO vendors within 500m)
- **Action:** Partner with Hanger Clinic or Ottobock for weekly pop-up clinic in SRAL lobby ($15K/yr) OR permanent satellite fitting station ($50K setup + $30K/yr)
- **Free socket checks & padding adjustments** at SRAL
- **DME manufacturer sponsorship** (tax-deductible)
- **KPI:** ≥50 DME interactions/month; 10% reduction in "prosthesis issue" cancel rate

### Strategy 3: "Warm Hands, Warm Access" — Heated Area + Winter Kits (~$33K/yr)
- **Target:** Chicago winter wind chill <0°F; thermal discomfort is a PRIMARY barrier
- **Action:** Heated waiting area in SRAL check-in zone ($25K one-time + $8K/yr); Winter Prosthetic Thermal Kits (wool socks, hand warmers, insulated bags, thermal liner)
- **CTA winter transit passes** ($60/participant); Indoor alternatives for outdoor assessments Nov-Mar
- **KPI:** ≥85% winter attendance rate (vs ~60% baseline est.)

### Strategy 4: "South Side Transit Pipeline" — CTA Shuttle + Tele-Trial (~$60K/yr)
- **Target:** South/West Siders need 1+ bus transfers (1+ hr rides) to reach SRAL
- **Action:** Dedicated CTA Access-A-Ride shuttle route ($60K/yr subsidized rides); **Tele-Trial Hybrid Model**:
  - Visit 1 (in-person): Consent + baseline + device fitting at SRAL
  - Visits 2-4 (telehealth): Remote check-in + device data
  - Final Visit (in-person): Outcomes assessment
- **Pre-loaded tablets** for participants ($15K for 30 tablets); **"Team Trials"** — 2-4 neighborhood participants attend together
- **KPI:** ≥40% no-show reduction for tele-trial arm; 15 tele-trial participants/study/yr

### Strategy 5: "Global Equity Bridge" — International Partnerships (~$50K + $50K/yr)
- **Target:** ~80 nations with ZERO prosthetic trials — Colombia (Bogotá), India (Delhi/Mumbai), Pakistan (Lahore/Karachi)
- **Action:** Design low-cost protocol adaptations (<$500/protocol); Shared REDCap data protocol; Pilot joint K2 powered knee study in South Asia (adapted NCT06194838 model)
- **Apply PCORI + NIH Fogarty International Center funding**
- **KPI:** 3+ MOUs in 18mo; 1 joint study in 24mo; 5 non-US participants/study

### Strategy 6: "Mental Health & Acceptance" — PHQ-2 + Peer Group (~$20K/yr)
- **Target:** 30-50% of amputees experience depression; excluded by most protocols
- **Action:** Universal PHQ-2 screening at trial intake; Partner with Medrina for culturally adapted mental health triage; Monthly peer support group (normalize trial participation as rehab, not "experimentation"); **Trial Ambassador** program — 5-10 past trial participants as ambassadors
- **Offer trial as part of standard rehab** (not separate "research")
- **KPI:** 25% intake PHQ-2 screening; 10 peer group attendees/month; quarterly Ambassador events

### Strategy 7: "Women, Children & Gender-Diverse" — Equal-Platform + Pediatric (~$35K + $15K/yr)
- **Target:** ~90%+ male adult-traumatic-amputation skew across all 427 trials
- **Action:** **Equal-Platform Protocol:** mandatory 30% enrollment minimum for women & family units; **Lurie Children's Pediatric Sub-Study** ($35K start): ages 10-17; child life specialist; gamified outcomes; **Family-Friendly Visit:** sibling care, refreshments, play area during trial waiting
- **Recruit via Chicago Women's Health Center + Gladys Academy**
- **KPI:** 30% female enrollment; 10 pediatric/yr; 1 gender-diverse/study; pediatric protocol in 12mo
