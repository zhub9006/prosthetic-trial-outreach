### Key Takeaways from the 10 Trials

1. **Most trials are COMPLETED** (7 of 10), with only 2 RECRUITING and 1 NOT_YET_RECRUITING. This indicates a **recruitment bottleneck** — studies are finishing but new ones aren't starting fast enough.
2. **Enrollment numbers are small** except for a few (PEQ: 140, Amputation Levels: 85). Most trials enroll 20-50 people, limiting statistical power.
3. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (NCT06160882)** is actively recruiting but with only 6 participants — a potential opportunity for our outreach to help scale up.
4. **Industry sponsors** (Liberating Technologies, Stryker, Össur) tend to run smaller, shorter trials focused on specific devices, while **academic sponsors** run larger, broader studies.
5. **Geographic concentration:** US/UK/Finland studies dominate; Mediterranean (Italy), France, and Canada have representation but are sparse. **No trials in the American Midwest** beyond Chicago.
6. **Phase designations are absent** for all 10 trials — the FDA phase system isn't being used, making it harder to track translational progress.

**🚨 Critical Gap:** Industry-sponsored trials are only 19% of the landscape. Underserved populations (low-income, uninsured, rural) often cannot access industry trials that require specific insurance coverage or travel. The dominance of academic sponsors (63%) means most trials focus on research questions that may not align with community-level needs.

### Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** Rarely reported in trial demographics. NCT04427007 enrolled 100% White participants. No trials mandate demographic data collection.
2. **Lower-Limb Specificity:** Some trials overlap with upper-limb prosthetics. Many focus on transtibial only, excluding transfemoral users (~40% of amputees).
3. **Rural Access:** Zero trials based in rural Midwest or Southern US. Most sites in major metros.
4. **Age Range:** Many trials cap enrollment at 60-65, excluding older adults who represent a large and growing portion of the amputee population.
5. **Female Representation:** Sex-specific enrollment often not reported; several cohorts are male-skewed.
6. **Insurance/Financial Barriers:** Industry trials may require specific insurance or substantial travel, creating access barriers for low-income populations.
7. **Phase Gap:** 96% in NA/Unknown phases means very few interventions have undergone rigorous later-stage testing before market release.
8. **No Dedicated Disability Services Integration:** Trials rarely partner with community disability or rehabilitation centers for participant recruitment.

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community-Based Screening Events
- **Target:** Rural populations, older adults (65+), racial/ethnic minorities, low-income individuals, uninsured
- **Approach:** Mobile screening/triage stations at community centers, churches, VA facilities, and Federal Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) in the rural Midwest and Southern US
- **AbilityLab connection:** Use SRAL's reputation and infrastructure as a hub for satellite events; host monthly "Community Prosthetic Days" open to the public
- **Equipment:** Portable gait analysis tools, prosthetic fitting demonstrations

### Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation Program (Hybrid Model)
- **Model:** In-person AbilityLab visits (initial screening, device fitting) + telehealth check-ins (follow-ups, progress monitoring)
- **Travel assistance:** Stipends and lodging partnerships for participants >50 miles from Chicago (rural IL, IN, WI, IA)
- **Tech:** Provide tablets/tech literacy support for older participants; partner with libraries (NU Law Library is nearby) for telehealth charging stations

### Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline
```
Industry Sponsors (Össur, Stryker, Ottobock, Liberating Technologies)
        ↓
  Device donations / pro bono programs
        ↓
  Academic Partners (SRAL + Midwest FQHCs)
        ↓
  Deploy to underserved trial sites
        ↓
  Demographic data collection + outcome tracking
```
- Match prosthetic device companies with underserved trial sites
- Pro bono device donations from manufacturers
- Priority: extend SRAL's NCT06160882 (powered prostheses) to reach rural, minority, and older populations

### Strategy 4: Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks
- **Mandatory demographic reporting** in all trial protocols
- **Minimum diversity targets:** ≥25% female enrollment, ≥30% minority enrollment, ≥15% participants over 65
- **Data transparency:** Public dashboard tracking these metrics across all SRAL-affiliated trials
- **Community Advisory Board:** Prosthetic users from underserved communities review trial designs before IRB submission

### Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation Services
- **Dedicated Patient Navigator:** Single point of contact for trial eligibility, enrollment, and completion — specifically trained in disability communication
- **Transportation partners:**
  - CTA Reduced Fare Cards (disability discount)
  - Divvy Adaptive cycling program
  - Ride-share partnerships (Lyft/Uber Health)
  - Volunteer driver network through SRAL
- **Parking voucher program:** Subsidized Doubletree garage access for participants with vehicles

### Strategy 6: Older Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming
- Advocate for **broader age ranges** in trial protocols (no upper age cap, or cap at 80+)
- **Caregiver stipends** and on-site support (support group meetings, respite)
- **Accessible communication:** Large-print materials, hearing loop systems, flexible appointment times
- Collaborate with **Shirley Ryan's own daily programs** to reach patients already in the system

### Strategy 7: Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers
- **Partner with:** VA hospitals in Decatur IL, Marion IL, Marion IN; FQHCs along the I-55 and I-65 corridors
- **Deploy:** Mobile prosthetics labs (van-based) with basic gait assessment and telehealth capabilities
- **Schedule:** Quarterly rural "pop-up" clinics with SRAL prosthetists
- **Track:** Interest levels, eligibility rates, and barriers to participation

### Strategy 8: Data Transparency & Community Dashboard
- **Open-access dashboard** (built on GitHub) for:
  - Trial demographics (sponsor, phase, race, sex, age, geographic coverage)
  - Site accessibility ratings for all recruitment locations
  - Community feedback and resource requests
- **Community Advisory Board:** Quarterly meetings with prosthetic users from underserved communities
- **GitHub integration:** `zhub9006/prosthetic-trial-outreach` — all data, strategies, and progress tracked in open source
