### 1.4 Geographic Equity Gap

- **>88%** of trials concentrated in US/EU
- **~80 nations** with zero lower limb prosthetic trial access
- Amputation incidence is highest in low-income countries, yet they have no trial infrastructure

## 3. Underserved Population Gap Summary

### The Core Problem

Lower limb prosthetic trials are overwhelmingly:
1. **Small and academic** (73% university/OTHER sponsors) → limited outreach capacity
2. **Stuck in early/pre-phases** (95% never reach Phase 3) → minimal efficacy data for underserved populations
3. **Accessible only to affluent, urban, English-speaking, male adults** → systemic exclusion
4. **Geographically concentrated** in Boston, Chicago, and VA campuses → 80+ nations have zero access

### What This Means for Underserved Groups

| Group | Primary Barrier |
|---|---|
| Low-income users | Cannot afford travel, time off work, childcare; no financial navigation support |
| Rural users | No transport infrastructure; 100+ mile trips to nearest academic center |
| Non-English speakers | Consent, screening, and follow-up barriers; no multilingual materials |
| Women & children | Not designed into trial protocols; gender-specific prosthetic fit rarely studied |
| Global South amputees | Highest incidence rates but zero trial pathway |
| Elderly (75+) | Comorbidity exclusion filters Remove most common amputee profile |
| Veterans | VA trials exist but are limited; no bridge between VA & academic research |

## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: "Bridging the Gap" — Community Trial Navigators

**Objective:** Bring trial awareness to underserved communities rather than waiting for them to come to SRAL.

- Hire **2–3 Community Trial Navigators** from Chicago's South Side (Englewood, Bronzeville, Austin) and West Side (Humboldt Park, Pilsen)
- Train navigators on clinical trial literacy, informed consent processes, and SRAL referral pathways
- Deploy navigators to community health fairs, churches, amputee support groups, WIC offices, and VA centers
- **Budget estimate:** $90,000/year (2–3 FTE + travel)
- **Success metric:** 30% increase in screened participants from historically excluded ZIP codes within 12 months

### Strategy 2: "Prosthetic Supply Corridor" — Pop-Up DME in SRAL Lobby

**Objective:** Eliminate the critical 500 m DME/prosthetic vendor gap identified in this audit.

- Negotiate with **Össur, Ottobock, or Hanger Clinic** for **quarterly 1-day pop-up DME booths** in SRAL's main lobby
- Services offered: socket checks, liner replacements, prosthetic adjustments, trial recruitment flyers
- Partner with **Insulza Foundation** or similar for low-cost/donated prosthetics for uninsured attendees
- **Budget estimate:** $15,000/year (12 pop-up days + vendor coordination)
- **Success metric:** 200+ prosthetic users served per year; 25% of pop-up attendees approach trial navigation

### Strategy 3: "Warm Hands, Warm Access" — Thermal Comfort + Winter Kit Program

**Objective:** Address thermal discomfort (per NCT07215442 findings on skin temperature perception and prosthetic thermoregulation) as a barrier to prosthetic use and trial participation.

- Convert a SRAL waiting area into a **heated prosthetic comfort zone** (≥72°F, prosthetic-friendly seating)
- Distribute **free winter prosthetic kits** (thermal socks, sock liners, moisture-wicking sheaths) to all attendees
- Screen for thermal discomfort using non-invasive thermode (adapted from NCT07215442 protocol)
- **Budget estimate:** $25,000 (one-time setup) + $10,000/year (consumable kits)
- **Success metric:** 40% reduction in self-reported thermal discomfort among SRAL prosthetic visitors

### Strategy 4: "South Side Transit Pipeline" — Paratransit + Tele-Trial Model

**Objective:** Bridge the Grand Red Line transfer gap for South/West Side residents.

- Partner with **CTA Paratransit** to establish a **dedicated SRAL shuttle** or subsidized transit voucher program
- Implement **tele-trial** protocols for initial screening, consent, and follow-up visits (reducing in-person travel burden)
- Create a **Grand Red Line corridor** outreach plan with station ambassadors at key transfer points
- **Budget estimate:** $40,000/year (transit vouchers + tele-trial platform)
- **Success metric:** 50+ South/West Side participants enrolled via tele-trial or shuttle within 12 months

### Strategy 5: "Global Equity Bridge" — International Co-Design

**Objective:** Address the complete absence of Global South trial infrastructure.

- Establish **MOUs with research institutions in Colombia, India, and Pakistan** (high amputation incidence, growing research capacity)
- Co-design a **<$500 prosthetic trial protocol** adapted for low-resource settings
- Create a **data-sharing framework** linking international sites with SRAL's outcomes registry (NCT06160882)
- **Budget estimate:** $75,000/year (MOU development, 2 international site visits, data infrastructure)
- **Success metric:** 3 active international partner sites within 18 months; 1 co-designed low-resource protocol published

### Strategy 6: "Mental Health & Acceptance" — Integrated Psycho-Social Support

**Objective:** Address the mental health and body image barriers that prevent prosthetic adoption and trial participation.

- Integrate **PHQ-2 depression screening** into trial intake (per NCT06160882 psychosocial outcomes data)
- Launch **monthly peer-support groups** led by amputee mentors (paid stipends)
- Provide **social work consultation** for insurance navigation, disability accommodations, and family counseling
- **Budget estimate:** $35,000/year (2 peer mentors + 1 social worker, 50% FTE each)
- **Success metric:** 60% of trial participants report improved prosthetic acceptance (using AMPRO scale) within 6 months

### Strategy 7: "Women, Children & Gender-Diverse" — Inclusive Trial Design

**Objective:** Correct the severe underrepresentation of women, children, and gender-diverse individuals in prosthetic trials.

- Launch a **Women's Prosthetic Cohort** with gender-specific socket fitting, hormonal cycle considerations, and maternity accommodations
- Establish a **Pediatric Sub-Study** in partnership with **Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital** (nearby in Chicago)
- Implement **gender-diversity accommodation standards** for all SRAL trials: inclusive intake forms, private changing facilities, pronoun protocols
- **Budget estimate:** $60,000/year (pediatric coordinator, women's cohort lead, facility modifications)
- **Success metric:** 30% female enrollment in new trials (vs. current ~10%); 1 pediatric protocol activated within 12 months
