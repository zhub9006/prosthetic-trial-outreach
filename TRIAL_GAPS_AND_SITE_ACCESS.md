**Key Takeaways from the 10 Trials:**
1. Only **2 of 10 are actively recruiting** (SRAL & K2 Ascend). Most are completed or not yet recruiting — a **recruitment bottleneck**.
2. Enrollment at SRAL is just **6 participants** — our outreach can help scale to a more representative cohort.
3. **Industry sponsors** (Otto Bock) focus on device-specific studies, while **academic sponsors** run broader studies.
4. No US Midwest academic site besides SRAL is recruiting. Rural populations are invisible.
5. **Phase designations are absent** — most are NA or Unknown, making regulatory tracking harder.
6. **VA-sponsored trials** (items 4, 6) are the best positioned for underserved US populations but are veteran-exclusive.

### Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type (427 Total Trials Analyzed)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Key Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic/University/Hospital)** | 313 | **73.3%** | Dominant but lack diversity & community reach; single-site, under-resourced |
| **INDUSTRY (Device Companies)** | 59 | **13.8%** | Severely underrepresented — limits tech access for underserved populations |
| **FED (Federal: VA/DoD/Medicare)** | 41 | **9.6%** | Best positioned for underserved (Veterans); concentrated at VA centers |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | **2.1%** | Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs |
| **NIH** | 2 | **0.5%** | Almost no dedicated NIH funding for lower limb prosthetics research |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | **0.5%** | Multi-site consortium trials |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | **0.2%** | Unclear sponsor |

**🚨 Critical Gap — Industry Underrepresentation:**
- Only **13.8%** of trials are industry-sponsored.
- Industry trials often provide **free prosthetic devices** to participants — academic/federal trials rarely do.
- Underserved populations (low-income, uninsured, rural) miss access to cutting-edge technology because they can't participate in industry trials requiring insurance or travel.
- The dominance of academic sponsors (73%) means most trials focus on research questions that may not align with community-level needs.

**🚨 Critical Gap — NIH Underfunding:**
- Only **2 trials** in 427 are NIH-funded. This represents a **chronic underinvestment** in NIH prosthetic research.
- NIH could drive the public-health research needed to address disparities but has almost zero presence.

**🚨 Critical Gap — Federal Concentrated at VA:**
- VA/DoD trials are the **only systematic pipeline** for underserved populations (Veterans, rural poor).
- Yet federal sponsors represent just **9.6%** of the landscape.
- No community health center, FQHC, or patient advocacy group sponsors any prosthetic trial.

**🚨 Critical Gap — Phase Pipeline Gap:**
- **96% of trials have NA or Unknown phases**. This means prosthetic devices often go to market without rigorous later-stage efficacy testing.
- Promising technologies rarely advance to Phase 3+ before commercial release, leaving underserved populations with unproven devices.
- This undermines evidence-based prescribing — critical for insurance coverage decisions for low-income users.

## Part 4: Outreach Strategy Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0-3 months)

1. **DME Access Station** — Partner with a local DME provider to place a prosthetic access station within the SRAL clinic or adjacent space. Address the #1 site barrier. Cost: ~$25K setup + ~$15K/yr operations.

2. **CTA Reduced-Fare Pass Program** — Provide CTAMonthly Passes and Divvy Adaptive memberships to trial participants who rely on public transit. Cost: ~$5K/yr.

3. **Community Advisory Board (CAB)** — Recruit 10-15 prosthetic users from underserved populations (South Side, Latino community, elderly, women) to advise on trial design and outreach. Cost: ~$3K/yr stipends.

4. **Patient Navigator** — Hire 1 part-time community health worker to assist with trial enrollment, insurance navigation, and transportation coordination. Cost: ~$10K/yr.

### 3-6 Month Actions

5. **Data Transparency Dashboard** — Publish trial demographics, gaps, and outcomes on an open-source GitHub dashboard. Builds trust and attracts underserved participants. Cost: ~$8K setup + ~$2K/yr.

6. **South Side Community Partnerships** — Partner with community organizations on Chicago's South Side (e.g., Bronzeville, Englewood) to establish satellite outreach and screening events. Cost: ~$15K/yr.

7. **ADA Parking Advocacy** — Work with City of Chicago and parking operators to designate ADA-accessible spots within 200m of SRAL. Cost: Advocacy only (~$2K for lobbying materials).

### 6-12 Month Actions

8. **Mobile Screening Events** — Deploy mobile screening units to VA clinics and FQHCs in rural Midwest and Chicago South Side. Target: 50+ new participants from underserved groups. Cost: ~$80K/yr.

9. **Industry-Academic Pipeline** — Negotiate pro bono device donations from industry sponsors for deployment to underserved trial sites. Cost: Partnership development only.

10. **Older Adult Programming** — Partner with senior centers for in-home prosthetic assessments and caregiver stipends. Cost: ~$20K/yr.

11. **Tele-Trial Hub** — Virtual visits, mail-in device checks, and team-based trial participation to reduce travel burden. Cost: ~$25K setup + ~$10K/yr.

12. **Mental Health Integration** — Partner with Medrina (0.6km) for peer support mentors and support groups at SRAL. Cost: ~$15K/yr.
