**Key Takeaways from the 10 Trials:**
1. Only **1 of 10 is actively recruiting** right now (SRAL NCT06160882, just 6 participants) — a **critical recruitment bottleneck**.
2. Two VA trials (NCT05990062, NCT05287646) are recruiting but **veteran-exclusive** — excluding the broader underserved population.
3. Enrollment at SRAL is just **6 participants** — our outreach can help scale to a more representative cohort.
4. **Industry sponsors** (Otto Bock) focus on device-specific studies, while **academic sponsors** run broader studies.
5. **No US Midwest academic site besides SRAL is recruiting**. Rural populations are invisible.
6. **Phase designations are absent** — most are NA or Unknown, making regulatory tracking harder.
7. **No pediatric trials** appear in the top 10 or the broader 427-trial dataset.
8. **No trials in Spanish** or targeting Hispanic/Latino populations.

| **OTHER (Academic/University/Hospital)** | 313 | **73.3%** | Dominant but lack diversity & community reach; single-site, under-resourced |
| **INDUSTRY (Device Companies)** | 59 | **13.8%** | Severely underrepresented — limits tech access for underserved populations |
| **FED (Federal: VA/DoD/Medicare)** | 41 | **9.6%** | Best positioned for underserved (Veterans); concentrated at VA centers |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | **2.1%** | Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs |
| **NIH** | 2 | **0.5%** | Almost no dedicated NIH funding for lower limb prosthetics research |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | **0.5%** | Multi-site consortium trials |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | **0.2%** | Unclear sponsor |

**🚨 Critical Gap — Industry Underrepresentation:**
- Only **13.8%** of trials are industry-sponsored.
- Industry trials often provide **free prosthetic devices** to participants — academic/federal trials rarely do.
- Underserved populations (low-income, uninsured, rural) miss access to cutting-edge technology because they can't participate in industry trials requiring insurance or travel.
- The dominance of academic sponsors (73%) means most trials focus on research questions that may not align with community-level needs.

**🚨 Critical Gap — NIH Underfunding:**
- Only **2 trials** in 427 are NIH-funded. This represents a **chronic underinvestment** in NIH prosthetic research.
- NIH could drive the public-health research needed to address disparities but has almost zero presence.

**🚨 Critical Gap — Federal Concentrated at VA:**
- VA/DoD trials are the **only systematic pipeline** for underserved populations (Veterans, rural poor).
- Yet federal sponsors represent just **9.6%** of the landscape.
- No community health center, FQHC, or patient advocacy group sponsors any prosthetic trial.

- This undermines evidence-based prescribing — critical for insurance coverage decisions for low-income users.

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Patient Navigation & Transportation Program (Immediate — ~$111K/yr)
- **CTA Reduced-Fare Cards** — Partner with CTA to provide monthly passes for prosthetic trial participants
- **Divvy Adaptive Cycling** — Adaptive bike-share for patients with mobility impairments
- **Ride-Share Vouchers** — Uber Health / Lyft Concierge for door-to-door medical transport
- **Patient Navigator** — Hire 1 community health worker ($10K/yr) to guide patients from screening to enrollment

### Strategy 2: Data Transparency Dashboard (3 months — ~$15K)
- Open-source dashboard on GitHub showing trial demographics, enrollment gaps, and outcomes
- Real-time display of which trials are recruiting and their eligibility criteria
- Available in English and Spanish

### Strategy 3: Community Advisory Board (CAB) (1 month to form — $5K)
- Recruit 10-15 prosthetic users from underserved populations
- Include community health workers from Chicago's South Side, Latino communities, and rural Illinois
- Monthly meetings to advise trial design and outreach priorities

### Strategy 4: Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks (12 months)
- Require ≥25% female enrollment in all SRAL-affiliated trials
- Require ≥30% minority enrollment
- Require ≥15% Medicaid/uninsured enrollment
- Report demographics publicly on the dashboard

### Strategy 5: Mobile Screening Events (6-12 months — ~$380K/yr)
- Deploy VA/FQHC mobile units to Chicago's South Side (35th-49th St corridor)
- Quarterly rural Midwest outreach via VA clinics in IL, MO, IN
- On-site prosthetic fittings and trial screenings

### Strategy 6: Industry-Academic Pipeline (6-12 months — ~$200K)
- Negotiate pro bono device donations from Otto Bock, Össur, and Ottobock
- Deploy demonstration devices to SRAL for trial participants
- Create device-lending library for underserved patients

### Strategy 7: DME Integration at SRAL (CRITICAL — 6 months — ~$150K)
- Establish a prosthetic/orthotic access station within 500m of SRAL (currently **ZERO vendors within 500m**)
- Partner with nearby DME providers for on-site socket checks and adjustments
- This is the **#1 site-level barrier** identified in our assessment

### Strategy 8: Older Adult Programming (6 months — ~$30K/yr)
- Caregiver stipends for elderly participants ($50/session)
- In-home assessments for mobility-limited seniors
- Partnerships with senior centers on the Near North Side

### Strategy 9: Tele-Trial Hub (6 months — ~$50K)
- Virtual trial visits via telehealth
- Mail-in device checks and outcome assessments
- Team-based trials to reduce individual burden

### Strategy 10: South Side Permanent Office (12+ months — ~$350K/yr)
- Establish a 24/7 outreach office on Chicago's South Side (~10km from SRAL)
- Target African American and Hispanic communities with culturally competent staff
- Co-locate with existing FQHCs and community health centers

### Strategy 11: Rural Outreach via VA (12+ months — ~$200K/yr)
- Mobile prosthetics labs quarterly to VA clinics in IL, MO, IN
- Tele-prosthetics consultations for rural veterans
- Partnership with VA's existing prosthetic rehabilitation programs

### Strategy 12: Mental Health Integration (6 months — ~$25K/yr)
- Peer support mentors at Medrina (~605m from SRAL)
- Amputee support groups in English and Spanish
- Integration of mental health screening into all trial enrollment protocols
