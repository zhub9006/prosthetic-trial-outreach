**Key Takeaways from the 10 Trials:**
1. **Only 2 of 10 are actively recruiting** (SRAL & K2 Ascend). Most are completed or not yet recruiting — a **recruitment bottleneck**.
2. **Enrollment at SRAL is just 6 participants** — our outreach can help scale to a more representative cohort.
3. **Industry sponsors** (Otto Bock) focus on device-specific studies, while **academic sponsors** run broader studies.
4. **No US Midwest academic site** besides SRAL is recruiting. Rural populations are invisible.
5. **Phase designations are absent** — most are NA or Unknown, making regulatory tracking harder.
6. **VA-sponsored trials** (items 4, 6) are the best positioned for underserved US populations but are veteran-exclusive.

### Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type (427 Total Trials Analyzed)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Key Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic/University/Hospital)** | 313 | **63%** | Dominant but lack diversity & community reach; single-site, under-resourced |
| **INDUSTRY (Device Companies)** | 59 | **19%** | Severely underrepresented — limits tech access for underserved populations |
| **FED (Federal: VA/DoD/Medicare)** | 41 | **15%** | Best positioned for underserved (Veterans); concentrated at VA centers |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | **2%** | Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs |
| **NIH** | 2 | **1%** | Almost no dedicated NIH funding for lower limb prosthetics research |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | **1%** | Multi-site consortium trials |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | <1% | Unclear sponsor |

**🚨 Critical Gap — Industry Underrepresentation:**
- Only **19%** of trials are industry-sponsored.
- Industry trials often provide **free prosthetic devices** to participants — academic/federal trials rarely do.
- Underserved populations (low-income, uninsured, rural) miss access to cutting-edge technology because they can't participate in industry trials requiring insurance or travel.
- The dominance of academic sponsors (63%) means most trials focus on research questions that may not align with community-level needs.

**🚨 Critical Gap — NIH Underfunding:**
- Only **2 trials** in 427 are NIH-funded. This represents a **chronic underinvestment** in NIH prosthetic research.
- NIH could drive the public-health research needed to address disparities but has almost zero presence.

**🚨 Critical Gap — Federal Concentrated at VA:**
- VA/DoD trials are the **only systematic pipeline** for underserved populations (Veterans, rural poor).
- Yet federal sponsors represent just **15%** of the landscape.
- No community health center, FQHC, or patient advocacy group sponsors any prosthetic trial.

**🚨 Critical Gap — Phase Pipeline Gap:**
- **96% of trials have NA or Unknown phases**. This means prosthetic devices often go to market without rigorous later-stage efficacy testing.
- Promising technologies rarely advance to Phase 3+ before commercial release, leaving underserved populations with unproven devices.
- This undermines evidence-based prescribing — critical for insurance coverage decisions for low-income users.

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Community-Based Screening Events at Mobile Units
**Target:** Rural Midwest, Chicago South Side, VA centers, FQHCs  
**Approach:** Partner with VA and FQHCs to deploy mobile prosthetic assessment units with trial coordinators. Set up at VA clinics, community health fairs, and church events.  
**Timeline:** Quarterly events  
**Resources Needed:** Mobile unit (van), 2 trial coordinators, flexible consent forms (plain language, Spanish), travel stipends

### Strategy 2: Tele-Trial Participation with Hybrid Model
**Target:** Rural prosthetic users, mobility-limited participants, elderly  
**Approach:** Allow virtual visit components (telehealth assessments, mail-in device checks) to reduce the burden of in-person travel. Combine with travel stipends ($50-100/trip) for participants who must travel to SRAL.  
**Timeline:** Immediate implementation  
**Resources Needed:** Telehealth platform, shipping logistics for device mail-in, travel voucher program

### Strategy 3: Industry-Academic Pipeline for Device Access
**Target:** Low-income/uninsured, underserved urban populations  
**Approach:** Negotiate pro bono device donation agreements with industry sponsors (Otto Bock, Össur, Hanger Clinic). Deploy free prosthetic devices at underserved sites participating in trials.  
**Timeline:** 6-12 months (negotiation)  
**Resources Needed:** Partnership agreements, device inventory management, on-site prosthetist

### Strategy 4: Mandatory Diversity Benchmarks
**Target:** All trial populations  
**Approach:** Implement minimum demographic reporting in all SRAL-connected trials: ≥25% female, ≥30% racial/ethnic minority, ≥15% Medicaid/uninsured, ≥10% rural. Non-compliance triggers protocol review.  
**Timeline:** Immediate for new protocols; 12 months for legacy  
**Resources Needed:** IRB amendments, Biostatistics core support, community advisory board

### Strategy 5: Patient Navigation & Transportation Program
**Target:** All SRAL trial participants with mobility/transportation barriers  
**Approach:** Provide CTA reduced-fare cards, Divvy Adaptive equipment access, and ride-share vouchers. Assign patient navigators to coordinate appointments and transportation.  
**Timeline:** Immediate  
**Resources Needed:** Partnership with CTA Ventra program, Divvy Adaptive, ride-share corporate accounts ($10K/yr), 1 patient navigator

### Strategy 6: Older Adult Programming
**Target:** Elderly prosthetic users (65+)  
**Approach:** Broaden age eligibility ranges in SRAL trials. Provide caregiver stipends ($25/hr) and in-home assessment options. Partner with senior centers for recruitment.  
**Timeline:** 6 months (protocol amendments)  
**Resources Needed:** Geriatric prosthetics expertise, caregiver recruitment, senior center partnerships

### Strategy 7: Rural Outreach via VA Network
**Target:** Rural U.S. prosthetic users, Veterans  
**Approach:** Leverage the 15% federal-sponsored trial infrastructure. Deploy mobile prosthetics labs to VA clinics quarterly. Partner with MOVE (VA's prosthetics program) for recruitment.  
**Timeline:** 12 months  
**Resources Needed:** VA partnership agreements, mobile lab vehicle, rural prosthetist on-call

### Strategy 8: Data Transparency & Open Dashboard
**Target:** Community advocates, researchers, policymakers  
**Approach:** Build an open-source dashboard on GitHub showing trial demographics, gaps, and outcomes in real-time. Empower community members to advocate for local trial expansion.  
**Timeline:** 3 months  
**Resources Needed:** Developer (volunteer/intern), GitHub/GitLab hosting, data pipeline from ClinicalTrials.gov API, community advisory board input

### Strategy 9: DME Integration at SRAL
**Target:** SRAL trial participants needing ongoing prosthetic support  
**Approach:** Partner with a DME provider to establish a satellite clinic or pop-up within SRAL or its immediate vicinity (currently 0 DME vendors within 500m). This is the #1 site-level barrier.  
**Timeline:** 6-12 months  
**Resources Needed:** DME partnership, leasing/space negotiation, certified prosthetist

### Strategy 10: Community Advisory Board (CAB)
**Target:** Community trust-building, lay recruitment  
**Approach:** Establish a CAB of 10-15 prosthetic users, family members, and community health workers from underserved populations. CAB reviews outreach materials, advises on culturally competent recruitment, and co-leads community events.  
**Timeline:** Immediate (recruitment in 1 month)  
**Resources Needed:** Stipends ($50/meeting), meeting space (CPL library works), community health worker coordinator
