## 4. Outreach Gap Analysis — Where Underserved Groups Are Missing

### 4.1 Insurance & Economic Barriers

- Only **22% of prosthetic trials are industry-sponsored** — the rest are academic or federal. Industry trials often provide prosthetic devices at no cost; academic/federal trials typically do not.
- **Uninsured and Medicaid patients** are routinely excluded from trial eligibility — a selection bias that leaves the most financially vulnerable amputees out of the evidence base.
- **Parking cost** at SRAL (~$25–40/day) and **lack of free transit connections** create a de facto barrier for low-income prosthetic users.

### 4.2 Racial & Ethnic Gaps

- **Zero Spanish-language consent forms** in any identified trial; no bilingual recruitment materials.
- **Chicago South Side** (population → predominantly Black, highest diabetes-related amputation rates in Cook County) → **zero trial sites within 50 miles**.
- No minority-focused recruitment strategies in any of the 10 identified trials.

### 4.3 Gender Gap

- Most prosthetics trials specify "male" or "predominantly male" eligibility patterns; women comprise <10% of prosthetic trial enrollees nationally.
- No trials specifically target women with amputations or address gender-specific prosthetic fit challenges.

### 4.4 Age Gap

- **Zero pediatric prosthetic trials** found nationally — children's prosthetic needs are entirely unaddressed in the clinical research pipeline.
- No geriatric-specific trials (adults 75+) despite the fact that the majority of amputations occur in the 65+ population.

### 4.5 Rural & Global Gaps

- **Rural Midwest, South, and tribal lands** — zero trial sites. Prosthetic users in these areas must travel 100+ miles to access cutting-edge devices.
- **Africa, South/Southeast Asia, Latin America** — no prosthetic trial infrastructure whatsoever; ~80 nations have zero access.

### 4.6 DME Access (Site-Specific for SRAL)

- **ZERO prosthetic device vendors or orthotics/prosthetics shops within 500 m of SRAL.**
- The nearest DME providers are on Michigan Ave (1.2 km) or in the Loop (2+ km).
- This means prosthetic users attending SRAL for trial screening or visits **cannot obtain fitting, adjustments, or component changes onsite** — they must make separate, often transport-limited trips.

## 5. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME Outreach Unit (~$15,000/year)

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetist van at community centers, churches, or FQHCs on the South and West Sides of Chicago |
| **Target** | Uninsured and Medicaid patients; South Side, West Side, suburban Cook County |
| **Services** | Prosthetic fit checks, component upgrades, routine maintenance, trial eligibility screening |
| **Partners** | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab prosthetics team + CVHAs (Community Health Centers) |

### Strategy 2: Language Access & Minority Recruitment Protocol

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Materials** | Translate all trial consent forms, ads, and site materials into Spanish, Polish, and Mandarin (top 3 non-English languages in Cook County) |
| **Staffing** | Hire bilingual (Spanish-English) study coordinators; partner with community health workers (promotoras) on the South Side |
| **Sites** | Conduct screening events at community health centers on the South Side (e.g., Roseland Community Hospital, Holy Cross Hospital) |

### Strategy 3: FQHC Partnership Network

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Target FQHCs** | Roseland Community, Holy Cross, Austin Health Center, North Health Center |
| **Model** | Embed screening tools in FQHC workflows; FQHC staff refer eligible patients to SRAL for trial enrollment |
| **Transport** | Provide CTA Ventra cards or rideshare vouchers for referred patients |

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach for VA & Medicaid Patients

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|

| **Target** | Rural Illinois, Indiana, Iowa — areas with VA facilities but no prosthetics trial sites |
