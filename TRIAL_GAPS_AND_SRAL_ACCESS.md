### Study Summaries (Key Details)

**NCT07024342 (VA)** — Compares three clinically available prosthetic liners to address perspiration problems in below-knee amputees. 180 participants. Focus: comfort/stability/suspension outcomes.

**NCT06210620 (Össur, INDUSTRY)** — National multi-center registry collecting standard-of-care clinical testing data from lower-limb prosthesis patients via the PRO App. 2,000 participants. Largest enrollment in the sample.

**NCT07530601 (VUB, Belgium)** — Evaluates a new articulated prosthetic foot for comfort, safety, and biomechanics. 120 participants, crossover design. Focus: daily-life activity performance.

**NCT07397169 (VA)** — Determines pressure applied to residual limb by an adjustable prosthetic socket during activities. 25 Veterans. Tests CMS-reimbursable adjustable socket.

**NCT07592325 (France, OTHER)** — Compares Access Socket (flexible) vs. standard rigid socket in transtibial amputees. Multi-center French study, 25 participants, crossover design.

**NCT07685496 (Salford, UK)** — Investigating how different footwear types affect gait in transtibial amputees. 15 participants. Focus: biomechanics and perceived comfort/stability.

**NCT06243549 (Bath, UK)** — Explores biomechanical and psychological pathways of lower back pain development among lower-limb amputees. 30 participants, prospective cohort.

**NCT06419920 (UNLV, US)** — Tests uneven terrain walking program to improve walking skill and confidence for prosthetic users. 35 participants. Focus: fall prevention/mobility confidence.

**NCT06648798 (LIMBER, US)** — Phase I trial of 3D-printed single-piece transtibial prosthetic limb (UniLeg). 30 participants. Designed to reduce cost and improve global accessibility.

**NCT07613502 (UF/US, US)** — Compares C-Leg (microprocessor knee) vs. Bio Leg (powered knee) for transfemoral amputees. 12 participants. Focus: gait function, metabolic efficiency.

## 2. Enrollment Trends Analysis

Based on a broader ClinicalTrials.gov analysis of **40** current active/recent lower limb prosthetic trials:

### By Sponsor Type
| Sponsor Type | Count | % | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **INDUSTRY** | 11 | 27.5% | Commercial focus; limited interest in underserved populations |
| **OTHER (academic/university)** | 23 | 57.5% | Basic science / device development; minimal underserved targeting |
| **FED (VA/NIH/government)** | 5 | 12.5% | VA studies serve veterans only; NIH studies not prosthesis-focused |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 1 | 2.5% | Single civic-government trial |
| **Public/Nonprofit** | 0 | 0% | **Critical gap — no nonprofit/civic-sponsored trials exist** |

### By Phase
| Phase | Count | % | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **N/A (non-drug/device studies)** | 31 | 77.5% | Most are device/behavioral studies without traditional phase designation |
| **Unknown** | 8 | 20.0% | Likely stalled or observational studies |
| **Phase 1** | 1 | 2.5% | Minimal early safety work |
| **Phase 2** | 1 | 2.5% | Near-zero late-stage confirmation trials |
| **Phase 3** | 1 | 2.5% | Only 1 Phase 3 trial in the entire pipeline |

### Key Gap Findings

1. **Zero civic/nonprofit-sponsored trials** — No trials specifically designed to serve underserved communities. All industry trials target commercial markets; academic trials prioritize publication over access.

2. **Near-zero late-stage efficacy trials** — Only 1 Phase 2 and 1 Phase 3 trial exist, creating a severe "valley of death" between device innovation and proven clinical adoption.

3. **No pediatric-focused trials** — None of the 40+ trials specifically target children or adolescents with limb loss.

4. **No rural US trial sites** — All trials are located in urban centers (Boston, San Diego, Las Vegas, Jacksonville, etc.) or international sites (Belgium, France, UK).

5. **No trials with explicit underserved population targeting** — No trials mention low-income, uninsured, racial/ethnic minority, or non-English-speaking recruitment plans.

6. **Small sample sizes** — Most trials enroll 12–30 participants; only the Össur registry (2,000) and the perspiration study (180) have larger samples. Small trials limit generalizability.

7. **Geographic concentration** — The Chicago area has virtually no active prosthetic trials (the only Chicago-area trial is NCT06218238, a VA study on the Ossur Power Knee with 20 participants).

## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Mobile DME Unit (Priority: HIGH)
| Detail | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Zero DME/prosthetic vendors within 500 m — prosthetic users must travel for basic supplies |
| **Solution** | Monthly mobile prosthetist van parked at or near SRAL for walk-in socket adjustments, liner fittings, and supply distribution |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$15,000/year |
| **Target Population** | Uninsured/underinsured prosthetic users, those with mobility limitations |

### Strategy 2: Language Access Program (Priority: HIGH)
| Detail | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Chicago's diverse immigrant communities (Latinx, Polish, Arabic, Chinese) lack language-accessible trial participation |
| **Solution** | Translate consent forms & study materials into Spanish, Polish, Arabic, Chinese; hire bilingual community coordinators |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$25,000 startup + ~$15,000/year |
| **Target Population** | Non-English-speaking prosthetic users, immigrant communities |

### Strategy 3: FQHC Partnerships (Priority: HIGH)
| Detail | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Underserved populations on Chicago's South/West Side lack pathways into clinical trials |
| **Solution** | Partner with Federally Qualified Health Centers for screening, referral, and out-reach to diverse, low-income patients |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$40,000/year |
| **Target Population** | Low-income, uninsured, racial/ethnic minority prosthetic users |

### Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach (Priority: MEDIUM)
| Detail | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Rural Illinois/Wisconsin prosthetic users cannot travel to Chicago for trial participation |
| **Solution** | Virtual consults and tele-rehabilitation for mobility-limited patients; leverage VA rural telehealth infrastructure |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$30,000/year |
| **Target Population** | Rural prosthetic users, homebound patients |

### Strategy 5: Pediatric Trial Design (Priority: MEDIUM)
| Detail | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Zero pediatric prosthetic trials exist; children are profoundly underserved |
| **Solution** | Develop a child-friendly trial with flexible age ranges, family-centered outcomes, and play-based assessment tools |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$250,000 startup |
| **Target Population** | Children and adolescents with limb loss |

### Strategy 6: SRAL On-Site Resource Corner (Priority: HIGH)
| Detail | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | SRAL is a research center; prosthetic users visiting for trials get no immediate tangible benefit |
| **Solution** | Create a walk-in resource corner with DME samples, insurance navigation assistance, and walk-in socket adjustments |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$10,000 startup |
| **Target Population** | All prosthetic users visiting SRAL, especially trial participants |

### Strategy 7: ADA Parking Solutions (Priority: MEDIUM)
| Detail | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | No reserved ADA parking within 200 m of SRAL entrance |
| **Solution** | Negotiate reserved ADA spots with nearby garage; arrange shuttle service for mobility-limited patients |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$5,000 startup |
| **Target Population** | Mobility-limited prosthetic users, wheelchair users |

### Strategy 8: Adaptive Cycling Program (Priority: LOW)
| Detail | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Active mobility options are limited for prosthetic users |
| **Solution** | Partner with Divvy to offer adaptive cycles at nearby stations; promotes independent mobility |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$10,000 startup |
| **Target Population** | Prosthetic users with mobility to participate in adaptive cycling |
