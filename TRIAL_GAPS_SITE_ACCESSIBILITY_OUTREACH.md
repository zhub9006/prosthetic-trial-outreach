### 1.4 Critical Gaps — Where Underserved Groups Are Missing

#### 🔴 Gap 1: No Trials in the Chicago Metro Area
- The nearest active trial site is **University of Illinois at Chicago** (41.85003, -87.65005) — ~3 km from Shirley Ryan AbilityLab.
- **No active prosthetic trials** are located in Streeterville/Near North Side where the assessment site is.
- **Implication:** Chicago-area prosthetic users must travel to access trial opportunities, creating a geographic barrier.

#### 🔴 Gap 2: No Trials Targeting Low-Income / Uninsured Populations
- All 10 trials recruit through academic medical centers, VA facilities, or specialized prosthetic clinics.
- **No trials** specifically target uninsured, underinsured, or low-income prosthetic users.
- **No trials** address prosthetic access, cost, or insurance coverage barriers.
- **Implication:** The population most in need of prosthetic rehabilitation — often low-income, elderly, and minority — is invisible in the clinical trial landscape.

#### 🟠 Gap 3: No Trials for K0/K1 (Household/Very Limited) Ambulators
- The Otto Bock trial (NCT04784429) explicitly requires **K2+ ambulators** (limited community ambulators).
- The Mayo Clinic trial (NCT05225792) requires **K3–K4** community ambulators.
- **No trials** include K0 (non-ambulatory) or K1 (household ambulator) populations.
- **Implication:** The most severely mobility-impaired prosthetic users — who arguably need the most advanced rehabilitation — are excluded from research.

#### 🟠 Gap 4: Minimal Representation of Racial/Ethnic Minorities
- The UIC trial (NCT04212299) reported: 3 Black, 2 White, 1 Hispanic out of 5 participants — very small sample, not representative.
- The VA trials (NCT03995238, NCT05437562) primarily serve Veterans, who are predominantly White and male.
- **No trials** report race/ethnicity recruitment targets or strategies for minority engagement.
- **Implication:** prosthetic rehabilitation research does not reflect the diversity of the amputee population.

#### 🟠 Gap 5: No Trials Focused on Aging Prosthetic Users (65+)
- Only the Otto Bock trial (NCT04784429) specifically targets **年龄 ≥65**.
- The VA trials target ages 40–89, but focus on specific conditions (diabetes, PAD).
- **No trials** focus on age-related prosthetic fit issues, skin integrity, or mobility decline in older adults.
- **Implication:** As the population ages and diabetes-related amputations increase, older prosthetic users are understudied.

#### 🟡 Gap 6: No Trials on Prosthetic Fit, Comfort, or Socket Design for Diverse Body Types
- One small pilot (NCT04212299, 5 participants) explored socket design.
- **No trials** address how prosthetic fit varies across body types, weights, or activity levels.
- **No trials** on socket comfort for women, children, or athletes.
- **Implication:** Fit and comfort — major predictors of prosthetic use — remain under-researched.

#### 🟡 Gap 7: No Trials on Psychological / Social Dimensions of Prosthetic Use
- One trial (NCT06160336) addressed phantom limb pain via TENS.
- One trial included a psychiatry referral (Medrina, nearby) but not as a study focus.
- **No trials** examine depression, social isolation, body image, or vocational rehabilitation.
- **Implication:** The psychosocial burden of limb loss is poorly addressed in clinical research.

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: Community-Based Trial Awareness Campaign

**Goal:** Reach underserved prosthetic users who are unaware of clinical trial opportunities.

- **Partner with Chicago-area prosthetic clinics** (Hanger Clinic, OTC Orthotic & Prosthetic, Freedom Innovations) to distribute trial information.
- **Host monthly "Prosthetic Research Awareness" sessions** at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab — open to patients, families, and caregivers.
- **Develop multilingual materials** (English, Spanish, Polish — reflecting Chicago's demographics) explaining trial participation benefits and rights.
- **Use community health workers (CHWs)** from South Side and West Side communities to conduct outreach in underserved neighborhoods.

### 3.2 Strategy 2: Transportation & Access Support Program

**Goal:** Remove geographic and mobility barriers to trial participation.

- **Establish a transportation voucher program** partnering with CTA and rideshare services (Uber Health, Lyft) for trial visits.
- **Create a "Prosthetic Research Navigator" role** at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab to help participants schedule visits, arrange transportation, and coordinate care.
- **Offer virtual/hybrid trial options** where feasible — leveraging the AbilityLab's telehealth infrastructure for follow-up visits.
- **Provide parking validation** or reserved parking spots for trial participants at nearby multi-storey facilities.

### 3.3 Strategy 3: Inclusive Trial Design Advocacy

**Goal:** Address the systematic exclusion of underserved groups from prosthetic trials.

- **Advocate for K0/K1 ambulator inclusion** in future trial protocols — working with sponsors like the VA and NIH.
- **Push for demographic reporting requirements** — race, ethnicity, income level, and insurance status in all prosthetic trial registrations.
- **Propose a "Chicago Prosthetic Equity Trial"** — a multi-site study specifically designed to include low-income, minority, elderly, and low-mobility prosthetic users.
- **Collaborate with UIC's prosthetics research program** (already conducting socket design trials) to expand their inclusion criteria.

### 3.4 Strategy 4: Psychosocial & Holistic Support Integration

**Goal:** Address the mental health and social dimensions of prosthetic use that are currently underserved.

- **Integrate mental health screening** into prosthetic rehabilitation programs at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, with referrals to nearby Medrina (psychiatry) and Northwestern Medicine.
- **Establish a peer support network** — connecting new amputees with experienced prosthetic users for mentorship and social connection.
- **Partner with vocational rehabilitation services** to address employment barriers for prosthetic users.
- **Develop a "Prosthetic Wellness" group program** addressing pain management, body image, and social reintegration.

### 3.5 Strategy 5: Pharmacy & Medication Access Partnership

**Goal:** Ensure prosthetic users have reliable access to medications and supplies.

- **Coordinate with the 3 nearby Walgreens locations** for prescription fulfillment and prosthetic-related supply availability.
- **Establish a medication assistance program** for uninsured prosthetic users who need pain management, antibiotics, or other medications.
- **Create a "Prosthetic Supply Kit"** with basic care items distributed through the AbilityLab and nearby clinics.

### 3.6 Strategy 6: Data-Driven Continuous Improvement

**Goal:** Use data to track and improve outreach effectiveness.

- **Build a dashboard** tracking: trial awareness rates, referral volumes, demographic reach, and participant retention.
- **Conduct quarterly community surveys** to identify new barriers and opportunities.
- **Publish findings** in open-access journals to share the outreach model with other cities.
- **Partner with the GitHub community** (this repo) to maintain and iterate on the outreach strategy.
