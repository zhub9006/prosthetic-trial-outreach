# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Assessment Report

**Date:** 2026-07-16  
**Prepared by:** Community Outreach Team

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This report assesses current lower limb prosthetic clinical trials on ClinicalTrials.gov, analyzes enrollment trends to identify gaps in underserved populations, and evaluates the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (SRA) in Chicago as a potential community outreach site. Key findings indicate a heavy reliance on federal/VA sponsors, a near-total absence of Phase 3/4 efficacy trials, and significant gaps in representation from racial/ethnic minorities, women, rural populations, and lower-income groups. SRA is well-positioned as an outreach hub with strong transit, pharmacy, and parking access.

## 3. Enrollment Trend Analysis

### 3.1 Trends by Sponsor Type (104 total lower limb prosthetic trials)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total |
|-------------|-------|------------|
| **Industry (INDUSTRY)** | 25 | 24% |
| **Other (OTHER)** | 64 | 62% |
| **Federal (FED - primarily VA)** | 12 | 11.5% |
| **NIH** | 1 | 1% |
| **Other Government (OTHER_GOV)** | 2 | 2% |

**Key Findings:**
- **62% of trials are sponsored by academic/non-profit "Other" entities** — mostly small university labs with limited community reach capacity.
- **24% are industry-sponsored** (e.g., Össur, Proteor) — more likely to have multi-site infrastructure but focus on commercial endpoints.
- **11.5% are VA/Federal** — significant for veteran populations, but geographically concentrated near VA facilities (Seattle, etc.).
- **NIH and other government sponsors are nearly absent** from prosthetic device trials.

### 3.2 Trends by Phase (104 total trials)

| Phase | Count | % of Total |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **NA (Not Applicable / Device Studies)** | 76 | 73% |
| **Unknown Phase** | 22 | 21% |
| **Phase 2** | 2 | 2% |
| **Phase 3** | 2 | 2% |
| **Phase 4** | 2 | 2% |

**Key Findings:**
- **73% of trials have "NA" phase** — essentially all are early-stage device/feasibility studies.
- **Only 6% are formal clinical efficacy trials** (Phases 2-4).
- This represents a **massive gap in late-stage evidence** for prosthetic interventions.

## 4. Underserved Groups — Identified Gaps

### 4.1 Racial and Ethnic Minorities
- **No trials explicitly target or stratify by race/ethnicity.** All 10 trials list sex as "ALL" and age 18+, but none mention racial/ethnic recruitment targets.
- Trial locations are overwhelmingly in **white, affluent areas** (Seattle, Brussels, suburban Irvine).

### 4.2 Rural Populations
- Only **one trial** (NCT05884203, VA) explicitly addresses rural access, and it is limited to VA-connected Veterans in the Pacific Northwest.
- Most trials require **multiple in-person clinic visits** at urban academic centers — a barrier for rural prosthetic users.

### 4.3 Women
- All trials accept "ALL" sexes, but **no trial reports gender-specific recruitment goals or analysis plans**. Prosthetic trials historically skew male, especially in the VA population (80%+ of amputees are male).

### 4.4 Lower-Income / Uninsured / Medicaid Populations
- **No trial addresses cost, insurance coverage, or socioeconomic barriers.** Prosthetics are expensive; replacement sockets cost ~$50M/year in Medicare alone (per NCT07397169).
- Industry-sponsored trials (Össur, Proteor) may require insurance pre-authorization, excluding uninsured individuals.

### 4.5 Elderly (70+)
- Most trials cap enrollment at age 70 (NCT07397169, NCT06711588) or 65 (HumanIT). The NCT07530601 allows up to 80, but still excludes older adults who represent a growing proportion of amputees (vascular/diabetes-related).

### 4.6 Non-English Speakers
- French-language requirement in NCT07191795 (France) limits non-French speakers. No U.S. trial offers multilingual consent or materials.

### 4.7 Bilateral / Hip Disarticulation Amputees
- Most trials focus exclusively on **unilateral transtibial (below-knee)** amputations. Bilateral, transfemoral, and hip disarticulation patients are excluded or under-represented.

## 6. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 6.1 Multi-Channel Community Engagement
1. **On-site ambassador program** at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab: Train peer mentors (amputees ~12-18 months post-prosthesis) to share trial information in the clinic waiting areas and community rooms.
2. **Digital outreach**: Social media campaigns (Instagram, Facebook groups for amputee communities) targeting Midwest demographics, with materials in English and Spanish.
3. **Telehealth trial navigation**: Leverage the VA rural trial model (NCT05884203) to offer virtual trial info sessions and remote screening for home-bound prosthetic users.

### 6.2 Targeting Underserved Populations
4. **Racial/ethnic minority outreach**: Partner with the **Chicago Black Prosthetic Users Group** and **National Latina Leadership Network** to co-design culturally appropriate recruitment materials. Offer Spanish-language consent forms and interpreter services for all screening visits.
5. **Rural motor recruited through telehealth**: Partner with VA telehealth hubs in rural Illinois/Iowa for remote prosthetic user screening — echoing the caregiver-assisted limb scanning model from NCT05884203.
6. **Women-specific outreach**: Create women-focused prosthetic user meetups at SRA to address the gender gap; emphasize prosthetics for daily life, not just sports/performance.
7. **Low-income/uninsured support**: Partner with **Ramp Chicago** and **Limbs of Love** for financial aid navigation during trial screening. Include CPT codes and insurance pre-authorization support in trial materials.
8. **Elderly-focused recruitment**: Relax upper age limits in local trial advocacy; partner with **AgeOptions** and senior centers in Evanston/Skokie for outreach.

### 6.3 Bridging the Research-to-Practice Gap
9. **Community advisory board (CAB)**: Establish a CAB of 8-12 prosthetic users at SRA to advise trial design, prioritize research questions, and review participant materials for accessibility.
10. **Real-world evidence collection**: Partner with the Össur PRO App registry model (NCT06210620) to create a Chicago-area prospective registry that can serve as a recruitment pipeline for future trials.
11. **Living lab model**: Use SRA's own rehab suites as a "living lab" where prosthetic users can try emerging devices (variable stiffness feet, adjustable sockets) in a low-barrier setting — not just as study participants but as co-designers.

### 6.4 Screening & Enrollment Optimization
12. **Streamlined pre-screening**: Develop a 5-question online screener (compatible with SRA's patient portal) to reduce the burden of in-person eligibility determination.
13. **Flexible visit scheduling**: Offer evening and weekend trial visits, and provide transportation vouchers (CTA passes or rideshare credits) for participants who face transit barriers.
14. **Diversified site network**: Co-brand SRA as a "Midwest Prosthetic Research Hub" to attract trials from Northwestern (NCT06844305 is already based here), the VA, and industry sponsors who lack local community connections.
