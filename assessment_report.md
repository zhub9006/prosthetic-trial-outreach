## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Close the K2 Community Gap 🎯

**Problem:** Most trials exclude K2 (limited community) ambulators and focus on K3+ users with high-tech devices.

**Proposed Action:**
- Launch a **K2 Prosthetic Trial Navigation Program** at SRAL targeting limited community ambulators
- Partner with Hanger Institute (NCT06937242) and VA GEM study (NCT03995238) for referral pathways
- Develop outreach materials in **Spanish, Polish, and Mandarin** (top Chicago languages)
- Host monthly **"Prosthesis Options & Trials 101"** workshops for K2 users and families at SRAL

### Strategy 2: Multi-Site Referral Network for Rural & Underserved Urban Populations 🌍

**Problem:** 72% of trials are single-site academic studies in major cities; zero trials in rural areas or developing nations.

**Proposed Action:**
- Establish **satellite outreach hubs** at VA facilities in underserved regions (rural IL, IN, WI)
- Create a **"Chicago Model" playbook** for prosthetic trial outreach that can be replicated in:
  - **Pakistan** (1 trial vs. 182 needed) — partner with Shaukat Khanum Memorial Hospital
  - **Nigeria/Ghana** (0 trials) — partner with Lagos University Teaching Hospital
  - **India** (0 trials) — partner with AIIMS Delhi
- Use **telerehabilitation principles** (like NCT05768802 MIRA study) to reduce geographic barriers

### Strategy 3: Address the Demographic Diversity Gap 🌈

**Problem:** Elderly, women, minorities, rural populations, and congenital deficiency users are systematically excluded.

**Proposed Action:**
- Require **demographic diversity quotas** in all SRAL-linked outreach trials:
  - Minimum 25% female participants
  - Minimum 30% racial/ethnic minority enrollment
  - Include participants >65 years old (target: 40%)
  - Include congenital LLA deficiency participants
- Partner with community organizations:
  - **Amputee Coalition of America** (Midwest chapter)
  - **Limbs International** (prosthetics for developing nations)
  - **National Black Men's Collective** (amputee support)
  - **Spanish Amputee Support Network (Chicago)**
- Create **peer ambassador programs** pairing current prosthetic users with trial participants

### Strategy 4: Leverage SRAL's Location for Maximum Reach 🏙️

**Problem:** SRAL's zero healthcare/pharmacy/groceries within 500m could limit participant retention for long studies.

**Proposed Action:**
- **Badge-based community access** SRAL → partnered pharmacy discount (CVS on Michigan Ave, ~1.2km)
- Coordinate **voucher-based rideshare (Uber Health / Lyft)** for participants with mobility limitations
- Establish a **mobile prosthetics clinic** (vannedevice lab) that rotates to SRAL + satellite sites
- Create an **on-site waiting/relaxation area** within SRAL with:
  - Prosthetic device charging stations
  - Peer counseling kiosks
  - Multilingual trial navigation assistants
- Partner with Divvy bike-share for **adaptive cycling options**
- Negotiate **garage prepaid parking** for trial participants (Doubletree 162m, 24/7)

### Strategy 5: Bridge the Phase Gap — From Feasibility → Implementation 📈

**Problem:** 80% of prosthesis trials are Phase NA (device feasibility) with no translational pathway to community implementation.

**Proposed Action:**
- Launch a **"Prosthetic Implementation Science" initiative** at SRAL
- Develop **community-based participatory research (CBPR)** protocols for prosthetic outcomes:
  - Community mobility as primary outcome (not just lab-based gait measures)
  - Social participation and return-to-work metrics
  - Quality-of-life measured by PROs (patient-reported outcomes)
- Advocate for **Phase 2/3 hybrid designs** in device trials that include real-world community settings
- Create a **"Prosthetic Trial Registry"** at SRAL that tracks all ongoing trials and matches participants

## Part 4: Site Assessment Summary & Recommendations

### Strengths of SRAL as an Outreach Site
✅ **World-class reputation** — prosthetic R&D home base
✅ **Transit access** — 48 CTA bus stops, 4 Divvy bike-share stations
✅ **Walkability** — 10/10 score
✅ **24/7 paid parking** — 162m away
✅ **Identity** — already a prosthetic research hub

### Weaknesses & Mitigations ⚠️

| Weakness | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Zero healthcare/pharmacy within 500m | SRAL IS the healthcare facility; clear GPS wayfinding; partner CVS/Walgreens |
| Zero parks/restaurants/groceries within 500m | Partner with nearby restaurants for meal vouchers; mobile clinic |
| No nearby rail station (~1km) | CTA bus schedule + rideshare vouchers |
| Neighborhood score 3.6/10 overall | Focus intensive digital outreach rather than walk-up |
| Limited inclusive fitness | Negotiate partnerships with Pure Barre/CorePower for adaptive classes |

### Final Recommendation
**PROCEED with SRAL as the flagship outreach site**, but invest heavily in:
1. **Digital-first outreach** (not walk-in based)
2. **Transportation/access support** (rideshare vouchers + garage discounts)
3. **Satellite extension** of SRAL services to underserved neighborhoods via mobile clinic
4. **International partnership development** for global prosthetic trial equity
