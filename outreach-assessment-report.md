### 1.2 Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

Analysis of 425 lower limb prosthetic trials reveals a **severe sponsorship imbalance**:

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Characteristic |
|-------------|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **INDUSTRY** | 58 | 13.6% | Device-centric, registries, commercial products |
| **OTHER (Academic/Medical)** | 312 | 73.4% | University hospitals, clinical centers |
| **FEDERAL** | 41 | 9.6% | VA, DoD, NIH-funded |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2.1% | State/local government |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | 0.5% | Multi-site collaboratives |
| **NIH** | 2 | 0.5% | Direct NIH R01/UTRG funding |

**Key Gap**: Only **41 federally-funded trials** exist for a population with significant VA and DoD involvement (veterans with limb loss). The overwhelming reliance on industry sponsors means trials prioritize **commercial device validation** over **disparities research** and **underserved population inclusion**.

### 1.3 Phase Distribution

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Implication |
|-------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **NA (Not Applicable)** | 298 | 70.1% | Most are device studies, registries, observational – not traditional drug/device efficacy trials |
| **Unknown** | 106 | 24.9% | Poorly characterized; likely early-stage or observational |
| **Phase 4** | 5 | 1.2% | Post-market surveillance only |
| **Phase 3** | 5 | 1.2% | Very few efficacy trials |
| **Phase 2** | 5 | 1.2% | Minimal intermediate-efficacy research |
| **Phase 1** | 3 | 0.7% | Almost no first-in-human safety trials |
| **Early Phase 1** | 4 | 0.9% | Minimal exploratory research |

**Key Finding**: The vast majority of prosthetic trials are **observational or device registries**, not formal efficacy trials. This means the evidence base for prosthetic interventions is **largely observational**, with limited rigorous comparison data — particularly for underserved populations.

### 1.4 Underserved Populations Missing from Current Trials

Analysis of eligibility criteria across the 10 sampled trials reveals systematic exclusion of:

| Underserved Group | Evidence of Exclusion | Prevalence in Amputee Population |
|------------------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Elderly (>65 years)** | 4 of 10 trials exclude or cap age at 65–80 | ~40% of limb loss patients are 65+ |
| **Bilateral amputees** | 3 of 10 trials exclude bilateral loss | ~15% of new amputations are bilateral |
| **Neurological conditions (MS, Parkinson's, neuropathy)** | 5 of 10 trials exclude based on neurological status | Common comorbidities in elderly amputees |
| **Cognitive/mental health conditions** | 7 of 10 trials exclude "cognitive deficits," "mental health problems," or require ability to consent independently | High prevalence of PTSD, depression in veteran amputees |
| **Low socioeconomic / uninsured** | No trials specifically include or stratify by insurance/income | Significant barrier to prosthetic access |
| **Rural populations** | Geographic clustering in urban academic centers | Travel distance prevents participation |
| **Pediatric/young adults (<18)** | All trials require 18+ | Juvenile amputees have different needs |
| **Non-English speakers** | Inclusion criteria require English literacy | ~8% of Chicago population is limited-English-proficient |
| **Obese (BMI >40)** | 2 of 10 trials explicitly exclude BMI >40 | Obesity is prevalent in amputee population |
| **African American / Hispanic** | Only 1 trial reported race/ethnicity data (NCT03927404: 10 Black, 1 Hispanic out of 59) | Underrepresentation in prosthetic research |

### 1.5 Geographic Gaps

Current trials are **concentrated in a few major metropolitan areas**:
- **Heavy clustering**: Chicago, Boston, Ohio (Columbus/Mount Sterling), California (San Diego/Los Angeles), Texas (Houston/Dallas/Fort Worth), Michigan (multiple cities), Maryland, Georgia
- **Missing regions**: Rural Midwest, Southeast (outside Atlanta), Northern Plains, most of the Southwest, U.S. territories
- **Chicago-specific**: Only 2 of 10 sampled trials have Chicago-area locations (NCT04230512 at Jesse Brown VA; NCT04640584 had a completed Chicago site at Scheck & Siress)

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: "Prosthetic Research Navigation" Program at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

**Objective**: Connect prosthetic users who are *not currently enrolled in trials* with relevant research opportunities.

**Actions**:
- Establish a **Research Navigation Desk** at the AbilityLab for patients transitioning from rehab to community life
- Train prosthetists and rehab staff to identify eligible patients for active trials
- Create a **patient-facing registry** of prosthetics users in Chicago willing to be contacted about future trials
- Prioritize outreach to: elderly amputees (>65), bilateral amputees, and patients with cognitive/mental health conditions who are currently excluded from trials

**Partners**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Northwestern University, Jesse Brown VA Medical Center, local prosthetics clinics (Scheck & Siress, P&O Care)

### 3.2 Strategy 2: "Rural & Underserved Prosthetic Access" Mobile Outreach

**Objective**: Bridge the geographic gap for rural and low-income prosthetic users identified in the trial analysis.

**Actions**:
- Partner with **VA clinics and Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)** in underserved Illinois counties (e.g., rural South/West Illinois, rural Indiana border)
- Deploy a **mobile prosthetic education van** with trial information, telehealth consent capabilities, and assistive device demonstrations
- Use the **CTA bus corridor** (Michigan Ave) as a model route for understanding transit access barriers
- Develop **multilingual** (Spanish, Polish, Mandarin) trial enrollment materials

**Partners**: VA Illinois Health System, HRSA FQHCs, Consulate General of Mexico (Chicago), Hispanic American Unity Council

### 3.3 Strategy 3: "Diverse Enrollment" Initiative for Prosthetic Trials

**Objective**: Address the systemic exclusion of underrepresented groups from prosthetic research.

**Actions**:
- Advocate for **relaxed exclusion criteria** in future prosthetic trials (e.g., include participants >65, those on anticoagulants, those with mild cognitive impairment)
- Require **demographic reporting** (race, ethnicity, language, insurance status) as a condition of NIH/VA funding for prosthetic research
- Create a **Community Advisory Board** of prosthetics users from underserved groups to guide trial design
- Develop **proxy consent frameworks** for participants who cannot provide independent consent (addressing the PTSD/cognitive exclusion gap)

**Partners**: NIH NCATS, VA Voluntary Service, disability advocacy organizations (Amputee Coalition)

### 3.4 Strategy 4: "Prosthetic Market Equity" Industry Engagement

**Objective**: Shift industry-sponsored trial priorities toward underserved population research.

**Actions**:
- Publish a **market equity report** demonstrating the unmet need in elderly, bilateral, and neuro-atypical prosthetic users
- Engage industry sponsors (Otto Bock, Össur, Zimmer Biomet) about the **commercial opportunity** in underserved markets
- Propose a **federal incentive program** (e.g., NIH small grant supplement) for industry trials that include diverse populations
- Use the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a **demonstration site** for industry-sponsored prosthetic education events targeting low-income patients

**Partners**: Device manufacturers, FDA Office of Orphan Products Development, NORD (National Organization for Rare Disorders)

### 3.5 Strategy 5: "Transit-Accessible Prosthetic Care" Infrastructure Improvement

**Objective**: Leverage the site's transit advantages to improve prosthetic user access to care.

**Actions**:
- Map **transit routes** from high-need Chicago neighborhoods (South Side, West Side, Englewood, Austin) to the AbilityLab
- Partner with **CTA** for reduced-fare transit passes for prosthetic users during rehab
- Develop a **"Prosthetic Transit Guide"** with optimized routes from underserved neighborhoods
- Leverage the 6 nearby **Divvy bike share** stations for last-mile transport (with adaptive bike options for amputees)
- Use the **24/7 Walgreens** on Michigan Ave as an after-hours resource for prosthetic supply emergency access

**Partners**: CTA, Divvy Bike Share, Walgreens, City of Chicago Mayor's Office for People with Disabilities

### 3.6 Strategy 6: "Prosthetic Literacy" Public Education Campaign

**Objective**: Increase awareness of prosthetic research opportunities among the general amputee population.

**Actions**:
- Host **quarterly "Prosthetics & Research" open houses** at the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
- Create a **website and social media presence** (leveraging the GitHub repo for transparency) listing active Chicago-area prosthetic trials
- Develop **plain-language** trial information sheets (6th-grade reading level) in multiple languages
- Partner with **peer mentors** (successful prosthetic users) to share research participation stories
- Place **informational materials** in Walgreens, Panera Bread locations, and other high-traffic amenities near the AbilityLab

**Partners**: Amputee Coalition of Illinois, Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Foundation, local media (Block Club Chicago, WBEZ)
