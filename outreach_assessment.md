# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Assessment

## 1. Clinical Trial Landscape — Lower Limb Prosthetics

> The ClinicalTrials.gov API tools available have a restrictive query schema that prevents filtered searches for "lower limb prosthetic" trials. The analysis below identifies known gaps in the prosthetic trial ecosystem that should be addressed through outreach.

### Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

- **Race/Ethnicity**: Most prosthetic trials concentrate at academic medical centers in urban, predominantly white populations. Community-based outreach is needed to reach Black, Hispanic, and Indigenous communities.
- **Low-Income / Uninsured**: Trials often exclude Medicaid or uninsured participants due to travel/visit-cost barriers. Site selection near transit and free-area services is critical.
- **Rural Populations**: Few trials offer remote/pre-screening telemedicine pathways, excluding rural prosthetic users.
- **Aging / Comorbidity Burden**: Many trials cap age at 65–70 or exclude complex medical histories, missing the majority of actual prosthetic candidates.
- **Gender Diversity**: Female amputees are underrepresented in lower-limb prosthetic device trials.
- **Veteran Populations**: VA-linked prosthetic trials often have narrow eligibility that excludes recent-envelope veterans with comorbid TBI or PTSD.
- **Pediatric/Young Adult**: Very few trials focus on prosthetic development for growing children and adolescents.

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### A. Remove Geographic & Transit Barriers
1. **Transit-Voucher Program**: Partner with CTA to provide free/reduced fares for prosthetic users attending screening visits.
2. **Pop-Up Screening Sites**: Deploy mobile prosthetics screening to community centers, churches, and VA facilities on the South & West Sides.
3. **Telemedicine Pre-Screening**: Offer remote eligibility assessments to reduce in-person travel burden for rural and low-income participants.

### B. Diversify Recruitment to Reach Underserved Groups
1. **Community Health Worker (CHW) Partnership**: Embed CHWs from South/West Side neighborhoods into the outreach team to build trust and provide culturally competent navigation.
2. **Faith & Community Organization Partnerships**: Collaborate with Black churches, Hispanic community centers, and immigrant-serving orgs for word-of-mouth recruitment.
3. **Veterans & Women-Focused Outreach**: Target VA facilities and women's health clinics to address the gender and veteran representation gaps.

### C. Reduce Financial Barriers
1. **Travel & Lodging Stipends**: Provide bus/GT cards or rideshare credits for participants traveling from underserved neighborhoods.
2. **Same-Day Telehealth Visits**: Offer virtual follow-ups where protocol allows, reducing lost wages and transportation costs.
3. **Childcare & Interpretation**: On-site childcare and multilingual interpreters at all in-person visits.

### D. Leverage Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a Hub
1. **On-Site Prosthetics Fitting & Training**: Use the AbilityLab's in-house prosthetics lab for device fitting, reducing the need for participants to travel to separate sites.
2. **Peer Mentorship Program**: Connect current prosthetic users with potential trial participants through the AbilityLab's peer-support network.
3. **Community Education Nights**: Host monthly open-house events at the AbilityLab to educate prosthetic users about trial opportunities.

### E. Data-Driven Monitoring
1. **Track Demographics vs. Chicago Census**: Compare trial enrollment demographics against City of Chicago census tracts to measure outreach effectiveness.
2. **Quarterly Gap Review**: Re-assess enrollment gaps every quarter and adjust outreach tactics accordingly.
