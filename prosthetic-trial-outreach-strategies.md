**Key Gap:** Industry sponsorship is severely underrepresented (only 13.7%). This means:
- Fewer trials for **commercially relevant** prosthetic devices
- Limited access for **low-income/uninsured** populations who rely on industry-sponsored trials for device access
- Reduced **employer/workers' compensation** recruitment reach

- **Underserved populations** (rural, low-income, minority, elderly) have almost no access to novel prosthetic interventions through trials
- The pipeline from early-phase to late-phase trials is **severely bottlenecked**

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Bridging the Trial Gap — Macro Strategies

1. **Establish a Midwest Prosthetic Trial Network**
   - Leverage Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a Midwest hub
   - Partner with VA systems (Hines, Indianapolis, Minneapolis) to create a contiguous recruitment corridor
   - Target: 3 new industry-sponsored Phase 2–3 trials per year in the Midwest

2. **Industry Sponsorship Recruitment Program**
   - 86.3% of current trials are non-industry; actively recruit prosthetic manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Hanger, Blatchford)
   - Offer **in-kind prosthetic fitting** as participant incentive to offset industry trial participation burden
   - Create a "Prosthetic Trial Participation Voucher" program for uninsured participants

3. **Phase Pipeline Expansion**
   - Current: 95% of studies are observational (NA/Unknown phase)
   - Target: Convert at least 10 observational studies to interventional Phase 1–2 trials within 24 months
   - Establish a **prosthetic device innovation incubator** at the AbilityLab to feed early-phase trials

### 3.2 Underserved Population Recruitment — Targeted Strategies

4. **Rural & Remote Recruitment**
   - Deploy **mobile prosthetic assessment units** to rural Illinois, Indiana, Wisconsin
   - Partner with rural hospitals and FQHCs for screening and referral
   - Offer **travel stipends + overnight accommodations** via participant assistance fund

5. **Minority & Health Equity Outreach**
   - Partner with community health centers on Chicago's South and West Sides (e.g., Federico Garcia Health Center, Lawndale Christian Health Center)
   - Hire **bilingual trial navigators** (Spanish, Polish, Mandarin — reflecting Chicago's demographics)
   - Implement **community advisory boards** with prosthetic users from underrepresented groups

6. **Low-Income & Uninsured Access**
   - Create a **prosthetic trial participation fund** to cover: transit fares, parking, prosthetic socks/liners, and co-pays
   - Partner with **Illini Service Network** and **Chicago Department of Rehabilitation Services** for eligible participant referrals
   - Offer same-day prosthetic follow-up care at the AbilityLab to reduce return-visit burden

7. **Elderly & Comorbidity-Focused Trials**
   - Design trials with **inclusive eligibility** (allow diabetes, vascular disease, arthritis comorbidity)
   - Provide **on-site geriatric consultation** during trial visits
   - Address the current exclusion of 75+ participants from most prosthetic trials

### 3.3 Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Site-Specific Strategies

8. **Transit-Accessible Participant Program**
   - Negotiate a **CTA institutional pass** for trial participants (unlimited bus rides for 6–12 month trial duration)
   - Coordinate with CTA for **accessible boarding assistance** at Grand & McClurg stop
   - Provide a **shuttle service** from the Sedgwick 'L' station to the AbilityLab for participants with mobility limitations

9. **Parking & Travel Assistance**
   - Reserve **discounted parking** at the Doubletree garage for trial participants (negotiate block pricing)
   - Offer **Surge-Pass parking vouchers** for participants traveling from outside Chicago
   - Partner with **Enterprise RideWorks** or **ATS Taxi** for medically-necessary transport reimbursement

10. **Pharmacy & Medication Access Integration**
    - Establish a **on-site pharmacy liaison** who can coordinate Walgreens prescriptions during trial visits
    - Provide **medication adherence packaging** for trial drugs/prosthetic-related supplies
    - Ensure all trial-related prescriptions are covered under the **Walgreens 24/7 locations** within 500m

11. **Community Outreach Hub Model**
    - Use the AbilityLab's Streeterville location as a **recruitment and screening hub**
    - Host **monthly "Prosthetic Open House" events** — free fitting assessments, trial education, peer support
    - Partner with **Limbs International** and **Prosthetic Hope International** for community engagement
    - Create a **peer mentor network** connecting trial participants with experienced prosthetic users

12. **Digital & Virtual Trial Options**
    - Offer **telehealth prosthetic follow-up** to reduce in-person visit burden
    - Deploy **wearable sensor data collection** (remote gait monitoring) to minimize clinic visits
    - Create a **mobile app** for trial participants to log prosthetic use, report issues, and access resources

## 4. Summary

The lower limb prosthetic clinical trial landscape is characterized by a **severe dominance of academic/observational research** (73.5% OTHER-sponsored, 95% NA/Unknown phase) and **minimal industry or late-phase investment** (13.7% industry, 3.6% Phase 1–4). This creates a critical gap where **underserved populations** — low-income, rural, minority, elderly, and uninsured — have virtually no pathway to access novel prosthetic interventions through clinical trials.

The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab site in Chicago offers a **strong healthcare and pharmacy ecosystem** within 500m but faces **transit and parking barriers** that disproportionately affect the very populations we aim to serve. The proposed outreach strategies address these gaps through a combination of **Michelin-proximate trial network expansion**, **targeted underserved recruitment programs**, **site-specific accessibility improvements**, and **digital/virtual trial innovations**.

**Bottom line:** The AbilityLab is a viable outreach site, but only if supported by dedicated funding for participant travel assistance, industry trial recruitment, and structured health equity partnerships.
