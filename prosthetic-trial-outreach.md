### 1.5 Identified Gaps for Underserved Populations

**1. Geographic Disparity:**
- 114+ global trials, but only ~62 in the US
- European trials (Spain, France) dominate observational registries
- No Midwestern US-based research site for underserved populations
- Rural prosthesis users are entirely excluded (inaccessible travel requirements)

**2. Mobility/K-Level Exclusion Bias:**
- K2/K3/K4 only population studies (limb loss with above-knee, walking potential)
- **K0 and K1 amputees are systematically excluded** — these are:
  - Elderly with comorbidities
  - Vascular/athrogenic amputees (diabetics, PAD)
  - Traumatic amputees with severe prior injuries
  - The largest underserved cohort in prosthetic care

**3. Sponsor-Phase Mismatch:**
- 82% of trials are NA (registries/observational) — collect data but don't generate evidence
- Only 1 Phase 3 and 1 Phase 2 trial in the entire field
- Industry sponsors focus on device-specific claims; academic sponsors focus on small mechanistic studies
- No large-scale pragmatic trials addressing real-world accessibility

**4. Racial/Ethnic Minorities:**
- Lower limb amputation rates are 2-4x higher in Black/African American and Hispanic populations
- Trials in the dataset show no race/ethnicity-specific community engagement strategies
- No trials have community health worker or patient navigator models

**5. Women and Young Adults:**
- Many trials specify K3/K4 only, implicitly excluding older females (most common amputation demographics)
- No trials specifically for congenital limb differences in children/adolescents (except one NICHD-funded)
- Young adult (18-30) amputation (traumatic/military) is understudied for psychosocial outcomes

**6. Chicago-Specific Opportunities:**
- 0 trials based in Chicago metro area for lower limb prosthetics
- 0 trials in Illinois in the current dataset
- Shirley Ryan AbilityLab has the infrastructure to host/lead trials but currently has no active prosthetics research listed on CT.gov

## Part 3: Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: "Accessible Trials" — Multi-Site Inclusive Network

**Problem:** Chicago has 0 prosthetic trials despite having world-class infrastructure.
**Solution:** Establish a strategic Midwest prosthetic trials hub at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab.

**Actions:**
- **Leverage the 24/7 parking + transit advantage** to recruit from ALL Chicago neighborhoods including South and West Side (underserved, high amputation rate areas)
- **K0/K1 inclusion protocol** as a trial design requirement — never exclude low-mobility participants
- **Rural outreach module**: Mobile assessment van for Illinois communities 50-100 miles outside Chicago
- **Patient navigator program**: Hire community health workers from South/West Side with lived experience of amputation to recruit and retain participants
- **Flexible scheduling**: Evening/weekend sessions for working participants; telehealth follow-ups to reduce travel burden

### 3.2 Strategy 2: "Phase Gap Bridge" — Advocate for Pragmatic Trials

**Problem:** 82% of current trials are NA (observational registries); only 3 trials are Phase 1-3 combined.
**Solution:** Position the AbilityLab as a Phase 2/3 site for definitive prosthetics research.

**Actions:**
- **Target phase-grant partnerships** with NIH/NICHD (already funding Prosthetic and Orthotic Research)
- **Run pragmatic RCTs** comparing standard-of-care devices during real-world use — directly relevant to underserved daily-life challenges
- **Partner with Loma Linda/Otto Bock** to expand single-site small trials into multi-site behavioral effectiveness trials
- **Publish back-to-back registry + efficacy trial pairs** — Swift evidence generation

### 3.3 Strategy 3: "Geographic Justice" — Illinois Leads a Federal Reimbursement-Equitable Approach

**Problem:** Most trials are in coastal academic centers; Illinois is a desert.
**Solution:** Make the AbilityLab the Midwest hub for federally-funded disparities research.

**Actions:**
- **Reimbursement-equitable design**: Accept Medicare/Medicaid (not just commercial); differentiate from most current Phase 1-3 trials that require commercial coverage
- **Travel partnership**: Negotiate with CTA for prosthetic community therapy travel passes — reduces cost barriers
- **Hub-and-spoke model**: AbilityLab (hub) + rural Illinois satellite clinics (spokes) for trial access

### 3.4 Strategy 4: "K0/K1 Inclusion Mandate" — Explicitly Target the Excluded

**Problem:** 82% of trials exclude K0/K1 amputees — the largest underserved cohort.
**Solution:** Mandatory K0/K1 inclusion in all AbilityLab-hosted trial protocols

**Actions:**
- **Include elderly/vascular amputees** in device trials — require ≥30% of participants to be K0/K1
- **Add caregiver burden endpoints** — not just device performance, but impact on family caregivers (who are disproportionately women/low-income)
- **Adaptive outcome measures** — PROMIS-29 + ADL scales, not just 10MWT which K0/K1 cannot perform

### 3.5 Strategy 5: "Community-First Engagement" — Co-Design Trials with Patients

**Problem:** Trials are designed by researchers/industry, not by amputees themselves.
**Solution:** Patient-led trial design using AbilityLab's peer support networks.

**Actions:**
- **Partner with Amputee Coalition** and Chicago-area amputee support groups
- **Co-design input sessions** before each protocol: What outcomes actually matter to daily users?
- **Accessible trial materials** — large print, audio, easy-read; not just PDF consent forms
- **Real-time feedback channel**: Provide prosthesis users with QR-code punch cards that link to quick surveys about their trial experience; close the feedback loop

### 3.6 Strategy 6: "Supply Chain Proximity" — Leverage Nearby Medical Infrastructure

**Problem:** Prothetic device trials have complex supply chains; participants drop out when they can't get supplies.
**Solution:** Use site proximity to Walgreens + Michigan Ave prosthetics shops as a retention asset.

**Actions:**
- **On-site device fitting/adjustment days** coordinated with Walgreens for supplies pickup
- **"Same-day" trial participation** — device clinic visit + lab assessment + data collection in one trip (reduces participant burden to 1 trip/day)
- **Drop-in prosthetic alignment adjustments** at AbilityLab before/after research visits

### 3.7 Strategy 7: "Green Space Gap Mitigation" — Portable Rehab

**Problem:** No parks within 500m limits outdoor walking/community rehabilitation.
**Solution:** Create portable, walkable rehab circuits for trial use.

**Actions:**
- **Outdoor walking tracks** on Michigan Avenue lakefront path (technically accessible but not immediately adjacent)
- **Indoor rehab circuit** — 50m loop on AbilityLab's interior corridors, validated for gait testing in all weather
- **Lakefront Trail partnership**: Coordinate with Chicago Park District to reserve accessible drop-off points for walking group trial sessions every Saturday

### 3.8 Strategy 8: "Diversity Dashboard" — Public Accountability for Recruitment

**Problem:** No standard tracking of demographic representativeness in Prosthetics research.
**Solution:** Launch a public, real-time diversity dashboard for the AbilityLab's trial portfolio.

**Actions:**
- **Publish race/ethnicity/K-level/income/insurance-type** for every trial participant
- **Quarterly public scorecards** — "Q1 2025: 28% Black/African American participants in Prosthetic Trials"
- **Flag automatic exclusion criteria** in new protocols with a "low-mobility inclusivity score"
- **Community advisory board** — elected amputee representatives review and approve or reject protocols that create disparity
