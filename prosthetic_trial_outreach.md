## 2. Enrollment Trend Analysis: Sponsor & Phase

### By Sponsor Type (33 total trials)
- **Academic/Other (NON_PROFIT):** 24 studies (73%)
- **Industry:** 5 studies (15%)
- **Federal (NIH/DOD/VA):** 3 studies (9%)
- **NIH-specific:** 1 study (3%)

### By Phase
- **Unknown/Unspecified:** 8 studies (24%)
- **No Phase (Device/Observational):** 23 studies (70%)
- **Phase 3:** 1 study (3%)
- **Phase 2:** 1 study (3%)

### 🔍 Key Gaps Identified for Underserved Populations

1. **Geographic Disparity:** 73% of trials are conducted outside the United States (UK, France, Norway, Belgium, Austria, Egypt, Palestine, Switzerland). Only 3 trials are US-based, and **none are in the Midwest** outside one completed Shirley Ryan AbilityLab study.
2. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** Very few trials report race/ethnicity data. The Otto Bock C-Leg study explicitly noted "Race and Ethnicity Not Collected." Most US-based trials lack demographic reporting that would reveal enrollment of minority groups.
3. **Socioeconomic Access Gap:** No trials appear to specifically target low-income or uninsured populations. The Gaza study (NCT07519746) is the only one addressing a conflict-zone/underserved context.
4. **Rural vs. Urban:** All listed trials are at urban academic medical centers. No rural outreach or community-based recruitment is evident.
5. **Phase/Early-Stage Research Heaviness:** 70% of trials have no phase designation (observational or device-feasibility), suggesting a gap in later-stage clinical efficacy research for prosthetic technologies.
6. **K1/K0 Ambulator Exclusion:** Many trials (e.g., NCT06937242, NCT02765035) restrict to K2+ ambulators. Lower-functioning individuals (K0-K1) — who may benefit most from outreach — are systematically excluded.
7. **Age Bias:** Most studies exclude older adults or have upper age limits, despite older adults being a growing amputee demographic.

## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Fill the Geographic Gap — Midwest Prosthetic Trial Hub
- Partner with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Northwestern, and Hanger Institute to establish a **Midwest prosthetic outcomes registry**
- Target recruitment from Illinois, Indiana, Wisconsin — a region currently absent from trial data
- Leverage Chicago's diverse population to improve racial/ethnic representation

### Strategy 2: Community-Based Participatory Research (CBPR)
- Partner with local amputee support groups (e.g., Chicago Amputee Society, War Amps)
- Deploy mobile outreach clinics to community centers, VA facilities, and shut-in populations
- Use plain-language consent forms and culturally adapted materials in Spanish, Polish, and other local languages

### Strategy 3: Address the K0–K1 Ambulator Gap
- Design inclusive trial protocols that don't exclude lower-functioning individuals
- Develop validated outcome measures for non-ambulatory prosthetic users
- Create a "prosthetic secondary prevention" trial focused on pain, skin integrity, and social participation

### Strategy 4: Diversify Recruitment Channels
- Partner with community health centers serving minority populations (e.g., Spanish Health Service, Arab American Health Center)
- Use telehealth/virtual visit screening to reach rural Illinois and surrounding states
- Employ peer ambassadors (amputees who have completed trials) to build trust

### Strategy 5: Leverage the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Site
- Use the site's adjacent hospital infrastructure for comprehensive health screenings during trial visits
- Capitalize on the 24/7 Walgreens for medication adherence support
- Coordinate with Divvy bike-share for adaptive cycling programs for amputee fitness/recreation
- Partner with Whole Foods and accessible restaurants for nutrition counseling components

### Strategy 6: Industry-Academic-Community Partnership
- Engage Otto Bock, Ottobock, and other prosthetic manufacturers for device donation/equity programs
- Create a "prosthetic equity" fund to cover transportation, parking, and lost wages for trial participants
- Develop a registry that tracks long-term outcomes across demographic groups to inform future trials

### Strategy 7: Data Transparency & Demographic Reporting
- Require race/ethnicity, income, education, and insurance status in all new trial protocols
- Publish subgroup analyses by demographic variables
- Use the 33-trial landscape to advocate for NIH R01 funding targeting health equity in prosthetics research
