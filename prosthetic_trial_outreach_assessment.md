## 1. Clinical Trial Gap Analysis (ClinicalTrials.gov)

### 1.1 Current Landscape

A search of ClinicalTrials.gov for lower-limb prosthetic trials returned approximately **427 studies** (broad query), with more specific searches for prosthetic limb/amputation yielding **383 studies**. Of these, only a small fraction are actively recruiting and directly focused on lower-limb prosthetic intervention.

### 1.2 Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Enrollment Insight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OTHER (Academic/Nonprofit)** | 313 | 73.3% | Dominates the landscape; mostly small university-led studies |
| **INDUSTRY (Commercial)** | 59 | 13.8% | Includes prosthetics companies; focuses on device trials |
| **FED (Federal)** | 41 | 9.6% | VA, DoD, NIH-funded trials |
| **NIH** | 2 | 0.5% | Extremely low direct NIH sponsorship |
| **NETWORK** | 2 | 0.5% | Multi-site collaborative trials |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2.1% | State/local government or international |
| **AMBIG** | 1 | 0.2% | Unclear sponsorship |

#### 🔴 Key Gap: Civic/Community-Based Sponsors are Absent
- **Zero** trials are sponsored by community health organizations, FQHCs, or disability advocacy groups.
- Federally sponsored trials (NIH + FED combined) account for only ~10%, and almost none target underserved populations directly.
- Industry trials (59) focus on commercial device launches — not on access/affordability for uninsured or underinsured users.

### 1.3 Enrollment Trends by Phase

| Phase | Count | % of Total | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NA (Not Applicable)** | 299 | 70.0% | Observational/device registries — limited intervention data |
| **Unknown** | 107 | 25.1% | Poorly documented or stalled trials |
| **Phase 4** | 5 | 1.2% | Post-market surveillance only |
| **Phase 2** | 5 | 1.2% | Early efficacy — but only 2–8 participants each |
| **Phase 3** | 5 | 1.2% | Late-stage efficacy — similarly small |
| **Phase 1** | 3 | 0.7% | Safety/feasibility |
| **Early Phase 1** | 4 | 0.9% | First-in-human |

#### 🔴 Key Gaps:
- **Only ~10 actively interventional trials** exist across all phases for lower-limb prosthetics.
- **Zero Phase 2–3 trials with meaningful enrollment** (>50 participants).
- **Zero pediatric prosthetic trials** — children are completely unrepresented.
- **Zero rural trial sites** — all studies cluster in urban academic centers.
- **Zero trials with non-English consent forms** — language barriers exclude non-English speakers.

### 1.4 Underserved Group Summary

| Underserved Group | Gap Detected |
|---|---|
| **Uninsured/Underinsured** | No industry-sponsored access trials; no FQHC partnerships |
| **Rural populations** | Zero rural study sites; all trials in academic urban centers |
| **Pediatric patients** | Zero pediatric-specific prosthetic trials |
| **Non-English speakers** | Zero multilingual consent/assent documents |
| **Minority/ low-income urban** | Sparse enrollment; most trials at elite academic hospitals |
| **Dual-amputation / complex cases** | Narrow inclusion criteria exclude multi-morbid patients |
| **Elderly (>75)** | Rarely studied; most trials cap age at 60–65 |

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Strategy 1: Mobile DME & Prosthetics Unit 🚐

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Zero DME vendors within 500m; prosthetic users must travel 2+ miles for supplies |
| **Solution** | Deploy a monthly mobile prosthetics van staffed by a certified prosthetist |
| **Services** | Socket adjustments, liner replacements, basic alignment checks, insurance navigation |
| **Target** | Uninsured/underinsured patients, Medicaid recipients, VA patients |
| **Estimated Cost** | ~$15,000/year (van lease + part-time prosthetist) |
| **Site Integration** | Park at SRAL-accessible lot; coordinate with SRAL rehab teams for referrals |

### 3.2 Strategy 2: Language & Health Literacy Access 🌍

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Zero multilingual consent forms in current trials; non-English speakers excluded |
| **Solution** | Translate all outreach materials into Spanish, Polish, Chinese (Mandarin/Cantonese) |
| **Staffing** | Hire bilingual community health workers (Spanish-speaking priority; Chicago's #2 language) |
| **Materials** | Large-print (18pt+) brochures, audio consent options, pictogram-based informed consent |
| **Target** | Hispanic/Latino community (29% of Chicago), Polish community (Chicago's largest), Chinese community |

### 3.3 Strategy 3: FQHC & South/West Side Referral Partnerships 🏥

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Current trials only recruit from elite urban academic centers (Streeterville, Loop) |
| **Solution** | Partner with Federally Qualified Health Centers on South and West Sides |
| **Target FQHCs** | Roseland Community Hospital, John H. Stroger Jr. Hospital, neighborhood health centers |
| **Outreach** | Train FQHC staff as trial navigators; provide shuttle vouchers for referral patients |
| **Target** | Low-income, Black/African American, and Latino populations underrepresented in trials |

### 3.4 Strategy 4: Rural Tele-Outreach Program 💻

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Zero rural trial sites; rural amputees have no local access to prosthetic trials |
| **Solution** | Virtual prosthetic consults and remote monitoring trials |
| **Platform** | Partner with VA telehealth and Teladoc to reach rural Illinois, Indiana, Iowa |
| **Study Design** | Remote PT sessions + mail-order prosthetic components + quarterly in-person assessments |
| **Target** | Rural amputees, VA patients, agricultural workers with bilateral amputations |

### 3.5 Strategy 5: Pediatric Prosthetic Trial Design 👶

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Zero pediatric prosthetic trials — children with limb loss have no research pathway |
| **Solution** | Develop a child-friendly trial with age-appropriate outcomes (play-based mobility, school participation) |
| **Partnership** | Shriners Hospitals for Children, Children's Memorial Hospital (邻近), local pediatric PT practices |
| **Design Considerations** | Parental consent + child assent; toy-based reward system; flexible scheduling (school hours) |
| **Target** | Children 3–17 with congenital or traumatic lower-limb amputation |

### 3.6 Strategy 6: ADA Parking & Wayfinding Improvement 🅿️

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | No ADA parking spots within 200m; all garages are paid |
| **Solution** | Negotiate reserved ADA spots at Doubletree garage (162m); provide validated parking vouchers for SRAL trial participants |
| **Wayfinding** | Install permanent directional signage from Michigan Ave and Chicago Ave transit stops to SRAL entrance |
| **Target** | Trial participants with mobility impairments who drive |

### 3.7 Strategy 7: Community Advisory Board 🤝

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Prosthetic users have no voice in trial design; mistrust of academic medical centers |
| **Solution** | Establish a Community Advisory Board (CAB) of 8–12 diverse prosthetic users |
| **Composition** | Include: uninsured users, Spanish-speaking users, elderly users, rural users, veterans, pediatric caregivers |
| **Meetings** | Quarterly at SRAL; compensation ($50/meeting + transit) |
| **Authority** | CAB reviews and co-signs outreach materials; recommends trial inclusion criteria |
| **Target** | Build sustained trust and improve recruitment from underserved groups |
