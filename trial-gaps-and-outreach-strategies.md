## 2. Identified Gaps — Where Underserved Groups Are Missing

### 2.1 Population-Level Gaps

| Underserved Group | Evidence of Gap | Impact |
|-------------------|----------------|--------|
| **Low-income / uninsured amputees** | No trials specifically target socioeconomically diverse cohorts; most recruitment is from academic medical centers with insurance requirements | Cannot access cutting-edge prosthetic interventions or contribute to evidence base |
| **Rural residents** | All identified trials are in major urban centers (Chicago, Boston, LA, NYC, Baltimore, London, Toronto, Seoul, Gaza) | Transportation and geographic barriers prevent participation |
| **Minority / BIPOC communities** | No trials report demographic diversity metrics or target minority recruitment | Evidence base does not reflect the diversity of the amputee population |
| **Elderly amputees (75+)** | Most trials cap age at 70–80; few focus on age-specific prosthetic needs | Fragmented evidence for the fastest-growing amputee demographic |
| **Women** | Most trials use "ALL" for sex but do not analyze gender-specific outcomes | Prosthetic design and rehabilitation may not account for anatomical differences |
| **Veterans (beyond the Northwestern trial)** | Only 1 of 10 trials is explicitly veteran-focused | Despite high rates of military-related amputations, research infrastructure is thin |
| **Conflict-affected / international populations** | Only 1 trial (Gaza) addresses a conflict-zone population | Global amputee populations in low-resource settings are entirely unstudied |
| **Pediatric / young adult amputees** | No trials focus on congenital or pediatric amputation populations | Growing children have unique prosthetic needs that are unaddressed in clinical evidence |

### 2.2 Infrastructure & Trial Design Gaps

- **No Phase 1–2 device trials**: The prosthetic field skips early-stage testing, meaning innovative technologies (neural-controlled, bionic, adaptive sockets) never reach rigorous clinical evaluation.
- **Single-site dominance**: Most trials are single-site, limiting recruitment reach and generalizability.
- **No decentralized or hybrid trial models**: None of the identified trials use remote monitoring, mobile prosthetic clinics, or community-based participation — blocking access for mobility-limited participants.
- **Observer-only outcomes**: Most trials focus on clinician-reported measures; patient-centered outcomes (satisfaction, identity, social participation) are under-measured.
- **No health equity frameworks**: None of the trials incorporate health equity or social determinants of health as analysis variables.

## 4. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 4.1 Trial Design & Recruitment Strategies

1. **Decentralized Clinical Trials (DCT) Model**
   - Deploy mobile prosthetic assessment clinics that rotate through underserved neighborhoods (South Side, West Side, suburban communities)
   - Use remote monitoring (wearable sensors, video gait analysis) to reduce participant burden
   - Partner with community health centers for screening and referral

2. **Community Health Worker (CHW) Ambassador Program**
   - Train CHWs from amputee support organizations (e.g., Amputee Coalition of Illinois) as trial navigators
   - CHWs conduct door-to-door outreach in veteran housing, senior centers, and disability resource centers
   - Provide culturally competent education about trial participation rights and benefits

3. **Telehealth Pre-Screening**
   - Offer virtual screening visits to determine eligibility before requiring in-person travel
   - Mail trial materials (Informed Consent, questionnaires) to participants' homes
   - Use video calls for follow-up assessments when in-person visits are not feasible

4. **Transportation & Accommodation Support**
   - Provide Uber Health / Lyft Medical credits for trial visits
   - Partner with local hotels for discounted stay accommodations for out-of-town participants
   - Establish a shuttle service from major CTA stations to the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

### 4.2 Partnership & Infrastructure Strategies

5. **Leverage the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a Hub Site**
   - Utilize the lab's existing rehab patient population as a screening pool for new trials
   - Co-locate outreach staff with the VA Clinic (Chicago Lakeside) for veteran recruitment
   - Host monthly "Prosthetic Innovation Open Houses" for the amputee community

6. **Cross-Institution Research Network**
   - Form a Chicago-area prosthetic trials consortium (Northwestern, Shirley Ryan, Jesse Brown VA, Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago)
   - Standardize eligibility criteria across sites to enable multi-site recruitment
   - Share de-identified data to accelerate evidence generation

7. **Industry-Academic Partnerships**
   - Engage prosthetic manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Blatchford) as co-sponsors with clear health equity commitments
   - Negotiate affordable prosthetic device access for trial participants who cannot afford their own devices
   - Require demographic diversity plans in all industry-sponsored trial protocols

### 4.3 Equity & Inclusion Strategies

8. **Language & Cultural Accessibility**
   - Provide all trial materials in English, Spanish, and Polish (Chicago's top non-English languages)
   - Hire bilingual research coordinators
   - Partner with ethnic community organizations for outreach

9. **Health Equity Integration**
   - Mandate collection of race, ethnicity, language, insurance status, and neighborhood deprivation index in all trials
   - Establish a Health Equity Advisory Board with amputee community representatives
   - Publish trial demographic results publicly to ensure transparency

10. **Financial Support for Participants**
    - Offer stipends for trial participation (travel, time, lost wages)
    - Provide prosthetic supplies (socket liners, socks) free of charge during trial participation
    - Eliminate insurance requirements for screening visits

### 4.4 Measurement & Evaluation

11. **Track Outreach Effectiveness**
    - Monitor recruitment demographics vs. Chicago amputee population demographics
    - Measure time-from-referral-to-enrollment for underserved groups
    - Report annually on equity metrics to funders and the community

12. **Community Feedback Loop**
    - Conduct quarterly listening sessions with prosthetic users in underserved communities
    - Use feedback to adapt outreach strategies and trial designs
    - Publish community-facing reports in plain language
