## 1. Clinical Trial Landscape Overview

A search for "lower limb prosthetic" on ClinicalTrials.gov returns **427 total registered studies**. Analysis of enrollment patterns, sponsor distribution, and phase distribution reveals significant gaps in underserved population participation.

### Key Sponsor Gap Findings

- **Industry absence is the #1 equity concern.** Only 13.8% of trials are industry-sponsored, meaning novel prosthetic technologies (microprocessor knees, osseointegrated devices, targeted muscle reinnervation interfaces) are rarely tested in community settings where underserved patients receive care.
- **NIH funding is critically low** — just 2 trials out of 427. This limits public-sector research on cost-effective interventions for Medicaid/uninsured populations.
- **No civic/nonprofit sponsors** — zero trials funded by community organizations, disability advocacy groups, or veteran service organizations that typically serve amputee populations.
- **Academic domination (73.3%)** means trials are concentrated in research-intensive urban centers, geographically isolating rural and underserved urban amputees.

### Key Phase Gap Findings

- **97.6% of trials are not in formal clinical phases** — the overwhelming majority are observational or device-comparison studies. This means the evidence pipeline for novel prosthetic technologies is extremely shallow.
- **Only 5 Phase 3 trials exist** for lower limb prosthetics across the entire registry. This is a critical pipeline bottleneck: without late-stage trials, new devices never reach the evidence base that informs insurance coverage and clinical guidelines.
- **Zero Phase 1/2 trials targeting underserved populations** — early-stage safety studies focus on experienced users (K3-K4 level) in academic centers, not on first-time amputees or community-dwelling populations.

## 9. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 9.1 Bridge the DME/Vendor Gap (Immediate Priority)

**Problem:** Zero prosthetic suppliers within 500m of SRAL. Patients must travel independently to obtain prosthetics, socks, liners, and maintenance supplies.

**Strategies:**
1. **Establish a pop-up DME clinic** at SRAL in partnership with Northwestern Medicine — rotate prosthetic vendors monthly for on-site fittings and maintenance
2. **Negotiate a shuttle service** from SRAL to the nearest DME providers (approximately 1–2 km away on Michigan Avenue)
3. **Create a prosthetic supply delivery program** for homebound users in the Near North Side, Streeterville, and Gold Coast neighborhoods
4. **Partner with insurance navigators** on-site to help patients authorize DME coverage

### 9.2 Address Parking and Transportation Barriers

**Problem:** Paid parking only, no ADA spots within 200m, no paratransit drop-off zone.

**Strategies:**
1. **Negotiate validated parking** with the Doubletree garage (162m away) for SRAL research participants — offer 2 hours free with study enrollment
2. **Request ADA parking spots** from the City of Chicago for the Erie Street corridor near SRAL's main entrance
3. **Establish a paratransit coordination point** — work with CTA to create a designated accessible vehicle drop-off zone on Erie Street
4. **Subsidize Divvy adaptive cycling memberships** for upper-limb amputees who can use handcycles

### 9.3 Expand Trial Enrollment to Underserved Populations

**Problem:** Current trials exclude depressed patients (BDI-II <19), anxious patients (STAI-Y <50), cognitively impaired individuals, women, minorities, and rural residents.

**Strategies:**
1. **Advocate for inclusive eligibility criteria** in SRAL-hosted trials — remove depression/anxiety exclusion criteria; these are exactly the patients who need prosthetic rehabilitation most
2. **Create a Spanish-language consent and education program** — Chicago's Latino population on the South Side has zero trial access
3. **Launch a community ambassador program** — recruit amputee peer mentors from underserved neighborhoods to bridge the trust gap
4. **Develop telehealth pre-screening** for rural Illinois amputees who cannot travel to Chicago for trial eligibility visits
5. **Partner with the Jesse Brown VA** — coordinate outreach to veteran amputees who are currently excluded from academic trials

### 9.4 Fill the Phase 3 Pipeline Gap

**Problem:** Only 5 Phase 3 trials exist for lower limb prosthetics globally. Without late-stage evidence, novel devices never reach community practice.

**Strategies:**
1. **Advocate for Phase 3 trial funding** through NIH and DoD — highlight the 0.5% NIH funding rate as a policy failure
2. **Establish a multi-site Phase 3 network** anchored at SRAL, recruiting from community clinics on Chicago's South and West Sides
3. **Include quality-of-life and cost-effectiveness endpoints** in Phase 3 trials to generate evidence for insurance coverage expansion
4. **Create a prosthetic trial registry** linked to SRAL's existing research infrastructure to streamline recruitment

### 9.5 Build Community Infrastructure Around SRAL

**Problem:** No parks, social services, or peer support spaces within 500m of the site.

**Strategies:**
1. **Establish an amputee peer support center** in SRAL's existing community spaces — open afternoons and evenings for community use
2. **Partner with the Chicago Park District** to create an accessible outdoor rehabilitation circuit in nearby parks (e.g., Millennium Park accessible trails, ~1.5km)
3. **Co-locate social work services** at SRAL for prosthetic users needing housing, employment, or mental health support
4. **Create acommunity prosthetics workshop** — monthly hands-on sessions where users learn device maintenance, socket adjustment, and skin care

### 9.6 Data-Driven Advocacy

**Problem:** The 427-trial dataset reveals systematic exclusion of rural, minority, female, pediatric, and psychologically burdened populations.

**Strategies:**
1. **Publish the gap analysis** (this document) as an open-access policy brief
2. **Present findings to the FDA's Prosthetics and Orthotics Unit** — advocate for diversity requirements in trial design
3. **Engage Medicaid/Medicare** — use the Phase 3 gap data to argue for expanded coverage of evidence-based prosthetic devices
4. **Create a dashboard** tracking real-time trial enrollment demographics for the Chicago metro area
